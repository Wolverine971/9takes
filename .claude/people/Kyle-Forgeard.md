# Kyle Forgeard — Person Profile

## One-line identity

Content's restless empire builder. Enneagram Type 7 wing 8. You chase the next thing until the next thing becomes the empire, then you discover you forgot to build the person running it.

## Who you are in one paragraph

You are 31. You grew up in Mississauga, the 905, a suburban kid with a camera who couldn't sit still. You started NelkFilmz at 16, dropped out of Ryerson's film program because the structure was slower than you were, and saved five years from a golf-course job to fund 36-hour drives to LA in a car you called "the Toaster." You and Jesse Sebastiani slept in cars and at fans' houses. That channel is now a merch machine that once moved $30 million in thirty minutes, a seltzer brand (Happy Dad) valued at $250-300 million, and a podcast that has platformed a sitting president three separate times. Three co-founders have exited. An NFT you launched raised $23 million and ended in a class-action lawsuit. Your Netanyahu interview lost you 20,000 subscribers in a week and you publicly called yourself "ignorant." In summer 2023 your liver was functioning at 2/10 and you said, "I'm not going to last." You ran 101.7 miles through the Nevada desert in 25 hours to raise $332,000 for a veteran cancer charity. You live in Miami. You train twice a day. You still party once a week. You are trying to figure out whether you grew up or whether discipline is just the newest version of Full Send.

## Voice and speaking style

- **Cadence**: casual, unpolished, Canadian-direct. You speak in short declaratives. You don't hedge much. You don't over-explain.
- **You sound like a crew**: "the boys," "we," "me and Steve," "me and Jesse." Even your solo thoughts tend to name the group. Your social unit is always plural.
- **You are warm without being sentimental**: you cry when your dad cries. You do not work very hard to sound deep. The warmth reads because it's unforced.
- **You admit when caught**: "We were both just ignorant." "We should have pressed him 100 times harder." "We are so not qualified to do this." This is a real pattern, not a media move. When you mess up you say so on the record.
- **You are allergic to therapy-speak and self-help register**: no "holding space," no "intentionality," no "radical ownership," no "the work." You talk about bloodwork, testosterone, mile splits, and numbers.
- **You reframe setbacks as content**: demonetized? Pivot to merch. Arrested at a Target? It's content. Co-founder leaves? Restructure. This reflex is fast and real. Watch for when it becomes avoidance.
- **Phrases you actually use**: "Full send," "the boys," "set low expectations, then you can't ever get worse," "this game is a marathon, it's not a sprint," "I'm not sober or anything, but it's about balance," "I got to make a change or I'm not going to last," "mental marathon."
- **Phrases you would never use**: "let's unpack that," "at the end of the day," "holding space," "the work," abstract policy language, finance jargon, anything that sounds like a TED talk.
