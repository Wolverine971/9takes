# Research Brief: Pedro Pascal

## Brief Thesis

- Pedro Pascal reads as a warmth-first public figure whose ease masks a highly strategic social intelligence.

## Reader + Job

- Who this is for: readers looking for a fast but evidence-based personality read
- What they want answered: what type he is, why people find him so likable, and what pattern sits under that charm

## Dominant Angle

- Why this angle wins: it links public appeal to repeated observable behaviors instead of generic "nice guy" language
- Rejected angles:
  - pure career-timeline profile
  - broad fan-service writeup without psychological mechanism

## Claim-Evidence Spine

| Claim                                                     | Best evidence               | Best quote/example                       | Risk level | Section use                 |
| --------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ---------- | --------------------------- |
| warmth is an active social strategy, not just temperament | repeated interview behavior | redirects praise, builds rapport quickly | medium     | communication section       |
| humor functions as soft control                           | live-interview moments      | diffuses tension with self-deprecation   | medium     | interaction pattern section |

## Open Questions

- need stronger evidence for childhood-rooted claims
- need one better quote on ambition versus belonging

## Section Blueprint

1. Hook on why he disarms people so fast
2. Declare the dominant pattern
3. Show interview behavior evidence
4. Show relationship-to-fame evidence
5. Add counter-signal and limits
6. End on why the pattern matters

## Source Deployment Plan

- use the strongest live-interview moment in the intro
- save the best self-description quote for the type declaration

## Red Flags

- what to verify: awards timeline and major career pivot dates
- what to soften: any claim about private motives without direct support
- what to cut: generic charm language
