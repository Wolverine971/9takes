## The Core Insight

After cross-analyzing comprehensive debate prep materials from both the left and the right -- each loaded with sourced data, anticipated rebuttals, and honest self-assessments of weakness -- the conclusion is clear:

**Each side is roughly right about 40-50% of issues and stubbornly wrong about the rest.**

The left is right about healthcare, inequality, climate, and reproductive rights. The right is right about enforcement, school choice, government efficiency, and free speech. On almost every issue, the data points toward a synthesis that neither party offers.

The tragedy of American politics is that you have to buy the whole bundle. Here's what you'd get if you didn't.

## 1. Healthcare: Universal Floor + Market Competition on Top

### What the data says

- The US pays **$14,885 per capita** on healthcare vs. **$7,371** OECD average -- nearly double for worse outcomes ([PGPF](https://www.pgpf.org/article/how-does-the-us-healthcare-system-compare-to-other-countries/))
- **36% of US adults** skip or delay care due to cost vs. just 3.6% in the EU ([NCH Stats](https://nchstats.com/us-vs-europe-healthcare/))
- The US spends over **$1,000 per person** on administrative costs alone -- 5x the average of other wealthy countries
- Medicare for All is projected to cost **$25-35 trillion** over 10 years (Urban Institute)

### Where the left is right

The status quo is indefensible. We pay double for worse outcomes. The right's own prep admits healthcare is their weakest issue.

### Where the right is right

Medicare for All at $25-35T is fiscally reckless. Government-run systems do have innovation and efficiency problems. Price transparency and market competition have genuine value.

### The fix

A **public option** (not single-payer) that guarantees a coverage floor for every American -- basic primary care, emergency care, preventive care, prescription drugs. Private insurance competes on top for premium coverage. Simultaneously:

- **Mandate price transparency** -- hospitals must publish real prices. No more surprise bills. (The right's best healthcare idea.)
- **Break the PBM cartel** -- pharmacy benefit managers skim billions as middlemen. Crack down.
- **Allow Medicare to negotiate drug prices** -- already started under the IRA, expand it.
- **Fund the NIH aggressively** -- most pharmaceutical breakthroughs start with government-funded research anyway.
- **Association Health Plans** for small businesses to pool together for coverage.

This is essentially what **Germany, Switzerland, and the Netherlands** do. Universal coverage, private insurers competing for supplemental plans, better outcomes than us at lower cost. Not single-payer. Not pure free market. What works.

**Cost**: Net savings. We're already overpaying by trillions. Eliminating administrative bloat and the emergency-room-as-primary-care dynamic alone saves enormous sums.

## 2. Immigration: Enforce Hard + Legal Pathways Wide Open

### What the data says

- After enforcement policies took effect, border crossings dropped **93%** -- from 249,700/month (Dec 2023) to 8,725/month (May 2025) ([CBP](https://www.cbp.gov/newsroom/stats/nationwide-encounters))
- Undocumented immigrant households paid **$89.8 billion** in federal, state, and local taxes in 2023 ([AIC](https://www.americanimmigrationcouncil.org/press-release/immigrants-keep-economy-strong-as-congress-debates-mass-deportation/))
- **46% of Fortune 500** companies were founded by immigrants or their children
- A 1% increase in migration inflow **increases native-born employment by 0.2%**
- Declining immigration is costing **0.75-1 percentage points of GDP growth** ([Brookings](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/macroeconomic-implications-of-immigration-flows-in-2025-and-2026-january-2026-update/))

### Where the right is right

Enforcement works. The 93% reduction is real. Borders matter. Orderly immigration systems matter.

### Where the left is right

Immigrants are a massive net economic positive. Mass deportation of 11 million people is logistically impossible and economically catastrophic. The legal immigration system is deliberately broken.

### The fix

- **Strong border enforcement.** Walls where terrain warrants it, technology everywhere else, adequate staffing. Crossings outside legal ports of entry should be genuinely difficult.
- **Massively expand legal immigration.** Double or triple the visa cap. Create a functioning guest worker program for agriculture, construction, and hospitality. Reduce processing backlogs from years to months.
- **Fast-track high-skill immigration.** It's insane that we educate the world's best at our universities and then send them home to compete against us.
- **Path to legal status** (not necessarily citizenship) for long-term undocumented residents who've been paying taxes and staying out of trouble.

The through-line: **make legal immigration so easy and efficient that illegal immigration becomes unnecessary.** Then enforce the border without apology.

The US has a demographic problem (aging population, falling birth rate). Immigration is the solution, not the problem. But it has to be orderly.

## 3. Climate & Energy: All-of-the-Above with Nuclear at the Center

### What the data says

- Climate change projected to cost **$38 trillion per year by 2049** ([U of Chicago/Potsdam Institute](https://epic.uchicago.edu/news/climate-change-may-cost-38-trillion-a-year-by-2049-study-says/))
- Climate stressors could cause **14.5 million excess deaths** and **$12.5 trillion in economic losses** by 2050 ([WEF](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2025/10/climate-change-cost-businesses-1-5-trillion-productivity-losses-2050-health-stories/))
- US natural gas displacing coal has been the **single biggest driver** of US emissions reductions
- US has reduced emissions **18% since 2005** -- more than most Paris Agreement signatories
- Solar and wind are now the **cheapest form of new electricity generation** in most of the world

### Where the left is right

Climate inaction is the most expensive policy there is. The cost data is overwhelming. Renewables are now cheaper than fossil fuels.

### Where the right is right

Natural gas has done more to reduce US emissions than any regulation. Killing domestic production just shifts it to countries with worse standards. Innovation has outperformed mandates.

### The fix

- **Nuclear energy as the backbone.** France gets 70% of its electricity from nuclear. Zero-emission, reliable, scalable, safest form of energy per kilowatt-hour. Streamline NRC permitting. Fund small modular reactors. Aim for **30-40% nuclear by 2040**.
- **Continue natural gas as a bridge fuel.** Don't ban fracking -- it's the main reason US emissions dropped 18% since 2005.
- **Accelerate solar and wind deployment.** They're already cheaper. Remove permitting bottlenecks.
- **Carbon tax with 100% dividend return to citizens.** Price the externality, let the market optimize. Every dollar collected goes back as a monthly check, making it progressive. This is the market-based solution the right claims to want.
- **Invest in carbon capture R&D** for industrial emissions that can't be electrified.

The US can be the world's **clean energy superpower AND the world's largest energy producer.** Those aren't contradictory.

## 4. Taxes: Simplify, Close Loopholes, Minimum Effective Rate

### What the data says

- The 400 wealthiest billionaire families pay an average effective tax rate of **8.2%** vs. **13% for average Americans** ([White House / Yale Budget Lab](https://budgetlab.yale.edu/research/who-paying-their-fair-share-taxes-new-analysis-and-interactive-tool))
- Capital gains and business profits are taxed **more favorably than wage earnings**
- Massachusetts's millionaire surtax generated **$5.7 billion** with **no significant outmigration** of the wealthy ([People's Policy Project](https://www.peoplespolicyproject.org/2025/11/17/do-millionaire-surtaxes-lead-to-millionaire-exodus/))
- The 2017 tax cuts did not pay for themselves per CBO, Federal Reserve, and virtually every independent analysis

### Where the left is right

Billionaires paying 8.2% while nurses pay 13% is broken. "Tax cuts pay for themselves" has been tried repeatedly and has never worked.

### Where the right is right

The tax code at 74,000 pages is absurdly complex. Punitive rates drive avoidance. Simplification has real value. Capital gains rates do encourage investment.

### The fix

- **Minimum effective tax rate of 20% on income above $10 million** (including capital gains, carried interest, and other currently favorable categories). Not confiscatory. Not punitive. Just: you can't pay less than your secretary.
- **Treat capital gains more like labor income above a high threshold.** Below $1M in gains: keep the current preferential rate (encourages middle-class investing). Above $1M: tax it as ordinary income.
- **Simplify the code.** Eliminate most deductions and credits in exchange for lower base rates. Most deductions disproportionately benefit the wealthy (mortgage interest, SALT, charitable giving of appreciated assets).
- **Corporate rate at 21-25%.** Competitive globally, not a race to the bottom. Close the loopholes that let profitable companies pay 0%.
- **Index brackets to inflation** so people don't get bracket-crept into higher rates without real income gains.

The "they'll all leave" argument is empirically false. Massachusetts proved it. Rich people live where they want to live.

## 5. Education: Fund Equitably + Allow Choice with Accountability

### What the data says

- Districts serving the most students of color receive **$2,700 less per student** than those with the fewest ([EdLaw Center](https://edlawcenter.org/research/making-the-grade-2025/))
- Nearly 1/3 of states provide **less funding to high-poverty districts** than low-poverty districts
- **23 of 31 research analyses** find positive outcomes from school choice; **none** found overall negative effects ([EdChoice](https://www.edchoice.org/what-the-research-really-says-about-school-choice/))
- Every dollar invested in early childhood education returns **$7-13 in societal benefits**

### Where the left is right

The quality of a child's education depending on the property values in their neighborhood is a policy choice, not an inevitability. Funding inequity is the root cause of most educational disparities.

### Where the right is right

School choice research is overwhelmingly positive. "Funding follows the child" is a compelling principle. Parents shouldn't be trapped by zip code.

### The fix

- **Equalize per-pupil funding within states.** Decouple school funding from property taxes. This is the single most impactful education reform available.
- **Allow school choice** -- charters, magnets, open enrollment, and vouchers -- **with accountability requirements**: standardized testing, financial audits, non-discrimination policies, acceptance of students with disabilities. Public money requires public accountability.
- **Pay teachers more in exchange for easier termination of low performers.** Teaching pays too little to attract top talent and tenure makes it too hard to remove bad teachers. Fix both.
- **Universal pre-K for 3-4 year olds.** The ROI is the highest of any education intervention. This is where early investment has the most impact.

## 6. Guns: High-Consensus Measures, Not Maximalist Positions

### What the data says

- Guns are the **#1 cause of death** for children and teens since 2020 ([The Trace](https://www.thetrace.org/2025/12/data-shooting-stats-gun-violence-america/))
- **408 mass shootings** in 2025 ([Omnilert](https://www.omnilert.com/blog/2025-gun-violence-statistics-show-signs-of-progress-and-reinforce-the-need-for-continued-prevention))
- **4.7% of Americans** have shown a firearm defensively to deter a perceived threat ([JAMA Network Open, 2025](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamanetworkopen/fullarticle/2831507))
- **2,338 Americans** die by firearm suicide every month
- Universal background checks are supported by **80-90% of Americans**, including gun owners

### Where the left is right

Guns being the #1 killer of American children is a crisis. No other wealthy nation comes close to our rate of gun violence. Universal background checks should have been enacted decades ago.

### Where the right is right

Defensive gun use is real and documented. Enforcement of existing laws is inadequate. Law-abiding gun owners are not the problem.

### The fix

- **Universal background checks.** 80-90% support. Close the private sale and gun show loopholes. The most popular unenacted policy in American politics.
- **Red flag laws** (extreme risk protection orders). Family members and law enforcement can petition courts to temporarily remove firearms from someone in crisis. Due process protections built in. Multiple states have these and they work.
- **Fund mental health massively.** Both sides claim to care about this. Actually do it. Crisis centers, school counselors, veterans' mental health, suicide hotlines, community clinics.
- **Enforce existing laws rigorously.** Straw purchases, illegal possession, domestic violence prohibitions. The ATF is chronically underfunded.
- **Don't pursue assault weapon bans.** Politically impossible, definitionally incoherent, and the 1994-2004 ban showed limited measurable effect. Spend the political capital on the high-consensus measures instead.

## 7. Government Efficiency: Permanent, Non-Partisan Accountability

### What the data says

- DOGE uncovered **$382 million in fraudulent unemployment payments** -- including to people with birthdates in the future
- **$312 million** in business loans issued to children 11 and under
- However, **40% of DOGE's claimed contract cuts** don't actually save money ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/02/19/nx-s1-5302705/doge-overstates-savings-federal-contracts))
- Senate investigations found **DOGE itself wasted $21.7B**

### Where the right is right

Government waste is real, shocking, and has been tolerated for too long. Payments to nonexistent people and loans to children are inexcusable. Someone should be looking.

### Where the left is right

A politically motivated operation run by an unelected billionaire with conflicts of interest is not the answer. Chaotic execution and inflated claims undermine the legitimate mission.

### The fix

- **Permanent, non-partisan Government Efficiency Commission** -- like the GAO but with teeth. Staffed by career auditors, not political appointees or billionaire allies.
- **Sunset clauses on every program.** Every federal program must be reauthorized every 10 years with a performance review. If it's not delivering results, it gets restructured or eliminated.
- **Performance-based budgeting.** Agencies get funded based on outcomes, not headcount.
- **Automatic fraud detection.** Modern data systems can catch "payments to people with birth dates in the future" algorithmically. The fact that this fraud existed means nobody was checking. That's a systems problem.

## 8. Criminal Justice: Fund Police AND Reform the System

### What the data says

- The US spends **$300 billion annually** to police and incarcerate **2.2 million people** ([AAF](https://www.americanactionforum.org/research/the-economic-costs-of-the-u-s-criminal-justice-system/), [Prison Policy Initiative](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/reports/pie2025.html))
- Federal incarceration costs **$43,000-$130,000 per person per year**
- California's sentencing reforms showed recidivism **as low as 3%** within a year for certain populations ([CalMatters](https://calmatters.org/justice/2025/09/california-prisons-recidivism-study/))
- The 2020-2022 crime spike occurred across ALL jurisdictions (reform and non-reform) and has been declining since

### Where the left is right

Highest incarceration rate in the world without being the safest country is a policy failure. Spending $130K/year to warehouse non-violent offenders instead of rehabilitating them is both cruel and fiscally insane.

### Where the right is right

The 2020-2022 crime spike was real. People want to feel safe. Police need to be funded and supported.

### The fix

- **Fund police generously** with requirements for training, de-escalation, mental health response capacity, and accountability (body cameras, use-of-force databases, independent oversight).
- **Mental health co-responders** on 911 calls involving mental health crises. Police shouldn't be the default mental health system.
- **Reduce mandatory minimums for non-violent drug offenses.** Drug courts and treatment programs cost a fraction and have lower recidivism.
- **Invest heavily in reentry programs.** Job training, transitional housing, mentorship. Recidivism is the engine that keeps incarceration numbers high.
- **Keep violent offenders locked up.** Reform doesn't mean leniency for violent crime. Draw a clear, bright line.

## 9. Debt & Fiscal Policy: Revenue AND Spending Discipline

### What the data says

- National debt: **$37.6 trillion** (US Treasury)
- Debt-to-GDP: **99.8%** -- nearly double the 50-year average ([CBO](https://www.cbo.gov/publication/61307))
- Interest payments: **$1.2 trillion/year** -- more than defense spending
- CBO projects deficits exceeding **$2 trillion annually** over the next decade
- Neither party has been fiscally responsible. Both contribute to the problem.

### Where the right is right

Spending is unsustainable. Interest payments exceeding defense spending is a crisis. Structural reform is needed.

### Where the left is right

You can't solve the debt from spending cuts alone. Revenue has been systematically undermined by tax cuts that didn't pay for themselves. Cutting social programs while cutting taxes for the wealthy is not fiscal discipline.

### The fix

- **Tax reforms above** (minimum effective rate, capital gains reform, closing loopholes) generate significant new revenue.
- **Healthcare reform** saves money -- we're currently overpaying by trillions.
- **Spending caps tied to GDP growth.** Government spending can grow, but not faster than the economy.
- **Social Security and Medicare reform.** Neither side wants to touch this, but the math is inescapable:
  - Raise the Social Security income cap (currently only the first $168,600 is taxed)
  - Means-test Medicare for high-income retirees
  - Gradually raise the retirement age to 69 over 20 years to reflect increased life expectancy
- **Pentagon audit.** The DoD has never passed a full audit. It gets $886B/year. Find the waste. There is a lot.

## 10. Reproductive Rights: Where Most Americans Already Are

### What the data says

- **63%** say abortion should be legal in most or all cases ([PRRI](https://prri.org/press-release/new-50-state-survey-data-more-than-six-in-ten-americans-continue-supporting-abortion-legality/))
- Only **13%** say it should be illegal in all circumstances ([Gallup](https://news.gallup.com/poll/321143/americans-stand-abortion.aspx))
- **39% of Republicans** support abortion legality
- **Every ballot measure** on abortion since Dobbs has gone in favor of reproductive rights -- including in Kansas, Kentucky, Montana, and Ohio

### The fix

Codify Roe-era standards at the federal level:

- **Legal on demand through ~15 weeks** (consistent with most European democracies)
- **Available later for medical necessity** (fetal anomaly, life/health of mother) with physician sign-off
- Simultaneously invest in everything that actually **reduces** abortion rates: contraception access, comprehensive sex education, paid family leave, and support for new parents

This is the position that roughly 60-65% of Americans hold when polled on specifics. Stop letting both extremes define the debate.

## 11. Voting: Easy to Vote, Hard to Cheat

### What the data says

- After the VRA was gutted, the turnout gap between Black and white voters widened ([SAGE Journals](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00219347251346186))
- More restrictive voting laws were enacted in 2024 than any year except 2021 ([Brennan Center](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/voting-laws-roundup-2024-review))
- Noncitizen voter fraud is already illegal and **extremely rare**
- **65% of Americans** say they are afraid to speak freely ([Freedom Forum](https://www.freedomforum.org/free-speech-facing-threats-2025/))

### The fix

- **Automatic voter registration** at 18 via SSA records.
- **National voter ID** -- but free, easy to obtain, and with a robust system for those without standard documentation.
- **Early voting** (minimum 2 weeks) and vote-by-mail in every state.
- **Paper ballot backup** for every electronic vote. Auditable trails.
- **Independent redistricting commissions** in every state. End partisan gerrymandering from both sides.

This satisfies the right's election integrity concerns AND the left's voter access concerns. The reason neither side proposes this package is that each benefits from the current system's flaws.

## 12. Trade: Strategic Protection, Not Blanket Tariffs

### What the data says

- **81% of CEOs** plan to bring supply chains closer to home
- But only **2% of companies** have completed reshoring plans
- Blue-collar employment actually **fell by 59,000 jobs** under tariff policies
- COVID exposed dangerous supply chain dependence on China for pharmaceuticals and critical minerals

### Where the right is right

Dependence on adversaries for critical goods is a national security risk. China's trade practices (IP theft, currency manipulation, state subsidies) are genuinely unfair.

### Where the left is right

Blanket tariffs are a regressive tax on working families. They raise prices on the people who can least afford it. Promised manufacturing jobs haven't materialized.

### The fix

- **Tariffs on national security items only**: semiconductors, rare earth minerals, defense-critical materials, pharmaceuticals.
- **Free trade on everything else.** Consumer goods tariffs are a tax on working families.
- **Invest in domestic manufacturing through R&D subsidies and workforce training** -- the CHIPS Act model is better than the blanket tariff model.
- **Aggressive trade enforcement** against IP theft, currency manipulation, and dumping -- through WTO and bilateral mechanisms, not unilateral tariff wars that invite retaliation against American agriculture.

## The Meta-Point

The frustrating truth that emerges from cross-analyzing both sides' best data:

**The evidence-based position on most issues is a synthesis that neither party offers.**

Poll every position in this document individually and you'd get 55-70% support on most of them. The reason this platform doesn't exist in American politics isn't because it's unpopular. It's because:

1. **The two-party system forces bundling.** You can't vote for border enforcement + universal healthcare + school choice + climate investment. You get one bundle or the other.
2. **The primary system rewards extremes.** Politicians who synthesize get attacked from both flanks. Moderates lose primaries.
3. **Media incentives favor conflict.** "Both sides have a point" doesn't generate clicks. "The other side is evil" does.
4. **Donor incentives favor ideology.** The pharmaceutical lobby doesn't fund candidates who want price transparency. The teachers' unions don't fund candidates who want school choice accountability. The fossil fuel industry doesn't fund candidates who want carbon pricing.

The positions above aren't centrist in the sense of splitting every difference. Some lean clearly left on the evidence (healthcare, climate, taxes, reproductive rights). Some lean clearly right (enforcement, school choice, government efficiency, free speech). The thread that connects them isn't moderation -- it's **following the data wherever it leads, even when it's inconvenient for your team.**

An AI president's biggest advantage: no tribe, no donors, no primary to win. Just: what does the evidence say, and what would help the most people?
