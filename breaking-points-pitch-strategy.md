# Pitch Strategy: Getting Breaking Points to Cover the AI President Platform

_Prepared: February 21, 2026_

---

## Why This Is a Perfect Fit for Them

Your document is literally their thesis stated back to them with better data. Their show's founding premise — "hate each other less, hate the ruling class more" — is exactly what your AI President platform demonstrates empirically. The meta-point about bundling, primaries, media incentives, and donor capture is their entire editorial mission.

The problem isn't relevance. It's cutting through the noise. Here's how.

---

## Pitching Saagar (Type 7 - The Enthusiast)

**What drives him:** Novelty, discovery, the thrill of finding something nobody else has seen yet. He brought a Hitler biography as a podcast gift because he _couldn't contain himself_. He films 3,000-word monologues in one take. He's a serial obsessor who chases the next intellectual fire.

**His trigger:** Frame this as a _discovery_, not a pitch. Saagar doesn't respond to "please cover my thing." He responds to "look what I found."

**The hook for Saagar:**

> "I gave AI comprehensive debate prep for both sides — sourced data, honest self-assessments of weakness, anticipated rebuttals — then asked it to cross-analyze and build a platform based purely on evidence. The result: each side is right about 40-50% of issues and stubbornly wrong about the rest. The synthesis that emerges polls at 55-70% on every individual position. The reason it doesn't exist isn't because it's unpopular — it's because of four structural forces that make it impossible."

That last part — the four structural forces (bundling, primaries, media incentives, donor capture) — is the intellectual bait. Saagar is a systems thinker. He wants to understand _why_ the machine is broken, not just _that_ it's broken. Your meta-point section is the real hook for him.

**His 6 wing angle:** Saagar's security-focused wing responds to threats. Frame the _inability_ to get evidence-based policy as a threat to the country. "The structural forces that prevent this synthesis are the actual national security risk — not any individual policy failure."

**Channel for Saagar:** Twitter/X. He's @esaagar and highly active. A thread that teases the methodology and the most counterintuitive findings (e.g., "the right's own prep admits healthcare is their weakest issue" or "the left's own prep admits immigration is their biggest liability") would catch his eye. Saagar loves when someone does the work he would have done himself.

---

## Pitching Krystal (Type 1 - The Reformer)

**What drives her:** Moral clarity. The gap between how things _are_ and how they _should_ be. She traces incentives upstream. "How the hell does that happen?" is her operating question. She channels anger through evidence, never through tantrums.

**Her trigger:** Frame this as a _moral indictment of the system_, not as clever analysis. Krystal doesn't care that the synthesis is intellectually elegant. She cares that real people are harmed by the fact that it doesn't exist.

**The hook for Krystal:**

> "63% of Americans support reproductive rights. 80-90% support universal background checks. Supermajorities want a public option. The evidence-based position on most issues is a synthesis that 55-70% of Americans already agree with — and the reason it doesn't exist is because the donor class, the primary system, and the media incentive structure make it impossible. The two-party system isn't failing to represent people. It's _succeeding_ at representing donors."

That last reframe — "succeeding at representing donors" — is Krystal's language. She traces the baby bottle to the lobbyist. She traces the Ohio River Valley to NAFTA. Give her the chain to follow.

**Her contempt sensitivity:** Krystal's deepest value is refusing to treat people with contempt. Your document does something she'd respect: it takes both sides' _best_ arguments seriously. It says "the left is right about healthcare" AND "the right is right about enforcement." It doesn't condescend to either side. Lead with that. "This document takes both sides at their strongest — sourced data, not straw men — and finds the synthesis neither party will offer because of structural incentives."

**Channel for Krystal:** Email or DM via Breaking Points contact. Krystal is more deliberate than Saagar. She doesn't chase novelty — she follows the thread when the moral stakes are clear. A concise, direct email that leads with the working-class angle ("the people who'd benefit most from this synthesis are the ones both parties abandoned") would resonate.

## The 9takes Angle — Your Secret Weapon

You've already written personality profiles for both of them. This is a relationship opener, not just content.

**How to use it:**

1. **Don't lead with the profiles.** If you open with "I did your Enneagram type and here's what I found," it feels like flattery or a gimmick. The platform document is the substance. The profiles are the relationship builder.

2. **Lead with the platform, then follow up with the profiles.** After the initial pitch, if either engages at all, _then_ you can say: "By the way — I run a personality-based platform called 9takes. I wrote analyses of both of you as part of understanding how to frame political analysis for different personality types. Happy to share if you're curious." This turns the profiles into a demonstration of your methodology rather than a pickup line.

3. **The meta-pitch:** Your actual business (9takes — personality-based Q&A, see the emotions behind every take) is philosophically aligned with what Breaking Points does. They show left and right reacting differently to the same news. You show 9 personality types reacting differently to the same question. If you ever get a conversation going, the platform itself is a segment-worthy concept.

## Tactical Playbook

### Step 1: Twitter Thread (Targeting Saagar)

- Post the AI President platform as a thread or long-form post
- Lead with the methodology: "I gave AI both sides' best debate prep — sourced, honest about weaknesses — and asked it to build a platform based purely on evidence."
- Hit the most surprising data points (billionaires paying 8.2%, border crossings down 93%, school choice 23/31 studies positive)
- End with the meta-point about why this platform can't exist in the current system
- Tag @esaagar and @krystalball
- Quote something from the document that echoes their own thesis: "What if we hated each other less, and the elites more"

### Step 2: Direct Outreach (Targeting Krystal)

- Email Breaking Points through their contact/pitch form
- Subject line: "Evidence-based policy platform that 55-70% of Americans already agree with — and why neither party will ever offer it"
- Keep it to 3-4 paragraphs max
- Lead with the moral stakes, not the methodology
- Include the document as an attachment or link
- One line at the end: "I run a personality-based analysis platform and have written about both of you. Happy to discuss."

### Step 3: The Proof of Concept

- If either responds, offer this: "I'd be happy to come on or contribute a written piece. The methodology itself is replicable — you could do this with any policy debate and get the same kind of synthesis."
- This positions you as a _contributor_, not a supplicant

---

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't pitch it as "AI wrote this."** That undersells it. The value is the methodology — forcing both sides to steelman their positions and then cross-analyzing. AI was the tool, not the author.
- **Don't send the full 3 documents unsolicited.** The platform doc alone is the pitch. The debate preps are supporting evidence if they ask.
- **Don't lead with 9takes or the personality profiles.** That's second-date material.
- **Don't be deferential.** Saagar respects people who've done the work. Krystal respects people who say what they mean. "I think you'd find this interesting" is stronger than "I'd be honored if you'd look at this."

---

## The One-Line Pitches (If You Get 10 Seconds)

**For Saagar:** "I cross-analyzed both sides' best debate data and built the platform neither party will ever offer — and the structural reasons why are more interesting than the policies themselves."

**For Krystal:** "I forced both sides to be honest about their weakest issues, and the synthesis that emerged is what 60% of Americans already believe — and the reason it doesn't exist is donor capture, primary incentives, and media economics."

Both designed to make them want to read the next sentence.
