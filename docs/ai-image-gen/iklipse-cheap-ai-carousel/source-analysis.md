## 2. The reusable skeleton (this post's beats)

Same engine as the "Weird AI" deck, run in **diagnostic mode** (name the flaw → prescribe the fix) rather than generative mode:

1. **Accusation as the title** — not "why X works" but "why YOUR thing is broken." Second-person indictment. Higher-stakes hook; it pokes an identity fear (looking cheap/amateur).
2. **Recognition + mystery** — "everyone can sense it, nobody can name it." This is the strongest beat in the deck: it promises the viewer the power to _name_ what others only feel. Open loop closed by the rest of the carousel.
3. **Diagnostic grid** — a 4-part "here's what's wrong" taxonomy, each with a one-line symptom. Same job as the "Weird AI" word-banks: the screenshot-able value. Closes with "most fail in one of these" so the viewer self-diagnoses.
4. **The reframe** — flips problem→desire ("premium feels human / cheap feels generated, not captured"). The one-line antithesis is the quotable.
5. **The formula slide** — a 3-tier **pyramid** (hierarchical, not additive). Compresses the system into one image; the "save this" peak.
   6–8. **One proof slide per layer** — each pyramid tier gets a full-bleed image + a one-line knife ("perfect skin is one of the fastest ways to expose AI"). Catches skimmers and adds depth.
6. **Payoff + comment-bait CTA** — restate ("now you know how to fix it"), trade keyword "REAL" for a checklist DM.

## 3. Visual / production DNA

- **Consistent chrome** top + bottom (brand, theme tag, URL) — identical system to the sibling deck.
- **One accent color** (coral/red) on the CTA gradient; photo slides are a moody cinematic grade, diagram slides are clean white.
- **Alternating rhythm:** moody full-bleed photo ↔ clean white diagram. Photos carry emotion, diagrams carry logic. The eye never fatigues.
- **Two inverted light slides** (grid + pyramid) as the logical peaks / save moments.
- **Type pairing:** heavy sans headline + **serif italic** subhead, every slide. Italic reserved for the subhead/swipe line.
- Imagery is on-theme proof: each photo _is_ an example of the layer being taught (the skin-texture slide literally shows real pores).
- 1:1 square, text top-weighted to survive the feed crop.

## 4. The transferable insight

Where the "Weird AI" deck teaches you to _make_ something, this one teaches you to _detect_ something — then fix it. Same revelation framing ("here's the hidden rule behind a thing you thought was intuition"), but the diagnostic mode is even more native to 9takes: **"you can feel when you've been misread, but you can't name why"** is the exact emotional truth the product solves. The pyramid's priority order (Emotion on top) also ports cleanly — for 9takes, emotion is likewise the top layer, the Nine Lenses are the technique layer, and Give-First is the small mechanic that proves the read is real.
