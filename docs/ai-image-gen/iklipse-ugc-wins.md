This is a **hybrid: teaching framework + prompt pack.** The first 3 slides are highly transferable (authenticity thesis + a 4-axis controllable framework); the back 8 are tool-specific prompt recipes (lower transfer, but the **repeating 4-axis breakdown table** is a strong consistency device).

## Highest-value extractables

- **The authenticity thesis**: "polished = unbelievable; raw/imperfect = trust." This is a **contrarian, on-brand** angle — and it's the inverse of "premium" decks, showing iklipse runs both "make it premium" and "make it raw" depending on goal.
- **The 4-axis framework (Camera/Lighting/Environment/Behavior)** is reused as a breakdown grid on every example — a clean, repeatable analysis lens.
- **"Stop making it look like an ad"** — a sharp, quotable CTA line.

## 9takes translation

The authenticity thesis maps directly onto give-first / real-takes: **"Polished opinions are fake. Real reactions feel messy — and that's what people trust."** A 9takes re-skin:

- Hook: _"Your hot take sounds smart, and that's the problem."_
- Reframe: _"Polished = performative. Honest = a little ugly."_
- Framework: control 4 things for a real take — **Situation / Trigger / Emotion / Tell** (instead of Camera/Lighting/Environment/Behavior).
- Examples: the same situation answered "performatively" vs "honestly," with a 4-axis breakdown.
- CTA: _"Stop performing. Comment your real take."_
  This deck's "raw beats polished" stance is a strong counter-programming option to the more polished flagship decks — and it rhymes with the give-first mechanic (messy, real contributions over performed ones).
