## 2. The reusable skeleton (9-beat teaching engine)

Strip the topic away and this is the structure worth stealing:

1. **Counterintuitive thesis as the title** — "Why [unexpected thing] works." A curiosity gap, not a how-to. The word that does the work is the surprising one ("weird").
2. **The cognitive mechanism** — explain _why the brain responds_. Here: an open loop / puzzle the mind wants to close.
3. **The reframe** — kill the obvious misreading ("it's not about being strange") and raise the stakes to _meaning_. This is the slide that separates a gimmick from a craft.
4. **Numbered rules, each shipping a word-bank** — the concrete taxonomy is the value (Cute+Dangerous; Power, Desire, Loneliness…). Word-banks are screenshot-able and save-able.
5. **A guillotine rule** — one ruthless _subtractive_ line ("if it doesn't add meaning, remove it"). Signals taste, not just accumulation.
6. **Restate the principle from a new angle** — "the weirdness should have a job." Catches skimmers who missed beat 3.
7. **A formula / diagram on one bright slide** — the visual peak, the "save this" frame. Compresses the whole deck into one image.
8. **A pre-flight checklist** — converts philosophy into 3 questions usable in 10 seconds. This is the practical takeaway people screenshot.
9. **Payoff + comment-bait CTA** — restate the thesis _with the "because,"_ then trade a keyword for "more frameworks."

## 3. Visual / production DNA

- **Consistent chrome** top + bottom on every slide (brand, theme tag, URL). Builds a recognizable format.
- **One accent color** (red) across an otherwise moody, cinematic, near-monochrome set.
- **One inverted light slide** for the formula → gives the deck a visual peak and a clear "save" moment.
- **Serif italic** reserved for "swipe" / payoff moments; heavy sans for headlines.
- Imagery is on-theme proof-of-concept: every photo is itself an example of the rule being taught.
- 1:1 square, text top-weighted so it survives the feed crop.

## 4. The transferable insight

The engine is not "AI images." It's: **take something people treat as random or intuitive, and prove it runs on rules.** That framing is what makes the deck feel like a revelation instead of a tip list.

For 9takes this ports directly, because _reading people_ is the canonical "everyone thinks it's intuition, but it actually runs on patterns" topic. The carousel's organizing force — contrast and contradiction — is literally the 9takes product (one situation, nine reactions, the gap between them is the insight).
