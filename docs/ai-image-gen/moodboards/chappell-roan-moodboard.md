## The through-line

The armor has a dead man's name on it — every spectacle is self-protection performed under a name she borrowed from the grandfather she lost, and she is strong everywhere except love.

## Person overview

- **The world punishes softness, so build the fortress.** An angry, depressed queer kid in an Ozarks town that taught her she was a sin — the Type 8 was forged there.
- **Every wound becomes ammunition.** Overheard "she's pretty but not hot" → "Hot to Go." Her own words: "The bad things that happen to me, I can monetize."
- **Kayleigh vs. Chappell.** Offstage: modest, blacks and tans, Fortnite. Onstage: "a drag queen, whether you like it or not." She talks about the gap openly instead of pretending the armor is all there is.
- **Catholic guilt is the engine under the armor.** Her deepest fear isn't failure — it's "feeling like a bad person… and then still realizing I'm actually bad."
- **Power with a direction.** Grammy stage → a labor demand. A critic → a $25,000 donation. The 8 _uses_ strength, doesn't just have it.
- **The fortress cracks at intimacy.** The woman who creates a "force field" once threw away her personality for a four-year relationship — Type 8 stress-line toward 2, in public.

## Generating in ChatGPT (tool + strategy)

These prompts are written for **ChatGPT image generation (GPT Image)**, not Midjourney. Key rules baked into every prompt below:

- **Format, not flags.** No `--ar`. Each prompt is a labeled production brief (scene → subject → details → camera → light → color/finish → constraints → size) and ends with **"square 1:1 (1024×1024)."**
- **Photorealistic + real texture.** Each opens with "photorealistic" and asks for real imperfections (skin pores, fabric wear, dust, 35mm film grain) to fight GPT's glossy look.
- **Beat the yellow-tint bias (protects our era contrast).** GPT Image pushes a global warm cast — which would flatten our cold eras into the same gold as the warm ones. So amber lives **only as a local light source** (a window shaft, a lamp, the bar glow, a gate sign), and every cold shot explicitly forbids a **global** warm/yellow grade and pins the shadows cool grey-blue. The amber signature survives; the era contrast survives.
- **Beat "too dark."** Low-key shots (CD, backstage, club, terminal) name the single light source and demand "shadow detail retained" so they don't render muddy.
- **Beat "too many subjects."** Crowds (the park, the live-show crowd, the airport phones) are specified as a **blurred, undetailed mass**, never many rendered faces — GPT's known weak spot.
- **Never render her face.** AI can't land a real likeness, so no shot shows her face — every one is POV, turned, distant, obscured, or (Shot 5) hidden by hair and hands with the identity carried by tells (red-orange curls, wardrobe, setting). Never name her either.
- **Workflow:** generate Shot 1, approve it, then **upload that frame as a style reference** for Shots 2–6 to lock the grain/amber/finish across the set. Refine conversationally (regenerate; don't overload one prompt). Captions are added **after** generation (bottom-right overlay) — never ask GPT to render text in-image.
