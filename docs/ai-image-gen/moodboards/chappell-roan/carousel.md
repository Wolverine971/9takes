## The architecture (why it works)

> **Slide 1: see _her_** (face, name, type) → **Slide 2: the facts that don't add up** → **Slides 3–8: go behind her eyes** (POV shots, no face — you _become_ her) → **Slide 9: the Type 8 reveal** → **Slide 10: the mirror turns to _you_.**

The face appears **once**, on the cover; then we drop behind her eyes for the whole story. That's the product thesis — "see the emotions behind every take" — built into the slide order. The cover is the glossy **persona**; the POV shots are the **real** story underneath. Stylized → real is the emotional turn.

## Custom theme — "Midwest Princess"

- **Brand chrome stays amber** (the series constant): mono name font, corner brackets, `9takes` / `THE FULL READ → 9takes.com` / `Analysis by DJ Wayne`.
- **Chappell's per-person accent = hot pink / magenta** (her signature; plays the role purple plays for Lana/Type 4). Use it for the **eye-bar**, the `TYPE 8 · THE CHALLENGER` label, and the cover vibe.
- **Base = black.** Amber + pink read against it; never let pink become the primary (brand rule: amber primary, accent only).
- POV slides (3–8) keep their cinematic real-photo look + a bottom-third dark scrim for the amber caption.

### Cover composite layout (Canva)

The background's center is a dark magenta-velvet negative space — drop everything there. The four corners are busy/bright, so text over them needs help.

1. **Face cutout:** remove the white background first (Canva bg remover), then center it in the dark column with her head around 35–45% down. Keep (or recolor → pink) the eye-bar. Scale so the glittery border elements still frame her.
2. **Name (`CHAPPELL ROAN` / `[ PERSONALITY ANALYSIS ]`):** top of frame — but the top is busy (disco ball, roses, hat), so add a subtle top-down dark gradient behind the name so amber text reads. Alternatively float it in the dark center, just above her head.
3. **`TYPE 8 · THE CHALLENGER` (bottom-left)** sits over the pink pony, and **`9takes` (bottom-right)** sits over the car/disco ball — give each a small dark scrim pill or a soft drop shadow so they don't get lost in the glitter.
