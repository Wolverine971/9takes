## The through-line

The man who read everyone built a fortress of paper to avoid reading himself — and watched it collapse in a single second.

## Person overview

- **Type 5 (5w4)** — turns a frightening world into something survivable by _studying_ it; safety = knowledge stockpiled in advance.
- The **wound**: a father who hid an entire inner life for 40 years; the world's great reader never read the man across the table.
- **Twenty years in the wilderness** (≈80 jobs, suicidal before 38) — exclusion was the tuition for his one subject: power.
- The "gift" is a **manual system** — 300–400 books and 3–4,000 color-coded index cards per project.
- Even **desire** he studied from the outside (_The Art of Seduction_, 2001) — the cold cartographer mapping heat.
- **2018 stroke** erased the fortress; the man who mapped power can't make his own breakfast — and found a strange peace in it.

## Shots (palette + story + caption + prompt)

### Shot 1 — The father's secret · Era: Origin

**Palette:** Mood tender / unknowing · World faded 1960s LA living room · Time golden late afternoon · Styling sample case, hidden notebook, net curtains · Light soft golden window haze · Finish faded old photograph, warm grain.

**Why this shot:** The wound the whole analysis turns on. Greene decoded everyone's hidden motives — yet the person closest to him hid an entire inner life in plain sight for 40 years, and Greene never read it. Shot as a faded memory because that's how it lives in him: understood too late.

**Overlay caption** (bottom-right, add after generation):

> Robert Greene's father sold cleaning supplies for 40 years.
> Only after he died did the family learn he had secretly
> wanted to be a journalist. The man who taught the world
> to read people never read his own father.

**Prompt:**
`A faded 1960s memory: a tired traveling salesman father in shirtsleeves sets a heavy case of cleaning-supply samples down by the door of a modest Los Angeles living room, his face turned away; a hidden leather notebook of unwritten journalism lies forgotten on the side table while a young boy watches quietly from the hallway. Slightly high angle, medium shot, 85mm. Soft golden afternoon light through net curtains, hazy and warm, colors faded like an old photograph, fine grain. --ar 1:1`
_Vary: the light — also try candlelit and cold blue-dawn versions of the same memory._ · _Kill rule: keep only the frame where the notebook reads as hidden/secret, not displayed._ · _Refine with /midjourney-prompt._

### Shot 2 — The closed door · Era: The wilderness

**Palette:** Mood excluded / bruised · World cold institutional corridor · Time grey daytime · Styling heavy office door, worn linoleum · Light hard cold window light, long shadow · Finish flat, slightly desaturated, grain.

**Why this shot:** For two decades, power happened on the other side of doors that kept shutting on him. Nearly 80 jobs, directors cutting his name from scripts, a boss who destroyed him for showing emotion — every closed door was reconnaissance he didn't yet know how to use. We put the camera in his eyes, always on the outside.

**Overlay caption** (bottom-right, add after generation):

> By 38, Robert Greene had held nearly 80 jobs —
> construction in Greece, a hotel desk in Paris, chasing
> debtors in LA — and was having suicidal thoughts.
> Twenty years of doors closing taught him power.

**Prompt:**
`First-person point of view looking down a dim institutional hallway at a heavy closed office door, a hand reaching toward the handle but not yet touching it — the feeling of being shut out of a job. Eye-level POV, 35mm. Hard cold light from a far window casting a long shadow across the floor, a single warm bulb glowing amber further down the hall. Muted grey-amber tones, fine grain. --ar 1:1`
_Vary: the angle — also try a low angle so the door looms, and a tighter shot on just the handle._ · _Kill rule: keep only the frame that feels like exclusion, not arrival._ · _Refine with /midjourney-prompt._

### Shot 3 — The notecards · Era: The cartographer

**Palette:** Mood still / obsessive / controlled · World book-walled study · Time late night · Styling 4×6 color-coded cards, fountain pen, towers of books · Light single warm desk-lamp pool in black · Finish low-key, warm grain.

**Why this shot:** Everyone calls his insight a gift. It's a system — a brutal, manual one. A Type 5 builds a fortress of knowledge against a world that feels unsafe, and this is what that fortress looks like up close. We stand at his shoulder so the viewer sees what he sees: "reading people" is really 4,000 cards.

**Overlay caption** (bottom-right, add after generation):

> For every book, Robert Greene reads 300–400 others and
> writes 3,000–4,000 index cards by hand — blue for politics,
> yellow for war, green for the arts, pink for strategy.
> The "gift" was always a system.

**Prompt:**
`Over-the-shoulder view from behind and slightly above a man hunched at a wooden desk in a study walled with books, looking down at thousands of color-coded 4x6 index cards fanned across the desk in blue, yellow, green and pink, a fountain pen in his hand mid-note. 50mm. A single warm amber desk lamp pools light over the cards while the rest of the room falls into darkness. Late night, low-key, fine grain. --ar 1:1`
_Vary: distance — also try an extreme close-up on one hand + one card, and a wider shot showing the whole study._ · _Kill rule: keep only the frame where the cards read as an obsessive system, not clutter._ · _Refine with /midjourney-prompt._

### Shot 4 — Mapping heat · Era: The "spicy" era

**Palette:** Mood sensual / curious / watchful-from-the-cold · World candlelit salon, deep reds and golds, velvet · Time evening · Styling candles, wine, an open notebook in shadow · Light warm candle glow on the scene, cool shadow on the observer · Finish rich warm film stock, grain.

**Why this shot:** The palette break that proves the point — and it's true to him. The famously reserved Greene wrote _The Art of Seduction_ by studying desire the way he studied war: from the outside, as a system. We let the room go warm and sensual, but keep _him_ in the cold edge of it, taking notes. Even heat, he maps from a distance.

**Overlay caption** (bottom-right, add after generation):

> In 2001, Greene followed The 48 Laws of Power with
> The Art of Seduction — cataloguing desire the way he
> catalogued war: from a careful, watchful distance.

**Prompt:**
`From a dim, cool corner of a candlelit salon, watching a sensual scene unfold across the room — two elegant figures leaning close in warm golden candlelight, deep red velvet and spilled wine — while in the shadowed foreground an open notebook and pen catch a sliver of the glow. The observer studies desire from the cold edge of the warmth. Over-the-shoulder from the shadows, 85mm with compression. Warm candle glow on the distant figures, cool shadow in the foreground. Rich warm film stock, fine grain. --ar 1:1`
_Vary: the warmth — also try fuller candlelight flooding toward the corner, and a tighter shot on just the notebook against the blurred lovers._ · _Kill rule: keep only the frame where the observer reads as studying, not partaking._ · _Refine with /midjourney-prompt._

### Shot 5 — The stroke · Era: The rupture — THE BRIGHT PEAK

**Palette:** Mood shattered / weightless · World no room — pure subjective space · Time the timeless instant · Styling none, just the splitting self · Light blown-out white-out, faint warm edges · Finish overexposed, ghosted multiple-exposure.

**Why this shot:** The man who spent his life building control had it erased in one second — by something as small as a wasp. And in that second he _experienced_ his own thesis: the self is not one thing. He felt himself split into competing selves, time stretching, certain he had died. The fortress fell. We render his subjective experience, not the hospital.

**Overlay caption** (bottom-right, add after generation):

> In 2018, a wasp sting led to a stroke that killed the
> left side of his body. As it happened, Greene felt himself
> splinter into "multiple competing selves," one minute
> stretching into ten. He was sure he had died.

**Prompt:**
`A subjective near-death hallucination: a man's body fracturing into several overlapping, translucent versions of himself, all dissolving toward a blinding white light, the sensation of dying as one minute stretches into ten. Dutch-tilted wide view, 24mm with slight distortion. Overexposed, blown-out white light flooding the frame, faint warm amber edges at the rim. Disorienting, weightless, fine grain. --ar 1:1`
_Vary: the multiplicity — also try 3 selves vs 7, and a version where the white-out is just beginning to swallow the frame._ · _Kill rule: keep only the frame where the white reads as transcendent/near-death, not a lighting error._ · _This is the **one** bright frame; every other shot is dark. Maps to the deck's single inverted slide._ · _Refine with /midjourney-prompt._

### Shot 6 — The breakfast · Era: After

**Palette:** Mood humbled / still · World large quiet modern kitchen · Time cold morning · Styling a single plate, an empty counter · Light flat cold even morning light, one warm appliance glow · Finish muted, minimalist, grain.

**Why this shot:** The closing irony, and the most human frame. The world's foremost authority on power — the strategies of kings, generals, seducers — defeated by a kitchen. Power over everything except his own left hand. We make him small in a big room so the viewer feels the humbling, and let his own line carry the strange grace he found in it.

**Overlay caption** (bottom-right, add after generation):

> Years later, the man who wrote the book on power still
> can't make his own breakfast. "Losing that half of my
> personality," he said, "de-stressed me a lot."

**Prompt:**
`A small lone man stands helpless before a single plate on the counter of a large, quiet modern kitchen — the writer who mapped power, now unable to make his own breakfast after a stroke. High overhead angle looking down, very wide shot emphasizing how small he is in the room. 35mm. Flat, cold, even morning light with one warm amber appliance glow in a corner. Muted, still, fine grain. --ar 1:1`
_Vary: the angle — also try a high (not full bird's-eye) angle and an eye-level wide._ · _Kill rule: keep only the frame where the figure reads as small/humbled by the room._ · _Refine with /midjourney-prompt._
