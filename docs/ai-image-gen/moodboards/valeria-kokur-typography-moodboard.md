## Who she is

Lisbon-based graphic & brand designer (also @crafta.girl on TikTok, "designing my life in Lisbon"). Studio @kokur.studio (~15K followers) — branding, web, packaging, social. Known for **typographic / letterform experiments built from everyday materials**: display type as the hero, playful but disciplined.

## The through-line

**The font you're looking for is already on your kitchen counter.** She doesn't _select_ type — she _builds_ letterforms out of ordinary stuff (spaghetti, ketchup, rice, oil, mayonnaise) and shoots them as hero type. The charm is the collision: mundane material, rigorous letterform. Craft hiding inside the casual.

## How 9takes uses this — cover slides & posters

The borrowable idea is **make the word the picture**, then constrain it to our brand. Not her literal food-type; her _principle_: one word, built with intent, owns the cover.

- **Cover slides:** Build the deck's anchor word (the emotion, the type number, the hook noun) as a **hero letterform** — the single largest element, everything else subordinate. One idea, one word, maximum contrast. This is a sharper cover instinct than dropping text over an image.
- **Material-as-meaning (within brand):** Where she uses pantry items, we can render the hero word in a **material that means something to the take** (e.g. a Type-5 word built from index cards, a "power" word from something cold/architectural) — borrowing her "letterform from real stuff" move, mapped to our personality content.
- **Restraint:** Steal the whitespace discipline. A 9takes cover should be one hero word + breathing room, not a collage.

### Brand constraint (non-negotiable — do not inherit her palette)

- **Primary = amber Streetlamp V5.** Dark text on amber. Her neutral/incidental-color palette is _inspiration only_; our covers stay amber-anchored.
- **Never red / teal / rose as primary.** Her ketchup-red letterforms are charming for _her_ — for us, red/teal/rose are banned as primary. Teal only ever as a `data-*` accent. Translate her tactile-letterform energy into **amber-on-dark or dark-on-amber**, never into her colors.
- The grab is **letterform craft + type-as-hero + restraint** — not her color story.

## Honest caveat

This is a **mood reference, not a repeatable system.** Her food-type gimmick doesn't port literally to a personality-psychology brand, and forcing it would look like cosplay. What ports is the discipline underneath: **one hero word, built with intent, given room to breathe, legible despite the play.** Pull that into amber; leave the spaghetti in Lisbon.
