## The idea (what this says)

Nobody in a triad would say the root word. The 5 says "I'm just observing," the 6 says "I'm being careful," the 7 says "I'm staying positive" — none of them say _"I'm afraid,"_ but all three behaviors **are** fear wearing three costumes. The carousel shows the costume and names the root underneath, across all three centers of intelligence.

**The carousel's spine:** the behavior you'd own vs. the emotion you won't.

## Emotional truth

The disguise of the core emotion — _three people quietly leave the moment without leaving the room._ (Per slide the arc is the same shape: same trigger, three signature dodges, one unnamed root.)

## Story test

- **Character:** three people we recognize instantly by posture (the watcher, the scanner, the escaper — and their anger/shame equivalents).
- **Conflict:** something just happened in this room that none of them can sit with.
- **Curiosity:** _what happened?_ We never see the off-frame trigger — only three bodies reacting to it. And: _which one are you?_

## Reference feel

A **Gregory Crewdson** tableau — a crowded room in charged stillness, the second after something shifts — lit like a Deakins night interior: warm practical lamplight pooled in the center, cold blue spilling from an open door. A party shot as a _film still_, not a snapshot. Passes "would I save this?" — the stillness inside a full room is the hook.

## Series structure

**Shape:** Nine reads, grouped by center. Three slides; each = **one situation → three type-reactions**.

**Setting (locked):** the **same crowded late-evening gathering** every slide — same warm world, same finish. Only the off-frame trigger changes.

- One warm-amber gathering · three figures in signature postures (foreground / mid / edge) reacting to an off-frame trigger we never see.

- Photoreal · film grain · **expressive but non-identifiable faces** (readable 3/4 profiles, no real-person likeness — _not_ all turned away; that killed the read in v1) · 1:1 square · deck chrome.

**Per-slide variable:** the root emotion, the three dodges, the trigger's flavor (uncertainty → violation → exposure).

## Frames (story + caption + prompt)

### Frame 1 — FEAR (Head: 5 · 6 · 7)

**The three figures, by signature posture (v2 — each oriented to the door, faces readable):**

- **5 — the watcher:** far left, at the dark edge of the doorframe, leaning back against the wall, drink held to the chest untouched, **eyes fixed warily on the doorway**. Withdrawn, watching from the shadow.
- **6 — the scanner:** center mid-room, **squared and leaning slightly toward the door**, weight on the front foot, brow furrowed and eyes narrowed in suspicion, one hand tightening on a glass. This is the clearest face in the frame, lit warm by the lamp — the wary scan is the hero beat.
- **7 — the escaper:** foreground right, **pointedly turned away from the door**, hunched over a phone, face lit cold-blue from below, a small forced half-smile. Refusing to look.

**Why this frame:** the disguise of fear lives in _posture aimed at a trigger_ — three bodies all responding to the same doorway, each in their signature dodge. (v1 lesson: a neutral back-turned hero figure and an unaimed crowd read as "a dim party," not fear. v2 separates the three, points them at the door, and lets faces carry the tells.)

**Overlay caption** (add after generation — bottom band, not rendered in-image):

> They'd call it observing, being careful, staying positive. **The Enneagram calls it fear.**
