# CTA Anatomy Audit — 2026-06-11

Read-only audit of every CTA across the major page types. Companion to `docs/audits/2026-06-11_state-of-9takes.md` (baselines) and Track A of `docs/planning/funnel-wiring-campaign-2026-06.md`.

## The headline

**The capture machinery works; the placement is the failure.** `/api/signups` is fully wired — validates, dedupes, inserts to `signups`, attaches first-touch fingerprint metadata, and sends a welcome email (`src/routes/api/signups/+server.ts:1-117`). The table is dead since 2023 not because the plumbing broke, but because **no capture form exists anywhere traffic actually goes.**

And the fix was already built: **`EnneagramCTASidebar.svelte` (378 lines, `src/lib/components/blog/EnneagramCTASidebar.svelte`) is a complete, context-aware, sticky email-capture sidebar — with per-route copy already written in `enneagramSidebarCopy.ts` — that is imported by zero routes.**

Other findings: `/questions` is linked from almost nowhere (not even the footer); BookSessionCTA exists _only_ on personality-analysis pages and _only_ at the very end; Email-Signup copy is identical and generic everywhere ("Get 9takes updates… ideas worth stealing") while tailored per-section copy sits unused in `enneagramSidebarCopy.ts:19-52`.

## Findings ranked by funnel impact

1. **EnneagramCTASidebar never mounted** — purpose-built to fix "nothing stops the scroll" (sticky, appears after 1200px scroll, context-aware copy, posts to /api/signups). Mounting it on the 5 blog slug routes is ~1 line each. CRITICAL / trivial effort.
2. **Zero email capture on the 45%-of-traffic template** (`/personality-analysis/[slug]` imports no capture component at all). A reader must navigate to the index and scroll to the bottom to subscribe.
3. **BlogPurpose confirms DJ's instinct**: styled like a content block, asks for nothing, redirects readers _away_ from what they came for (celebrity analyses) toward /enneagram-corner. It occupies the best mid-article real estate on the highest-traffic template.
4. **BookSessionCTA placement explains the 18 visits/30d**: it exists, copy is decent, UTMs are wired — but it appears only at the point of maximum reader fatigue on one page type.
5. **TestYourTypeCTA is a dead end**: quiz → type result → no "save your type / get tips for Type X" capture. Warmest handoff on the site, uncollected.
6. **Homepage captures nothing** — all navigation, no relationship-building.
7. **Generic email offer everywhere** despite written-and-unused per-context copy.

| 4   | BookSessionCTA: add mid-article compact variant; extend to enneagram-corner + pop-culture                                           | Coaching visibility                    | 2–3 h  |
