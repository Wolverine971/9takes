# Fresh-Eyes Corpus Assessment — Pass 2 (Blogs 51-100)

**Date:** 2026-04-29
**Reviewer:** Claude (cold-read, 6 independent agents, no shared context with each other or pass 1)
**Scope:** Next 50 most recently edited drafts in `src/blog/people/drafts/` (positions 51-100 by mtime)
**Coverage:** Silicon Valley founders/VCs, podcasters, royalty, historical figures, and political figures

## Headline finding

**The autopilot tics have intensified over time.** Older blogs (this batch) are _frequently better-written sentence-level_ than the recent 50 — stronger hooks (Putin's funeral walk, Newsom sitting with his mother's body, Clinton's golf club, Bill Clinton's hospital swearing-in, Amelia Dimoldenberg's Garfield-in-LaQuan-Smith opener, Augustus-as-Zuckerberg-mirror, "the most visible invisible woman" for Kylie). The _originality of the central insight_ in the older batch is **higher** than the recent batch, not lower.

What's worse in the older batch: bolted-on Enneagram theory in the typing section, Wikipedia-style H2s (Diana, Hillary), structural bloat (Hillary, Tim Cook-equivalents), "Both X and Y are true" paradox-as-resolution as a substitute for picking a side. What's worse in the recent batch: more contrast-pair tics, more counter-typing rule-of-three deployments, more closing-image autopilot.

**Translation:** the structural infrastructure (TESTIMONY LEDGER, HEADING MIX LEDGER, content*quality grading) has improved the \_checklist* but is also turning the writing into a _checklist_. The recent blogs hit the system more reliably; the older blogs missed more, but missed in interesting ways.

## Cross-corpus universals (confirmed in both pass 1 AND pass 2)

These four are universal autopilot tics. Both passes flagged them independently:

1. **The contrast-pair tic — _"It is not X. It is Y."_** Verified by quick grep: Sofia-Vergara has 5 instances, Donald-Trump has 4, Tucker has 3, Bill Clinton has 3. Universal. Most pervasive single tic in the corpus.
2. **Counter-typing rule of three — _"A Type 3 would / A Type 7 would / But X does..."_** Drake has 4 instances, Tate has 2. Older political blogs run this in dedicated _"Why not Type N?"_ subsections that read like encyclopedia entries.
3. **Triple front-loading of thesis — TL;DR + bolded intro paragraph + "What is X's personality type?" all restating the same insight.** Universal in both passes; reader has nowhere to be surprised before the body begins.
4. **Single-sentence dramatic-emphasis paragraphs.** Universal autopilot rhythm. _"Probably." "Known." "Good."_ Once per blog earns its weight; six times reads as AI rhythm.

## NEW patterns discovered in pass 2 (these are not in the pass-1 report)

### 5. Enneagram-as-defense-counsel

**The pattern:** When the subject is sympathetic, type theory diagnoses. When the subject failed badly, type theory **excuses** by reframing the failure as a Type N feature.

**Examples:**

- **Tucker Carlson:** Dominion lying and Fuentes platforming reframed as Type 6 features rather than failures.
- **Reagan:** AIDS-era inaction softened.
- **Bernie:** Three-houses critique skipped; "still in the courtroom" framing.
- **Bill Gates:** Epstein softened into "Type 5 blind spot" abstraction.
- **Reid Hoffman:** Epstein again softened.

**Why this is the corpus's biggest credibility risk:** A reader who knows the failure notices the duck and starts discounting the analysis. The Enneagram becomes a sympathy machine, not a diagnostic tool.

**Fix:** When a Type N's signature pattern _produced harm_, the piece must say so plainly without reframing. The type explains the mechanism — it does not excuse the outcome. Sorkin (Taibbi) and Sofia (Charo) are the only blogs in either pass that get this right.

### 6. Sympathy-by-self-conception

**The pattern:** The corpus does not split left-right. It tilts _toward whichever frame the subject self-identifies with._ Bernie is read through his moral self-conception, Obama through his "long view," AOC through her vigilance, Tucker through his counter-phobic skepticism, Reagan through his protector-Nine, Churchill through his protector-Eight.

**Why this matters:** Looks balanced across subjects but functionally means _no subject ever gets the full hostile read their critics would deliver._

**Fix:** Every blog needs a "what their critics actually believe" beat — written with steel-manned sympathy for the critic, not a hostile-witness summary.

### 7. The American-political tilt

**The pattern, narrowed and sharpened:** The corpus tilts left on American politics specifically.

- **American Democrats** (Bernie, Obama, AOC, Hillary, Pelosi, Michelle, Kamala, Biden, Bill Clinton, Newsom): sympathetic-from-inside-the-tent, defang Republican critics, don't engage credible left-critique.
- **American Republicans** (Trump, Tucker): tilt strongly anti-subject, psychologize without entertaining a steel-manned defense from supporters.
- **Non-American politicians** (Trudeau, Xi, Putin, Stalin, Churchill): more balanced — geographic distance forces fairness.

**A right-leaning reader would notice immediately. A left-leaning reader would not.**

**Fix:** Apply the Newsom/Trudeau treatment to all American politicians: name the strongest critic from both sides, let them speak in their own framing, then commit to the read. The piece can still take a position; it just needs the steel-manned alternative on record first.

### 8. "Both X and Y are true. Both things are real." paradox-as-resolution

**The pattern:** Especially in historical figures (Stalin, Xi, MLK) — the writer surfaces a contradiction and uses the Enneagram to "resolve" it by saying both halves are true. Reads as the framework being a superhero.

**Fix:** Pick one side and commit. If the contradiction is irreducible, name it as irreducible — don't dissolve it into Enneagram syrup.

### 9. "Most people see X. But that misreads the engine entirely."

**The pattern:** A common-misread-then-correct setup phrase used as a default opener for diagnostic sections (Palmer, Elon, Sacks). Distinct from the rule-of-three but does similar work.

**Fix:** Cap at one per blog. Vary the misread setup.

### 10. "Watch him when… / Watch him in… / Watch him at…" anaphora

Three-in-a-row anaphora paragraphs (Elon uses three; Friedberg, Caldwell each have one). Fresh once; pattern when repeated.

### 11. Triple-rejection rule of three

**The pattern:** _"Not as a tech villain. Not as a misunderstood genius. As a person whose..."_ Distinct from counter-typing — this is rejecting public _frames_, not other _types_. Used in Travis, Lex.

### 12. Engaging institutional critics over named human ones

**The pattern:** UN Sec-Gen abstracted in Palmer, Hindenburg in Chamath named only once, Lionel Friedberg as the only Monsanto critic in Friedberg. The corpus systematically engages institutional or abstract critics rather than naming a specific person and engaging _them_ in their own words.

**Fix:** Name a human critic in every blog. Give them at least 2 sentences in their own framing.

### 13. Closing parallel-binary as substitute for landing on a scene

**The pattern:** _"The spreadsheet and the verse, the strategy and the grief"_ / _"the optimistic skeptic, the loyal questioner"_ / _"always in the room, never quite on top of it."_ Appears in 6 of 9 closings in the founder batch — a parallel-construction binary that _resolves_ the piece without committing to a final scene or image.

**Fix:** End on a scene, a question, a forward-looking beat, or a direct quote. The parallel-binary is too neat.

### 14. Verbatim cross-blog duplication

**The pattern:** Two separate blogs contain the same line: _"He wasn't performing composure. He was composure."_ — appears verbatim in **Barack-Obama.md** and **Ronald-Reagan.md** (verified by grep). When the writer reaches for the same diagnostic sentence twice, it's a sign that the _thinking-template_ is doing the work, not the subject.

**Fix:** Run a grep across the corpus for distinctive phrases. Any phrase appearing in 2+ blogs verbatim should be flagged and rewritten.

### 15. Editor-feedback that didn't get applied

**The pattern:** Internal review comments at the top of files flagging fixes that were never applied. Lex flagged "fortress" 3x and it's still 3x in the body. Margot CTA flagged and still there. Ice-Spice 2023-2024 chronology flagged in pass 1 and still missing.

**This is a process gap, not a writing tic.** The review-then-revise loop is leaking.

**Fix:** When a review comment block is added at the top of a file, no merge until those items are addressed or explicitly rejected (with a reason).

### 16. Fortress / cocoon / armor metaphor overuse

**The pattern:** Mark Zuckerberg uses "fortress" 8+ times. Tyler Cowen recycles "fortress became infrastructure / hoard became commons" three times. Kylie Jenner has "cocoon / Cloud Bed / armor / glass walls" as a mini-cluster. The metaphor stops being a metaphor and starts being a noun.

**Fix:** One signature metaphor per blog. Use it twice — at thesis and at close. Anywhere else, find another image.

### 17. Stat-as-shock-paragraph

**The pattern:** Large numbers floated as single-sentence paragraphs without engagement. Hoffman's "60% effectiveness." Kylie's "1.3 billion / 391 million followers." Zuck's "61% voting / 92% voted to strip." Numbers are decoration, not argument.

**Fix:** Every stat that gets its own paragraph must have a follow-on sentence that does work with it.

### 18. Type-plural diagnostic crutch

**The pattern:** _"Type 9s insulate through…" "Type 5s wake each morning with…" "Sixes don't abandon. They stress-test."_ Once typing has been called, the corpus over-deploys plural-Type generalizations as connective tissue.

**Fix:** After the diagnosis section, switch from plural-Type-claims to subject-specific claims. _"Type 9s insulate"_ becomes _"Snoop insulates"_ or doesn't appear.

### 19. Wikipedia-style H2s in older blogs

**The pattern:** Princess Diana has _"Major Accomplishments,"_ _"Drama and Controversies,"_ _"Family Life."_ These are biography-encyclopedia headers. Newer blogs have voicier H2s ("The Showman Who Became the Show").

**Fix:** Audit the older blogs for Wikipedia-style headers and rewrite to thesis-advancing voicier ones.

### 20. Service-as-vocabulary autopilot

**The pattern:** Mamdani's Type 2 piece uses _"shows up / showing up / show up"_ 6+ times. Type-specific verb-clusters become reflexive vocabulary.

**Fix:** Once a Type-signature verb is established, find synonyms after the second deployment.

## Comparative read: older vs. recent

| Dimension                                         | Older blogs (pass 2)                                                                                                             | Recent blogs (pass 1)                      |
| ------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Hook quality**                                  | Often stronger (Putin, Newsom, Clinton, Zuckerberg, Amelia)                                                                      | Generally good but more formulaic          |
| **Scene-level prose**                             | Better narrative anchors                                                                                                         | More efficient but less memorable          |
| **Originality of central insight**                | Higher (Augustus-mirror, "most visible invisible woman", "Eight builds a throne, Six builds a bunker")                           | Strong but more pattern-driven             |
| **Counter-typing rule of three**                  | Less common; when used, deployed as dedicated subsections                                                                        | More common; deployed as in-prose flourish |
| **"Child still doing what child did" closer**     | Less universal                                                                                                                   | Universal autopilot                        |
| **TESTIMONY/HEADING/DISTRIBUTION LEDGERS**        | Mostly absent                                                                                                                    | Standard                                   |
| **Wikipedia-style H2s**                           | Common (Diana, Hillary)                                                                                                          | Rare                                       |
| **"Both X and Y are true" paradox-as-resolution** | More common (Stalin, Xi, MLK)                                                                                                    | Less common                                |
| **Critic engagement**                             | More variable — some good (Travis, Tyler Cowen, Mark Zuckerberg, Newsom), some bad (Bill Gates Epstein, Hoffman Epstein, Bernie) | Uniformly weaker                           |
| **Currency / contemporary-relevance**             | Worse on historical figures; uneven on living ones                                                                               | Worse — pieces dated 2026 read like 2023   |
| **Bloat**                                         | Worse in older long pieces (Hillary, Tim Cook)                                                                                   | More distributed bloat                     |
| **Where they're formulaic**                       | Back half (after typing section)                                                                                                 | Throughout                                 |

**One-sentence summary:** Older blogs are formulaic-in-the-back-half. Recent blogs are formulaic-throughout. The structural-craft system has improved consistency but reduced surprise.

## Updated priority moves (pass 1 + pass 2 combined)

The pass-1 priority moves still stand. These are added or sharpened:

1. **(Pass 1, sharpened) Add a "Why critics call X..." section to every blog — and steel-man the critic.** Pass 2 confirms: the corpus engages institutional critics over named human ones. Fix the structural slot AND the depth of engagement.
2. **(Pass 1) Cap contrast-pair "X is not Y. It is Z." at one per blog (thesis only).** Verified by grep — Sofia has 5, Trump has 4. Search-and-cut.
3. **(Pass 1) Cap counter-typing "A Type N would..." at one per blog (diagnosis only).**
4. **(Pass 1) Add a "[Person] in 2026" current-tense beat near the close. For historical figures, add a "How [person] reads in 2026" or "What revisionists argue" beat.**
5. **(NEW from Pass 2) Audit American-political blogs for partisan tilt.** Apply the Newsom/Trudeau treatment: name the strongest critic from both sides in their own framing, then commit. American Democrats need credible-left and credible-right critics. American Republicans need steel-manned supporters.
6. **(NEW from Pass 2) Run a corpus grep for verbatim cross-blog duplication.** The Obama/Reagan "wasn't performing composure" duplicate is a smell. Any phrase appearing in 2+ blogs verbatim is a candidate for rewrite.
7. **(NEW from Pass 2) Audit older blogs for Wikipedia-style H2s and rewrite to voicier thesis-advancing ones.** Diana, Hillary, others.
8. **(NEW from Pass 2) Adopt a "no merge until top-of-file review feedback resolved" rule.** Lex's "fortress" 3x, Margot's CTA, Ice-Spice's chronology — all are visible review comments that survived merges.
9. **(NEW from Pass 2) The Enneagram-as-defense-counsel rule.** When a Type N pattern produced harm, the blog must say so plainly. Type explains the mechanism, not the absolution.
10. **(NEW from Pass 2) One signature metaphor per blog.** Use it at thesis and close. "Fortress" 8x is a tell.

## How to use this report

1. **Read alongside `2026-04-29-fresh-eyes-corpus-assessment.md`** (pass 1). Pass 2 confirms pass 1's findings and adds 16 new patterns.
2. **Per-blog `<!-- FRESH-EYES ASSESSMENT — 2026-04-29 PASS 2 -->` blocks** are appended to all 50 pass-2 blog files. Use those for blog-by-blog edits.
3. **The 10 priority moves at the top are the corpus-level levers.** Items 5-10 are new from pass 2. Item 5 (American-political tilt) and item 9 (Enneagram-as-defense-counsel) are the two findings that change the credibility ceiling of the corpus more than any prose-level fix.
4. **Defend the strengths.** Older blogs have stronger hooks and originality of insight. The recent infrastructure makes consistency higher but originality lower. The fix is not to junk the system — it's to remove the autopilot tics inside the system.
