## See the emotions behind every take

Early in my marriage, my wife and I kept having the same fight. She would worry about something, and I would say, "Don't worry about that." I thought I was helping. I was actually telling her that the channel she experiences life through doesn't count.

I'm an Enneagram 8. I run on instinct, and my alarm system is anger. She's a 7. She runs on thinking, and her alarm system is fear. I understood anger from the inside: the simmer, the spike, the embarrassment afterward. Fear made no sense to me, so I treated it like a malfunction and tried to logic it away. It took a personality framework, and a wife patient enough to hand me one, to see what I was doing.

Here's the thing: everyone does this.

Your strongest channel feels like common sense. Everyone else's feels like a bug. Thinkers call feelers irrational. Feelers call gut-people insensitive. Gut-people like me tell everyone to stop overthinking and act. We're not arguing about the issue. We're arguing about which alarm system is allowed to be real.

### What we believe

Human nature runs on three channels: the intellect, the emotions, and the instincts. Philosophers and neuroscientists have been independently rediscovering this for 2,400 years ([the receipts are here](https://9takes.com/community/consensus-on-human-nature)). Each channel has its own alarm: fear for the head, shame for the heart, anger for the gut. There are nine common ways people rank those three channels. That's where the name comes from. Any situation you can describe has nine takes, and none of them is a malfunction.

A few convictions fall out of that:

1. Most disagreement is channel mismatch before it's a fact mismatch.
2. The person you find irrational is running an alarm you don't have.
3. You can't get someone's honest take after they've seen the crowd's.
4. Understanding why someone is the way they are generates empathy you can't fake. I've written personality analyses of people I don't like, and finished them liking the person more. Every time.
5. Keep it uncomplicated. Anger, fear, shame. Three intelligences. The wings and subtypes can wait.

### Why the internet can't show you this

The internet is built to bury the nine takes under one. On Reddit, the first comments soak up the votes, the lurkers stay silent, and every thread converges on whatever the early crowd anchored. You don't get nine perspectives on a question. You get one perspective with an audience.

So 9takes runs on one rule: **give first.** You answer the question before you see anyone else's answer. No anchoring. No performing for the room. What you actually think, placed next to what eight other kinds of people actually think. That's the product, and it's also the point: the fastest way to discover that people fundamentally see the world differently is to commit to your take and then read the other eight.

### What we refuse to become

We will never tell you your type. I spent years in personality forums watching "experts" diagnose strangers who didn't ask, and I did my share of typing people before they were ready. It doesn't work. People don't want to be labeled; they want to recognize themselves. Personality is a door you open from the inside.

So 9takes will not be a place that types you, scores you against your friends, or turns your psychology into a gimmick. Self-discovery over diagnosis. Empathy over labels. Questions over verdicts.

### Where this goes

Winning looks like this: when someone says "I want to know what people really think about this," they ask it on 9takes. Questions go viral because the answers genuinely surprise people. And somewhere in reading nine takes on a situation they thought had one obvious read, a person catches their own pattern in the wild, without anyone telling them a thing.

One situation. Nine takes. See the emotions behind every take.
