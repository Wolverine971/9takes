## Target Audience

### Primary ICP

**Who:** Young men seeking social/dating advantage; readers of tactical psychology ("The Game" audience who want deeper understanding); people navigating difficult relationships or workplace dynamics.

**Pain:** They're misreading social situations. They think people are "being difficult" when they're actually seeing patterns they don't understand. They want to improve social outcomes but find generic advice useless.

**Desire:** To "read the room" accurately. To decode why people act the way they do. To handle conflict skillfully. To understand themselves well enough to operate at high effectiveness.

**Trigger:** A repeated social failure. Relationship conflict they can't resolve. Workplace dynamics that frustrate them. Curiosity after seeing Enneagram content elsewhere.

**Emotional State:** Frustrated (seeking relief) + Ambitious (seeking growth) + Confused (seeking clarity)

### What They Believe

- There ARE patterns to human behavior—most people just don't see them
- Understanding yourself is a competitive advantage
- Generic personality tests are too shallow to be useful
- Social skills can be systematically improved
- People are both more similar and more different than they appear

### What They're Tired Of

- Vague self-help advice that doesn't work in real situations
- Personality content that's fluffy entertainment rather than practical
- Being told "just communicate better" without HOW
- Oversimplified personality frameworks that don't capture nuance
- Academic psychology that's disconnected from daily life

## Competitive Position

### Market Context

**Category:** Enneagram-based personality platform (Q&A + content + coaching)

**Alternatives:**

- Other Enneagram sites (Enneagram Institute, The Narrative Enneagram)
- Personality tests (MBTI, Big Five)
- Relationship advice (generic blogs, therapists)
- Self-help content (generic productivity/mindset)
- Reddit/forums (unstructured crowdsourced advice)

**Our Lane:** Practical, tactical Enneagram application for people who want real-world social advantages, not just self-discovery entertainment.

### Differentiation

**What we do differently:**

1. **Give-first mechanic** - Comment before seeing others' takes (prevents groupthink)
2. **Tactical framing** - Enneagram as competitive advantage, not just self-discovery
3. **Pattern + action** - Every insight connects to something you can try tonight
4. **Crowd-sourced wisdom** - 9 perspectives on any situation, not one "expert" opinion

**Why it matters:** You get unbiased perspectives (including your own authentic take) AND actionable patterns you can use immediately in real situations.

**Why believable:** The Enneagram is 2,500+ years of wisdom systematized. The give-first mechanic is provably different from any other Q&A platform. The content consistently delivers "try this tonight" actions.

## Strategic Guardrails

### Always

- Lead with practical benefit, not theory
- Show the pattern (emotional logic behind behavior)
- Connect to real-world interactions
- Provide a small experiment or micro-action
- Explain without judging or stereotyping
- Use conversational language (would I say this to a friend?)

### Never

- Academic/theoretical tone
- Hedging language ("tend to", "might")
- Generic descriptions that could apply to anyone
- Unsubstantiated claims
- Corporate jargon
- Fluffy advice without specific action

## Writing Style

### Mechanics

- **Rhythm:** Hook → Insight → Action step
- **Verbs:** decode, navigate, map, read, unlock, resolve
- **POV:** Second person ("you" and your situations)
- **Numbers:** Numerals ("9 types")
- **Punctuation:** Em dashes for sharp insights; minimal parentheses; Oxford comma

### Social Media Template

1. Hook line (<120 chars, practical benefit)
2. 2-3 tactical points (one per line, actionable)
3. Small experiment CTA (immediate micro-action)

**Example:**

> Your brain defaults to "they're being difficult" when you don't understand their type.
> → Type 1: Needs things done right
> → Type 6: Needs security/reassurance
> → Type 8: Needs directness
> Small experiment: Next conversation, guess their type first → 9takes.com
