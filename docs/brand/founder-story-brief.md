# Founder Story — Brief & Question Bank

**Created:** 2026-06-11 · **Status:** awaiting interview (`/founder-interview`)

## End state (why this exists)

The docs audit found 9takes has a coherent brand voice but **no person in it** — no origin story, no why-Enneagram, no documented founder. The goal is NOT one essay. It's a **source-of-truth narrative plus a tidbit bank** of quotable fragments that plug into specific surfaces:

| Destination                                                                                    | What it needs from the story                                                           |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `docs/brand/founder-story.md` (canonical, created by the interview)                            | Full narrative + tagged tidbit bank                                                    |
| `docs/VISION.md` (to be written)                                                               | Origin paragraph + why-Enneagram + red lines                                           |
| BlogPurpose component rewrite                                                                  | 2–3 sentences that stop the scroll — a _human_ reason to care, not a mission statement |
| About/founder page on site                                                                     | 300–500 word public version                                                            |
| Outreach self-intros (`docs/planning/personality-analysis-outreach-positioning-2026-05-11.md`) | 1–2 line credibility hooks                                                             |
| `/book-session` "who am I" section                                                             | Why DJ is the person to decode you                                                     |
| Social bios (IG/X/Quora)                                                                       | One-liner variants                                                                     |

**Quality bar:** scenes, not abstractions. "I realized people see things differently" is unusable. "My [person] and I had the same fight for the Nth time and I finally understood they weren't being difficult — they were being a [type]" is usable everywhere.

## Question bank (starting point — the interview will follow up)

### Origin

1. When did you first encounter the Enneagram? The actual scene: where were you, who introduced it, what was going on in your life that made it land?
2. What were you struggling with at the time that the Enneagram explained and nothing else had? A specific conflict, relationship, or failure.
3. What's your type, how did you figure it out, and what was the hardest thing about accepting it?

### Background

4. Your path before 9takes — career, skills, what you were building. How does your professional pattern-recognition connect to reading people?
5. Was there a specific person (family member, boss, ex, friend) you completely re-understood through the Enneagram? Tell that story.

### The build

6. What was the moment you decided to _build_ 9takes instead of just consuming Enneagram content? What did you see missing?
7. Where did the give-first mechanic conviction come from? Was there a moment that proved (to you) that people answer differently before they see the crowd?
8. Why personality analysis of famous people? What happened the first time you wrote one?

### Conviction & limits

9. Your honest relationship with the Enneagram's validity. What do you say to "it's astrology for tech bros"? Where do YOU think it breaks down?
10. What does 9takes refuse to become? (Red lines — the Neil Strauss question.)
11. What would winning look like in 5 years? And what evidence would make you quit?

### Tidbit mining (the interview digs here)

- Surprising facts about you that earn a second look
- Contrarian opinions you'll defend in public
- Failures you're willing to publish
- Phrases you actually say (your words beat any copywriting)

## How to run it

Run `/founder-interview` in a session when you have 30–45 minutes. Answer raw and unpolished — voice-dictation-quality is perfect. The flow asks 2–3 questions at a time, follows up until it has scenes and specifics, then drafts `docs/brand/founder-story.md` with the tidbit bank for your edit.
