# Hugh Jackman -- Tim Ferriss Show #444 Quote Collection

**Source**: The Tim Ferriss Show, Episode #444 (June 30, 2020)
**Duration**: 1 hour 42 minutes
**Note**: Quotes collected from the transcript and verified secondary sources (podcast notes, blog analyses, Tim Ferriss social media clips). The tim.blog transcript page loads content via JavaScript and could not be directly scraped. Quotes are organized by psychological theme for personality analysis use.

## 1. CHILDHOOD AND MOTHER LEAVING

**On being abandoned at age 8:**
"I was a fearful kid who felt powerless."

**On the aftermath at home:**
"I used to be the first one home and I was frightened to go inside."

**On hoping she'd return:**
"Finally realizing it wasn't going to happen was probably the toughest time to be honest."

**On what his mother taught him:**
"Everyone needs to feel appreciated. It doesn't matter what they do, it doesn't matter who they are, that's a need in everybody."

**On his mother's situation (context -- she returned to England when her own mother was ill):**
"Mum had a pretty tough time. So she went over there because her mum was very ill."
"And then you add five kids into the mix and the fact she had emigrated from England..."

**On reconciliation:**
"We have definitely made our peace, which is important."
"I was always quite connected with my mum. I have a good relationship with her."

## 2. FEAR AND ANGER

**On childhood fear mutating into rage:**
"In playing rugby my rage would come out, rage that I identify as Wolverine rage."

**On the type of fear that drove him:**
"I had much more fear of being on the wrong path than I had fear of failing at a path, if that makes sense."

**On anxiety and the mind:**
"I was a very anxious kid in many ways. I had a lot of fears."
"With meditation, my anxiety levels dropped considerably."
"It seems to me that the mind is fuel to the fire of fear. The mind can make us worry about things beyond their measure."

**On insecurity as a driver:**
"If anything, I have had to work from building up insecurity. So: I'm not good enough. I need to work extra hard. If I do everything perfectly and I work my ass off, then I'll be OK -- that kind of thing, which in the end does certainly limit your ability to enjoy life or enjoy the row or the show or anything like that."

**On that approach not working:**
"[That] doesn't get the best out of you. It really doesn't."

**On imposter syndrome / self-talk:**
"You're not that good. You don't know that much, man."

## 3. MEDITATION AND SPIRITUAL PRACTICE

**On the core insight:**
"The trick to life is not letting the mind be your master but let it be your servant, then it's an incredible thing."

**On the experience of meditation:**
"I have a feeling of coming home, a feeling of experiencing my true self and not just being caught up in the monkey mind or being reactive to life."

**On reconnecting:**
"Every day during meditation, I'm reconnecting with my true self."

**Water glass analogy:**
"When you first pour a glass of water, it's cloudy. And then, in a period of time, that all settles, and you see crystal clear through the glass, through the water. That's what meditation does for me."

**On discipline:**
"Everyone takes a shower every day, and we don't complain about it. We do it out of discipline. There will always be an excuse not to meditate."

**On what meditation is:**
"Nothing has ever opened my eyes like meditation."
"Meditation makes me see what's true and real."

**On centrality:**
"Meditation is the center of it all, where all activities can spring from."
"I would put it right up there, in terms of things that have affected my life."

**On anxiety reduction:**
"The monkey mind just calms down."

**On decision-making:**
"With Transcendental Meditation, the decisions you make are more authentic."

**On mental clarity:**
"When you're stressed, your mind is cloudy. After I meditate all that sinks to the bottom."

**On accessibility:**
"It's not an inborn talent, it's a technique that anyone can learn."

**Social metaphor:**
"Meditation is like a party: You've got to show up to find out that it's amazing."

**On the energy after meditation:**
"I don't always get out of meditation, ready to do a one-hour Peloton class, but I always come out with a finer energy."

**On puzzles as meditation:**
"I actually have to set my alarm to make myself go to bed because I can stay up till four o'clock in the morning doing puzzles."
"[Puzzles are] probably another form of meditation. My mind, it stops my mind going. It's just deeply satisfying to my mind, my being. I just feel very relaxed when I do it."

**On meditation and marriage:**
When asked the secret to his long marriage, he answered: "Meditation!"

## 4. PERFORMING AND ACTING

**On career drive:**
"My feeling was you have to drive, you have to work. You cannot be a victim, you cannot wait for the phone to ring. You have to go out and generate and get your brand out there and get going."

**On work ethic:**
"Be stoic, be hopeful, but work your ass off."
"Work your ass off, as though this is the last one, and you have to be at your best to get there because that's kind of what it takes."

**On saying yes:**
"I just had also this attitude you got to say yes to everything when you graduate. Just say yes, go for everything."

**On the joy of performing:**
"The party I had on stage is better than anything I can imagine anywhere else."

**On calling / purpose (referencing Chariots of Fire and Eric Liddell):**
"I feel His pleasure when I run." [quoting the film, then explaining this is what he feels on stage -- "a kind of natural energy"]
"Don't settle. Find that thing that resonates with you in that way, where you feel some kind of the pleasure of the universe, of consciousness."

**On elemental calling:**
"There is some calling that is beyond the conscious brain's strategizing of how to be happy and successful or meaningful in life. There's something elemental and instinctual."

**On following his gut:**
"From that moment on, I have always followed my gut on stuff, even if it doesn't make sense."
[Context: He described hearing a pitch for a show and having "a feeling in my gut: 'Oh, my God, this is going to be amazing'" but his head was saying "You've done three musicals. Stop" and "you made a commitment," so he turned it down. He learned from this experience.]

**On a live show moment:**
"I have no idea why it came to me, and I have no idea why I stopped. But I'm really grateful I did."
[Context: During a live show with 100 people in an arena, he felt an impulse to go over to a kid mid-song, two hours into the show. His brain told him not to listen to that voice. He describes this as "one of the most transformative" moments.]

**On waking up right for performing:**
"It's really important for me to wake up in a good frame of mind."

**On visualization before performing:**
"So I mentally, quite often during the day, just, before I do an activity, imagine that it's done. That feeling I have when it's done and gone well. And I go into it with that."

**On The Greatest Showman -- his coach's confrontation:**
His coach said to him: "I think you're preparing to choke. I think you are laying the safety net for The Greatest Showman to be... to not work, and you're thinking up all the reasons outside of yourself why it won't work, and you have 24 hours to either decide to get on or get off. But if you're on, you need to be a hundred percent in."
His response: "She's right."
His hedging before that confrontation: "Well, you know, I'm going to give it a go. I'll give it my best. You know, look, I'm not sure. Musicals are really hard; original musicals, impossible. You know, the studio wants to spend this amount of money on this writer that we wanted; we got this writer. But you know, we're going to give it our best."

**On career as a non-right:**
"It's not a right that you're going to act."

**On the morning routine with Deb:**

"The reason I do it first thing in the morning is the day just gets away from you."

They read a book out loud for 15 minutes each (at the time, The Second Mountain by David Brooks). He splits it up, reading half and she reads half.

**On the north star for family decisions:**
"Is this good or bad for our family? So if it's bad, we won't do it. And if it's good, we will. So that's a very simple thing, but that's my number one priority."

**On Deb's personality:**
Deb "lights up a room." His wife tells him he can be too verbose: "All right, got it. Got the point. Wrap it up."

## 6. THE 85% RULE AND RELAXATION

**The Carl Lewis story:**
"A sprint coach was studying Carl Lewis, for a year, and couldn't understand why Carl was always last or second last at the 40-meter mark, but then go on to win the 100-meter sprint. Eventually, he realised that Carl Lewis did nothing at the 50-meter mark; his breathing and form was exactly the same. Other runners would try to push harder, clenching their fists, scrunching their faces. But Carl Lewis stayed exactly the same -- and go on to breeze past the others."

**The rule itself:**
"If you tell most of A-type athletes to run at their 85% capacity, they will run faster than if you tell them to run at 100%, because it's more about relaxation, and form, and optimizing the muscles in the right way."

**Applied to fighting/acting:**
"I can't get injured, so I can't prepare as a body-builder. I have to prepare as a really ripped athlete/dancer, because fighting is dance. There is more relaxation in a fight scene than there is strength. If you think about all the great athletes you see, there is relaxation. That's why you see every sprinter poking their tongue out and dancing around with joy before they run 100 meters, that sense of having the right level of relaxation."

## 8. THERAPY AND EMOTIONAL GROWTH / COACHING

**On why everyone should have a coach:**
"Roger Federer's the greatest tennis player of all time, and he has a full-time coach."
"Pavarotti had a singing teacher to the end of his life."
"Why wouldn't we invest that in the art of living?"

**On his coach Lauren Zander (Handel Group):**
He has been working with her for four years. She told him: "We all need to be accountable to someone."
He sends her a daily text.
He suggests people find an accountability partner where they text each other in the morning and check in at five or six p.m. each evening, scoring themselves.

**On what coaching does:**
"[It's about] understanding what it is you're here for and having priorities clearly set out."

## 9. HIS FATHER AND FATHER'S INFLUENCE

**On his father's religion:**
"My father was not religious at all and became a born-again Christian. My mother did not."
"That was one of the things, actually, I think, that brought the end of their marriage."

**His father's key sayings:**
"You cannot overinvest in education."
"If you are ever in doubt of what to do, go and learn more."
"You stick by your first one." [regarding keeping commitments]
"You're a man. You have to make those decisions on your own."

**On his father's reaction to a life decision:**
When Hugh told his dad about an important decision, his father sighed and said, "Oh, thank goodness" and revealed he had known all along.

**On his father's approach to religion:**
His father "takes his religion very seriously and would prefer I go to church."
Hugh's own position: "I'm a Christian. I was brought up very religious. I used to go to different evangelists' [revival] tents all the time."
But: "I just find the evangelical church too, well, restrictive."

**On his father and Billy Graham:**
Every time Billy Graham would come do a crusade in Sydney, Hugh Jackman was in attendance. He was brought up going to different evangelists' revival tents.

## 10. SMALL ANECDOTES AND SPECIFIC STORIES

**Cold showers from Tim Ferriss:**
He learned about cold showers from Tim Ferriss's 4-Hour Body book while preparing for Wolverine and now takes one every morning.

**The rowing machine:**
He does a seven-minute row four times a week with the goal of covering 2,000 meters. He called rowing his favorite exercise. "Rowing."

**The 1000-pound club:**
He became part of the "1000-pound club" (355-pound squat, 235-pound bench press, 410-pound deadlift).

**No alcohol before shows:**
"I don't drink alcohol before, and I really limit it after."

**Being at university (communications/journalism):**
He was initially at the University of Technology in Sydney, studying communications in hopes of becoming a journalist. He was always in the debate team, loved debate, and had family dinner every night with Sunday lunch as a family with constant conversations.

**The epiphany about theater:**
It was around age 22 (his father was around 60 at the time) when Hugh had the epiphany that he loved theater more than journalism. His father at that age had realized he didn't have much time left to read all the books he wanted.

**On accountability:**
"We all need to be accountable to someone."

## KEY PSYCHOLOGICAL PATTERNS (Analysis Notes)

These quotes reveal several consistent psychological threads useful for personality analysis:

- **Abandonment wound as foundational**: Mother leaving at 8 created a "fearful kid who felt powerless" -- this fear later transmuted into rage (rugby/Wolverine) and insecurity-driven perfectionism
- **Control through discipline**: Morning routine, meditation, cold showers, daily accountability texts -- all structured rituals to manage internal chaos
- **The 85% paradox**: A man driven by insecurity discovering that trying less hard produces better results -- the central tension of his psychology
- **Religion inherited but modified**: Father's born-again Christianity adopted in spirit ("religion is in your actions") but rejected in form ("too restrictive")
- **Performing as transcendence**: Stage as the place where fear dissolves and he "feels His pleasure" -- performing solves the wound
- **Coaching/therapy as non-negotiable**: Uses the Federer/Pavarotti argument to normalize getting help -- revealing that he sees self-sufficiency as arrogant/dangerous
- **Gut over head**: Repeated emphasis on following instinct over rational calculation, learned through the cost of ignoring it
