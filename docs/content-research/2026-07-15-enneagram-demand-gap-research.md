# The Translation Gap: Where Enneagram-Shaped Demand Actually Lives

Date: 2026-07-15
Method: 12 blind web sweeps in native problem-language, 81 raw clusters, 24 merged clusters, per-cluster demand check + adversarial skeptic + repo coverage check + adjudication. ~130 agents.
Status: **Iteration 1. Findings are directional, not final. Read the Limitations section before acting on anything here.**

## 1. The thesis

The demand is real and we found it. The explanation we currently sell to capture it does not survive contact with a competent skeptic.

Twenty-four distinct question-clusters were found where people describe a fixed motivational pattern in the language of their symptom, never using the word "enneagram." Every one of them drew a specific, well-sourced refutation of the claim that the Enneagram explains the pattern. Not vague skepticism. Named literature: operant conditioning for withdrawal, Gottman's flooding protocol, Christensen and Heavey on demand-withdraw, Mikulincer and Shaver on deactivating strategies, Tennov on limerence, Safren and Ramsay's CBT for adult ADHD, Gross's process model.

The strategic conclusion is not that the opportunity is fake. It is that **we have been selling the one thing we cannot defend, and giving away the thing we can.** The Enneagram's defensible contribution here is not etiology. It is a non-pathologizing vocabulary for motivational difference, plus a product that shows you eight motives that are not yours. That second thing makes no contested claim at all, and 23 of 24 clusters scored HIGH fit for it.

## 2. Defining the gap

An Enneagram-shaped question asked in non-Enneagram language is one where:

- The asker describes a **recurring** pattern, not a mood or a one-off situation.
- The asker has already tried the obvious advice and it failed **on them specifically**.
- The confusion is about **motive**, either their own or another person's, not about behavior.
- The asker reaches for a **substitute label** (attachment style, ADHD, trauma response, narcissist, MBTI) because it is the only vocabulary available.

The disqualifier that matters most: if the pattern is better explained by a situation, an incentive, or a diagnosis, the Enneagram is being forced. Eleven of 81 raw clusters were rejected on exactly this basis.

## 4. What the Enneagram cannot honestly claim

This section is the point of the document. Do not skim it.

Every cluster was handed to a skeptic instructed to refute. All 24 did, with force. The representative kill, on our highest-recurrence cluster:

> "Delete the numbers and the answer is unchanged. The load-bearing insight is that withdrawal is negatively reinforced. It ends the conversation, every time, which is why it never stops. That is operant conditioning. It is not Enneagram-derived."

Others, in brief:

- **`dating-withdrawal-when-real`**: limerence is sustained by uncertainty and extinguishes on resolution (Tennov). The Enneagram adds nothing the uncertainty account does not already give.
- **`adhd-remainder`**: CBT for adult ADHD plus Big Five for the trait-shaped residue. A real differential the Enneagram cannot produce.
- **`husband-shuts-down`**: EFT and Gottman's flooding protocol, both with measured mechanisms.
- **`which-self-is-real`**: attachment theory's own literature concedes domain-specificity, so cross-domain constancy is not the Enneagram-only finding we claimed.

### The do-not-write list

These sentences must not appear in 9takes content. Each was named by an adjudicator as an overclaim that fails.

1. **"Attachment theory tells you WHAT your pattern is. The Enneagram tells you WHY you run it that way."**
   **This is live right now**, in bold, at `src/blog/enneagram/attachment-styles-and-enneagram-types.md:64`, with a softer restatement at line 46. The WHAT/WHY framing recurs across six files: `attachment-styles-and-enneagram-types.md`, `astrology-and-the-enneagram.md` (3 hits), `neurodiversity-vs-personality.md`, `the-missing-middle.md`, `love-languages-and-enneagram-types.md`, `enneagram-vs-meyers-briggs.md`. It is our most quotable line and it is the exact claim the skeptics dismantled. Mikulincer and Shaver define deactivation _functionally_, as a goal-directed strategy with a purpose. It is already a why. Our differentiator is built on a misreading of the rival's literature.
2. **"Subtract everything your nervous system does without asking you, and what's left is your type."** Rhetorically excellent, unfalsifiable.
3. **"Once you spot his type from how he goes silent, you'll know which lever to pull. Raise the stakes with an 8 and he'll arrive instantly; lower them with a 9."** Typing a stranger from one behavior, then acting on it.
4. **"The neurology gave you the volume; your type chose what you're sensitive about."** Asserts a causal division we cannot support.
5. **"Of 400 people who described going silent, 31% were protecting the connection, 22% a resource, 18% suppressing anger."** First-party stats presented as an answer to a question they do not answer.

## 5. What survives, and it is better than what we lost

The honest claims the adjudicators found converge on one shape, independently, across 24 clusters:

> **"The advice didn't work on you because it wasn't written for you. The same behavior runs on different motives, and the moves that help one person are the moves that hurt another. Here's what nine different people say about the exact situation you're in. Find the one that sounds like your head."**

Note what this does not do. It makes no causal claim. It does not explain why he goes silent. It does not require the Enneagram to be valid. It requires only that **the same behavior runs on different motives**, which is observably true and which every substitute framework fails to account for.

This is why `give_first_fit` came back HIGH on 23 of 24 clusters. The give-first wall is not a growth hack bolted onto a content site. It is the only asset we have that answers these questions without making a claim a skeptic can kill. "Am I the problem or am I overreacting" is answered better by nine real perspectives than by any framework, and that answer is unattackable.

**The cross-surface finding.** Twelve researchers on twelve independent surfaces each hit the same incumbent failure mode: every substitute terminates in a noun plus a referral, locating the cause somewhere the person cannot reach (1998, the amygdala, the nervous system, the sky, the diagnosis). You are told what you are and then handed off. Nobody is served at the moment they ask. That convergence, from blind researchers, is the strongest signal in this study.

## 6. Coverage: we already wrote it, and hid it

**27 of 27 coverage checks returned "partially covered."** Not one returned "not at all." We have written this material. We titled it in framework language, so the people who need it cannot find it.

This makes the first move repositioning, not production. That is dramatically cheaper than a content calendar.

## 7. The pathway

1. **Kill the WHAT/WHY framing across all six files.** It is our most-repeated differentiator and it is indefensible. This is a same-day fix and it is the highest-leverage item here, because everything else we publish inherits its credibility from claims like this one.
2. **Re-title existing assets into native problem-language.** 27 of 27 partial coverage means the inventory exists. Start with the clusters that map to `enneagram-corner`, which already carries 224k impressions at 0.72% CTR. CTR is the constraint, and mis-titling is a plausible cause.
3. **Retire the explanatory pitch; ship the difference pitch.** Replace "the Enneagram explains why" with "the same behavior runs on different motives, and here are nine." Test it on one cluster first.
4. **Put the give-first wall on the highest-fit clusters.** 23 of 24 scored HIGH. This is the product, not a conversion tactic. `am-i-the-problem`, `is-it-just-me-denominator`, and `husband-shuts-down` are the natural first tests because they are the ones where nine real answers beat any framework outright.
5. **Do not scale content until 3 and 4 are validated on one cluster.**

## 8. Limitations. Read these.

- **No demand figure in this document is grounded.** Every keyword tool was blocked: Google Trends returned 429 to both WebFetch and curl, Reddit 403 on every route, Ahrefs/Semrush/keywordtool.io are JS-walled or auth-gated. The agents refused to invent numbers, which was correct, but it means the 7 REAL_AND_LARGE vs 20 REAL_BUT_SMALL split is **soft**. Grounding it needs authenticated keyword-tool access. This is the single biggest hole.
- **The instruments returned constants.** 27/27 partial coverage, 24/24 refuted, 24/24 pursue-with-narrower-claim, 23/24 HIGH give-first fit. A test that returns the same verdict on every input carries little information. The general thesis is well-supported; the **ranking between clusters is not.** Do not read the table order as a priority list.
- **The skeptic prompt was weighted toward refuting.** It was told to default to refuted. The arguments hold up on their own merits, and I kept them for that reason, but the 100% rate is partly an artifact.
- **Wave 2 and the originality critic did not complete** on the final adjudicated data. The blind spots the completeness critic flagged (non-US, non-English, religious communities, and men specifically, given that the Enneagram audience skews female) remain unexamined.

## 9. For iteration 2

1. **Ground the demand.** Authenticated keyword access, then re-rank. Without this, cluster prioritization is guesswork.
2. **Build a discriminating instrument.** Force ranking, not per-cluster verdicts. Ask "which 5 of these 24 are best and why," so the answer cannot be a constant.
3. **Test the difference pitch against the explanation pitch** on one live cluster. This is an empirical question and we can settle it.
4. **The men question.** If the audience skews female and the work/boss clusters (`employee-goes-opaque`, `boss-micromanages`) skew male, that is either the biggest unclaimed segment or a reason those clusters underperform. Unexamined.
5. **The deeper question this raises.** If the give-first product answers these questions without needing the Enneagram to be true, what is the Enneagram actually for on this site? Scaffolding for the nine, or a claim we are still making because it is familiar? Worth answering deliberately rather than by default.
