# Ana de Armas — The Star-Vehicle Run (2022–2025)

Research brief for the 4w5 personality-analysis draft. Fills the gap between _Blonde_ (2022) and _Ballerina_ (2025): her streaming-era / action-lead phase. Evidence-first, with verbatim quotes and the "is this really a Norma-underneath artist?" question addressed head-on.

Date compiled: 2026-06-28.

---

## Inline-Sourced Facts

### The Gray Man (Netflix, 2022 — dir. Russo brothers, with Ryan Gosling)

- **Role:** CIA operative **Dani Miranda**. ([Wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Gray_Man_(2022_film)>))
- **Critical reception:** Mixed. Rotten Tomatoes **45%** (276 critics), avg 5.5/10; praise for cast/action, criticism of "clichéd script and breakneck pacing." de Armas's action work was a recurring bright spot in reviews. ([Wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Gray_Man_(2022_film)>), [Rotten Tomatoes](https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/the_gray_man_2022))
- **Viewership:** Limited theatrical July 15, 2022; Netflix July 22, 2022. **88.55 million hours viewed in its first three days** — the **fifth-best launch for a Netflix movie** since the streamer began publishing viewership data in 2021. Most-watched film in 84 countries. ([IndieWire, July 2022](https://www.indiewire.com/features/general/the-gray-man-netflix-red-notice-viewers-ratings-1234745612/))
- **Pay (single-sourced, flag):** Reportedly paid ~$400,000, vs. Gosling's reported $10M and Evans's $15M. Sourced to aggregator/Parade reporting, not a primary doc — **treat as unverified**. ([Parade](https://parade.com/celebrities/ana-de-armas-net-worth))

### Ghosted (Apple TV+, 2023 — dir. Dexter Fletcher, with Chris Evans)

- **Role:** **Sadie**, a secret agent / CIA operative; ordinary-guy Cole (Evans) falls for her and gets swept into an international spy adventure. She **replaced Scarlett Johansson**, who exited over scheduling. Reunion with Evans after _Knives Out_ and _The Gray Man_. ([Wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghosted_(2023_film)>), [Variety](https://variety.com/2023/film/reviews/ghosted-review-ana-de-armas-chris-evans-1235588641/))
- **Critical reception:** Mixed-to-negative. Critics faulted a movie that "listlessly wafts between action, comedy, and romance"; Variety/THR/CNN broadly lukewarm, though some credited the two leads' chemistry. ([Variety](https://variety.com/2023/film/reviews/ghosted-review-ana-de-armas-chris-evans-1235588641/), [Hollywood Reporter](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-reviews/ghosted-review-chris-evans-ana-de-armas-1235392722/))
- **Viewership:** Per **Samba TV**, **328,500 U.S. households in its first two days** (dropped April 21, 2023) — at the time **Apple TV+'s biggest movie debut**. Completion rate ran ~20% above Apple TV+'s average. Note the irony angle: most-watched _and_ among its most-disliked. ([Deadline](https://deadline.com/2023/04/chris-evans-ana-de-armas-ghosted-viewership-apple-tv-record-1235338734/), [Collider](https://collider.com/ghosted-biggest-apple-tv-plus-debut/))

### From the World of John Wick: Ballerina (2025 — dir. Len Wiseman)

- **Role:** **Eve Macarro**, a ballerina-assassin trained by the Ruska Roma; revenge arc, set within the John Wick universe (Keanu Reeves cameos as Wick). ([Wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ballerina_(2025_film)>))
- **Critical reception:** Rotten Tomatoes **75%** Certified Fresh (305 critics); audience **92%**. Metacritic **59/100** (49 critics, "mixed or average"). **CinemaScore A–**, PostTrak 87% positive. Praise for action/choreography; criticism for a thin/repetitive plot. ([Wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ballerina_(2025_film)>), [Rotten Tomatoes](https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/ballerina_2025))
- **Box office:** Budget reportedly **~$90M** (inflated by reshoots; some outlets cite higher all-in figures). Worldwide **~$140.2M** ($58.1M domestic / $82.1M international). Out-grossed the original _John Wick_ (~$85M) but fell short of Chapters 2–4 — widely framed as a commercial **underperformance/disappointment** relative to budget+marketing. ([Wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ballerina_(2025_film)>), [Screen Rant](https://screenrant.com/ballerina-2025-movie-51-million-domestic-box-office/), [Collider](https://collider.com/ballerina-global-box-office-passes-wrath-of-man-100-million/))
- **Streaming redemption (later, flag dates):** By late 2025 / early 2026 reported as a streaming hit — No. 1 most-streamed in the U.S. on HBO Max and high on Prime Video charts. Sourced to entertainment-aggregator pieces (Collider/CBR/Screen Rant), dates Dec 2025–Apr 2026 — **directionally reliable, exact ranks vary by outlet/week**. ([Collider, Dec 2025](https://collider.com/ana-de-armas-90-million-john-wick-replacement-ballerina-starz-streaming-success-december-2025/), [CBR, Apr 2026](https://www.cbr.com/ana-de-armas-ballerina-john-wick-replacement-streaming-success-april-2026/))
- **Training (supplements draft):** ~90 min daily strength training, 4–5 hrs stunt coordination at 87Eleven (action studio 8711), firearms practice, treadmill in a weighted vest. She has said she hoped to do her own stunts "as good as Keanu." ([brobible](https://brobible.com/culture/article/ana-de-armas-doing-own-stunts-pride-ballerina/), [Express Tribune](https://tribune.com.pk/story/2549303/ana-de-armas-reveals-intense-fitness-and-firearms-training-for-john-wick-spin-off-ballerina))

### Eden (2024 TIFF / 2025 release — dir. Ron Howard)

- **Role:** Part of an ensemble (Jude Law, Vanessa Kirby, Sydney Sweeney, Daniel Brühl) in a historical survival thriller about European settlers on Floreana Island, Galápagos, pre-WWII. de Armas plays a vivid, amoral character — notably a **villain-coded, against-type** turn, not an action lead. ([Wikipedia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eden_(2024_film)>), [Variety](https://variety.com/2024/film/reviews/eden-review-jude-law-ana-de-armas-ron-howard-1236137262/))
- **Critical reception:** Divided. Rotten Tomatoes **~57%**. NYT's Glenn Kenny singled out her performance; Screen Rant's review headline "I Loved To Hate Ana De Armas." Several critics called the film overall "an ambitious disappointment." ([Rotten Tomatoes](https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/eden_2024_2), [Screen Rant](https://screenrant.com/eden-movie-review-2024/), [The Film Stage](https://thefilmstage.com/tiff-review-ron-howards-eden-is-an-ambitious-disappointment/))
- **Relevance to the draft:** _Eden_ is the best counter-example to a pure "paycheck phase" read — a character-actor swing inside the commercial run. Useful for nuance.

## Thesis Assessment

**The complication is real and should not be curated away.** _The Gray Man_, _Ghosted_, and _Ballerina_ are franchise/star-vehicle work — replacing Scarlett Johansson in _Ghosted_, slotting into the John Wick IP machine, headlining a Netflix tentpole. The evidence does **not** show her describing these as "Norma underneath" interior excavations. By her own account she did not even see herself as an action actor; she came to it through _training and capability_, not a hunt for hidden inner lives. The honest framing the draft should adopt: **not every role is a Norma — some are the job a movie star takes, and some (like the action run) are about proving she can do a thing she didn't think she could.**

**But two things keep the 4w5 read alive in a more defensible form:**

1. **Reinvention-as-identity.** Her stated motive — "reinvent myself… approach different characters… what's fun about being an artist" — is a 4 framing (individuality, the artist self-concept), not a payday framing. The 5 shows up as the _mastery/competence_ drive: she repeatedly narrates the action turn as a craft-skill problem to be solved (training detail, doing her own stunts, the Keanu benchmark).
2. **Eden is the tell.** Dropped into the most commercial stretch of her career, she took a divisive, villain-coded, against-type ensemble role under Ron Howard that critics flagged as the most alive thing in the film. That's a 4w5 reaching for texture inside the commercial machine, not abandoning it.

**Recommended editorial move:** Use this section to _earn trust_ — concede that the 2022–2025 run is mostly commercial, name _Ghosted_ and _The Gray Man_ as paycheck/star-vehicle work plainly, then argue the 4w5 pattern shows up in _how she talks about_ the work (reinvention + mastery) and in the _Eden_ swing, rather than claiming every role is a buried-self project. Lead with the "my lane" and "reinvent myself" quotes; let _Ballerina_'s box-office disappointment-but-streaming-redemption stand as evidence she's a genuine commercial draw, not just an arthouse figure.

**Figures to cite carefully:** Ballerina budget (~$90M) and worldwide gross (~$140M) are solid (Wikipedia/Box Office aggregators); the "underperformance" verdict is the critical consensus. The $400K Gray Man pay gap and the streaming-rank claims are aggregator-sourced — attribute and hedge.
