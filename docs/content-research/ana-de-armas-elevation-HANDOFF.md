### 2a. CRITICAL — fix the Cuba factual error

The draft's opening states _"She has not lived in Cuba since,"_ and frames her as an exile who can't go home. **This is wrong** (Brief 04): she bought a house in Cuba (~2017–18), lived there much of a year, filmed _Wasp Network_ there, and has **visited repeatedly**, including a tearful 2023 birthday trip. She's also been politically silent (even on her brother's 2020 State Security interrogation) and was linked in 2024 to the Cuban president's stepson — so do **not** frame her as an anti-regime dissident exile.

- Rework the intro and the Cuba framing: she didn't _flee and never return_. She keeps leaving, and the leaving is **internal, not political**.
- This arguably makes the 4w5 read _cleaner_: it was never about Cuba being unsafe, it was about "something is missing." Lean into that instead of the exile angle.
- Check the FAQs and body for any other "never went back / couldn't return" implications and reconcile them. (The "no escape used about both Cuba and LA" beat can survive — it's about the internal feeling, not literal exile.)

### 2b. Promote the visible/invisible thesis (the upgrade)

A pure "she wants to vanish" read is falsifiable — she holds **three concurrent luxury ambassadorships** (Estée Lauder since Feb 2021, Louis Vuitton since June 2023, Natural Diamond Council's first-ever celebrity ambassador since Sept 2020) and clearly enjoys glamour. The sharper, defensible thesis:

> **She performs an elaborate public self precisely so the private self stays unreachable.** The mask protects. She sells the face (Marilyn) and hides the self (Norma).

- Weave the brand identity in as _evidence for_ this, not as a contradiction to apologize for. The frontmatter already lists "Model" as an occupation that the body never supported — this fixes that gap too.
- Consider promoting her own Marilyn/Norma quote (if verified) to a load-bearing position, possibly challenging or complementing the current epigraph.

### 2c. Add the Paloma beat (concede, don't force)

Her most _beloved_ role (Paloma in _No Time to Die_, 2021) is currently reduced to a fame-marker phrase. Paloma is a fun, weightless, comedic scene-stealer with **no tragic interior** — it genuinely contradicts the narrow "she only picks hidden-interior roles" claim.

- **Concede it openly.** Narrow the thesis from "only picks interior roles" to "**refuses flat archetypes**" — supported by her own line that Bond girls have always been unrelatable and "my character feels like a real woman," and her instrumental framing ("even a tiny part in a James Bond film will make you known globally").
- A thesis that can absorb its best counterexample reads as _more_ credible, not less.

### 2d. Add the 2022–2025 star-vehicle bridge

The draft jumps Blonde (2022) → Ballerina (2025), skipping the streaming-era run (The Gray Man / Netflix, Ghosted / Apple, then Ballerina; plus the against-type villain swing in Ron Howard's _Eden_). These are largely **commercial** roles, not "Norma underneath" excavations.

- Acknowledge honestly: _not every role is a Norma — some are just the job a movie star takes._
- The 4w5 thread survives in **how she frames** the work ("reinvent myself… that's what's fun about being an artist") and the mastery/training fixation. One honest paragraph inoculates the whole thesis against the "you curated the evidence" critique.

### 2e. Firm up the craft verdict

Broaden it (Brief 04): the **Blonde paradox** is the strongest evidence — critics who _savaged the film_ (Dargis, Chang, Lane, etc.) explicitly **exempted and praised her performance**; RT's consensus splits exactly that way. Add the confirmed Best Actress Oscar nomination (first Cuban-born lead-acting nominee). Optional 4w5 counter-beat: she's said the nomination didn't feel like validation.
