## 2026-05-19 — Vanessa Van Edwards "5 winner body language cues"

- **Source**: YouTube interview, _Body Language Expert: This Is Why People Don't Take You Seriously | Vanessa Van Edwards_ on the Craig Groeschel Leadership Podcast — [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zuSf_wSbrXM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zuSf_wSbrXM) (55:06, uploaded 2025-06-04). Full transcript cached at `/tmp/youtube-transcripts/winner-body-language.md` (re-download if expired).
- **The hook**: Vanessa drops the 5 winner body language cues in a single sentence around the 17–19 min mark — the cues leaders use to "infect" a nervous room with confidence instead of catching their fear.

### The 5 cues (verbatim source quote, transcript line 880-883)

> _"That comes from stillness, breath, visible hands, direct eye contact, angling your body towards the person speaking."_

| #   | Cue                                | What it signals                                                                                             |
| --- | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **Stillness**                      | No fidgeting. Calm = confident.                                                                             |
| 2   | **Breath**                         | Speak on the _outbreath_, not the inbreath. Holding breath = nervous "Hello." Releasing = grounded "Hello." |
| 3   | **Visible hands**                  | Palms up = "no weapons, I'm safe." Hidden hands = subconsciously read as threat.                            |
| 4   | **Direct eye contact**             | "I see you, I'm here."                                                                                      |
| 5   | **Body angled toward the speaker** | Broad body pointed at the person = full presence.                                                           |

Bonus cue she keeps circling: **vocal resonance** — speak the first 10 words in the _lowest natural end_ of your range with **no question inflection**. Question inflection = liars and people begging permission.

### Angles to consider

1. **Straight listicle** — "5 body language cues high-charisma leaders never skip." Broad SEO appeal, generic competition.
2. **Vanessa's framing** — "Why people don't take you seriously (and the 5-second fix)." More clickable, still generic.
3. **Enneagram differentiator** _(recommended)_ — "Which Enneagram type breaks which winner cue?" Type 6 fidgets (stillness), Type 9 avoids eye contact, Type 4 hides hands, Type 7 question-inflects everything, Type 3 over-angles. This is the post nobody else can write — earns the click instead of competing with the generic listicle universe.

### Why it's parked

DJ wasn't sure on commitment date. The Enneagram-angle version is the strongest play but needs a real research pass on which type-to-cue mappings hold up (don't ship stereotypes — diagnose them). Could also live as a 5-slide Instagram carousel or a Twitter thread before a full blog.

### Status
