## Contradictions

- She markets _Radical Optimism_ as grace amid chaos, but describes an unusually controlled revision process in which every song was repeatedly changed.
- She appears unbothered in public, yet says the stage-presence meme was humiliating and pushed her off Twitter.
- Critics experience emotional distance in her music, while she describes that distance as a deliberate refusal to use other people's private lives for attention.
- Her manifestation language can sound mystical, but her examples reveal detailed targets followed by sustained work.
- Service95 can be read as empire expansion and as genuine service. The 2026 library and festival role support both readings, so the article should preserve the tension.

## Gaps

- No credible primary or high-quality source was found for the old draft's school-choir rejection scene, the teacher's "better luck next time" wording, or the claim that she kept singing in the choir afterward.
- No traceable source was found for the old Stephen Kozmeniuk epigraph.
- Public evidence cannot establish an Enneagram wing or instinctual stacking with high confidence.
- No official release details for a fourth studio album were identified as of July 2026. British Vogue reported that early work had begun in 2025.

- best scenes/examples to use: remembering the exact meme years later; writing down the Friday-night Glastonbury slot; revising every song repeatedly; opening the Manifesto Library; choosing to speak publicly while keeping private lives out of songs.
