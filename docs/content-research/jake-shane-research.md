# Jake Shane Research — March 2026

## 1. Vanity Fair Oscar Party 2026 Aftermath

### What Happened

Jake Shane (age 26) was hired alongside Quen Blackwell and Brittany Broski to host the red carpet livestream at the Vanity Fair Oscar Party following the 98th Academy Awards in March 2026.

### The Key Incidents

**Julia Fox interaction:**

- Julia Fox praised the Oscar-nominated film _If I Had Legs I'd Kick You_ as "every mother's story"
- Jake interjected: "Do you think the kid was annoying? You know that kid was so damn annoying."
- Fox pushed back: "No, I can't say that... She had issues"
- Fox then delivered a substantive response: "Can I tell you something? It's not that it's the mother's fault or the child's, it is society's fault. It sets mothers up to fail."
- Fox added: "Even the fact that, like, school gets out at 3, but most jobs are done at 5 or 6."

**Damson Idris interaction:**

- Jake tried the same bit with F1 star Damson Idris: "Did you find the daughter so annoying?"
- When Idris declined — "I'm not gonna say that, but..." — Jake doubled down: "You know it! She just went 'Mommy! Mommy! Mommy!' Shut the f\*\*\*k up! Damn!"
- Idris laughed uncomfortably and pivoted away.

**Kris Jenner interaction:**

- Jenner greeted Jake and Quen Blackwell and asked: "How did you get this gig?"
- Jake replied: "I don't know, honestly."
- BuzzFeed headline: "'Kris Asking The Real Questions': People Are Losing It Over Kris Jenner's Hilariously 'Shady' Reaction To Influencers On The Red Carpet At The 2026 Vanity Fair Oscar Party"

**Lewis Pullman interaction:**

- Jake asked Pullman about a trip to Mexico together, implying a personal connection.
- Pullman responded: "God, no, Jake... Who do you think I am?"
- When called out, Jake claimed the question "was written" — implying poor research by his team.

### Social Media Backlash

Specific viral posts/reactions:

- "julia fox beautifully explaining the significance of if i had legs i'd kick you after jake shane's only comment on the film was 'wasnt the kid annoying'" (viral X post)
- "NO WAY he did it AGAIN with damson idris.. find a new joke" (viral X post)
- "no more influencers as hosts... We need people with a degree in journalism or seasoned comedians only" (X user)

- "Jake Shane was trying to be glib and fell on his face... if you're not [genuine], everyone will see through you" (X user)
- Quen Blackwell's response was lighter — she posted: "Julia Fox for president"
- One X user noted: "[Fox] really knows how to put things into perspective... definitely missed the point" (re: Jake)

### Media Coverage

- **Variety** ran a column by Daniel D'Addario: "Jake Shane's Questions at the Vanity Fair Oscar Party Prove Influencers Shouldn't Be Red Carpet Reporters." D'Addario wrote that "Shane's repeated denigration of the film If I Had Legs I'd Kick You struck a strange and discordant note."
- **Refinery29** ran: "Jake Shane's Joke Is Why Rose Byrne's Film Is Necessary"
- **Fast Company** ran: "The 'bestie-fication' of media is doing a disservice to everyone"
- **Pedestrian.tv** ran: "Jake Shane's Oscars Party Red Carpet Coverage Is So Spicy, It's Kicked Off The Journos Vs Influencers Discourse"
- **The Tab** ran: "Jake Shane called out over 'tone deaf' Julia Fox interview at Vanity Fair Oscars party"
- **Daily Dot** ran: "The people demand no more influencers as red carpet hosts after TikTok star fumbles Julia Fox interview"

### Jake Shane's Response

- **No formal apology was issued.** His representatives did not respond to requests for comment.
- His only public statements were from a **pre-existing Rolling Stone interview** (published March 14, before the Oscar party), where he had already addressed the influencer vs. journalist discourse:
  - "I think it is insulting to journalists to say what I do is journalism. I'm not a journalist."
  - "I didn't go to school for journalism. There are real journalists out there asking real, thoughtful, hard questions."
  - "What I am having with people is a conversation... it's not hard-hitting."
- **No dedicated TikTok or Instagram apology was found** in any source.

### Did Vanity Fair Respond?

- No statement from Vanity Fair was found in any of the coverage.
- Vanity Fair's own TikTok posted the clips (the Kris Jenner one was posted by @vanityfair), suggesting they leaned into the virality rather than distancing from it.
- A separate Hollywood Reporter piece covered broader issues at the event: "Inside the Vanity Fair Oscar Party Disaster: Bad Lighting, Brutal Photos, and One Very Nervous Editor" — suggesting the event had problems beyond just Jake.

### Nepotism Angle

- Some observers noted Jake's family entertainment background, framing the hire as an example of nepotism in casting decisions for these events.

## 3. Jake Shane's 7 Wing Energy in Action

### Pop Culture Hyperfixation (OCD Connection)

- **Wondermind essay:** "Something about female pop stars made the world go quiet. Hyperfixation, a symptom of OCD itself, was in fact my only comfort."
- **The Face:** "Each of her albums usher in a new era of my life. She is so good" (about Taylor Swift)
- "Female singer-songwriters are my jam. That's my favorite thing to listen to."
- His hyperfixations include Lady Gaga, Taylor Swift, Lorde, Beyonce, Olivia Rodrigo
- In 2023, his love for pop stars came full circle when he collaborated with Olivia Rodrigo to promote "Vampire" from her album GUTS.
- **Hollywood Reporter:** "I have faith in myself that I'm still gonna be Jake and I'm still gonna be hyper-fixating"

### The "Pusscription" Format (7 Wing as Structure)

- On Therapuss, each session includes a "pusscription" — a pop culture remedy for callers' problems
- Examples:
  - Prescribed "Daddy I Love Him" by Taylor Swift to a caller dating an older man her friends disapprove of
  - Prescribed watching _Jersey Shore_ for a fan whose ex was going on _Love Island Australia_
  - Prescribed an Olivia Rodrigo album, a _Clueless_ rewatch, or a deep dive on Elizabeth Holmes' friendship with Jen Shah
- The format itself is 7-wing energy channeled through 6 structure: taking chaotic pop culture obsessions and turning them into "prescriptions" — making the hyperfixation useful to others.

### Speed/Velocity Energy

- **Rolling Stone/SXSW:** "I have really bad OCD of everything going away. So I like everything to move very fast."
- **Hollywood Reporter:** "That instant gratification is like a literal drug" (about viral success)
- The podcast launched in January 2024, reached #3 on Spotify's Comedy charts and #8 Overall on premiere. By 2026 it's on Netflix.
- His career velocity itself is 7-wing energy: octopus reviews → 100K followers in a week → million in a month → WME signing → Forbes 30 Under 30 → Netflix → Broadway → Hulu series.

### Chaotic/Enthusiastic Behavioral Examples

- **Wonderland:** "I felt that [humor] was the value I brought to the table as a friend" and "if I made people laugh, they would want to be around me"
- **The Face:** "The whole idea of comedy is kind of like, 'Please laugh at me so we can all make sure that this is fine!'"
- **Wonderland:** He can't drive. "Because I can't drive." "I'll Uber the kids." "I seriously can't." (said about future parenthood — the energy of someone who just barrels forward)
- He dresses up as pop stars with friends (Benito Skinner, Terrence O'Connor) as pop stars who "saved 2024"
- His podcast creates "meme-worthy moments" and "unexpected hilarity" per SCMP
- **Hollywood Reporter:** "My toxic trait is that I think everything is golden" — the 7-wing optimism slipping through
- Contrast: "I'm a very, very, very insecure person" (same interview) — the 6 core underneath

### The Contrast (6 Core vs 7 Wing)

The contrast between his anxious interior and his chaotic exterior is well-documented:

- **The Face:** "I'm one of the most insecure people I know, which is why I find it to be such a fun outlet."
- **Hollywood Reporter:** "My toxic trait is that I think everything is golden" vs. "I'm a very, very, very insecure person" (same interview)
- **Wonderland:** "I do want to be famous" + "Definitely, that stems from insecurity. I need this external force to tell me I'm desirable" + "I still feel that way"
- **The Face:** "That I really don't know myself at all. When your job becomes yourself, it's like, who am I?"
- **E! Online:** "I catastrophize a lot of things. Part of my anxiety has always been that when something is going good, all I can think about is how it could go bad" vs. the manic speed at which he pursues everything
