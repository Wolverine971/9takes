## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL RESEARCH BY TYPE

### Type 1: The Perfectionist

**Core Fear:** Being wrong, bad, or corrupt
**Core Wound:** Felt criticized for not being perfect enough

**Red Flag Behaviors:**

- Constant criticism disguised as "helping you improve"
- Rigid rules about "the right way" to do things
- Inability to relax or have fun without guilt
- Making you feel like you're never good enough
- Resentment that builds silently then explodes
- Controlling behavior justified as "doing what's right"

**Root Cause:** Their inner critic tortures them, so they project it onto you

**Healthy vs. Unhealthy:**

- Healthy 1: Offers constructive feedback, accepts imperfection
- Unhealthy 1: Makes you feel perpetually flawed

---

### Type 2: The Helper

**Core Fear:** Being unwanted, unloved
**Core Wound:** Felt they had to earn love through giving

**Red Flag Behaviors:**

- Giving with strings attached (keeping score)
- Making you feel guilty for not needing them enough
- Intrusive "helpfulness" that ignores your boundaries
- Becoming a martyr ("I do everything for you!")
- Triangulating (being everyone's savior to feel needed)
- Emotional manipulation through guilt

**Root Cause:** They give to get; when the return isn't there, resentment builds

**Healthy vs. Unhealthy:**

- Healthy 2: Gives freely, respects boundaries
- Unhealthy 2: Uses giving as control mechanism

---

### Type 3: The Achiever

**Core Fear:** Being worthless or a failure
**Core Wound:** Only valued for achievements, not who they are

**Red Flag Behaviors:**

- Image management (you're a trophy, not a partner)
- Workaholism that leaves no room for relationship
- Lying or exaggerating to maintain image
- Competing with you instead of supporting you
- Emotional unavailability (feelings = weakness)
- Chameleon behavior (different person with different people)

**Root Cause:** Their identity is their success; relationship is secondary

**Healthy vs. Unhealthy:**

- Healthy 3: Vulnerable, authentic, present
- Unhealthy 3: Performing constantly, even with you

---

### Type 4: The Individualist

**Core Fear:** Being ordinary, having no identity
**Core Wound:** Felt fundamentally different/defective

**Red Flag Behaviors:**

- Emotional volatility (everything is a crisis)
- Push-pull dynamics (intense pursuit then withdrawal)
- Comparing your relationship unfavorably to an idealized fantasy
- Making everything about their feelings
- Chronic dissatisfaction ("something is missing")
- Using melancholy to get attention

**Root Cause:** They're chasing an emotional intensity that can never be sustained

**Healthy vs. Unhealthy:**

- Healthy 4: Emotionally deep but stable
- Unhealthy 4: Drama addiction, chronic dissatisfaction

---

### Type 5: The Investigator

**Core Fear:** Being useless, incompetent, overwhelmed
**Core Wound:** Felt their resources (time, energy) were inadequate

**Red Flag Behaviors:**

- Extreme withdrawal (disappearing for days)
- Emotional unavailability (intellectualizing feelings)
- Hoarding time/energy at relationship's expense
- Making you feel like a burden
- Compartmentalizing you out of their "real" life
- Superiority through intelligence

**Root Cause:** They protect themselves by minimizing needs (including yours)

**Healthy vs. Unhealthy:**

- Healthy 5: Present, sharing inner world
- Unhealthy 5: Fortress mentality, you're outside the walls

---

### Type 6: The Loyalist

**Core Fear:** Being without support, guidance
**Core Wound:** Learned the world is dangerous, trust is risky

**Red Flag Behaviors:**

- Testing your loyalty constantly
- Worst-case-scenario spiraling (anxiety-driven)
- Suspicion without cause
- Flip-flopping (trusting then doubting)
- Seeking reassurance that's never enough
- Projecting fear onto you ("you're going to leave")

**Root Cause:** Their anxiety makes them see threats everywhere, including in you

**Healthy vs. Unhealthy:**

- Healthy 6: Loyal, courageous, trusting
- Unhealthy 6: Paranoid, accusatory, clingy

---

### Type 7: The Enthusiast

**Core Fear:** Being trapped in pain, deprivation
**Core Wound:** Learned to escape pain through positivity/distraction

**Red Flag Behaviors:**

- Avoiding serious conversations
- Commitment phobia (always keeping options open)
- Minimizing your problems ("just be positive!")
- Scattered attention (you're one of many interests)
- Impulsivity that affects your stability
- Running when things get hard

**Root Cause:** They flee from anything that threatens their freedom or forces them to feel pain

**Healthy vs. Unhealthy:**

- Healthy 7: Present, committed, handles hard things
- Unhealthy 7: Escapist, unreliable, emotionally avoidant

---

### Type 8: The Challenger

**Core Fear:** Being controlled, harmed, vulnerable
**Core Wound:** Learned vulnerability = danger, must be strong

**Red Flag Behaviors:**

- Dominating conversations and decisions
- Anger as default emotion (intimidation)
- "My way or the highway" attitude
- Dismissing your feelings as weakness
- Power plays in the relationship
- Inability to admit being wrong

**Root Cause:** They equate control with safety; vulnerability feels like death

**Healthy vs. Unhealthy:**

- Healthy 8: Protective, vulnerable with you
- Unhealthy 8: Controlling, intimidating, ruthless

---

### Type 9: The Peacemaker

**Core Fear:** Loss, separation, conflict
**Core Wound:** Learned their needs don't matter, must keep the peace

**Red Flag Behaviors:**

- Passive aggression instead of direct communication
- "Going along to get along" then resenting you
- Numbing out (physically present, emotionally absent)
- Procrastinating on important relationship issues
- Stubbornness disguised as agreeableness
- Making you the bad guy for having needs

**Root Cause:** They merge with others to avoid conflict, losing themselves and you

**Healthy vs. Unhealthy:**

- Healthy 9: Present, assertive, engaged
- Unhealthy 9: Checked out, passive-aggressive, stubborn
