# 9takes Enneagram Mask Expression Research

## Working decision

The 3×3 Mask Mosaic remains the strongest logo direction. The next version should use nine masks from one coherent sculptural family, with each mask carrying a restrained facial tension derived from an Enneagram passion.

The governing art-direction rule is:

> **Passion in the expression. Virtue in the restraint.**

The masks should not be nine exaggerated “sin faces.” Each should remain recognizably human, calm enough to belong to the same identity system, and expressive enough that its particular psychological tension can be felt.

## Canonical Enneagram source for this project

For consistency, this project will use the table on Wikipedia’s [Enneagram of Personality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enneagram_of_Personality#Nine_types) page as the canonical passion/virtue mapping:

| Type | Passion | Virtue |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 1 | Anger | Serenity |
| 2 | Pride | Humility |
| 3 | Deceit | Truthfulness |
| 4 | Envy | Equanimity |
| 5 | Avarice | Detachment / non-attachment |
| 6 | Fear | Courage |
| 7 | Gluttony | Sobriety |
| 8 | Lust | Innocence |
| 9 | Sloth | Action |

The table notes that Enneagram teachers differ on terminology. This is a symbolic identity system, not a claim that Enneagram passions are scientifically established emotion categories.

## What facial-expression research can and cannot do

The [Facial Action Coding System](https://www.paulekman.com/facial-action-coding-system/) is an anatomically based vocabulary for visible facial movement. It can help us specify brows, eyelids, cheeks, lips, jaw, and head position instead of relying on vague prompt words.

It does **not** give every Enneagram passion a universal face:

- Anger and fear have well-established facial configurations.
- Pride has a recognizable nonverbal display, but research emphasizes a small smile, head tilted slightly back, and expanded body posture—not the face alone. See Tracy and Robins, [“Show Your Pride”](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1111/j.0956-7976.2004.01503008.x).
- Deceit has no dependable “lying face.” A meta-analysis found that behavioral cues to actual deception are weak, and common stereotypes such as avoiding eye contact are poor signals. See Hartwig and Bond, [“Why Do Lie-Catchers Fail?”](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21707129/).
- Envy is strongly context-dependent. Research shows observers infer envy from the relationship between a face, another person, and a valued object—not from one universal expression. See [“You’re Just Envious”](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34990194/).
- Avarice, gluttony, lust, and sloth are motivational habits in Enneagram usage, not basic momentary emotions.

Therefore, the masks should be treated as **designed psychological translations**, supported by facial anatomy where possible, rather than scientific emotion diagrams.

## Enneagram meaning corrections

Several words must be translated from their Enneagram meaning rather than their everyday cliché:

- **Type 3 deceit** is image-identification and self-deception, not a criminal or shifty liar. The Narrative Enneagram describes Threes as taking on a role so completely that contact with the inner self is lost. [Type 3](https://www.narrativeenneagram.org/enneagram-type/type-3-the-performer/)
- **Type 5 avarice** is conserving time, space, energy, knowledge, and the self—not a money-hungry grin. [Type 5](https://www.narrativeenneagram.org/enneagram-type/type-5-the-observer/)
- **Type 7 gluttony** is appetite for options, stimulation, ideas, and pleasurable experience—not eating. [Type 7](https://www.narrativeenneagram.org/enneagram-type/type-7-the-enthusiast/)
- **Type 8 lust** is intensity, excess, impact, and forceful life energy—not seduction. [Type 8](https://www.narrativeenneagram.org/enneagram-type/type-8-the-protector/)
- **Type 9 sloth** is self-forgetting, inertia, and disengagement from personal priorities—not merely sleepiness. [Type 9](https://www.narrativeenneagram.org/enneagram-type/type-9-the-mediator/)

## Proposed expression specification

Expression intensity should stay around **30–40%**. No screaming, snarling, crying, leering, or cartoon mugging. Each mask gets two or three dominant cues.

### Type 1 — Anger as contained correction

**Inner state:** anger held under discipline; resentment and critical tension rather than an outburst.

**Mask cues:**

- brows lowered and drawn slightly inward;
- upper eyelids firm and eye openings narrowed;
- lips compressed into a precise horizontal line;
- jaw set; face vertically rigid and symmetrical.

**Avoid:** bared teeth, flared nostrils, shouting mouth, or explosive rage. Those belong too close to Type 8.

**Distinctive read:** **constricted force**.

The Narrative Enneagram describes Ones as controlling anger and carrying tension especially in the jaw and related musculature. [Type 1](https://www.narrativeenneagram.org/enneagram-type/type-1-the-improver/)

### Type 2 — Pride as indispensable warmth

**Inner state:** “I know what you need; I am important because I am needed.”

**Mask cues:**

- small, closed, welcoming smile;
- cheeks gently lifted and eye openings softly narrowed;
- chin raised a few degrees;
- upper face open and attentive rather than domineering.

**Avoid:** unilateral smug smirk, contempt, or royal arrogance. Type 2 pride should remain relational.

**Distinctive read:** **warm self-importance**.

Pride research supports a small smile and slight backward head tilt, while Enneagram descriptions emphasize being indispensable to others. [Type 2](https://www.narrativeenneagram.org/enneagram-type/type-2-the-giver/)

### Type 3 — Deceit as the perfect performance

**Inner state:** the public expression is polished, but the feeling behind it is inaccessible.

**Mask cues:**

- perfectly symmetrical social smile in the mouth;
- eye and cheek region remains smooth and emotionally neutral;
- immaculate, highly controlled facial symmetry;
- optional single fine central seam to suggest image versus inner self.

**Avoid:** shifting eyes, crossed eyes, long nose, villain smirk, or “caught lying” anxiety. Research does not support a universal deceit face.

**Distinctive read:** **a smile that does not reach the eyes**.

Research distinguishes mouth-only social smiles from smiles involving both mouth and eyes. See [Sheldon, Goffredi, and Corcoran](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/03010066211027052). Type 3 should occupy the center cell because the mask/persona metaphor is most literal here.

### Type 4 — Envy as longing toward what is missing

**Inner state:** comparison, longing, and awareness of deficiency—not hostility toward another person.

**Mask cues:**

- inner brow lifted slightly, producing a restrained melancholy tension;
- mask or eye apertures angled subtly toward the neighboring Type 3 mask;
- chin lowered slightly;
- lips closed, with one corner just perceptibly lowered.

**Avoid:** green color, angry glare, sneer, or melodramatic sadness.

**Distinctive read:** **sideways longing**.

The Narrative Enneagram describes Type 4 attention as going to what is missing, distant, or unattainable. [Type 4](https://www.narrativeenneagram.org/enneagram-type/type-4-the-individualist/)

### Type 5 — Avarice as withholding

**Inner state:** conserving inner resources and observing without being intruded upon.

**Mask cues:**

- narrow, level eye openings;
- small sealed mouth with minimal lip volume;
- cheeks slightly withdrawn rather than expressive;
- mask sits subtly deeper in its cell, as if retreating from contact.

**Avoid:** greedy smile, money imagery, grasping mouth, or exaggerated suspicion.

**Distinctive read:** **guarded inwardness**.

### Type 6 — Fear as vigilant uncertainty

**Inner state:** scanning for what may go wrong while trying to remain prepared.

**Mask cues:**

- inner and outer brows raised but lightly drawn together;
- eye openings wider than every other mask;
- lower eyelids tense;
- mouth slightly parted or horizontally stretched;
- mask recoils a few millimeters within the cell.

**Avoid:** screaming, horror sockets, tears, or a helpless childlike expression.

**Distinctive read:** **alert recoil**.

The Narrative Enneagram describes Type 6 as vigilant, doubtful, and oriented toward possible hazards. [Type 6](https://www.narrativeenneagram.org/enneagram-type/type-6-the-questioner/)

### Type 7 — Gluttony as anticipatory appetite

**Inner state:** the next possibility already looks exciting; attention reaches beyond the present option.

**Mask cues:**

- outer brows raised;
- eyes open and directed slightly upward/forward;
- symmetrical anticipatory smile;
- lips lightly parted, without showing teeth;
- mask projects slightly forward in its cell.

**Avoid:** food, tongue, chewing, bloated cheeks, or manic laughter.

**Distinctive read:** **eager expansion**.

Research on functional smiles found that reward smiles are symmetrical and accompanied by eyebrow raising, which gives us an anatomically plausible base for anticipatory pleasure. See [Rychlowska et al., “Functional Smiles”](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0956797617706082).

### Type 8 — Lust as intensity and excess

**Inner state:** strong life force moving outward; impact, appetite, and refusal to be controlled.

**Mask cues:**

- direct, level eye openings with a steady gaze;
- brows low but not pinched together;
- nostrils subtly widened;
- broad jaw and chin pushed forward;
- mouth slightly parted, as if about to speak directly.

**Avoid:** seduction, half-lidded bedroom eyes, lip biting, sexualization, or an anger snarl.

**Distinctive read:** **expanded force**.

This must contrast with Type 1: One compresses and contains force; Eight expands and projects it.

### Type 9 — Sloth as self-forgetting

**Inner state:** low activation, diffuse attention, and absence from one’s own priorities.

**Mask cues:**

- brow smooth and almost neutral;
- upper lids lowered, but eyes not closed;
- mouth relaxed with no decisive directional pull;
- jaw and cheeks soft;
- mask sits slightly deeper and receives subtly lower contrast.

**Avoid:** yawning, sleeping, drooling, sadness, or comic laziness.

**Distinctive read:** **soft disengagement**.

## Collision matrix

The following pairs are the highest risk and should be tested side by side:

| Collision | Required distinction |
| --- | --- |
| 1 anger vs. 8 intensity | 1 contracts and compresses; 8 expands and advances. |
| 2 pride vs. 3 performance | 2 has warmth in eyes and cheeks; 3 smiles only with the mouth. |
| 2 pride vs. 7 appetite | 2 tilts back and feels self-assured; 7 leans forward and anticipates. |
| 3 performance vs. 7 appetite | 3 is controlled and polished; 7 is open and energized. |
| 4 envy vs. 6 fear | 4 looks sideways/down toward what is missing; 6 scans outward with widened eyes. |
| 5 avarice vs. 9 sloth | 5 is tight and guarded; 9 is soft and diffuse. |
| 6 fear vs. 8 intensity | 6 recoils; 8 advances. |

## Recommended 3×3 arrangement

Use the traditional centers of intelligence as rows:

```text
8  9  1   Body / gut center
2  3  4   Heart / image center
5  6  7   Head / fear center
```

This order is documented by both [The Narrative Enneagram](https://www.narrativeenneagram.org/three-centers-of-intelligence/) and the Wikipedia overview. It gives the final icon an internal system rather than an arbitrary reading order:

- the body row carries tension primarily through jaw, weight, and muscular compression/expansion;
- the heart row carries tension through smile, image, and relational orientation;
- the head row carries tension through eye aperture, attention, and approach/withdrawal;
- Type 3 occupies the center, making persona and mask the literal center of the mark.

For review sheets, every cell should still be labeled clearly with its type number outside the production artwork.

## Production methodology

### Phase 1 — Neutral master mask

Create one androgynous, age-neutral theatrical mask with simplified sculptural planes. No culturally specific makeup, costume, hair, or ornament. Use one neutral bone material and one controlled light setup.

### Phase 2 — Nine individual expression studies

Derive nine masks from the same neutral master. Generate and review each mask individually so facial geometry can be controlled. Keep camera, proportions, material, lighting, and crop fixed; change only expression-relevant planes.

Each study should include:

- front view at 512–1024 pixels;
- a small 128-pixel preview;
- a short expression label;
- the two or three intended facial cues.

### Phase 3 — Pairwise collision tests

Create comparison sheets for the risky pairs in the collision matrix. The target is not that an uninformed viewer guesses the exact theological word. The target is that each mask feels psychologically distinct and matches the intended direction after explanation.

### Phase 4 — Blind read

Show the unlabeled nine-mask sheet to 5–10 people. Ask for one or two words describing each face. Record:

- dominant perceived emotion;
- activation level: withdrawn, neutral, or advancing;
- whether any two masks feel identical;
- whether any face feels villainous, sexualized, sleepy, or horrific.

Revise masks whose dominant read conflicts with the brief.

### Phase 5 — Assemble the mosaic

Composite approved individual masks deterministically into the 8–9–1 / 2–3–4 / 5–6–7 grid. Do not ask an image model to redraw all nine simultaneously after approval; that would lose expression fidelity and consistency.

### Phase 6 — Material and virtue layer

Only after expressions are approved, test whether the three centers receive subtle material families or lighting behavior. The virtue should be expressed through overall restraint, clarity, repair, and equal dignity—not through a second competing facial expression.

### Phase 7 — Scaling

- 512–1024 px: full expression and material detail.
- 128–192 px: masks and primary brow/mouth differences remain visible.
- 64 px: nine faces and broad activation differences remain visible.
- 32 px: the 3×3 mask system is the primary read.
- 16–24 px: use a future simplified companion mark rather than forcing nine detailed masks into a favicon.

## Approval gates before final artwork

1. Approve the nine written expression briefs.
2. Approve one neutral master mask.
3. Approve the nine individual expression studies.
4. Approve the triad arrangement.
5. Approve the assembled monochrome mosaic.
6. Explore palette and material variants.
7. Replace live application assets only after final approval.

## Study 01 implementation — July 22, 2026

The first controlled expression set is saved in [`source-assets/brand/exploration/2026-07-22/mask-expression-v5/`](../../source-assets/brand/exploration/2026-07-22/mask-expression-v5/).

Production followed the intended identity-lock method:

- one neutral warm-bone master mask was generated first;
- each expression was derived separately from that same master;
- all nine masks were assembled locally in the triad order rather than regenerated as a group;
- Types 2, 3, and 8 received a targeted second pass after the first comparison sheet;
- 512, 256, 128, 64, and 32-pixel scaling tests were created from the same deterministic composite;
- no live application icon, favicon, or production logo asset was replaced.

### Initial visual read

The first study confirms that the expression system can remain coherent while producing visible psychological contrast. The clearest collision-test wins are:

- Type 1 contracts while Type 8 braces and advances;
- Type 4 lowers and turns inward while Type 6 opens into vigilance;
- Type 5 seals and withholds while Type 9 softens and disengages;
- Type 3 remains controlled while Type 7 expands into open anticipation.

Type 2 versus Type 3 was the least resolved pair in the first assembly. A focused cleanup replaced Type 2 with a more expressive study: prominently lifted cheeks reshape the eye apertures into warm crescents, while Type 3 retains neutral eyes and cheek planes with a precise mouth-only smile. The revised distinction is now obvious on the focused collision board and remains more legible in the 64-pixel mosaic. It should still be included in blind-read feedback.

The generated masks also show small identity and material drift between derivatives. This is acceptable for concept validation, but final production artwork should normalize outer silhouettes, scale, value range, and surface grain before adoption.

### Focused cleanup — Pride versus Deceit

The initial Pride and Deceit derivatives were nearly identical. Three new candidates were generated: two warmer Pride variants and one colder Deceit fallback. The selected solution swaps only Pride and preserves the existing center Type 3 mask.

- **Selected Pride:** genuine cheek activation, eye apertures narrowed from below into warm crescents, broader closed smile.
- **Preserved Deceit:** neutral upper face, open almond eye apertures, small immaculate mouth-only smile.
- **Rejected Pride alternate:** clear and attractive, but too cheerful and therefore closer to Type 7 Gluttony.
- **Unused Deceit fallback:** slightly colder, but not sufficiently better than the existing Type 3 to justify another identity shift.
