# 9takes Logo Methodology V2 — The Night Agora

**Status:** Active concept reset  
**Date:** 2026-07-22  
**Scope:** Symbol first. No palette, material, app-icon rendering, or live replacement until the monochrome mark is approved.

## The corrected brand idea

9takes is not primarily a `9`, a personality test, or a technology product.

It is a **meeting place for perspectives**: one person speaks, the others listen, and attention moves around a group of equals. The contemporary expression is a private meetup beneath a streetlamp. Its historical ancestor is the agora and the symposium: people gathering around an open center to test ideas through conversation.

### Symbolic hierarchy

The logo cannot carry every part of the story equally. The order is:

1. **Gathering and mutual attention** — the first visual read.
2. **Ancient lineage of public conversation** — the distinctive cultural layer.
3. **Multiple equal perspectives** — encoded through balance, not a camera-like radial diagram.
4. **Nine** — allowed to be implicit; the name already carries the number.
5. **Night and firelight** — expressed later through the identity palette, not required in the black silhouette.

## Historical basis

- *Agora* means an open place of assembly. The Athenian Agora was the civic center for meetings, events, public life, and philosophical pursuits.
- Greek symposia were structured social gatherings for conversation. Participants communicated across an open central space, and a shared krater occupied a prominent central position.
- The civic hearth represented the unity and vitality of the community. Hestia was associated with the prytaneion and bouleuterion, the symbolic civic center where public business was conducted.
- The Nine Muses provide a genuine Greek precedent for nine distinct domains of knowledge and expression, but they are supporting mythology rather than the primary logo metaphor.

## Translation rule

Historical accuracy informs the metaphor; it does not require archaeological illustration.

The final mark should feel like it **could have been carved into a stone meeting place**, but it must still operate as a modern app icon at 16 px. We will not draw Plato, a temple façade, nine tiny statues, an Enneagram diagram, or an ornamental Greek key.

## Concept territories

Each territory uses no more than two Brandkit concept methods.

### A — The Common Hearth

**Methods:** Metaphor fusion + negative space  
**Fusion:** civic hearth + open assembly

Two equal stone forms hold an open center. The negative space becomes a small shared flame or pool of attention. It should communicate “we gathered here” before it communicates fire.

**Reject if it resembles:** Olympic cauldron, candle app, religious flame, location pin, power button, or camera aperture.

### B — The Meeting of Minds

**Methods:** Metaphor fusion + negative space  
**Fusion:** equal philosopher profiles + shared idea

Two reduced, classical profile forms face one another. Neither dominates. The space between them forms the shared object of inquiry: a flame, opening, or uncarved stone column.

**Reject if it resembles:** therapy, theatre masks, Janus, a university seal, legal services, or a generic chat icon.

### C — The Open Agora

**Methods:** Product action + construction geometry  
**Action:** attention passes among equal participants

Nine seat-like stones form an open horseshoe rather than a closed radial wheel. The center remains empty, and one seat may angle inward to indicate the current speaker without changing its size or status.

**Reject if it resembles:** camera aperture, loading spinner, clock, crown, Wi-Fi symbol, stadium, cult seal, or Enneagram diagram.

## Non-negotiable tests

1. **Five-second association:** show the mark without explanation and record the first noun and verb it suggests.
2. **One-color test:** black on white and white on black before palette.
3. **16/32/64 px test:** the defining negative space must remain open.
4. **Silhouette test:** no internal detail may be essential to recognition.
5. **Mask test:** square, squircle, and circle.
6. **Historical cliché test:** Greek lineage without defaulting to columns, helmets, laurels, Omega, or ornamental meanders.
7. **Equality test:** no throne, podium, central authority, oversized speaker, or ranked participant.

## Decision rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Meeting / mutual-attention read | 25 |
| Distinctive ancient-to-modern lineage | 20 |
| Small-size legibility | 20 |
| Ownability / distance from category clichés | 15 |
| Equality of participants | 10 |
| Flexibility across icon, wordmark, and seal | 10 |

A mark must score at least **84/100**, pass every non-negotiable test, and beat the existing candidates in an unlabelled comparison.

## Approval gate

This round creates monochrome concept sheets and one refined candidate only. It does not replace the live icon. Palette development and platform asset generation begin only after the symbol is approved.

## Execution results

### Rejected directions

- **Common Hearth:** reduced to mirrored brackets and power-button associations. It did not communicate people or conversation.
- **Meeting of Minds — two classical profiles:** communicated conversation, but drifted toward therapy, legal services, or a university program.
- **Open Agora:** literal seats became a loading indicator or clock at small sizes.
- **Night Symposium seal:** the gathering was emotionally accurate but became illustrative clip art, and exact participant counting was unreliable.
- **Theta / Greek numeral nine:** theta did carry the value nine in Greek numerals and later became an angle symbol, but it also carries historical death-marker associations and strong mathematical/fraternity associations. The explanation was more compelling than the mark.

### Selected territory — Across Time

The selected construction combines:

- one reduced ancient philosopher profile;
- one equal modern hooded profile;
- a shared secular hearth / question in the negative space;
- an outer near-circle completed by both participants.

The mark does not literally draw all nine people. It represents the **kind of meeting** 9takes creates; the brand name carries the count.

**Rationale:** Ancient voice. Modern voice. Same fire.

| Criterion | Provisional score |
| --- | ---: |
| Meeting / mutual-attention read | 23 / 25 |
| Distinctive ancient-to-modern lineage | 19 / 20 |
| Small-size legibility | 16 / 20 |
| Ownability / distance from category clichés | 12 / 15 |
| Equality of participants | 10 / 10 |
| Flexibility across icon, wordmark, and seal | 8 / 10 |
| **Total** | **88 / 100** |

The symbol passes monochrome, reverse, and 16/32/64 px rendering tests. Its open risk is first association: the central hearth must read as shared inquiry rather than religion. That requires a blind five-second test before palette work.
