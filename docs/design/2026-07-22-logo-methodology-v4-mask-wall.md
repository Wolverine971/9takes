# 9takes Logo Methodology V4 — The Mask Mosaic

## Reset

The previous “Nine-Voice Agora” mark failed because the profiles were reduced past recognition. The resulting wedges communicated segmentation and rotation before they communicated people, masks, or perspective.

The new reference is `static/blogs/s-cracked_mask_wall.webp`. Its useful visual language is:

- immediately recognizable sculpted human masks;
- hollow or deeply shadowed eyes;
- strong brow, cheek, nose, and mouth planes;
- controlled differences between faces;
- material contrast between ivory, dark stone, and aged metal;
- theatrical museum lighting against a black field;
- a collection of selves presented as one system.

The reference is treated as visual-language inspiration only. The new mark does not reproduce its masks, arrangement, case, lighting fixtures, labels, or culturally specific traditional designs.

## Core idea

> **Nine masks. Nine perspectives. One cabinet of mind.**

The preferred construction is a **3×3 Mask Mosaic**:

- nine actual sculpted masks make the number nine literal;
- the 3×3 matrix retains the satisfying facelet logic of the original cube;
- dimensionality comes from the masks’ relief, not a thin isometric side;
- the night-black field connects to the intimate after-dark conversation atmosphere;
- differences in expression and material communicate perspective without explanatory symbols.

## Symbol hierarchy

1. **Nine recognizable faces** — human perspectives, not abstract geometry.
2. **A single square system** — nine takes held together.
3. **Distinct psychological presence** — each face sees and responds differently.
4. **Theatrical mask lineage** — the self as a role, lens, or visible persona.
5. **Sculptural depth** — historical weight expressed through material and light.

## Construction constraints

- Exactly three rows and three columns.
- Exactly one mask per cell.
- Nine masks total.
- Every mask is front-facing and clearly human.
- Differences come from broad facial geometry, not costume or cultural stereotype.
- Narrow, substantial charcoal gutters; no fragile hairline side planes.
- The full icon remains square and safe under iOS corner cropping.
- Material palette is limited to warm bone, charcoal, and muted bronze.
- One repaired crack is permitted as a detail; shattered or injured faces are not.
- The mood is mysterious and intelligent, never horror.

## Exploration outcome

### Route A — Nine-Mask Cabinet

Nine freestanding sculpted masks on a black field. This established the correct facial language and material range. It is the strongest atmospheric brand image, but its loose spacing is less effective as an app icon.

### Route B — Dense Mask Mosaic

Nine close-cropped masks in recessed cells. The grid and icon silhouette worked, but realistic wrinkles, aggressive brows, and large empty eye sockets pushed the result toward horror.

### Route C — Calm Mask Mosaic — selected

The final refinement keeps the 3×3 relief construction while simplifying the planes, softening the eyes, limiting age detail, and giving each face a restrained emotional presence. It reads as a designed identity object rather than a museum photograph or horror prop.

## Scaling strategy

- **64–1024 px:** use the complete nine-mask mosaic.
- **32–48 px:** the primary read is a 3×3 field of faces; individual expressions become secondary.
- **16–24 px favicon:** a future micro-mark should use one selected mask or a simplified nine-eye matrix. The full sculptural artwork should not be forced into a size where it becomes noise.

This is a raster identity direction. If approved, the next production phase should create:

1. a controlled high-resolution master;
2. a simplified vector companion mark for favicon and monochrome use;
3. final palette variants;
4. iOS, Android, PWA, and social exports;
5. an updated wordmark relationship.

## Generation record

The three raster explorations were created with the built-in image-generation tool in `logo-brand` mode. The supplied mask-wall image was used as a visual-language reference, not as an edit target. Prompts required exactly nine original masks, a 3×3 structure, non-horror emotional tone, culturally nonspecific sculptural forms, and no text or copied traditional mask designs.

No live application or favicon assets have been replaced.
