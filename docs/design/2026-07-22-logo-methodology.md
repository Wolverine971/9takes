# 9takes Logo Methodology

**Status:** Active identity exploration  
**Date:** 2026-07-22  
**Scope:** Primary logo mark, wordmark relationship, favicon, and installed-app icon

## Why the prior rounds failed

The previous work treated the app-icon rendering as the logo. Material, glow, depth, and polish arrived before the underlying symbol was proven.

The rejected directions are now explicit constraints:

- the perspective cube becomes a stray side line at small sizes;
- the flat 3×3 grid is generic and says “app launcher” more than “9takes”;
- the bronze `9` / speech-bubble fusion is too literal and too dependent on rendering;
- the aperture reads as a camera or turbine before it reads as nine perspectives.

No future direction may use polish to compensate for a weak symbol.

## Research basis

- The installed **Brandkit** skill requires strategy before symbols and limits each route to one or two concept methods: monogram + meaning, product action, metaphor fusion, negative space, or construction geometry.
- AIGA’s branding guidance says concepts should spring from something innate to the business and that the logo is only one part of the identity system.
- Henderson and Cote’s empirical study of 195 logos found that recognition and positive image are associated with harmony, naturalness, and moderate—not maximal—elaboration.
- Apple’s app-icon guidance prioritizes a unique, memorable icon that communicates purpose and personality at a glance.
- Google’s launcher-icon guidance says to simplify, avoid text and excessive effects, preserve contrast, test masks, and prefer vector artwork.

## Strategy capsule

| Dimension | Decision |
| --- | --- |
| Category | Personality-based Q&A and social-intelligence platform |
| Product action | Give your honest take → unlock the chorus → compare nine emotional readings |
| Emotional promise | Turn “they are being difficult” into “I can see the alarm system they are using” |
| Core metaphor | One situation opens into a chorus of perspectives |
| Audience | People who want practical help reading conflict, relationships, and social dynamics |
| Personality | Tactically direct, socially savvy, curious, quietly provocative |
| Cultural position | Ancient question-first discourse made modern; warm-tech rather than mystical |
| Visual world | Streetlamp Symposium: deep warm stone, sodium amber, disciplined negative space |

### The single brand truth

> Your take feels like reality until the room opens and reveals eight other coherent ways to see it.

### The logo’s job

The mark must identify 9takes at a glance and encode **perspective becoming plural**. It does not need to explain the Enneagram, depict nine literal people, or carry the entire Streetlamp visual world by itself.

## Hard constraints

The mark must:

1. work in one color before any palette or material is applied;
2. remain recognizable at 16, 32, and 64 px;
3. survive square, squircle, and circular masks;
4. have a one-sentence rationale tied to the product action;
5. work beside the lowercase `9takes` wordmark and alone as an app icon;
6. be reconstructable as clean vector geometry;
7. remain distinctive without glow, texture, shadow, or a mockup.

The mark must not resemble:

- a camera aperture, turbine, gear, or settings control;
- a generic chat bubble, quotation mark, question mark, or notification icon;
- an app-launcher grid or Rubik’s cube;
- an eye, pyramid, sacred-geometry diagram, or mystical seal;
- a personality-test cartoon or generic AI sparkle.

## Concept territories

Each route uses at most two Brandkit methods.

### Route A — The Reveal

**Methods:** Product action + negative space  
**Idea:** A compact closed form separates into two planes; the space between them becomes the revealed perspective. The symbol should feel like an unlock or curtain opening without depicting a literal door or lock.

### Route B — The Chorus

**Methods:** Product action + construction geometry  
**Idea:** Nine equal modules form an open, asymmetric council around one shared situation. It must read as multiple voices becoming a coherent whole, not as an aperture or Enneagram diagram.

### Route C — The 9t Signature

**Methods:** Monogram + negative space  
**Idea:** A custom `9t` ligature or a modified `9takes` wordmark becomes the primary identity. The `9` and `t` share one structural move that suggests a change in viewpoint. This route favors ownability and recognition over literal symbolism.

## Creation process

### 1. Monochrome divergence

Generate at least six flat black-and-white sketches per territory. No gradients, textures, lighting, shadows, bevels, mockups, or color.

### 2. Semantic rejection

Remove any sketch whose first association is a banned category. A clever explanation cannot rescue the wrong first impression.

### 3. Thumbnail test

View survivors at 16, 32, and 64 px on both light and dark backgrounds. Remove anything that closes up, becomes a stray line, or loses its defining negative space.

### 4. Mask and silhouette test

Preview square, squircle, and circular crops. Convert the mark to a solid silhouette. It must retain its identity in every state.

### 5. Scored selection

| Criterion | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Brand truth / product-action fit | 25 |
| Distinctiveness and first impression | 20 |
| Small-size legibility | 20 |
| Ownability / low genericity | 15 |
| Flexibility across wordmark, app, and monochrome | 10 |
| Fit with Streetlamp Symposium | 10 |

A route must score **80/100 or higher** and pass every hard constraint.

### 6. Vector refinement

Rebuild the selected mark as deterministic SVG. Normalize optical balance, spacing, corner behavior, and stroke/fill relationships. Create horizontal wordmark, mark-only, monochrome, reverse, and small-size variants.

### 7. Color and application

Only after the vector is approved, apply the Streetlamp palette and create Apple, Android, maskable, social-avatar, and favicon outputs. Rendering may enrich the app icon, but it cannot change the underlying mark.

## Approval gate

The currently deployed icon assets remain provisional. A new mark replaces them only after the monochrome concept, small-size preview, and one-sentence rationale are approved together.

## Execution: round one

Eighteen monochrome sketches were generated across the three territories, followed by six refinements of the strongest `9t` construction.

### What survived

- **Route A — The Reveal:** one split-plane sketch survived the semantic pass, but it looked like a generic portal or open book at small sizes.
- **Route B — The Chorus:** the nine-part arrangements carried the best story at large size, but their individual modules collapsed at 16 px and several drifted toward an aperture or grid.
- **Route C — The 9t Signature:** the flowing `9t` ligature was the only direction that remained identifiable at 16 px without relying on color or rendering.

### Candidate 01 — The Turn Mark

**Rationale:** One take enters as the `9`; the shared rising stroke turns the viewpoint; the mark resolves as `t` for takes.

The selected construction was redrawn as deterministic SVG with smooth Bézier curves and one protected negative-space seam. It has no strokes, effects, masks, or raster dependencies.

| Criterion | Score |
| --- | ---: |
| Brand truth / product-action fit | 21 / 25 |
| Distinctiveness and first impression | 16 / 20 |
| Small-size legibility | 19 / 20 |
| Ownability / low genericity | 11 / 15 |
| Flexibility across wordmark, app, and monochrome | 10 / 10 |
| Fit with Streetlamp Symposium | 9 / 10 |
| **Total** | **86 / 100** |

The candidate passes the monochrome, 16/32/64 px, reverse-color, and square/squircle/circle mask tests. Its weaker score is ownability: `9t` is a naturally crowded monogram category. An informal web check did not surface this exact construction, but it is not a legal trademark clearance. Formal image and class-based clearance is required before registration or a high-cost identity rollout.
