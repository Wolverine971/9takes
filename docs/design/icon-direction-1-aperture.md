# Icon Direction 1: The 9-Blade Aperture

## Concept

A camera aperture (iris) with exactly 9 blades. The aperture is a precision instrument that controls how you see — and that's exactly what 9takes gives you. It's not decoration, it's a tool for perception.

## Why This Works for 9takes

### The metaphor is airtight

An aperture controls the depth and clarity of what a camera sees. 9takes controls the depth and clarity of how you see people. When you "open your aperture," you let in more light — more perspectives. When you focus, you isolate one type's viewpoint with precision.

### The 9 is built into the geometry

9 blades forming the iris. Not arbitrary — each blade IS a type. The entire Enneagram system is encoded in the icon's structure without needing to explain it. People who know the Enneagram see 9 immediately. People who don't just see a clean, techy, purposeful shape.

### It says "superpower" not "personality quiz"

Most personality platforms use soft, round, human-shaped icons — faces, hearts, speech bubbles. The aperture says: "This is a precision instrument. You will see things others can't." That's the Solo Leveling energy — you've awakened an ability, and this is the visual interface of that ability.

### It's a tool, not a symbol

The magnifying glass says "search." The speech bubble says "chat." The aperture says "controlled seeing." It positions 9takes as a system for reading people — not just a place to talk.

## Visual Details

### Shape

- Circular outer ring (the lens housing)
- 9 curved blades overlapping to form the iris opening
- Central void/opening in the middle (what you're looking at / the truth)

### Color Application

- **Monochrome version**: Teal on dark, or dark teal on light — clean, minimal
- **Type-colored version**: Each of the 9 blades tinted with its Enneagram type color (indigo, pink, amber, purple, sky, green, amber, red, emerald) — this is the "full power" version
- **Glow version**: Teal aperture with a subtle glow emanating from the center opening — "powered on" state, like the ability is active

### Sizes and Contexts

- **Favicon (16-32px)**: Simplified to concentric circles with 9 notches. The iris shape reads even at tiny sizes because it's geometric
- **App icon (512px)**: Full detail — blades with subtle gradients, center glow, maybe slight depth/shadow
- **Marketing/hero**: Animated — blades slowly opening and closing, revealing light from the center
- **Loading state**: Blades rotating or pulsing — natural "processing" animation
- **Watermark**: Light single-color outline version

### Animation Potential

- **Page load**: Aperture opens from closed → fully open (revealing the page content)
- **Theme toggle**: Blades rotate slightly when switching light/dark
- **Hover**: Subtle blade shift, center glow intensifies
- **"Reading" state**: When AI is analyzing or when you're viewing someone's type, the aperture could pulse

## Brand Language It Unlocks

- "Widen your aperture" — see more perspectives
- "9 lenses, one truth" — tagline variant
- "Adjust your focus" — zero in on a specific type
- "Open" vs "closed" — understanding vs ignorance
- "Depth of field" — how deeply you understand someone
- "Exposure" — revealing hidden personality dynamics
- "f/9" — a camera aperture setting, could be a clever brand mark (f-stop 9)

## Comparable Brands Using Similar Energy

- **Palantir** — the all-seeing eye / palantir stone (data intelligence)
- **Iris** (various) — eye/lens metaphors for insight products
- **Aperture Science** (Portal) — precision, testing, controlled observation (playful association)
- **Camera brands** (Leica, Fuji) — the aperture is synonymous with professional-grade seeing

## What to Watch Out For

- Don't make it look like a surveillance/spy symbol — keep it warm with the teal color and rounded blade edges
- Don't make it too mechanical — slight organic curves on the blades keep it human
- The center opening should feel inviting, not threatening — light coming FROM the center, not darkness
- At small sizes, it could look like a gear icon — make sure the blade shapes are distinct from gear teeth (curves, not straight edges)

## Connection to Existing Brand

- **Teal glow** from the theme = the aperture "powered on"
- **Warm stone backgrounds** = the instrument sits on something organic, not cold
- **JetBrains Mono font** for the tech spec feel = this is precision instrumentation
- **Rajdhani display font** = bold, confident, matches the "ability unlocked" energy
- The Enneagram type colors map 1:1 to the 9 blades
