# Logo Brainstorm: The Temple of Nine

_9takes logo and brand illustration concepts — Greek + Solo Leveling fusion_
_Created March 2026_

---

## Concept Overview

**Inspiration**: The opening scene of Solo Leveling where Sung Jin-woo enters the Double Dungeon temple and is surrounded by massive ancient statues. Reinterpreted through a Greek aesthetic for 9takes.

**The core image**: A lone figure standing in an ancient Greek temple, surrounded by 9 towering stone statues — each representing one of the 9 Enneagram types. The statues are ancient, cracked, and glowing with purple/cyan energy. The wisdom was always here. Now it's waking up.

### Mapping Solo Leveling to 9takes

| Solo Leveling              | 9takes Translation                                              |
| -------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| The Double Dungeon temple  | The Enneagram framework — you've entered the system             |
| The massive stone statues  | The 9 types — ancient archetypes of human nature                |
| They tower over Jin-woo    | These patterns are bigger and older than any individual         |
| They have rules/tests      | Each type has its own logic, its own way of seeing              |
| Jin-woo looking up         | The user at the start of their journey — small, about to awaken |
| The awakening that follows | "Seeing the emotions behind every take" — your eyes open        |

**The emotional beat**: "You thought you understood people. Then you walked into this temple and realized there are 9 ancient forces shaping every human interaction, and you didn't even know they existed."

## Scene Variations

### Version 1: "The Council"

Nine massive Greek statues **seated** in a semicircle or full circle, on stone thrones, like a council of gods deliberating. One figure stands in the center of the circle, small, looking around at them. The Enneagram symbol is carved into the stone floor beneath everyone, glowing faintly with purple light.

**Feeling**: You've been summoned. These 9 archetypes have always existed. Now you're face to face with them. It's a place of wisdom, not combat — more Plato's Academy than a boss fight. The council is ancient, patient, waiting for you to understand.

**Best for**: Homepage hero, about page, brand centerpiece illustration.

## Logo Derivations

These scenes are illustrations. Here's how each one could **simplify into a logo mark**:

### From "The Council" / "The Arena"

- **9 abstract figures in a circle** — simplified silhouettes arranged in a ring, with a smaller figure or dot in the center
- **Top-down enneagram floor glyph** — the glowing floor pattern from the council scene becomes the mark itself

### From "The Gauntlet"

- **Two columns of figures flanking a path** — abstract, geometric, suggests the journey

### From "The Awakening"

- **A cracked stone shape with light bleeding through** — could be applied to any form (circle, the number 9, the enneagram symbol). The "cracked ancient stone glowing with energy" texture IS the brand.

### From "Looking Up"

- **A single stylized stone face with glowing eyes** — the most icon-ready concept. Classical Greek face, minimal detail, two glowing purple points for eyes. Instantly recognizable at any size.
- **The glowing eye alone** — one classical eye with purple glow. Ultra minimal.

### The Texture as Brand Element

Regardless of which logo shape you choose, the **cracked marble with purple energy bleeding through** treatment can be applied to anything — the existing cube, the number 9, the enneagram symbol, text. This texture IS the fusion of ancient Greek + Solo Leveling.

## Midjourney Tips

- Use `--s 100-250` for cleaner/more literal results, `--s 400-600` for more artistic interpretation
- Add `--no text, letters, words, writing` if unwanted text appears
- Use `--ar 1:1` for logos/icons, `--ar 16:9` for hero images, `--ar 9:16` for mobile/vertical
- Once you find a direction, use `/describe` on the best result to refine further
- For the scene illustrations, try `--chaos 20-40` to get more varied results across the 4 images
- Add `--style raw` if results are too "Midjourney pretty" and you want more control
