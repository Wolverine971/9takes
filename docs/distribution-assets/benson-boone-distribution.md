## Reddit Post — r/Enneagram

**Title:** Type 7 Case Study: Benson Boone — The Pop Star Who Can't Stop Moving (and Knows Why)

**Body:**

Benson Boone is a compelling Type 7 case. Here's the evidence:

**Relentless experience-seeking.** Backflips off pianos at the Grammys. Climbed the walls of Wembley. Jumped 629 feet off Auckland's Sky Tower. Self-taught on piano, guitar, drums, bass. Studied architecture. Owns an ice cream truck. The man cannot sit still.

**Self-acknowledged addictive personality.** Never tried drugs or alcohol — not because of his Mormon upbringing (he left the church), but because he knows himself. When asked how he knows: "Dude, candy." Then: "If I started, I would do it so much that my health would just decline."

**Fear of being trapped.** Quit American Idol after Katy Perry told him he'd win. Left the Mormon Church. Dropped out of BYU-Idaho. All to avoid being defined by something he didn't choose.

**The stress move to 1.** His body image struggle is striking: "I'm so incredibly hard on myself. There's always something more I could do. My arms could be bigger." Under pressure, 7s move toward Type 1 perfectionism. The same energy that drives the backflips also whispers that his shoulders aren't big enough.

**Reframing pain.** When critics called him "a copy of a copy" and TikTok creators compared his music to plain white food, he made a music video wearing a "One Hit Wonder" shirt, visiting "Industry Plant Records," and selling his sparkly jumpsuits. Comedy over confrontation.

**Where it gets interesting.** "Beautiful Things" sits in fear rather than escaping it. His ego checks, his sobriety, his body image honesty — these don't belong to someone purely avoiding pain. They belong to someone learning that the most thrilling experience might be standing still.

**Title:** The psychology behind Benson Boone's constant motion — why the backflips, the sobriety, and the ego checks all connect

**Body:**

I wrote a deep dive into what makes Benson Boone tick, and I think fans who pay attention to who he actually is (beyond the performances) will find it interesting.

A few things that stood out:

**The sobriety isn't religious.** He left the Mormon Church. He stays sober because he knows himself: "I have an addictive personality." When asked how he knows without testing it: "Dude, candy." That's a 23-year-old who understood something about himself that takes most people decades.

**The body image stuff is real.** "There's always something more I could do. My arms could be bigger. My shoulders could be bigger. It's never-ending." For someone who does backflips for a living, the vulnerability is striking. He's explicitly said he doesn't want to be "the sex icon of the century" or rely on his physical form.

**The ego checks.** Every two weeks with his manager Jeff Burns. Once snapped at his mom on the phone and felt so awful he called back 20 minutes later. "It is incredibly easy to get carried away with my ego."

**The creative process.** Every song starts at the piano, usually late at night when sleep won't come. "Beautiful Things" was written at 2 AM on his grandmother's old piano. The two parts were in different time signatures (4/4 and 6/8) and took a month to bridge.
