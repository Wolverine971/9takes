# How Each Enneagram Type Gets in Their Own Way — 9takes Carousel

> Mode: Gallery (diagnostic variant) | Format: Custom self-sabotage gallery, 12 slides | Theme tag: The Nine

## The visual signature (NON-NEGOTIABLE — this is what makes the set ownable)

**The amber fault-line crack.** Every type slide is a single Greek marble statue caught mid-pattern, with **one glowing amber crack** running through it exactly where that type's self-sabotage lives. The crack is not damage — it's the protective instinct that outlived its use, lit from within. Same device, nine placements. The source used a glowing blue head; we use a glowing amber fracture. Cohesive, on-brand (Streetlamp V5 amber), and impossible to confuse with the original.

**Render approach (locked): photographed sculpture, not a 3D render.** Each frame is shot as a real museum photograph of an _actual marble sculpture_ — true Carrara veining, hairline chips, fine stone dust, real film grain — with the amber crack as a real lit seam (the only warm light in the frame). This keeps the statue+crack signature while killing the generic glossy-CGI look the decoded engine mocks (`iklipse-dont-prompt-ai.md`, the account's #1 post). **No** `Unreal Engine / Cinematic / 32k / Super-Resolution / hyper realistic / Global Illumination` tails — those _are_ the look we're escaping.

**Directed angles, not default eye-level.** The camera angle is chosen per type off the angle→emotion cheat sheet (`iklipse-camera-angle-cheat-sheet.md`) so the framing _does_ the feeling: high angle shrinks the hiders (5, 2, 4), low angle makes the controllers loom (3, 8), a Dutch tilt destabilizes the anxious one (6), a wide witness frame lets the 9 vanish. Lens alone isn't direction.

The payoff slide pays this off: on the bright peak, the cracks are **filled with gold (kintsugi)** — _the crack isn't the flaw, it's where the light gets in._

## Voice: compassionate coaching

Every knife names the pattern as something that once **protected** you, then shows the cost now. Never accusatory, always "I see why you do this — and here's what it's costing." Headlines: `THE [N] [does the self-sabotage]`. Knife: protective origin → present cost.

## Why this deck works (engine check)

- **The reframe:** Getting in your own way feels like a character flaw — random, shameful, "just how I am." This deck proves it runs on **nine nameable patterns**, and that each one started as protection. Intuition → system, the 9takes core flip, applied to self-sabotage.
- **Hook pattern used:** §E felt-truth/identity hook — _"You're not stuck. You're in your own way."_ Implicates the viewer without shaming.
- **The save-able payload:** the nine type slides themselves are the taxonomy — a reader screenshots their own type, and recognizes three others.
- **★ beats present:** Hook (1) · Recognition (built into the nine) · Taxonomy/gallery (2–10, one per type) · Reframe peak (11, bright kintsugi) · Payoff + CTA (12, participation).
- **The emotional arc (the spine the light follows):** **recognition → grief → release.** Slides 2–10 are the quiet ache of being seen (moody, low-key, the crack the only warmth); slide 11 is the turn where naming becomes the break (the one bright, high-key frame); slide 12 is the invitation back to the room. Every lighting and angle choice serves that movement — the deck _feels_ darker before it opens, so the kintsugi peak lands as relief instead of decoration.
