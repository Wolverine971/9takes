## Why this deck works (engine check)

- **The reframe:** Reading people looks like an innate gift. Greene proves it's a _system_ — 300 books and 4,000 handwritten notecards per project. Intuition → discipline. That's the engine's core flip, embodied by the man who literally wrote the manual.
- **Hook pattern used:** §E "Don't X. Y instead." → "You don't read people. You react to them." (felt accusation = the Diagnostic lever)
- **The save-able payload:** Slide 4 — "4 ways your read of people stays shallow," each flaw + one-line symptom, as chips.
- **★ beats present:** Hook (1) · Recognition + mystery (2) · Taxonomy/diagnostic grid (4) · Reframe (5) · Formula peak (6, bright) · Payoff + CTA (11). Plus mechanism (3), proof slides (7–9), checklist guillotine (10).

## Caption (90–160 words)

Are people hard to read — or are you just reacting instead of reading?

Robert Greene held 80 jobs and got crushed by power for 20 years before he could name what was happening to him. The "gift" everyone credits him with? It was 300 books and 4,000 handwritten notecards per project. A system. Not a talent.

Here's the part most people skip: behavior is the surface. You judge what someone _does_ and stop there. Greene learned to read one level down — the fear, the want, the thing they're protecting.

Reading people was never magic. It's nine repeatable lenses on one situation.

Which lens do you read people through? Comment your Enneagram type below — we'll show you what you're built to see (and what you keep missing).
