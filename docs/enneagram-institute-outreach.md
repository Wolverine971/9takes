# Enneagram Institute Outreach Strategy

_Prepared: 2026-03-16_

---

## Background Research

### About the Enneagram Institute

- **Founded:** 1997 by Don Richard Riso (deceased) and Russ Hudson
- **Current President:** Annette Peter (President and General Counsel)
- **Key Staff:** Russ Hudson (President Emeritus), Brian Taylor (VP), Joyce Stenstrom (VP Creative Development), Matthew Bean (Director of IT)
- **Location:** Stone Ridge, New York
- **Revenue Model:** RHETI test ($20/test with bulk discounts), workshops/events, books, certifications, EnneaThought daily email service
- **No visible partnership/collaboration page** — inbound collab pitches are likely rare

### Their Current State

- Site is functional but shows aging infrastructure (fragmented test platform on separate subdomain, limited interactivity)
- Strong on educational content and type descriptions
- Revenue heavily tied to paid assessments and in-person workshops
- No community/interactive features
- No visible content around celebrity typing, situational applications, or user-generated perspectives

---

## Strategic Framing

### Positioning

The Enneagram Institute is the **academic/certification authority**. 9takes is the **interactive, community-driven application layer**. These don't compete — they reinforce each other.

### What NOT to Do

- Don't lead with "your site needs updating" — insulting to a legacy institution
- Don't position 9takes as competition
- Don't be vague about what you want
- Don't mention their site needing updates in the first email

### What TO Do

- Lead with respect for Riso/Hudson's legacy — their community is protective of the founders' work
- Offer immediate, tangible value (sending them paying RHETI test takers)
- Have a specific small-scope first project ready if they say "maybe"
- Contact Annette Peter directly (LinkedIn or their contact form)

## Tactical Notes

### The "Referral Pathway" is Your Trojan Horse

You're offering to send them paying customers (RHETI test takers at $20/each). That's immediate, tangible value with zero effort on their part. This is what gets the conversation started.

### If They Say "Maybe" — Have a Small First Project Ready

Something like: "We could start by linking our type description pages to your RHETI test." Low lift, mutual benefit, builds trust.

### If a Call Happens

Organically explore: "Are you all thinking about adding more interactive or community features?" — let them voice the gap themselves. Don't tell them what's wrong with their site.

### Contact Strategy

1. **Primary:** LinkedIn message to Annette Peter
2. **Secondary:** Contact form on enneagraminstitute.com/contact
3. **Follow-up:** If no response in 7-10 days, try reaching Russ Hudson directly (he's more public-facing and active in the Enneagram community)
