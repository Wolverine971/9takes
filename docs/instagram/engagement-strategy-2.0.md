# 9takes Instagram Engagement Strategy 2.0

_Research-based strategy for finding better accounts and improving engagement quality_

**Date:** March 22, 2026  
**Author:** Kite (OpenClaw)  
**Purpose:** Replace high-follower/low-visibility strategy with targeted engagement on emerging accounts

---

## The Problem with Current Strategy

### What You're Doing Now

- Engaging with Tier 1 Enneagram accounts (250K-500K followers)
- Tier 2 psychology/therapy accounts (1M+ followers)
- Some MBTI bridge accounts

### Why It's Not Working

| Issue                        | Impact                                                          |
| ---------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Flooded comments**         | Your comment is 1 of 500+ — invisible                           |
| **Creator doesn't see it**   | Big accounts get thousands of comments, can't read them all     |
| **No relationship building** | Can't form connections with creators who are too big            |
| **Low algorithmic reward**   | Instagram doesn't boost your visibility from these interactions |
| **Time inefficient**         | 30 min of engagement → minimal reach                            |

### The Real Goal

**Build relationships with creators who will:**

- Notice and respond to your comments
- Engage back with your content
- Collaborate on cross-promotion
- Become part of your "engagement pod"

## The "Rising Tide" Strategy

### Core Principle

**Target accounts in the 10K-100K follower range**

## 2025 Instagram Algorithm Priorities

### What the Algorithm Rewards (In Order)

| Signal       | Weight    | How to Trigger                                    |
| ------------ | --------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **DMs**      | Highest   | Content people send to friends ("This is so you") |
| **Shares**   | Very High | Save-worthy reference guides, type comparisons    |
| **Saves**    | High      | Carousels, "9 types of..." posts, guides          |
| **Comments** | Medium    | Polls, questions, controversial takes             |
| **Likes**    | Lowest    | Basic engagement, not algorithmically significant |

### Content Format Priority

1. **Carousels** — Highest save rates
   - "9 types reacting to..."
   - "Type X vs Type Y comparison"
   - "How each type handles [situation]"

2. **Reels under 15 seconds** — Algorithm boost
   - Quick type identification tips
   - "POV: You're a Type X"
   - Fast cuts, text overlays

3. **Stories with engagement stickers** — Daily touch
   - Polls: "Which type is most likely to..."
   - Questions: "Ask me anything about Type X"
   - Quizzes: "Guess the type based on this behavior"

**Daily Routine (20 min):**

- Coordinate posting times (post when they post)
- Reference each other's content ("As [@account] pointed out yesterday...")

## Engagement Quality Framework

### Bad Engagement (Don't Do This)

❌ "Great post! 🔥"  
❌ "Love this! ❤️"  
❌ "So true! 👏"  
❌ Generic emoji-only comments

**Why it fails:** Adds no value, creator won't remember you, algorithm ignores it

### Good Engagement (Do This)

✅ **Add specific insight:** "Your point about Type 3s performing competence — I've noticed this shows up differently for 3w4 vs 3w2. The 3w4 performs authenticity, the 3w2 performs helpfulness."

✅ **Ask thoughtful question:** "Have you seen this pattern change under stress? I'm curious if the wing becomes more or less visible when Type X is in growth."

✅ **Connect to their past content:** "This builds on your post from Tuesday about Type 7s — I'm seeing how the fear of limitation drives both the enthusiasm AND the scatteredness."

✅ **Share relevant experience:** "I work with a lot of Type 5s in tech, and this pattern you're describing is exactly what I see when they hit information overload."

✅ **Offer collaboration:** "This would be perfect for a joint post — I'd love to explore the Type [X] angle while you cover Type [Y]. DM me if you're interested!"
