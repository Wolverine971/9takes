# Gen Z Instagram Posting Cheat Sheet (9takes)

Last updated: 2026-02-25
Owner: 9takes

## 0) What this is

This is a practical operating system for Instagram posting:

- `Alex Hormozi style`: direct, punchy, high-value, no fluff.
- `GaryVee style`: one big idea turned into many contextual micro-posts.
- `9takes edge`: strong POV on personality patterns, backed by observed behavior.

Use this to decide what to post, how to frame it, and how to ship consistently.

## 1) Brand position (non-negotiable)

`9takes` is not gossip and not fan content.

`9takes` is: pattern recognition in public behavior through personality frameworks.

Every post should feel like:

- Entertaining enough to watch.
- Educational enough to learn something useful.
- Expressive enough to show a clear 9takes point of view.

## 2) Your 2x3 principle stack

### A) Content Value Triad

Every post must score at least 1 point in each bucket:

- `Entertaining`: tension, surprise, stakes, or emotional contrast.
- `Educational`: a clear model, mechanism, or takeaway.
- `Expressive`: a real, specific POV (not neutral summaries).

### B) Copy Quality Triad (Harry Dry rules)

Every key line (hook + thesis) should pass:

- `Can I visualize it?` Set a scene or moment in time.
- `Can I falsify it?` Make a claim that can be debated/tested.
- `Can nobody else say this?` Include a 9takes-only angle or evidence base.

Shortcut: if you get 3 no's, rewrite it. [11]

### C) Transcript Execution Rules (Harry Dry -> 9takes)

These are mandatory writing behaviors, not "nice to have":

1. `Don't talk, only point.` Replace adjectives with observable proof.
2. `One Mississippi, Two Mississippi.` If it does not click in about 2 seconds, rewrite.
3. `Never write a line a competitor can sign.` If any account could post it, make it more ownable.
4. `Facts. Facts. Facts.` Use quotes, dates, behaviors, moments, and outcomes.
5. `Every example needs conflict.` Show tension, contradiction, cost, or tradeoff.
6. `Rewrite for compression.` Any word not helping is hurting.

## 3) The no-waste rule (opening standard)

You said: "I don't want to waste people's time."

Operationalize it:

- First 1-2 seconds (or first line) must state the payoff.
- No throat-clearing intros.
- Start with the conflict, not background.

Bad:

`"Hey guys, today I want to talk about..."`

Good:

`"Pete Davidson spending $200k removing tattoos is a Type 9 tell. Here's why."`

## 4) Hook system (Gen Z attention model)

Use one hook pattern per post:

1. `Contrarian`: "You think X. It's actually Y."
2. `Prediction`: "Within 12 months, this person will..."
3. `Pattern interrupt`: "Most people miss this 5-second clip."
4. `Identity callout`: "If you're Type 7, this is your blind spot."
5. `Evidence-led`: "After analyzing 200+ profiles, here's what repeats."
6. `High-stakes binary`: "This is either confidence or fear-masking. Nothing in between."

Hook checklist:

- Concrete nouns (person, event, quote, behavior).
- Time/place context (what moment? when?).
- Implied tension (what's at stake if true?).

## 4A) Hook Build Method (First Line, Second Line)

Build hooks with two lines:

- `First line`: concrete scene + tension.
- `Second line`: interpretation payoff.

Examples:

- "He laughed when asked about commitment on live TV."
- "That reaction is classic Type 7 escape behavior."

- "She praised the team, then took control in the same breath."
- "That is Type 3 image management under pressure."

If line two repeats line one, delete and rewrite.

## 5) Falsifiable psychology framework (without fake certainty)

Because your analyses are interpretive, use `structured falsifiability`:

1. `Claim`: "X behavior suggests Y pattern."
2. `Evidence`: cite observed public behavior or quote.
3. `Boundary`: when this interpretation may fail.
4. `Counter-signal`: what would disconfirm your read.
5. `Disclaimer`: speculative, public-data-based.

Template:

`Based on [observable behavior], my read is [type/pattern].`
`If this were wrong, we'd expect [alternative behavior].`
`Speculative analysis only; not a diagnosis.`

## 5A) "Don't Talk, Only Point" Rewrites for 9takes

Use this conversion table while drafting:

- `Abstract`: "He's insecure."
- `Pointed`: "In the 2026 interview, he laughs, looks down, and changes subject when asked about failure."

- `Abstract`: "She's authentic."
- `Pointed`: "She posts unedited studio clips the same day she records them, including mistakes."

- `Abstract`: "He's a people pleaser."
- `Pointed`: "He says yes to two conflicting commitments in the same week, then apologizes publicly to both sides."

- `Abstract`: "She has strong boundaries."
- `Pointed`: "She ended the interview after the third question about her ex and stated the boundary on camera."

Drafting rule:

- For each major claim, add at least one "pointed" sentence with a quote, timestamped moment, or concrete behavior.

## 6) 9takes "only we can say this" bank

Use at least one credibility anchor in 70% of posts:

- "We've analyzed 200+ public figures through this lens."
- "Across our archive, this pattern appears most in Types X and Y."
- "Most takes stop at behavior; we map motive + fear + coping style."
- "Our model predicts what happens under stress, not just public image."

Don't force this into every post. Use when it increases authority.

## 7) Format playbooks

## 7A) Reel formula (30-60s)

- `0-2s`: Hook (stakes + curiosity).
- `3-10s`: Scene (the specific moment/clip/quote).
- `11-25s`: Interpretation (your POV with mechanism).
- `26-40s`: Proof (2-3 behavior examples).
- `41-55s`: Falsifiable prediction or challenge.
- `56-60s`: CTA (comment binary: agree/disagree + why).

Production rules:

- Captions always on.
- Visual change every 1-3 seconds.
- Keep dead air out.
- One core idea per reel.

## 7B) Carousel formula (6-8 slides)

- `Slide 1`: Hook in one hard line.
- `Slide 2`: Scene-setting moment.
- `Slide 3`: Your thesis.
- `Slide 4-6`: Evidence stack.
- `Slide 7`: Counterpoint / falsifiability.
- `Slide 8`: CTA + link to full analysis.

Text rule:

- One sentence chunks.
- Hard line breaks.
- Zero paragraph walls.

## 7C) Caption formula

Use this structure:

1. Hook line
2. 3-5 short lines of evidence
3. Interpretive thesis
4. Falsifiable test/prediction
5. Disclaimer
6. CTA question

Caption hard rules:

- Paragraphs max two lines on mobile.
- Keep one idea per paragraph.
- If a paragraph still works when one sentence is removed, cut that sentence.

## 8) Content pillars for 9takes

Use 4 pillars so your feed has both depth and repeatability:

1. `Public Figure Pattern Reads`
2. `Live Event Decodes` (interviews, clips, controversies)
3. `Type Mechanics` (how each type behaves under stress/security)
4. `Debatable Predictions` ("What this pattern leads to next")

Target mix per 10 posts:

- 4 pattern reads
- 2 event decodes
- 2 type mechanics
- 2 predictions/contrarians

## 9) Weekly posting cadence (quality-first)

Base cadence:

- `Reels`: 4/week
- `Carousels`: 2/week
- `Stories`: daily (polls, behind-the-scenes, quick takes)
- `Lives`: 1 every 2 weeks (Q&A or postmortem)

Batch workflow:

- 1 long-form analysis -> 1 reel + 1 carousel + 3 stories + 1 quote card.

This matches the GaryVee pillar-to-micro model. [9] [10]

## 10) Testing and experimentation

Use `Trial Reels` weekly for low-risk hook testing:

- Publish 2-3 trial variants with different hooks.
- Let 24h data settle.
- Promote the winner to full audience.
- Archive losers and extract what failed.

Instagram's Trial Reels were built for exactly this behavior. [3]

A/B variables (one at a time):

- Hook line
- First visual frame
- Reel length
- CTA wording

Add one copy variable every week:

- `Abstract vs concrete lead` (always expect concrete to win on saves/shares).

## 11) Metrics that actually matter

Track at 24h and 7d:

- `Views` (distribution)
- `Shares` (pass-along value)
- `Saves` (future utility)
- `Comments` quality (depth, not just count)
- `Profile actions` (follows, link taps)

Derived metrics:

- `Share rate = shares / views`
- `Save rate = saves / views`
- `Action rate = (follows + link taps) / views`

Interpretation:

- High views + low saves/shares = good hook, weak substance.
- Low views + high saves/shares = good substance, weak packaging.
- High comments disagreement = good falsifiability; keep iterating.

## 12) Comment strategy (to increase signal, not noise)

End posts with a `forced-choice` prompt:

- "Is this Type 3 adaptation or Type 9 merging? Pick one."
- "True or false: this behavior is conflict-avoidance, not humility."

Reply strategy:

- Reply fast in first 60 minutes.
- Reward thoughtful disagreement.
- Pin best counterargument + your response.

This strengthens expressive POV and community quality.

## 13) Guardrails (brand safety + recommendation health)

Before publishing, run this check:

- No mental health diagnosis claims.
- No definitive certainty for speculative reads.
- No low-effort reposting.
- No sensitive/shocking framing that adds no value.

Also check `Account Status` regularly so recommendation issues do not silently suppress reach. [2]

## 14) Reusable templates

## 14A) Reel opener templates (fill-in)

- "Everyone saw [public event]. Nobody noticed [behavioral tell]."
- "[Person] did [specific action]. That's not random. It's [pattern]."
- "After 200+ personality analyses, this move usually means [prediction]."
- "Hot take: [widely accepted read] is wrong. Here's the better read."

## 14B) Falsifiable claim templates

- "If [type read] is right, we'll see [specific future behavior]."
- "If I'm wrong, [clear alternative] should happen instead."
- "This isn't 'good' or 'bad' personality - it's a tradeoff: [tradeoff]."

## 14C) Conflict and Contrast templates

- "Publicly, [image]. Privately, [behavior]. That's the tension."
- "[Common interpretation] sounds right, but [better interpretation] explains more."
- "This is not [label A]. It's [label B], and here's the evidence."
- "The strength that built them is now the thing costing them."

## 14D) Disclaimer templates

Short:

`Speculative personality read based on public behavior, not diagnosis.`

Long:

`Disclaimer: This Enneagram analysis is speculative, based on publicly available information, and may not reflect the person's actual personality type.`

## 15) 14-day execution sprint

Week 1:

- Define 3 recurring series names.
- Publish 3 reels + 1 carousel.
- Run 2 trial reels for hook testing.
- Build baseline metrics sheet.

Week 2:

- Publish 4 reels + 2 carousels.
- Recreate top performer with different subject.
- Start weekly "Prediction Review" story.
- Cut bottom 20% tactics; double top 20%.

## 16) Post scorecard (ship/no-ship)

Score each post 0-2 on each line:

- Entertaining
- Educational
- Expressive
- Visual
- Falsifiable
- 9takes-unique

`Minimum ship score: 8/12`

If below 8, rewrite hook and thesis.

Pass/fail checks (all must pass):

- `2-second clarity`: hook passes One Mississippi test.
- `Pointed evidence`: no unsupported adjective claims.
- `Competitor-signability`: main line is 9takes-ownable.
- `Compression`: no paragraph over two lines in caption/story copy.
