# Carousel: "Why I Read Famous People" — Personality Series Launch

**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Format:** 7-slide carousel (Instagram, 1080x1350px / 4:5)
**Account:** @9takesdotcom
**Purpose:** Standalone series-launch post that frames the upcoming personality-analysis carousels.
**Through-line:** Famous people as mirrors → pattern recognition → empathy + self-understanding.
**Companion source:** Per-person facts live in `docs/instagram/post-ideas/2026-04-29_top-tier-people-tidbits.md` (42 people).

---

## Slide 1 — Cover / Hook

```
I read famous people
for a living.

Not to gossip.
To understand the rest of us.
```

**Design notes:** Bold cover. "I read famous people for a living." gets the most weight. "Not to gossip." in slightly muted gray — the disarmer. Last line in white/accent.

---

## Slide 2 — What I do

```
I write personality analyses —

deep dives on the people whose lives
play out loud enough that you can
actually see the pattern.
```

**Design notes:** Quieter slide. "the pattern" can be emphasized in accent color. Sets the premise.

---

## Slide 3 — Why famous people

```
Famous people aren't a different species.

They're the same wiring,
on a bigger stage.

Same loops. Same wounds. Same fears.
Just with louder consequences.
```

**Design notes:** "Same wiring, on a bigger stage." is the load-bearing line — give it the most visual weight. The triplet ("Same loops. Same wounds. Same fears.") could be stacked on three lines for rhythm.

---

## Slide 4 — The mirror move

```
Which is the trick.

Once you can see the pattern
on someone you'll never meet,
you start seeing it on yourself.

On your boss. Your sister.
Your ex. Your dad.

The thing they can't stop doing
is some version of the thing
you can't stop doing.
```

**Design notes:** This is the pivot — frame it with a small visual break or divider. "Your boss. Your sister. Your ex. Your dad." can be its own beat. Final two lines are the punch.

---

## Slide 5 — Empathy through pattern

```
It quietly kills
the cheap version of judgment.

You stop being mystified by people.

You don't have to forgive anyone.
You just stop wasting energy
not understanding them.
```

**Design notes:** "the cheap version of judgment" — the phrase doing the most work here. "You don't have to forgive anyone." is intentional anti-therapy-talk; keep it sharp, don't soften.

## Editorial notes

- **The disarmer:** Slide 1's "Not to gossip" neutralizes the surface read of the premise before it becomes an objection.
- **The load-bearing line:** "Same wiring, on a bigger stage" (Slide 3) is the empathy bridge — everything after it depends on this image landing.
- **Anti-soft framing:** Slide 5 deliberately avoids therapy-coded language ("you stop wasting energy not understanding them" instead of "compassion"). This is the brand voice — empathy through clarity, not warmth.
- **CTA earns the follow:** "Drop a name. We'll go." pulls people into the series rather than asking for a passive follow.
