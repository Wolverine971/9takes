## Synthesis — What 9takes should actually do

The four posts agree on one thing: **invisibility is a fixable problem, and most of the fixes are mechanical, not creative**. Below is what to lift, ranked by leverage.

### Tier 1 — Do this week

**1. Fix the @9takesdotcom bio to be SEO-optimised.** Currently we likely have a vibe-y bio. Rewrite to Social Runway's spec:

- Display name: "9takes | Enneagram + Personality" (puts keywords in the searchable field)
- Username: already good (`9takesdotcom`)
- Bio structure:
  - Line 1 — positioning ("See the emotions behind every take")
  - Line 2 — problem we solve ("Decode why people react the way they do")
  - Line 3 — benefit + CTA ("Free Enneagram test ↓")
  - Use emoji line breaks; fit under the "more" fold
- Action: pull current bio, draft 3 alternates, test for a week each.

**2. Turn on "Similar account suggestions" on desktop.** Free, takes 30 seconds, surfaces us in the suggested-follow carousel of any account we resemble. (Social Runway slide 4 step 1.)

**3. Pin 2 strategic comments on every existing post — within minutes of publishing going forward.** One asks a question that invites a reply ("Which type is YOUR stress type?"), one drops a related-post link. Apply retroactively to top 10 posts this week.

### Tier 2 — Rework the carousel template

Tyler Archer's minimalist "one sentence per slide" format is the cleanest carousel design pattern of the four. We should A/B test it against our current denser layouts for the next "Enneagram in X" series. Specifically:

- Slide 1: Number + intrigue ("7 reasons your 5 keeps shutting you out")
- Slides 2–8: One single short sentence each, huge type
- Slide 9: "Hey, I'm 9takes. Follow for [specific outcome]."

This converts faster on swipe-through rate. Save behavior follows numbered-list format reliably.

### Tier 3 — Hook templates retrofitted for personality content

From Michelle Gifford's 131-hook list, the formulas that work for **personality / Enneagram** content:

- "What your **Enneagram type** says about you"
- "What your **Type 8 boss** isn't telling you"
- "The **trauma response** no one is talking about"
- "You've been reading **your partner's silence** wrong"
- "5 things I'd do if I was **a Type 3 again**"
- "I don't know if you're ready to hear this **about your Type 1 parent**"
- "Steal my **personality decoder for arguments**"
- "What your **9w1 wishes** you knew"
- "MYTH: **all 5s are introverts**"
- "Red flags to look for in a **Type 6 relationship**"
- "Every **Enneagram Type 4** has this in it"

These map directly to existing 9takes blog content — many can be repurposed as hook → slide-2 → "full post at link in bio" carousels with zero new research.

### Tier 4 — Avoid Tyler Archer's 7 traps

Self-audit our current grid against:

1. **Tips-only with no depth** — are we giving framework + insight, or just listicles?
2. **Too generic** — does every post feel like it could be on any Enneagram account?
3. **No clear niche position** — are we "Enneagram for X" or just "Enneagram"?
4. **Vague CTAs** — does each post tell people _exactly_ what to do next?
5. **Trend-chasing without strategy** — Reels for reach vs. content that actually qualifies leads?
6. **Unclear "who you help"** — does the bio + grid make it obvious who 9takes is for?
7. **No inspiration to act** — do we move people from "interesting" to "I need to do this"?

### Tier 5 — Operational checklist (steal from Social Runway)

For every new post going forward:

- [ ] Niche keyword in the first 5 words of the caption
- [ ] 3–4 niche-specific keywords in the caption body
- [ ] Mix of broad + specific hashtags in caption (NOT first comment)
- [ ] Keyword in the post itself (cover slide or first 3s of Reel)
- [ ] 2 owner-pinned comments within 5 minutes of publishing
- [ ] Engagement window: respond to first-hour comments within 10 minutes

### Things to NOT copy

- **The "comment KEYWORD below" DM-funnel lead capture.** It works for course sellers; our funnel goal is different (we want them on-site for the test, not in DMs). Skip unless we're launching a specific lead magnet.
- **Heavy CTA promo slides at the end** (Social Runway slide 8). Distracts from the educational throughline. We can do this for the personality test launch but not as default.
- **Verified-creator-style "Hey I'm X, follow me" closing card.** Reads aggressive on our brand voice. Use a softer "Save for later" or "Tag a [Type] who needs this."
