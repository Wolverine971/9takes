# Content Idea: Four stress surfaces, nine different fuels

## Core Reframe

Same surface behavior, different engine. Two people "overthinking" under stress can be a 6 (running threat scenarios), a 1 (auditing what went wrong), or a 5 (retreating into analysis to avoid demand) — three completely different internal moves wearing one outward look. 9takes adds the engine read: don't type someone by what they do under stress; type them by what the behavior is _for_. Falsifiable claim: if the four surfaces (loud / disappear / overthink / busy) mapped 1:1 to types, the framework would only need 4 types, not 9. Counter-signal: ask the person what relief looks like — the answer separates a 6's "overthink" (relief = certainty) from a 5's "overthink" (relief = solitude) from a 1's "overthink" (relief = it's finally correct).

## Hook Options

- Hook A: Three people are "overthinking." One's a 6. One's a 1. One's a 5. Different engine, same noise.
- Hook B: "Stress style" tells you less than you think. The fuel underneath does the actual work.
- Hook C: Stop typing people by their stress behavior. Type them by what the behavior is trying to fix.

## Evidence Needed

- Three short examples per surface — one for each candidate type that produces it. Six total surface-to-engine pairs is enough for a 45–60s reel.
- "Relief test" framing: what each type wants once the stress resolves. This is the falsifier that separates types sharing a surface.
- Counter-evidence: someone's behavior shifts surface (busy → disappear) under prolonged stress. Address: types have stress _arrow_ behaviors that explain the shift (3→9, 7→1, etc.). Not a contradiction — a deepening.
