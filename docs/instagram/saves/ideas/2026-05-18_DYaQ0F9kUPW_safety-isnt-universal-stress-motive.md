# Content Idea: Stress isn't safety-seeking for every type

## Core Reframe

Most Enneagram content collapses stress to one motive: "trying to feel safe." That frame fits 6s, 9s, and parts of 2/4 — and breaks for the rest. 3s in stress chase visibility, not safety. 7s chase options. 8s chase command. 1s chase correctness. 9takes adds the motive-layer split: the surface behavior (loud / disappear / overthink / busy) is real, but the fuel is type-specific. The falsifiable claim: if "safety" were the universal stress motive, no type would escalate under pressure — but 3s, 7s, and 8s reliably do. Counter-signal: an 8 in real stress doesn't get quieter; they confront. A 7 doesn't seek shelter; they multiply exits.

## Hook Options

- Hook A: Every Enneagram coach says stress = trying to feel safe. That's only true for half the 9 types.
- Hook B: A Type 3 under stress isn't hiding. They're performing harder. Safety isn't the motive — visibility is.
- Hook C: 9 types. 4 stress motives. "Feeling safe" is just one of them.

## Evidence Needed

- Public moments where a 3, 7, or 8 escalates rather than retreats under observable stress (interview meltdowns, founder under fire, athlete post-loss interviews).
