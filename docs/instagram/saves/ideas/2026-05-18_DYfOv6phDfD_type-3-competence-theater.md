# Content Idea: Type 3 has all 5 winner signals — and that's the problem

## Core Reframe

Type 3s arrive with Vanessa Van Edwards' five "winner" signals already installed: steady eye contact, open posture, slow movement, genuine-_looking_ smile, controlled gestures. It's why they get hired, promoted, and chosen first. It's also why their close relationships flatten — the same calibration that lands the room also performs at the kitchen table. 9takes adds the tradeoff: the strength that built the 3 is the thing costing them at 2 a.m. when the room is empty. Falsifiable claim: if Type 3 "winner body language" were the same as a healthy 3 being real, the room would feel _both_ impressed _and_ close to them. It usually feels only impressed. Counter-signal: a healthy 3 deliberately breaks one of the five signals at home — eye contact drops, smile drops, the cadence slows below "competent" speed. That's the off-stage tell that the person is integrated, not the on-stage signals.

## Hook Options

- Hook A: Type 3s already have Vanessa's 5 "winner" signals. That's the problem.
- Hook B: The body language that gets you hired is the body language that flattens your marriage.
- Hook C: A healthy Type 3 breaks one of the 5 winner signals on purpose at home. Which one?

## Evidence Needed

- One public figure read where a Type 3 displays the 5 signals flawlessly in a professional setting and visibly performs them in a personal setting too (interviews vs candid couples content, founder talks vs partner appearances).
- Internal 9takes Type 3 analyses — the competence/image-management core.
- Counter-evidence to address: "isn't this just being polished?" Polish is the surface. The 9takes layer is the cost of polish in low-stakes contexts where calibration reads as distance.
- Reference vvanedwards' framing to set up — this is a Type-3-specific extension, not a refutation.
