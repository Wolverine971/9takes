# Content Idea: Why "confidence training" fails for half the types

## Core Reframe

Body-language training assumes the signals are the variable and the person is the constant. The Enneagram inverts that: the signals are universal but the _source_ of the signal — where it comes from in the person's internal state — is type-specific. So a Type 5 who learns steady eye contact runs it from a different internal place than a Type 3 who's run it since age six. Under low stakes, you can't tell. Under genuine stress, you can tell instantly. 9takes' falsifiable claim: confidence training (the kind sold as "learn these 5 signals and you'll read as a winner") has a predictable failure mode for 5s, 6s, and 1s — the trained signals collapse first when the room turns adversarial, because they were never sourced from the internal state the signal is supposed to express. Counter-signal: a Type 3 under genuine stress _keeps_ the calibrated signals (sometimes too much — that's the tell). A trained 5 under stress drops eye contact first; a trained 6 over-controls gestures and reads jittery; a trained 1 tightens the smile.

## Hook Options

- Hook A: Body-language training works in the calm room. It collapses in the adversarial one. There's a type pattern for which signal fails first.
- Hook B: You learned steady eye contact. Then the meeting turned hostile and you lost it in 30 seconds. That's not a confidence problem — that's a sourcing problem.
- Hook C: Three types break the "winner" signals under stress in three different orders. Yours is predictable.

## Evidence Needed

- The "first signal to collapse" pattern by type — needs 1–2 named public examples per type (5, 6, 1) where you can show the moment the trained signal failed. Tougher evidence ask than the other ideas; may need DJ + research-analyst to compile.
- A Type 3 example where the calibration _intensifies_ under stress rather than collapses — the counter-signal that proves the framework's directionality.
- Counter-evidence to address: "5s/6s/1s can train to a level where the signals survive stress." Address: yes, but it takes years and integration, not a workshop. The shortcut version is what fails.
