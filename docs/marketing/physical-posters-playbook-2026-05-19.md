# 9takes Physical Posters & Sticker Playbook (2026-05-19)

Companion to [guerrilla-marketing-playbook-2026-05-14.md](./guerrilla-marketing-playbook-2026-05-14.md) — that doc is all digital (Reddit, Twitter, IG). This one is the **IRL** counterpart: posters, stickers, decals, wheatpaste, and where to legally put them.

Cross-reference [marketing-log.md](./marketing-log.md) for active workstreams and [9takes-cluster-strategy.md](./9takes-cluster-strategy.md) for which blogs to drive traffic to.

## The strategic frame

**Don't post a 9takes ad. Post a question.**

The audience is personality-curious, pop-culture-aware, often Gen Z / millennial. They scan QR codes. They photograph weird street art. They follow rabbit holes. They do _not_ read posters that look like business ads.

The wedge: **mystery + QR + sharp question > logo + tagline + URL.**

Three principles:

1. **Match the poster to the venue's tribe.** A laundromat poster ≠ a coffee shop poster ≠ a record store poster.
2. **Ask, don't pitch.** Curiosity converts. Pitches get ignored.
3. **Measure or skip.** Unique QR per location with UTM params, or you'll never know what worked.

## Where to post — ranked by audience fit

### Tier 1 — pop-culture / personality-curious tribe lives here

High fit. Low friction. Start here.

- **Indie coffee shops** — ask a barista, not the manager. Baristas say yes.
- **Record stores** — Gen Z + millennial music heads overlap massively with personality-curious
- **Indie bookstores** — staff picks crowd; high-intent audience
- **Comic shops** — Enneagram type debates already happen there
- **Board game cafes** — captive social audience, personality-curious by default
- **Vintage / thrift stores** — huge Gen Z overlap
- **College campus bulletin boards** — most schools have a "stamping" system. Get stamped = legal. Hit the student union, library, dorm common rooms.
- **Public libraries' community board** — ask the librarian; they're allies, not gatekeepers

### Tier 2 — captive-audience plays

People stuck waiting with nothing to read. Underrated.

- **Laundromats** — 30–60 min dwell time, no entertainment, zero competition. _Wildly_ underrated. Perfect for a long-form personality quiz poster.
- **Tattoo shops** — long waits, personality-curious clientele
- **DIY music venues / small clubs** — bathroom posters especially
- **Hostels** — built-in pop-culture-curious traveler audience
- **Food halls** — long lines, captive
- **Makerspaces, climbing gyms, yoga studios** — community boards, niche tribes
- **College dorm common rooms** — need a student insider to post for you
- **Bathroom stalls in bars / coffee shops / venues** — controversial but works. Captive eyes, no competition. Use a sticker, not a poster.

### Tier 3 — high reward, edge cases

Legal grey zones. Higher reward, more risk.

- **Construction-site plywood fencing** — you can actually _lease_ this legally in most cities, cheaply. Look up "wild posting" + your city.
- **Wheatpaste in arts districts** — technically illegal most places, enforcement near-zero in arts zones: Bushwick (NYC), RiNo (Denver), Wynwood (Miami), Mission (SF), Wynwood, Deep Ellum (Dallas), East Austin, Wicker Park (Chicago), Allston (Boston)
- **Long-term cafe window decals** — trade: offer the cafe a free "What's your barista's type?" decal in exchange for one wall poster inside

### Skip outright

Don't do these. Either illegal, ineffective, or brand-damaging.

- **Utility / telephone poles** — OSHA-prohibited (lineman safety hazard), fineable, looks spammy
- **Mailboxes** — federal violation, real consequences
- **Private property without explicit permission** — trespass
- **Car windshields** — universally hated, near-zero conversion
- **Anywhere with active "no posting" signage** — easy fine, not worth it

## Materials matrix — when to laminate, when not to

| Surface                                             | Material                              | Lamination?              | Cost                               |
| --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------ | ---------------------------------- |
| Indoor community board                              | 100 lb gloss text                     | No — they cycle weekly   | Cheap (home printer or VistaPrint) |
| Outdoor community board (covered)                   | 100 lb gloss + clear acrylic spray    | Light spray only         | Cheap + $8 spray can               |
| Outdoor exposed / wheatpaste                        | Thin uncoated text stock + wheatpaste | No — paste _is_ the seal | $1–3/poster + DIY paste            |
| Long-term outdoor (cafe window, construction fence) | Vinyl decal or full lamination        | Yes — full laminate      | $5–15/unit                         |
| Sticker bombing                                     | Die-cut vinyl, weatherproof matte     | Already weatherproof     | $0.50–1/sticker bulk               |

**Rule of thumb:**

- **Wheatpaste** = "I'm never seeing this poster again." Commit to that surface, walk away.
- **Laminated + thumbtacked** = "I want this to last on a community board." Reusable.
- **Indie cafe wall** = anything goes; they'll toss it in 2 weeks anyway. Don't overspend on materials.

**Wheatpaste recipe (DIY, free):**

1. 1 part wheat flour, 3 parts water
2. Boil, stir until pancake-batter consistency
3. Cool, apply to wall with brush, smooth poster on, brush more paste over top
4. Non-toxic, biodegradable, semi-permanent (3–12 weeks depending on weather)

**Pro materials (when DIY isn't enough):**

- Commercial wallpaper paste or PVA-based adhesives — stronger hold, faster drying, better in humid/rainy climates
- Clear acrylic spray sealant for laminated effect on paper

## Design angles for 9takes specifically

### Poster archetypes that fit the brand

1. **Mystery / curiosity poster**
   - Single sharp question, a QR, no logo
   - _"Why does Taylor Swift always do this?"_ → QR → blog
   - Works because curiosity > pitch

2. **Type-bait poster**
   - _"3 signs you're an Enneagram 8 (most people miss #2)"_
   - QR → personality test or type-specific blog
   - Self-identification = scan trigger

3. **Site-specific meta poster**
   - In a coffee shop: _"What's your barista's Enneagram type?"_
   - In a laundromat: _"What does your laundry pile say about you? (Honest version)"_
   - In a record store: _"Type the cast of your favorite album"_
   - Meta = memorable

4. **Numbered series (the "ridiculously amazing" play)**
   - 9 posters, one per type, same neighborhood, one weekend
   - _"You probably know a Type 7. They're the one who…"_
   - Reddit + IG users will find it and photograph the full set = free amplification
   - Made-for-social, made-for-screenshots

5. **Decoder poster**
   - _"Decode any celebrity's Enneagram in 90 seconds"_ — visual checklist + QR
   - Useful = scan trigger

### Design rules (research-backed)

- **2-second test**: poster must communicate within a 2-second glance
- **High contrast** with urban surfaces (avoid white-on-white in cluttered boards)
- **Text large and minimal** — fewer than 20 words on the whole poster
- **QR + 6 words above it** is the conversion unit. That's enough.
- **No 9takes logo on mystery posters** — logo kills the curiosity. Reveal on the landing page.

### Sticker concepts (separate from posters — high ROI)

- _"if you got promoted today, would you tell your mom first?"_ + QR
- _"the worst way to break up by Enneagram type"_ + QR
- _"your barista is probably a 4w3"_ + QR
- _"unhinged Type 8 behavior"_ + QR
- _"Type 9s read this last"_ + QR

Sticker-bombing reach > poster reach at the same cost. Lamp posts, laptops at coffee shops, bathroom stalls, water fountains, the back of street signs.

## Conversion mechanics

### QR strategy

- **Unique QR per location** with UTM params (e.g., `?utm_source=poster&utm_medium=irl&utm_campaign=denver_laundromat_01`)
- Confirm referrer breakdown is visible per blog post in admin analytics before scaling
- Use a QR generator that gives you analytics (Bitly, QR Code Generator Pro, or self-host a redirect)

### Landing page strategy

**Do not** drive to homepage. Drive to:

- A single celebrity blog (matched to the poster's hook)
- The Enneagram test (gated email signup)
- A specific gated giveaway / lead magnet
- A custom QR-only landing page with a hook-matched offer

### Tear-tabs are dead

For your demo (Gen Z / millennial), QR + 6 words has replaced phone-number tear-tabs. Don't bother with the tear-tab era flyer format.

## The "ridiculously amazing" level

Plays that compound beyond the poster itself.

### 1. The 9-poster city drop

All 9 types, one neighborhood, one weekend. Each poster sharp and standalone, but the full set becomes a treasure hunt. Photograph each one, post the set on Instagram, tease "find all 9" — Reddit r/popculturechat or r/Enneagram will pick it up and do the amplification for you.

### 2. Sticker swarms beat posters per dollar

$50–100 buys 100+ die-cut vinyls. One sharp question per sticker. Coverage = 10x posters at same cost. Lower per-unit reach, much higher total reach.

### 3. Cafe window-decal trade

Offer the cafe a _free_ branded window decal (_"What's your barista's Enneagram type?"_ with their shop name on it) in exchange for one wall poster inside. Trade beats permission. You get: window real estate, wall real estate, barista buy-in.

### 4. Treasure hunt across cities

9 stickers in 9 cities (or 9 neighborhoods of one city). Each QR → different page. Full set unlocks a hidden landing page / exclusive content. Instagram-able by design.

### 5. Numbered laundromat series

Hit 9 laundromats in one city over one weekend. Each gets the type-bait poster for one Enneagram type. Long dwell time + zero competition = highest conversion rate per poster of any venue.

### 6. Bathroom stall takeover

Pick one venue (a bar, club, or coffee shop with multiple stalls). Sticker each stall with a different type-bait sticker. People talk about it. People photograph it. Cheap, memorable, polarizing.

## Legality cheat-sheet

Quick reference before posting anywhere.

| Surface                                         | Legal?                   | Caveat                                                          |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Community bulletin board (library, coffee shop) | ✅ Yes — with permission | Always ask, get verbal yes from staff                           |
| College campus board (stamped)                  | ✅ Yes                   | Follow the stamping process                                     |
| Cafe / shop window (asked)                      | ✅ Yes                   | Get permission in writing if possible                           |
| Public park bulletin board                      | ✅ Usually               | Check local rules                                               |
| Sidewalk handout (you handing)                  | ✅ Usually               | Some cities require handbill license (Albany, Philly, San Jose) |
| Utility / telephone pole                        | ❌ No                    | OSHA-prohibited, fineable                                       |
| Private wall / fence without permission         | ❌ No                    | Trespass                                                        |
| Mailbox                                         | ❌ No                    | Federal violation                                               |
| Construction site (leased)                      | ✅ Yes                   | Need to lease — but cheap                                       |
| Wheatpaste in arts district                     | ⚠️ Technically no        | Enforcement near-zero in known arts zones                       |

**Always check local laws.** Rules vary city-to-city. Library + a 10-minute Google for "[city] posting flyers ordinance" before any big push.

## Implementation checklist

Before any physical drop:

- [ ] **Pick the city / neighborhood** — start with one you know well
- [ ] **Map the venues** — at least 10 Tier 1 + 5 Tier 2 locations
- [ ] **Confirm legality** — Google "[city] posting flyers ordinance"
- [ ] **Design 1–3 posters** — pick from archetypes above
- [ ] **Generate unique QRs** with UTM params per location
- [ ] **Confirm analytics work** — referrer + UTM visible per blog
- [ ] **Decide materials** — laminate? wheatpaste? sticker? per use case
- [ ] **Print** — VistaPrint, StickerMule, or local print shop
- [ ] **Build a route** — single weekend, batch the trip
- [ ] **Photograph each placement** — for IG / Reddit amplification
- [ ] **Measure** — check analytics 7, 14, 30 days after drop

These need DJ's input before scaling:

- **City to start with?** Denver? Somewhere else?
- **One archetype or three?** Cheaper to start with one design × many locations.
- **Sticker-first or poster-first?** Sticker-first has higher reach per dollar; poster-first is more brand-controlled.

- **Wheatpaste — yes or no?** Higher risk, much higher visual impact, very Instagram-able.
