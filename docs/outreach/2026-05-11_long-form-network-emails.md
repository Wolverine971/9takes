## Pre-send checklist (apply to every send)

- [ ] Open the target's profile URL in a browser. Confirm clean render. If 404, skip.
- [ ] Open the 2–3 adjacent-link URLs in the email. Confirm clean render.
- [ ] Confirm the opener specific is actually in their piece. (I pulled each from the FEEDBACK comment in each draft — should match, but verify the wording before send.)
- [ ] Pick one subject line per recipient from the options.
- [ ] Log the send in your outreach tracker (campaign, recipient, channel, subject, date).
- [ ] If multiple sends planned same day, space them out by at least 4 hours.
- [ ] Wait **5–7 days** for reply before follow-up (revised from 14 — research says shorter cadence lifts reply rate from ~3% → ~13%). Move on after a second follow-up at day 14 with no reply.

### "With or without you" inevitability framing (DJ's voice preference)

The piece is **already published.** The email is post-publication courtesy, not a pitch. This is how journalists handle subject outreach — and it's strictly more effective than supplication for high-status Threes who are pitched constantly.

**Load-bearing line (use some variant):** _"It's already up — felt strange to write [N] words about someone without telling them."_

**What it does:**

1. **Removes power asymmetry.** DJ isn't a fan begging for attention. He's a published writer offering refinement on something that exists.
2. **Lowers threat.** Recipient owes nothing — no validation required, no obligation to engage.
3. **Recognizes their humanity.** The act of writing about them is the implicit signal you value their work. No sycophancy needed on top.
4. **Inverts the dynamic.** Instead of "look at me, please react" → "I made this, take or leave."

**Avoid:** "I'd love to send this to you" / "Would you be open to" / "Hoping you'll" / "If you have time" — all read as supplication. A Three will clock it instantly.

**Prefer:** "It's already up" / "Felt strange not to share" / "If you'd refine anything, I'd love that. If not, glad you saw it."

This is now applied to Chris (#1), Alex (#2), Ali (#3), and Steven (#6); needs to be back-propagated to the remaining drafts.

### Subject-line template (revised 2026-05-14 — kill the generic primary, kill the Enneagram jargon)

**No generic template.** Each recipient gets three options pulled from THEIR piece. The earlier `[FirstName] — a personality analysis of you` template was killed: identical across 12 sends defeats the personalization signal, and "personality analysis" reads as clinical pop-psychology to recipients who don't speak Enneagram. Wing/type subjects (`3w4 read`, `Type 5 read`, `7w6 read`) are also killed — Hormozi doesn't know what `3w2` means, Theo doesn't know `7w6`, and even recipients who vaguely know Enneagram exists will read the jargon as cult-coded or "I'm about to box you." (Side note: an earlier draft of this doc used `3w8` as an example — that's not even a valid wing combo. Type 3 wings are only 3w2 or 3w4, since wings must be adjacent on the circle. DJ flagged this 2026-05-18.)

**The four levers, ranked by pull strength:**

1. **Signature scene or image (primary for most).** One specific phrase or moment from the piece — `The Coriolis moment`, `The tic that became a career`, `The chicken shop coin`. Irresistibly specific to them; creates instant "wait, that's me" recognition. Proves you read past the surface before the email is even opened.
2. **Their own line back at them.** They said or wrote it; they'll click to see it framed back. Ego-irresistible without supplication. (e.g., `Duty, not delight`, `"Don't ever cry"`, `Raise kids who never have to`.)
3. **The "no ask, FYI" disarm (DJ-validated 2026-05-18).** `alex — no ask, just FYI I wrote 5,000 words on you`. Load-bearing moves: lowercase first name (peer-to-peer, not pitch); `no ask` disarms the transactional pre-read every high-status Three has running; the specific word count proves real effort; `on you` is the curiosity hook (every Three opens an email about themselves). DJ picked this format over wing-naming subjects after explicitly testing both. Use this when (a) you have no irresistible signature scene to lead with, (b) the recipient is inbound-saturated (Hormozi, Abdaal), or (c) the channel is IG/X DM where the subject IS the opener and the disarm has to do the entire job. Slightly longer (~50–60 chars) than the other levers — accepts mobile truncation tradeoff in exchange for the disarm.
4. **Status-neutral journalistic framing.** `[Name] — wrote a long read on you` or `[Name] — a profile of you`. No jargon, no fluff, no supplication. Same register a profile writer at The Atlantic or New Yorker would use. Use when the signature-image option doesn't fit the channel, or when the gatekeeper needs a clearer "this is personalized, not mass-blast" cue.

**The wing/type specificity now lives in the first sentence of the body**, where it lands as the payoff to the curiosity hook the subject opened — not buried, just earned.

**The implicit CTA.** No subject explicitly asks for action. The pull comes from the open loop: a signature image or their own line creates the "I have to know what this says about me" itch — that IS the call to action. They must open to close the loop. Explicit asks ("Did I get this right?" / "Is this fair?") still read as supplication; they stay killed. Refinement-welcome framing lives in the body, where it lands as payoff, not as pitch.

**Constraints applied to all 36 options below.** 16–41 chars where possible. 3–7 words. No fake `Re:` prefixes (2026 spam trigger). Mobile-truncation-safe (<40 chars target).

### Type 3 Achiever-specific layering

All 4 of Chris/Steven/Alex/Ali are Threes. Threes are the **most time-scarce** Enneagram type and most allergic to feeling "managed." ([Enneagram Institute](https://www.enneagraminstitute.com/type-3/), [Cloverleaf](https://cloverleaf.me/blog/enneagram-type-3-the-achiever/))

- A 200-word personal letter reads as "this person doesn't value my time" — the _opposite_ signal you want to send a Three.
- The wing distinction (3w2 vs 3w4) lives in the **first sentence of the body**, NOT the subject. Recipients don't speak Enneagram; "3w4 read" reads as cult-coded jargon or "I'm about to box you" — a Three pre-reacts to both. The wing lands harder as payoff to the curiosity the subject opened.
- The personalization signal in the subject IS the signature image (Coriolis moment / chicken shop / duty-not-delight). One specific phrase from THEIR piece does more work than naming the wing — it proves you read, not that you have a framework.
- The signature image (Coriolis moment / chicken shop) should be ONE sentence in the body, not three.
- "No ask attached" is the right close for a Three — they expect every email to be transactional. Subverting that earns the second of attention.

### Strategy change: kill the adjacency-link cluster on first touch

Original strategy doc has a "network-proof rule: 2–3 cluster member URLs per email." That's right strategy, wrong placement.

- **First touch:** Mention the cluster in one sentence, **no URLs.** ("also wrote on Peterson, Huberman, Bartlett")
- **Follow-up touch (day 7):** Send the adjacent URLs as the new value-add. This gives you a natural reason to follow up — which is where reply-rate uplift lives.

This keeps total first-touch URLs at 1 (the recipient's own piece). Better deliverability, less "mass send" smell, smarter sequencing.

### Tier legend & channel strategy

- **T1 (4 of 12):** Direct email confirmed — but these are **gatekeeper inboxes** (`jonathan@`, `press@`, `media@`, `shawnryan@`). The principal rarely reads them; their assistant/business partner does. **Implication:** the subject line has to work for TWO readers — the gatekeeper (does this earn a forward?) and the principal (if forwarded, does it earn a click?). The signature-image-first format (`The Coriolis moment` for Chris, `The chicken shop coin` for Steven, etc.) does both: gatekeeper sees the email is clearly NOT a mass-blast (specific scene from his piece, not a template), principal sees their own content recognized. The "no ask attached" framing also helps gatekeepers — nothing to coordinate, nothing to schedule, easy forward.
- **T2 (7 of 12):** Form-fill or DM only. Paste the cold-version body into the form's message field. For inquiry-type dropdowns, pick "general" / "feedback" / "personal note." Don't try to be clever about category.
- **T3 (1 of 12 — Rogan):** No public email. X DM has <0.1% odds. **Strategy: skip the cold send. Hold for warm intro** if any of the other 11 reply.
