# Tim Ferriss — Cold Outreach Email

**Date staged:** 2026-06-29
**Target:** Tim Ferriss (author, The Tim Ferriss Show, tim.blog)
**Context:** Post-publication outreach for the Enneagram Type 1 analysis at https://9takes.com/personality-analysis/tim-ferriss
**Voice notes:** Honest familiarity ("followed your work for a while," not "long-time listener"), refinement-ask only, no supplication. Em-dashes removed. The hook is the "misbehave" word-choice read, which proves the analysis isn't generic. ~170 words (primary) / ~90 (lean).

---

## Subject line options

- I wrote an Enneagram breakdown of you _(honest, specific — default)_
- The word "misbehave" gave you away
- An Enneagram read on you (Type 1)

---

## Primary version (~170 words)

```
Subject: I wrote an Enneagram breakdown of you

Hi Tim,

I've followed your work for a while, did a real dive into your story, and ended up writing a full personality breakdown of you on my site, 9takes.com.

Short version: I think you're an Enneagram Type 1, the Reformer. The tell that convinced me wasn't the productivity stuff. It was one word. You've said you build systems that make it "next to impossible to misbehave." Not fail, not underperform. Misbehave. That's someone who experiences falling short as a moral problem, not a performance one.

Read that way, fear-setting stops looking like a life hack and starts looking like the system you built to quiet a mind that won't stop prosecuting you. And the recent turn, the "Self-Help Trap" essay, relationships over protocols, reads like real movement.

It's an argument, not a verdict, so it's the kind of read worth pushing back on:
https://9takes.com/personality-analysis/tim-ferriss

No agenda, and it's already published. If anything's off, I'd want the correction. If it landed, no need to reply.

DJ Wayne
9takes.com
```

## Send notes

- Tim points readers to comments/tweets rather than a public inbox. Best channels: a short note via tim.blog/contact, a comment on a recent tim.blog post (phrased as a reader's note, not a pitch), or @tferriss on X.
- One link only. No attachments.
