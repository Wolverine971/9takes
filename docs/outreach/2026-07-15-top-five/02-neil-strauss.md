# Neil Strauss — Official Contact-Form Packet

**Status:** Ready  
**Platform:** [Neil Strauss’s official contact form](https://members.neilstrauss.com/contact)  
**Objective:** Invite a writer to correct the governing metaphor of a profile about him  
**Profile:** [Neil Strauss: The Man Who Kept Trying On Selves](https://9takes.com/personality-analysis/neil-strauss)

## Why this channel

Neil’s own [First Time Here?](https://www.neilstrauss.com/first-time/) page tells readers to contact him and says his team responds to every email. His [Super FAQ](https://www.neilstrauss.com/super-faq/) says the inbox is checked daily, while warning that high volume means messages sometimes slip through.

Use the official form. Do not reconstruct the obfuscated Inner Circle address, buy an address, or send the same message to Society support. If DJ later becomes a genuine Inner Circle reader and receives a relevant personal email from Neil, replying to that specific email is reasonable; joining only to manufacture a route is not.

## Exact execution

1. Open the official contact form.
2. Enter `DJ Wayne` and `dj@9takes.com`.
3. If the form presents a category, choose **feedback**, **personal mail**, or the closest non-business option.
4. If it presents a subject field, use `The boy without house keys`.
5. Paste the message below with blank lines intact.
6. Include one link only. Do not attach the Neil square card.
7. Submit Tuesday through Thursday between 9:00 and 11:00 a.m. Pacific time.
8. Save the confirmation page or receipt in the outreach log.

## Subject

`The boy without house keys`

## Send this

> Neil—
>
> You once described growing up without keys to your own house. I used that image as the first door in a profile about the pattern across your books.
>
> My argument: the rock stars, pickup artists, survivalists, and sex-addiction work weren’t just subjects. Each was a candidate self you entered deeply enough to inhabit, then wrote your way back out of.
>
> The question only you can answer: does writing help you **find a self**, or keep you **free from having to settle on one**?
>
> [Neil Strauss: The Man Who Kept Trying On Selves](https://9takes.com/personality-analysis/neil-strauss)
>
> —DJ Wayne

## Why this wording

- The house-key image is specific enough to earn attention without putting “personality analysis” in the subject.
- “First door” uses his material as craft, not as trauma spectacle.
- The four subject worlds prove DJ sees a career pattern, not only _The Game_.
- The closing question is about the function of writing. It gives a professional writer a reason to answer that is larger than liking or disliking his type.
- The two options protect complexity. Either answer can overturn part of DJ’s interpretation.

Use the primary version unless DJ personally feels uncomfortable sending it. Authentic comfort matters more than theoretical hook strength.

## If staff replies

### “We forwarded this to Neil”

> Thank you. No scheduling or coordination is needed; I only wanted to give him a clean opportunity to correct the central argument.

Then stop.

### Generic acknowledgment

Log it as staff acknowledgment, not a Neil reply. Do not use the acknowledgment as a reason to pitch an interview.

## If Neil replies

### He disputes the metaphor

> Thank you. That is more useful than agreement. I’m going back through the piece with your distinction in mind and will send the corrected passage once it is live.

### He answers the question

Thank him and reflect his distinction in one sentence. Do not immediately ask a second identity question.

### He says the piece is interesting or accurate

> Thank you. The sentence I most wanted pressure-tested was “each book was a self he tried on.” If that survives your read, I know what the spine is.

## Follow-up rule

Do not submit the contact form a second time. If the form creates an email receipt with a replyable thread, one short clarification after 10–14 days is acceptable:

> One clarification: there’s no share, blurb, or interview ask behind this. One wrong sentence is the most useful reply I could receive.

Otherwise, stop after the form submission.
