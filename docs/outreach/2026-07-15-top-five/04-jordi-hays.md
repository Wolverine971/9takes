# Jordi Hays — LinkedIn Two-Step Packet

**Status:** Ready  
**Primary platform:** [Jordi Hays on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jordi-hays-559199113)  
**Account to use:** DJ’s personal LinkedIn profile  
**Objective:** Get Jordi to correct the thesis that spectacle is distribution for serious work  
**Profile:** [Jordi Hays: The Dead Serious Showman Who Sold Silicon Valley’s Loudest Stage](https://9takes.com/personality-analysis/jordi-hays)

## Why LinkedIn

Jordi’s LinkedIn has roughly 12,000 followers, current activity, and a clear professional identity as TBPN co-founder and host. That is meaningful reach, but not celebrity-scale message volume. DJ’s personal LinkedIn—with 500+ connections and a credible USMC/engineering/builder history—provides more trust than a sparse X or Instagram account.

[TBPN’s official site](https://www.tbpn.com/about) also links Jordi’s LinkedIn as an official host channel. The platform fits the topic: the message is about brand and craft, not fandom.

## Step 1: one real public comment

Use a post from the previous seven days. Comment only if the topic overlaps something DJ can actually extend. Do not paste the article link or say that Jordi has been “typed.”

If Jordi posts again about tech borrowing only from tech, this comment is ready:

> “Tech copies tech” feels like a distribution problem as much as a creativity problem: the same reference pool makes ideas instantly legible to insiders, but it also makes the output interchangeable. One of the interesting things about TBPN is how much it imports from sports, fashion, and live television instead.

If the available post is unrelated, write a fresh comment or skip the warm-up. Never force this prepared comment under the wrong post.

Wait 24–48 hours after the comment.

## Step 2: connection request

Send from DJ’s personal profile. Do not include the profile URL in the connection note.

> Jordi—I wrote a long profile arguing the spectacle isn’t a mask; it’s distribution for the serious machine underneath. The pants-change scene made the opening. I have one correction question, not a pitch. Mind if I send it?

This fits inside LinkedIn’s connection-note limit. It proves specificity without asking him to read before accepting.

## Step 3: after he accepts

Send this within one business day:

> Thanks, Jordi. Here’s the actual question.
>
> You’ve said, “We don’t take ourselves seriously, but we take the work super seriously.” My profile argues the spectacle isn’t a mask; it’s distribution for the work—the suits, gong, and bronze horse make a narrow business story feel like live sports.
>
> Am I giving the bit too much strategy, or is that essentially the model?
>
> https://9takes.com/personality-analysis/jordi-hays
>
> —DJ

## Why this wording

- It mirrors Jordi’s own distinction between taking himself lightly and taking the work seriously.
- It treats taste and spectacle as professional craft, not narcissism.
- The suits, gong, and bronze horse are unmistakably his world.
- “Too much strategy” creates a legitimate way to disagree. He can say the bit is intuitive, emergent, or simply fun.
- The question does not ask him whether he is a Type 3. It asks whether DJ has correctly modeled how the media product works.

## Do not send the image first

The square card at `static/types/3s/s-Jordi-Hays.webp` is polished, but attaching a portrait card to a connection request would make the interaction feel like a marketing launch. Let the specificity of the note create curiosity. Use the card only after he replies or if he shares the profile.

## If he accepts but does not answer

Do not send a “just bumping this” message. After seven business days, one value-adding follow-up is permitted only if there is something real to add:

> One narrower version of the question: did the TBPN visual world begin as a deliberate distribution strategy, or did the audience teach you which parts of the bit were the product?

No link is necessary; it is already in the thread. Stop after that.

## If he does not accept

Wait ten business days, then stop. Do not chase him on X, Instagram, or the TBPN company account. Continue leaving occasional genuine comments only if DJ would have made them without the outreach campaign.
