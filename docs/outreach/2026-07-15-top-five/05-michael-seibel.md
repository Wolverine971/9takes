## Why this wording

- The DJ introduction earns its space because it explains why public service, duty, and DC register personally.
- The 20s/30s/40s plan is Michael’s own structure and the profile’s strongest signature detail.
- “Apprenticeship” is more respectful and testable than saying tech was merely a distraction.
- The email isolates the most debatable interpretive sentence instead of asking him to approve the whole type.
- “Wrong model, or basically right?” fits his public reputation for direct feedback and permits a terse answer.

## Do not mention

- Garry Tan, the YC succession, or Michael being passed over. The article covers this, but using it as an email hook would make the contact feel like gossip bait.
- A request for startup feedback, investment, or an introduction. That would retroactively make the profile look instrumental.
- The Type 1 label in the subject or first paragraph.
- The number of words in the article. Precision is useful here; effort signaling is not.
