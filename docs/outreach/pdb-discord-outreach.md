# Personality Database & Discord Community Outreach

---

## Personality Database (personality-database.com)

### Why PDB Users Are Perfect Recruits

PDB users spend hours researching and debating celebrity typings. They already:

- Know multiple personality systems (MBTI, Enneagram, Big Five, Socionics)
- Write detailed justifications for their typings in comments
- Research interviews, biographical details, and behavioral patterns
- Care deeply about getting the typing right (to a fault sometimes)

The gap: they're doing this work in comment sections that disappear. We give them a real platform.

### Where to Post on PDB

1. **Profile comments** — When you see a well-argued typing comment on a celebrity's PDB page, reply to the commenter directly
2. **Community discussions** — PDB has forum-style discussions. Look for threads about celebrity typings.
3. **Related Discord servers** — PDB has an active Discord. See Discord section below.

### PDB Comment/Reply Template

Use this when you find a well-argued typing comment on a celebrity's PDB page:

> This is one of the best typing breakdowns I've seen on here. The evidence you pulled from [specific thing they mentioned] is exactly the kind of analysis that's missing from most Enneagram content online.
>
> I run 9takes.com — we publish full-length (2,500 word) personality analyses of public figures using the Enneagram framework. Your analysis of [Person] is the kind of depth we're looking for from contributors.
>
> If you've ever wanted to turn a PDB comment into a published article with your name on it, DM me. We pay $50–150 per piece and handle all distribution. No strings attached — just looking for people who actually care about getting the typing right.

### PDB Power User Identification

Look for users who:

- Have 50+ votes on their typing arguments
- Write paragraph-length justifications (not just "vibes")
- Reference specific interviews, quotes, or behavioral patterns
- Engage in nuanced debates (acknowledging alternative typings before arguing for theirs)
- Are active across multiple celebrity profiles

### Target Servers

| **Personality Database Discord** | Celebrity typing debates, personality systems | Link from personality-database.com or search Discord |

| **Typology Central Discord**     | Multi-system personality typing               | typologycentral.com links                            |

| **r/Enneagram Discord**          | Reddit Enneagram community's Discord          | Linked in r/Enneagram sidebar                        |

### Discord Outreach Strategy

**Step 1: Join and lurk (1 week)**

- Read the vibe. Every server has different norms.
- Identify the most active/insightful members.
- Note which channels have the best discussions (usually #typing-debates, #celebrities, #enneagram-general).

**Step 2: Contribute before recruiting (Week 2)**

- Share your own analyses. Post a link to one of your published pieces when it's relevant to a discussion.
- Engage in typing debates. Show you know what you're talking about.
- Answer questions about types. Be helpful first.

**Step 3: Recruit (Week 3+)**

- Only after you've established presence, post in an appropriate channel:

### Discord Recruitment Post

**For #general or #opportunities channels:**

We've published 60+ deep dives (Taylor Swift, Joe Rogan, Zendaya, etc.) — not surface-level listicles, but 2,500-word narrative analyses with quotes, behavioral evidence, and real type theory.

> Looking for 2–3 contributors from this community. If you've ever written a detailed typing argument and thought "this should be an article," this is your chance.
>
> **What you need:**

> - Solid Enneagram knowledge (wings, arrows, instinctual variants are a plus)
> - Ability to write conversationally (no academic tone)
> - Interest in pop culture or public figures
>
> **What you get:**
>
> - $50–150 per published piece
> - Full byline credit
> - We handle distribution (Twitter, Reddit, email outreach)
> - Published portfolio piece
>
> DM me if interested. I'll share our contributor guidelines + published examples.

## Other Communities to Target

### Facebook Groups

| Group                                        | Size   | Notes                                                 |
| -------------------------------------------- | ------ | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Enneagram and Coffee (Sarajane Case's group) | Large  | Warm audience, be respectful of group rules           |
| Enneagram Memes and Discussions              | Large  | More casual, but active power users                   |
| The Enneagram Journey                        | Medium | More serious/educational crowd                        |
| Enneagram for Personal Growth                | Medium | Growth-oriented, good for finding coaches-in-training |

**Facebook approach**: Don't post recruitment directly. Instead, share a published analysis when relevant to a discussion, then let interested people come to you. Add a comment like "We're also looking for guest contributors if anyone wants to write one."

### Typology Central (typologycentral.com/forums)

Old-school forum with dedicated personality typing enthusiasts. Post in their writing/opportunities section. The audience skews older and more analytical — which means their contributions will need less editing.

### Personality Cafe (personalitycafe.com)

Similar to Typology Central. Large, established forum community. Post in their Enneagram subforum.
