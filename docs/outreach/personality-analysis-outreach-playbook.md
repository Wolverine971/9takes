# 9takes Personality-Analysis Outreach Playbook

**Crystallized:** 2026-05-15 from the long-form network campaign (12 recipients, `docs/outreach/2026-05-11_long-form-network-emails.md`).
**Use this for:** any cold outreach to a public figure whose personality analysis 9takes has published. For general business outreach, see `docs/outreach/cold-outreach-system.md` — the broader operating system this playbook descends from.

---

## Core principle

**Trust is the scarce resource, not sends.** Every email to a public figure spends trust with them AND their network (gatekeepers triage and forward; peers compare notes). Optimize for **qualified conversations started per unit of trust consumed** — not opens, not meetings booked, not replies-as-vanity-metric.

---

## The 6 gates (apply in order; if a gate fails, don't write)

| Gate                | Question                                                                                                 | Pass condition                                              |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Right person**    | Does the published analysis genuinely earn the right to address them?                                    | Yes, with cluster fit                                       |
| **Right moment**    | Is the piece itself the moment? Or are they in a relevant cycle (book launch, recent ep, public moment)? | At minimum: piece is fresh; ideally: timed to their cycle   |
| **Right reason**    | Can you state WHY this person, NOW, in one sentence?                                                     | Yes, and it's not "they're famous"                          |
| **Right offer**     | What's useful TO THEM about this piece?                                                                  | Per-person; not "look what I wrote"                         |
| **Right ask**       | What's the smallest useful yes?                                                                          | Correction > read > engagement; never "let's hop on a call" |
| **Right follow-up** | Day-7 redirects with NEW value, not re-pitch                                                             | Adjacent URL, related person, FAQ answer — not a re-share   |

## Subject line

**Format options (ranked by pull strength):**

1. **Signature scene from their piece** — `The Coriolis moment` / `The tic that became a career` / `The chicken shop coin`
2. **Their own line back at them** — `"Don't ever cry"` / `Duty, not delight` / `Raise kids who never have to`
3. **Status-neutral journalistic framing** — `[Name] — wrote a long read on you` / `[Name] — a profile of you`

**Hard constraints:**

- 16–41 chars, 3–7 words (21–40 char sweet spot per 2026 data)
- **No Enneagram jargon** in subject (3w4, Type 5 — kills the curiosity loop, triggers the "being boxed" reflex)
- **No fake `Re:` prefixes** on first touch (2026 spam trigger; legitimate `Re:` only on actual day-7 reply follow-ups)
- **No supplication** ("Is this fair?", "Got a sec?", "Quick question")
- Mobile-truncation-safe (<40 chars target — 68% of opens are mobile)

## Body — the 4-layer template

### Layer 1: Earned connection (1–2 sentences)

**Job:** Prove you're not a stranger BEFORE you ask anything. Name specific intellectual touchpoints from their actual work that shaped how YOU write/think.

**Format:** "I've been a [show/work] listener for [duration]. The X, the Y, the way you Z — those have shaped how I write."

**Specifics rule:** Each touchpoint should be (a) verifiable from their actual published/recorded work, (b) tied to a method or discipline (not a vibe), (c) something that genuinely shaped DJ's craft.

**Reject:**

- Generic fan-speak ("I love your show," "huge fan")
- Trivia signals ("you said in episode 247...")
- Name-dropping shared circles you don't actually have
- Anything that requires the recipient to mentally verify ("I think you said something like...")

### Layer 2: Occasion + truth hook + labeling-sensitivity acknowledgment (1 paragraph)

**Three jobs in one paragraph:**

(a) **Who you are** — one line: "I run 9takes.com — long-form personality analyses of public figures."

(b) **What you made + why telling them directly** — one line: "I just published a [N]-word piece on you, and wanted to tell you directly rather than hope it found you."

(c) **The universal truth hook + labeling-sensitivity beat** — one paragraph. This is the load-bearing move. Standard wording (tune slightly per recipient):

> "Here's the thinking: people are always trying to understand themselves and the public figures they spend hours with — that's just being human. The question is whether that understanding gets written down in a way the subject can engage with, or whether it stays in podcasts and group chats. Long-form podcasts like yours changed how ideas get tested in public; personality writing hasn't caught up. I'm trying to do the long-form version — the kind of read worth pushing back on, instead of one you can't reach the writer of."

**Why it works:**

- **"People read public figures constantly — that's just being human"** disarms the "I'm being classified" reflex by naming the activity as universal, not invasive. Recipient now sees themselves as part of a shared human pattern, not a target.
- **"Kind of read worth pushing back on"** reframes the piece as ENGAGEABLE, not extractive. The recipient is invited to disagree — opposite of being categorized.
- **Positions DJ as REACHABLE** — unlike podcasts/comments where the writer is out of reach. They have an interlocutor, not a verdict.

### Layer 3: The argument in PLAIN ENGLISH + URL (1–2 sentences + link)

**Format:** "So the piece is an argument, not a verdict. The core claim: [the typing argument in plain English]. [Signature image from the piece]."

**HARD RULE:** **No Enneagram jargon in the email body.** No `3w4`, `Type 5`, `Achiever`, `Reformer`. The framework label lives inside the piece, accessible via the URL. The email expresses the IDEA of the claim, not the framework name.

**Plain-English translations:**

| Type                              | Plain-English version (use as starting point, tune per recipient)                                                                                                   |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1 (Reformer)                      | "the kind of mind whose moral framing is the through-line, not the knowledge accumulation or the combat appetite"                                                   |
| 2 (Helper)                        | "the kind of person whose self-worth has always been tangled with whether they're needed"                                                                           |
| 3w2 (warmer Achiever)             | "a particular kind of driven — the warmer kind, the kind whose ambition has always been tangled with the reflex to bring people in"                                 |
| 3w4 (sensitive Achiever)          | "a particular kind of driven — the kind whose ambition has always been matched by something quieter and more sensitive underneath"                                  |
| 3w8 (direct Achiever)             | "a particular kind of relentless — the cleanest version of the build-and-acquire engine, with a directness underneath that refuses to let the scoreboard get fuzzy" |
| 4 (Individualist)                 | "the kind of person who lives a few feet behind the eye, watching themselves feel"                                                                                  |
| 5 (Investigator)                  | "the kind of mind that organizes itself by stepping back"                                                                                                           |
| 6 (Loyalist)                      | "the kind of person whose vigilance has always been a love language"                                                                                                |
| 7w6 (Adventurer w/ Loyalist wing) | "the kind of person who metabolizes pain through movement and humor — but the part underneath stays loyal to the structure they built telling it"                   |
| 8 (Challenger)                    | "a steady, low-frequency force trained early that feeling is danger and impact is safety"                                                                           |
| 9 (Peacemaker)                    | "the kind of person who can hold multiple positions without flattening any of them"                                                                                 |

After the plain-English claim, name the signature image from the piece so the recipient knows you read the actual work. Then the URL.

### Layer 4: The offer + ask (2 sentences)

**Per-person offer in one sentence.** Mirror their OWN epistemic method back at them where possible (peer-craft, not extraction).

**Examples of per-person offer variation:**

- Chris (curious-podcaster): "same way you'd treat a wrong claim in a guest's argument"
- Hormozi (publicly-correct): "your correction is more valuable to me than the piece landing right"
- Huberman (mechanism-first): "the typing is testable; the correction matters more than the verdict"
- Peterson (formal): "the way I'd want one written about me"
- Theo (loose): "I tried to write the way you talk — sharp where it earns it, gentle where it needs to be"

**Standard ask:** "If any of it is wrong, the correction matters more than the piece itself. If any of it is useful, take it. No ask attached."

### Closing

- **Network proof (one sentence, no URLs):** "For context, I've also written on [2-3 cluster names] — same long-form discipline."
- **Sign-off:** "Best, DJ Wayne / 9takes.com"
- **CAN-SPAM cover (P.S.):** "Reply 'no thanks' if you'd rather I stop."

## Day-7 follow-up

**Subject:** `Re: [original signature scene]` — this `Re:` is legitimate (actual reply in the thread).

**Job:** Redirect with NEW value, NOT a re-pitch. Examples:

- A clip pull from their work the piece directly responds to
- A related person you're considering analyzing next
- An FAQ their audience has that the piece answers
- One adjacent personality-analysis URL from the cluster

**Hard stop:** After two follow-ups, drop. No third touch.

## What's universal across every send

- The 4-layer body structure
- The truth hook + labeling-sensitivity paragraph (near-identical wording)
- Plain English typing (Enneagram jargon stays inside the piece via URL)
- Inevitability voice (see [[outreach-inevitability-voice]] memory)
- CAN-SPAM P.S.
- One link maximum (the recipient's own piece)
- Network proof in body, URLs in follow-up only

## What's customized per recipient

- **Layer 1 (earned connection):** Specific intellectual touchpoints from THEIR work that shaped DJ's craft
- **Layer 3 (argument):** Plain-English version of THEIR typing + signature image from THEIR piece
- **Layer 4 (offer):** Per-person epistemic-mirror line
- **Subject:** Signature scene or their own line from THEIR piece
- **Channel (T1/T2/T3):** Per-person research
- **Network proof:** Rotated cluster members (don't reuse the same 3 for everyone)

---

## Reject list

- Fake warmth ("Hope you're well")
- Self-introduction at the top before earned-connection
- Asking for a call as the first touch
- "Quick question" / "got a sec?" subject lines
- Hidden sales pitch
- Wing/type numbers anywhere in subject OR body (lives only inside the piece via URL)
- Signature-extraction asks ("would you tweet this?")
- Generic personalization (just `{first_name}`)
- Anything that requires recipient to admit an embarrassing problem
- Anything that fails the screenshot test (would you be embarrassed if this got posted publicly?)
- Multiple URLs in first touch
- Forwarding their own quotes back at them as flattery
- Anything Threes will read as "managed" (over-engineered openers, etc.)

## Channel tier strategy

| Tier   | Path                                                    | Subject works for                       | Body adjustment                                                                          |
| ------ | ------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **T1** | Gatekeeper inbox (`press@`, `media@`, business partner) | Gatekeeper + principal                  | Standard 4-layer body; gatekeeper sees clear personalization signal that earns a forward |
| **T2** | Form-fill or DM                                         | Single reader (form admin or principal) | Paste body into message field; for dropdowns, pick "general" or "feedback"               |
| **T3** | No public path                                          | N/A                                     | Skip cold send; hold for warm intro                                                      |

## Pre-send checklist

- [ ] Open the target's profile URL in a browser. Confirm clean render. If 404, skip.
- [ ] Confirm the Layer 1 touchpoints are verifiable in their actual work.
- [ ] Confirm the Layer 3 signature image actually appears in your published piece.
- [ ] Pick one subject line. Confirm <40 chars, no jargon, no fake `Re:`.
- [ ] Log the send in your outreach tracker (campaign, recipient, channel, subject, date).
- [ ] If multiple sends planned same day, space them out by at least 4 hours.
- [ ] Wait **5–7 days** for reply before follow-up. Move on after second follow-up at day 14 with no reply.
