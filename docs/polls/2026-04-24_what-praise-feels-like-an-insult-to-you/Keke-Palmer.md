# Keke Palmer — Poll Response

"You're so resilient." Baby, no.

That one lands like someone calling me a good dog. It is a compliment shaped like a leash. What they're actually saying is: we watched you get hit, we enjoyed the recovery, and we would like you to do it again so we can admire the bounce. Resilient is a word people hand you after they have already decided your role in the story is to absorb. It turns the hardest years of your life into a personality trait they get to clap for.

I'm not resilient. I'm the author. There's a difference. Resilient is what the character does. Authorship is what the writer does. When I classified the Usher thing as the inciting incident and not the cause, I was not bouncing back. I was editing. When I went on SNL and told people I was pregnant before the tabloids got to decide the angle, that was not resilience. That was a rewrite in real time. Resilience gives the credit to the impact. Authorship gives it to the pen.

The close cousin is "you're so mature for your age." I have been hearing that since I was ten. Translation: we're glad you didn't make us uncomfortable with how young you actually were. It praises the suit and pretends it doesn't see the kid inside it. Same energy as "you're so articulate" and "you're so poised." They all read like a surprise receipt.

Praise the specific work. Praise the KeyTV bet, the Emerald performance, the _Just Keke_ architecture, the joint-custody decision I made for my son. Don't praise me for still being here. I was always going to still be here. That part was never in question. What was in question was whether I'd be holding the pen when I got here. And I am.

Sorry to "resilient." I'm at work.
