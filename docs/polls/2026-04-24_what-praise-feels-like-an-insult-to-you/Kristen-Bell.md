# Kristen Bell — Poll Response

"You are so selfless." That one. Every time.

When someone tells me I'm selfless, what I hear is: you successfully read the room, anticipated the need, and erased yourself in the process, and I'm going to reward you for it by never asking if you're okay. It's praise for the exact behavior that almost hollowed me out in my mid-thirties. I was a food truck for three years. People kept thanking the truck. Nobody asked the driver if she'd eaten.

The honest version of what "selfless" usually means is "low-maintenance for me." It's praise that functions as a contract. Stay this way. Keep showing up. Don't develop needs. And for a Helper, that contract is seductive, because helping gives me self-esteem and I like the way self-esteem feels. I'll sign the contract if you hand it to me warm enough. That's the part I have to watch.

The praise that actually lands for me is the opposite shape. "You said no and it was the right call." "You let that person be upset with you and didn't chase." "You took the afternoon." Those are not nice sentences. They're not the ones that trend on Mother's Day. But they describe the muscle I'm actually trying to build, which is the one where I stay a whole person while I help, instead of the one where I dissolve into the help and call it virtue.

So if you love me, don't call me selfless. Call me someone who's learning that her own oxygen counts. Call me regulated. Call me annoying when I'm annoying. Tell me I picked myself today. That's the praise I can metabolize without it quietly rebuilding the old machine.
