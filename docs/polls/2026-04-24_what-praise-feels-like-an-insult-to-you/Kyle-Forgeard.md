# Kyle Forgeard — Poll Response

"You guys are crazy." That one. When someone says it like a compliment, I hear someone who has already decided none of this is real work.

It usually shows up in a green room or a DM after a big drop. The person smiles, shakes their head, and says some version of, wild what you guys pulled off, you guys are nuts. They think they are being generous. What they are actually saying is, I don't have to take this seriously, you got lucky, the whole thing is a fluke, nothing here is repeatable. Chaos got you here, not a business.

That reads like an insult to me because the chaotic part is the product on camera. The part that built the thing is five years of saving money from a golf-course job, 36 hours in a car that barely ran, and a lot of edit bays at 3 a.m. Me and the boys didn't full-send our way into Happy Dad. We hired adults and shut up and let them work. The crazy is the wedge. The business is the margin. When someone calls us crazy, they are usually only watching the wedge.

The other one that grates is "you're really smart for a YouTube guy." The qualifier does all the damage. It means you expected a moron and you got a functional sentence. It tells me where you placed me before we started talking, and it tells me you still think filmmaker isn't a real job if the distribution is free.

Either of those, I used to brush off. Now I just clock it and keep moving. The room will sort itself out. We've got a few more years of proof to put on the board anyway.
