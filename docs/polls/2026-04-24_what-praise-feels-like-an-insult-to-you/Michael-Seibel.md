# Michael Seibel — Poll Response

"You're so articulate." That one. Every time.

It's the praise that tells you the person giving it expected less, and is pleasantly surprised the sentence landed. The compliment is a confession. You're not being admired. You're being reclassified. For a second they had to update a file in their head, and the update came out of their mouth by accident.

The close cousin is "you're so blunt, but in a good way." Translation: I was bracing for something I'd have to forgive, and I don't. That one tells you the speaker was sitting in the chair trying to figure out whether to be offended, and decided not to be. Great. I wasn't asking.

The one that actually stings, though, is different. It's when a founder I've spent real time with tells me I was "tough but fair." That is the "thanks, coach" version of a eulogy. It means they heard the notes as a performance of rigor instead of as information about their company. Fair is the floor. If all you got out of six months of office hours is that the guy was fair, I failed you. You were supposed to ship something different on the other side of that conversation. Not feel good about the conversation.

There's a pattern underneath all three. Praise for the delivery instead of the thing delivered. Praise for how I came across instead of what changed because I said it. If the compliment is about my tone, my polish, my evenness, my being surprisingly anything, it means the work didn't move. The work moving is the only scoreboard I care about. The rest is people making themselves comfortable about an interaction they weren't sure how to file.

Tell me a company I worked with is still alive. Tell me a founder stopped lying to themselves about PMF in time to save six months. That's the praise. Everything else is noise I've learned to nod through.
