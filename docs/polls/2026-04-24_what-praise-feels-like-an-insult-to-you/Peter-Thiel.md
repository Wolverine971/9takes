# Peter Thiel — Poll Response

"Visionary." When someone calls me a visionary, I assume they have not read anything I have written, and also that they want something from me.

It is a word people reach for when they need a placeholder. It is the polite noise you make when you cannot name the specific thing a person saw that other people missed. If you could name the secret, you would name the secret. The fact that you reached for "visionary" instead tells me you are repeating a description you heard from someone else who also did not look closely. That is mimesis. It is the opposite of the thing the word pretends to honor.

There is a cousin to it that bothers me more. "Contrarian." I used to not mind it. Now it lands like an insult with a ribbon on it. A contrarian is someone who tracks the consensus and does the opposite of it. That is still the crowd, just on the other side of the room. The interesting move is not against consensus. It is orthogonal to it. The truth that almost nobody is looking at, not the truth everyone is looking at from the wrong side. When you call me a contrarian, you are telling me you think I am playing the crowd's game in reverse. I would rather you said I was wrong.

The last one. "You won." Said warmly, at a dinner, by someone who means it as generous. I know what they mean. I do not experience my life that way. Winning is the frame of the people who never left Sullivan and Cromwell, who never left the clerkship track, who stayed inside the tournament and climbed. If the compliment slots me back into that tournament, even affectionately, it has misread the whole thing. The point was to stop playing, not to finish first.

Praise that treats me as a protagonist of the crowd's story, rather than someone who walked out of it. That is the insult.
