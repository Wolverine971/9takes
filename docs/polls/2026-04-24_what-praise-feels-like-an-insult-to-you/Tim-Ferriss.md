# Tim Ferriss — Poll Response

"You're so disciplined."

When someone says that, I hear a compliment on the outside and a diagnosis on the inside. Discipline, for me, has not always been a virtue. For long stretches it was a survival response, a way to quiet a voice that has been running a trial against me since I was a kid. Calling that discipline is like complimenting someone's limp for being consistent.

The close cousin: "You've optimized your life." No. I've spent decades trying to turn pain into protocols. Some of that produced useful work. Some of it was a very expensive way to avoid sitting still. I wrote a whole piece last month called "The Self-Help Trap" precisely because I do not want to be congratulated for the thing that almost ate me.

The one that actually stings, though, is more specific. "You're so self-aware." People mean it kindly. What I hear is: you have built a beautiful map of your own damage, now stop redrawing it. Self-awareness without change is rumination with better vocabulary. I know the shape of my inner critic, the Princeton logic, the seventeen behaviors that traced to one source. Knowing did not fix it. A week of accelerated TMS, a decade of therapy, and the slow work of investing in relationships did more than any insight I ever had about myself. Praise me for the insight and you are quietly rewarding the loop.

The praise I actually want, and rarely get, is the kind that notices when I did less. When I did not answer the email. When I did not optimize the vacation. When I finished a crappy first draft and let it be crappy. That one costs me something. Call that out and I will feel seen. Call me disciplined and I will nod politely and feel, for a second, exactly twenty-two years old again in a used minivan, getting an A for effort on a life I was about to end.
