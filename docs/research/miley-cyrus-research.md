## 1. RECENT DEVELOPMENTS (2025-2026)

### New Album: "Something Beautiful" (May 30, 2025)

- Ninth studio album, released via Columbia Records
- Accompanied by a musical film (June 6, 2025) that premiered at Tribeca Film Festival
- Deluxe edition (September 19, 2025) added two tracks featuring Fleetwood Mac members Lindsey Buckingham and Mick Fleetwood, plus David Byrne
- Visual album with existential themes centered around healing from trauma and finding beauty in the darkest moments of life
- Inspired by Pink Floyd's _The Wall_ and the film _Mandy_
- Cyrus executively produced with Shawn Everett; collaborated with Alvvays members, Danielle Haim, Flea, Pino Palladino, Adam Granduciel (War on Drugs)
- Naomi Campbell and Brittany Howard appear as guest artists
- Sputnikmusic called it "an absolute triumph that casts aside any qualifiers to make a strong bid for the best major pop album of 2025 so far"
- Quote: "This to me is pop music in its fullest form." (Zane Lowe interview)
- Quote: "With Something Beautiful, I wanted to reclaim and reimagine the word... and what it means to me." (NYT interview)
- The album is described as "medicine" for a "sick culture"
- Source: [Wikipedia - Something Beautiful](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Something_Beautiful_(Miley_Cyrus_album)>), [Variety](https://variety.com/2025/music/news/miley-cyrus-something-beautiful-deluxe-lindsey-buckingham-1236522051/)

## 2. DEEP BIOGRAPHICAL MATERIAL

### Childhood

- Born Destiny Hope Cyrus, November 23, 1992, in Nashville, Tennessee
- Grew up on a 500-acre farm in Franklin/Thompson's Station, Tennessee
- Nicknamed "Smiley" as infant because she smiled so much; shortened to "Miley"
- Attended Heritage Elementary School
- Raised Christian, baptized in Southern Baptist church, attended church regularly, wore purity ring
- Billy Ray deliberately kept children out of spotlight; the isolated farmhouse was chosen for that purpose
- In 2001 (age 8), family moved to Toronto while Billy Ray filmed the TV series Doc
- After seeing Mamma Mia! at the Royal Alexandra Theatre in Toronto, Miley grabbed her father's arm and said: "This is what I want to do, daddy."
- Background in competitive cheerleading -- "Travelling as a cheerleader really set me up for touring... the show is only 90 minutes, but that's your life."
- Source: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miley_Cyrus), [E! Online](https://www.eonline.com/news/1429958/miley-cyrus-on-billy-ray-cyrus-presence-during-childhood-fame)

### Hannah Montana Audition

- Learned about casting through a Nashville talent agent at age 11
- Initially auditioned for the best friend role (Lilly Truscott)
- Producers asked her to audition for the lead but rejected her as "too small and too young"
- She kept coming back; after six months of further casting, was asked to re-audition at age 12 and won the role
- During one audition, wore a T-shirt that read: "I should have my own TV show"
- Quote on her audition: "Disney was on a mission to rebuild and reimagine the company -- that's why they hired [CEO] Bob Iger and me."
- Source: [Rolling Stone](https://www.rollingstone.com/tv-movies/tv-movie-news/flashback-miley-cyrus-auditions-for-hannah-montana-195249/)

### Siblings and Family Structure

- Full siblings: Braison and Noah Cyrus (children of Billy Ray and Tish)
- Half-siblings: Brandi and Trace Cyrus (Tish's children from first marriage to Baxter Neal Helson)
- Half-brother: Christopher (Billy Ray's son from previous relationship with Kristin Luckey)
- Close with Brandi (girls' trips, hiking, travel together)
- Close with Noah (seven years younger), though Noah sided with Billy Ray during family split
- Trace has been supportive of Miley; she helped him through a difficult breakup
- Source: [Brit + Co](https://www.brit.co/miley-cyrus-siblings/), [The Sun](https://www.the-sun.com/entertainment/10601222/cyrus-family-tree-miley-siblings-noah-feuds-tish/)

### Dolly Parton (Godmother)

- Billy Ray and Dolly became close touring together in early 1990s; he asked Dolly to be Miley's godmother
- Dolly played "Aunt Dolly" on three episodes of Hannah Montana
- They performed "Wrecking Ball" cover together for Dolly's Rockstar album (November 2023)
- Dolly still communicates by fax; sent Miley a Mother's Day message: "How much do I love you? As much as my heart can hold and as far as my arms can reach" -- left Miley "choked up"
- Miley jokes she has to use her lawyer's fax machine because "the lawyer is the only person who can still receive a fax"
- Miley says Dolly is "like a mother" to her; her idol for her career and her role model for her "steadfast morals and values"
- In 2024 Zane Lowe interview, Miley discussed a "talking to" she received from Dolly
- Source: [Today](https://www.today.com/popculture/music/dolly-parton-miley-cyrus-godmother-rcna125711), [Biography.com](https://www.biography.com/musicians/miley-cyrus-dolly-parton-relationship), [NBC](https://www.nbc.com/nbc-insider/are-dolly-parton-miley-cyrus-related)

## 4. SPECIFIC QUOTES AND SMALL MOMENTS

### On Herself (Most Honest)

- "I think I've always admired and somewhat envy people that know just exactly who they are." (NPR 2025)
- "My independence and, I guess, my survivalist instincts make it where I can seem selfish." (on her personality)
- "I was an adult when I was supposed to be a kid. So now I'm an adult, and I'm acting like a kid."
- "It's so much easier to know who you are when there aren't a thousand people telling you who they think you are."
- "With yourself, I think you have to decide the kind of person that you really want to be, and for me, it's just a sweet girl."
- "You have so many thousands of people screaming at you, so dopamine, you're feeling a lot of love. And then you totally crash at the end of the show, and you start thinking that one person loving you is not enough, it needs to be 10,000, it needs to be 80,000." (NYT)

### What Collaborators Say

- Kid Harpoon: Miley and Harry Styles "are very similar -- they're giant pop icons, but their process is like indie kids who just want to have fun and don't really care about the pop stuff."
- Producers describe her creative references as unique: Rather than picking a specific band or record, "she'll say things like 'I want to sound like this Gucci dress, or this Dolce & Gabbana thing from 1982 that I found' or bring in a painting."
- "Even in day sessions, she gets real quickly and has been so exposed in her life that she's willing to be vulnerable creatively."
- "You feel a jolt of this creative energy from her, almost at all times -- it's sporadic and it's crazy and it's wild, but it's the best kind."
- "She's not afraid to go after what she wants."
- On Flowers creation: She was "adamant in her desire to first perfect the songs, and then tackle the production."
- Songwriting sessions described as "pseudo therapy sessions" focused on getting to the heart of emotion and finding the most universal way to convey it
- Source: [Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/music/pop/miley-cyrus-endless-summer-vacation-collaborators-secrets-grammy-preview-1235589394/), [Variety](https://variety.com/2023/music/news/michael-pollack-miley-cyrus-flowers-hitmaker-month-1235516912/)

### On Her Songwriting Process

- Almost every record starts on the piano
- Songs start as "pseudo therapy sessions"
- On "Flowers": The chorus was originally "I can buy myself flowers, write my name in the sand, but I can't love me better than you can." -- Miley and co-writer Greg flipped it to "Yeah, I CAN love me better than you can" -- described as one of the biggest moments of the writing process
- The demo was a stripped-back piano ballad; later transformed into disco anthem when Kid Harpoon, Tyler Johnson, and Cyrus jammed for 30 minutes
- Quote: "I can just do what I want to do, and make the music I want to make." (on Dead Petz)
- Source: [Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/music/pop/miley-cyrus-flowers-michael-pollack-song-interview-1235283078/), [Music Week](https://www.musicweek.com/interviews/read/hitmakers-kid-harpoon-on-miley-cyrus-flowers/088981)

### Animals and Activism

- Pet mom to 8 dogs, 3 cats, 2 horses, and a pet pig (almost all adopted/rescued)
- Went vegan in 2014 after the death of her dog
- Founded Happy Hippie Foundation (September 2014, publicly launched May 2015)

## 6. CONTROVERSIES AND GROWTH

### 2013 VMA Performance -- Retrospective

- Quote: "Me and Robin (Thicke) the whole time said, 'You know we're about to make history right now.'"
- Quote: "How many times have we seen this play out in pop music? Madonna's done it, Britney's done it."
- Used Jimmy Kimmel appearance in June 2013 as a "trial run," dancing as a teddy bear half-naked to test the concept
- NYT interview (2025) reflection: "It felt really shocking, but at the end of it, when you watch it back, it really wasn't that wild. I was dressed as a Teddy bear!"
- Quote: "Not only was culture changed, but my life and career were changed forever."
- Said it "inspired me to use my platform for something much bigger" and led to her becoming an activist: "I realized if that many people were going to talk about something that I did or something that I do that I should make it a good thing."
- Source: [Geo TV](https://www.geo.tv/latest/605954-miley-cyrus-gets-honest-about-her-2013-vma-twerking-performance), [Refinery29](https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2013/08/52220/miley-cyrus-vma-reactions)

### Sobriety Journey

- 2017: Quit marijuana ("I stopped smoking because, to sit here and to talk about what I'm doing, I wanted to be really clear")
- 2020: Quit alcohol; in recovery for Xanax addiction since 2020
- Relapsed during pandemic: "I was so close to who is sitting here right now but life had more to teach me. It had more lessons for me. I know I needed to fall one more time."
- On stigma: "It's really hard because, especially being young, there's that stigma of 'you're no fun.'"
- On self-assessment: "I don't have a problem with drinking, I have a problem with the decisions I make once I go past a certain level."
- On alcohol's impact: "It really, really affects my relationships. I'm not the best partner; I'm not the best daughter; I'm not the best sister. I can be a little unreliable."
- On benefits: "The thing that I love about it is waking up 100%, 100% of the time. I don't want to wake up feeling groggy."
- 2025 status: "Sobriety is -- that's like my God. I need it, I live for it. It's changed my entire life."

## 10. THE BANGERZ / VMA ERA (2013-2014) — DETAILED

### The 2013 VMA Performance (August 25, 2013)

**What happened**: At the MTV Video Music Awards at the Barclays Center in Brooklyn, Miley performed a six-and-a-half minute medley of "We Can't Stop" and Robin Thicke's "Blurred Lines."

**The performance in detail**:

- She emerged from inside a giant teddy bear, tongue out. The stage was designed by artist Todd James, who created a 20-foot-tall background bear, 12 bubble-gum pink dancing bears, and 6 twerking bear suits. Miley had sent Todd a selfie of her doing the tongue thing that inspired the bears' expressions — "like a selfie bear."
- She performed "We Can't Stop" in a teddy-bear onesie before stripping down to a flesh-colored latex two-piece bikini.
- She joined Robin Thicke for "Blurred Lines," grinding/twerking against him while using a giant foam finger to touch herself and Thicke's crotch area, sticking her tongue out throughout.
- The performance was deliberately provocative: "When rehearsing, they were aware that if Cyrus touched Thicke with the foam finger on his crotch, people would have something to say about it, but that was the whole point — to excite and provoke."

**The reaction**:

- The show attracted 10.1 million viewers — up 4 million from 2012.
- A photo of the Will Smith family appearing shocked in the audience went viral — though it was later debunked (they were actually reacting to Lady Gaga's opening performance).
- Massive media outrage. Parents' groups, critics, and commentators all condemned the performance. Taylor Swift's "cringe" reaction shot also went viral.
- The Parents Television Council called it "sexually provocative."
- Robin Thicke's then-wife Paula Patton attempted to deflect, calling the foam finger "respectful" (this was widely seen as sarcasm/damage control).

**Miley's contemporaneous quotes**:

- On rehearsal: "Me and Robin the whole time said, 'You know we're about to make history right now.'" (2013 interview)
- On cultural precedent: "How many times have we seen this play out in pop music? Madonna's done it, Britney's done it." (2013)
- She used a Jimmy Kimmel appearance in June 2013 as a "trial run," dancing as a teddy bear half-naked to test the concept before the VMAs.
- **SNL hosting monologue (October 5, 2013)**: "I'm not going to do Hannah Montana, but I can give you an update on what she's been up to — she's been murdered."
- **Harper's Bazaar (2013)**: "I was an adult when I was supposed to be a kid. So now I'm an adult, and I'm acting like a kid."
- On the provocation being about who she was, not what she did: "What I did wasn't shocking, it was who I was that was shocking." (pointing out her Hannah Montana past made it feel more extreme to the public)

**Miley's retrospective quotes**:

- On losing everything: "It was really hard for me in 2013. I lost everything during that time in my personal life because of the choices I was making professionally." (Monica Lewinsky's podcast, 2025)
- "There was even a time where my brother and sister didn't want to go to school, because of how humiliated they were to be related to me."
- On its legacy: "Not only was culture changed, but my life and career were changed forever." She said it "inspired me to use my platform for something much bigger" — leading to activism: "I realized if that many people were going to talk about something that I did or something that I do that I should make it a good thing."
- 2025 NYT reflection: "It felt really shocking, but at the end of it, when you watch it back, it really wasn't that wild. I was dressed as a Teddy bear!"

### The Bangerz Album (Released October 8, 2013)

**What it was**: Fourth studio album, first under RCA Records after leaving Hollywood Records/Disney. Produced primarily by Mike WiLL Made It. Featured Pharrell Williams, Nelly, French Montana, Big Sean, Ludacris, and Future.

**Musical direction**: A deliberate pivot to hip-hop, trap, and EDM-infused pop, departing sharply from her earlier teen-pop and country-inflected sound. Over half the beats came from Mike WiLL Made It.

**Themes**: The vast majority of tracks revolve around a breakup — specifically the split with Liam Hemsworth (they had been engaged, called it off in September 2013). "Wrecking Ball" — which became her first US #1 single — is a pop ballad about a dysfunctional relationship where she tried to get him to open up and got hurt. The album features "dramatic breakup songs" alongside party anthems.

**Miley's motivation (contemporaneous)**:

- MTV documentary "Miley: The Movement" (premiered October 2, 2013) covered production and the lead-up: "Not everyone came to me and said okay, you've got the first single. I had to say no, I know this is it and I'll go to battle if I have to, against anyone who doesn't believe in it. Now I have this freedom to do whatever I want, because people trust me."
- She intended to produce a "very adult and sexy and believable" final product, and was "having fun making music for the first time in her career."
- She described feeling "disconnected" from her earlier work: "I was 16 or 17 when I made it. When you're in your twenties, you just don't really know that person anymore." (Billboard)
- On Disney's restrictions: "Disney's always been on my back" — she said she always wanted to make an album like Bangerz. "I was basically carrying two people's careers and trying to make mine the priority." (Cosmopolitan, 2013)

**The transformation**: In 2013, a 20-year-old Cyrus launched what was seen as a rebellious rebrand. She swapped the Hannah wig for a bleached-blond, slicked-back pixie cut. She hung out with rappers and smoked a lot of weed. She stuck out her tongue so much it prompted think pieces in The Atlantic and The Guardian. And she incinerated the remains of her Disney Channel image with the VMAs performance.

**Commercial performance**: Debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200. "Wrecking Ball" was her first US #1 single.

**Critical reception**: Mixed reviews; Metacritic score of 61/100. Consequence of Sound called it a flawed but compelling pivot. Billboard's track-by-track noted Cyrus's "oft-underestimated vocal heft."

## 11. MILEY CYRUS & HER DEAD PETZ (2015) — DETAILED

**What it was**: Fifth studio album, surprise-released for free on SoundCloud on August 30, 2015 — announced live when Miley hosted the 2015 MTV VMAs. A 23-track psychedelic/experimental album written and produced primarily with Wayne Coyne of The Flaming Lips, with additional contributions from Mike WiLL Made It, Ariel Rechtshaid, and Oren Yoel.

**Why it was free**: Miley's manager said she was "prepared to buy herself out of the label" if RCA didn't cooperate. While Bangerz cost "a couple million" to produce, Dead Petz cost about $50,000. RCA did not hear the final product until it was complete. Miley mentioned the free album plan in a Marie Claire interview (August 2015).

**Musical style**: Described as psychedelic pop, psychedelic rock, and alternative pop. A trippy, drug-fueled experiment compared to Bangerz's polished hip-hop production. Cyrus was fascinated by The Flaming Lips' live instrumentation and its contrast with Bangerz's computerized elements.

**The name**: The album is named after Miley's deceased pets, particularly her dog Floyd, who died in April 2014, devastating her.

**Miley's quotes about it**:

- "I created my surroundings, my own world. What seems like fantasy or trippy, it's not to me. It's my actual reality." (New York Times, 2015)
- "When I made Bangerz, it was as true to me then as this record is now... It just happened naturally in my head." (NYT 2015)
- "It's really scary. If one of my friends doesn't see me for two or three weeks, you have to re-get to know me in a way. My soul will still be the same, but everything around me can be different, and I won't dress the same and maybe different kinds of people will be around." (NYT 2015)
- "I can just do what I want to do, and make the music I want to make." (on the creative freedom of the project)

**Critical reception**: Mixed — Metacritic average of 60/100 based on 21 reviews. Early reviews found it self-indulgent (Pitchfork), amateurish (Vulture), and unimaginative (Spin). BUT notable champions emerged:

- **John Mayer** called it "a masterwork of whack genius," praising Cyrus's demonstration that "you can be one artist one year and the next discover some crazy light inside yourself."
- **Elton John** deemed it "fucking brilliant," comparing Miley to Frank Zappa.

**How it fits the identity pattern**: Dead Petz represents the most extreme version of Miley choosing authenticity over commercial viability. She made a 23-track psychedelic album, gave it away for free, bypassed her label, and did not care about chart performance. For a Type 4, this is the purest form of the identity search — sacrificing mainstream acceptance to explore a version of herself that felt more real, even if the world didn't understand it.

**Later released commercially**: April 10, 2017, through RCA Records on streaming services and digital stores.

## 12. YOUNGER NOW (2017) — DETAILED

**What it was**: Sixth studio album, released September 29, 2017, by RCA Records. A country pop/pop rock record that saw Miley pivot back to her Nashville roots. Featured guest vocals from godmother Dolly Parton.

**Context / why she pivoted**: The album was influenced by her reconciliation with Liam Hemsworth in 2016. She also quit smoking marijuana during this period and was pursuing a cleaner, more grounded image.

**Sobriety quotes (contemporaneous 2017)**:

- Billboard cover story: "I haven't smoked weed in three weeks, which is the longest I've ever [gone without it]. I'm not doing drugs, I'm not drinking, I'm completely clean right now! That was just something that I wanted to do."
- Tonight Show with Jimmy Fallon: "I stopped smoking because, to sit here and to talk about what I'm doing, I wanted to be really clear."
- "I like to surround myself with people that make me want to get better, more evolved, open. And I was noticing, it's not the people that are stoned. I want to be super clear and sharp, because I know exactly where I want to be." (Billboard)
- On her pre-sobriety dreams: She had a nightmare of being "so stoned that she would die."

**The innocence play (later reflection)**: "For 'Younger Now' — the album — the reason I kind of played into this innocence was because I think that was a way to keep and a way to stay [innocent]." She was trying to mirror the relationship reconciliation with Liam externally.

**Commercial performance**: Debuted at #5 on the US Billboard 200 with 45,000 album-equivalent units in its first week (33,000 pure sales) — **her lowest-charting studio album to date**.

**Critical reception**: Mixed reviews — critics felt the songwriting and production lacked substance.

- Pitchfork: 4.7/10 — "Bland production and weak songwriting hamstring the personalized nature of Younger Now." (Claire Lobenfeld)
- The Guardian (Dave Simpson): "She has taken control of the songwriting and production and emerges as a conservative, big-lunged, country-tinged pop star with songs about breaking free."
- Spin (Jordan Sargent): Called it her "least honest album ever" — "Where her past albums felt messy but painfully sincere, Younger Now comes off as safe and overly sanitized."
- Idolator (Mike Wass, November 2018): Defended it as "a potent reminder that you don't need dozens of collaborators and an A&R team the size of Texas to make an excellent pop album."

**Type 4 significance**: The Spin critique is the most telling — Younger Now is arguably the one era where Miley tried to be what she thought someone wanted her to be (Liam, the relationship, stability) rather than what she actually was. The commercial and critical punishment for that inauthenticity reinforces the Type 4 pattern: when she performs genuine identity exploration (Bangerz, Dead Petz, Plastic Hearts), the work resonates more deeply, even if it's messier. When she performs "settling down," the audience and critics sense the falseness.

## 15. THE HAPPY HIPPIE FOUNDATION — DETAILED

**Mission**: To rally young people to fight injustice facing homeless youth, LGBTQ youth, and other vulnerable populations.

**Origin story**:

- Miley began planning the foundation in summer 2014 after getting involved with My Friend's Place, an LA-based homeless youth organization.
- At the **2014 MTV VMAs** (August), her Video of the Year award for "Wrecking Ball" was accepted by a young man named **Jesse Helt** in honor of "the 1.6 million runaways and homeless youth in the United States who are starving, lost and scared for their lives right now." This was a deliberate provocation — using her biggest moment of mainstream vindication to spotlight homelessness.
- She drove harder to launch the foundation publicly after the death of **Leelah Alcorn** in late December 2014 — a transgender teenager whose suicide attracted international attention.

**Key activities**:

- 2014-2016: Served nearly 1,500 homeless youth in LA, reached 25,000+ LGBTQ youth
- Donated 40,000 meals, 20,000 snacks, 40,000 pairs of underwear/socks
- 2015: Released a new set of "Backyard Sessions" videos to publicize the launch, starting May 6 with a performance of "Different" with Joan Jett, collaborating with Facebook for donations
- Renamed to **Miley Cyrus Foundation** in 2024

**Connection to her identity journey**: The foundation was launched at the peak of the Dead Petz era and her pansexuality coming-out — the period of maximum identity fluidity. The fact that she channeled her identity exploration not just into art but into concrete advocacy for young people experiencing their own identity crises (homeless LGBTQ youth, trans youth) is significant. She took what could have been pure narcissistic self-exploration and directed it outward.

## 16. BLACK MIRROR "RACHEL, JACK AND ASHLEY TOO" (2019) — DETAILED

**Episode details**: Third and final episode of Season 5, released June 5, 2019, on Netflix. Written by Charlie Brooker, directed by Anne Sewitsky.

**The plot**: Ashley O (Miley Cyrus) is a mega pop star whose creativity is increasingly restricted by her controlling aunt/manager, Catherine (Susan Pourfar). Catherine drugs Ashley into a coma using medication that suppresses her personality. She then uses technology to extract music directly from Ashley's brain, generating new songs from an unconscious, captive version of the artist. Meanwhile, Rachel (Angourie Rice) — a shy teenage fan — owns an "Ashley Too" AI companion doll that contains a copy of Ashley O's personality. When the doll's limiters are removed, it reveals Ashley's real, profane, angry personality — vastly different from the sanitized pop persona.

**The Hannah Montana parallel**: The episode is explicitly a dystopian version of the Hannah Montana premise — a pop star forced to maintain a manufactured public persona while her real identity is suppressed. But instead of a wig that comes on and off, Ashley O's real self is literally put to sleep. The "peppy, cookie-cutter pop star" immediately evokes Hannah Montana, but the Black Mirror version is "distinctly bleaker."

**Miley's quotes on the role**:

- "The character is me." (MTV interview)
- "The industry is already pretty dark. And at times, I've definitely felt like Ashley O. I still do. Making this record, I felt like Ashley O at times." (MTV)
- She found the script's portrayal of the music industry "realistic, in terms of its overt exploitation of artists" and the way decisions are "driven by performance metrics rather than creativity."

**Charlie Brooker's perspective**: Executive producer Annabel Jones said the episode was about "a musician trying to find her identity and break out from the commercial machine." Brooker had been "discussing the rise of holographic versions of artists — Prince and Whitney Houston and Amy Winehouse," finding it "extremely ghoulish" that people who "have been chewed up by the fame industry" would be brought back.

**A lot of Miley's real life was engineered into the script**, and she had extensive input on the music, the performance, the look, and the relationship between the idol and the fan.

**Musical note**: Ashley O's hit single "On a Roll" was based on Nine Inch Nails' "Head Like a Hole," reworked as a bubblegum pop track — a darkly funny commentary on how the industry sanitizes raw artistic expression.

**Why it matters for the personality analysis**: Miley chose this role deliberately. She was filming it while her real house was burning down in Malibu. She was simultaneously playing a fictional version of her own life — a pop star trapped in a manufactured identity — while the physical structures of her real life were literally being destroyed. The episode's central question ("What happens when you extract the real person and replace them with a performing doll?") is the question Miley had been living since she was 11.

## 17. WHY TYPE 4, NOT TYPE 7 OR TYPE 3? — DETAILED ANALYSIS

### Why Not Type 7 (The Enthusiast)?

**The case for Type 7**: On the surface, Miley looks like a textbook Seven — constantly seeking new experiences, refusing to be pinned down, shifting interests, boundary-pushing, apparently hedonistic (the drugs, the partying, the sexual openness), and maintaining an upbeat public energy. Some sources classify her as a 7w8.

**The critical differences that distinguish her as a Type 4**:

1. **Relationship to pain**: This is the single biggest differentiator.
   - **Sevens avoid pain.** They reframe, redirect, and look to the positive. They fill painful moments with new plans, new experiences, new stimulation.
   - **Miley dwells in pain and transforms it into art.** When her house burned down, she called it "the biggest blessing." When her marriage collapsed, she wrote "Flowers." When she discovered stored rage in her body, she made "Something Beautiful." She doesn't avoid suffering — she mines it. "We've been taught that rage and hatefulness, that there's not a beauty in it — but it lets you know that you're alive." That's the opposite of a Seven's instinct.

2. **Quality of the reinventions**:
   - **Sevens reinvent to escape.** They get bored, they move on, they seek novelty. The changes are lateral — one fun experience to the next.
   - **Miley's reinventions are vertical — they go deeper.** Each era represents a specific identity question being worked through. Bangerz wasn't "fun new experience" — it was "I need to destroy the person I was told to be." Dead Petz wasn't "ooh, psychedelic sounds are cool" — it was "I need to make something with zero commercial pressure to find out what I actually sound like." These are excavations, not escapades.

3. **The envy of wholeness**:
   - Sevens don't envy people who are stable and whole. They pity them — boring, stuck, missing out.
   - Miley said: "I think I've always admired and somewhat envy people that know just exactly who they are." That envy of solid identity is the Four wound, not a Seven thought.

4. **She's said she doesn't love change**: In an Apple Music interview, she confessed she's "not a person that loves change" — contradicting the public image. A Seven loves change. A Four is compelled by identity exploration even when it's painful.

5. **Introversion under the extroversion**: Sevens are genuinely energized by external stimulation. Miley's collaborators describe her as someone who "gets real quickly" and whose sessions are "pseudo therapy." Her current life is deliberately quiet — protective of her relationship, not touring, "winding down attachment to mainstream success." A Seven in health moves toward depth (like a Five); Miley's whole trajectory has been FROM spectacle TOWARD depth.

### Why Not Type 3 (The Achiever)?

**The case for Type 3**: Miley is obviously ambitious, performs at the highest level, has a public persona, is image-conscious, and has achieved massive commercial success. Threes are the performers of the Enneagram.

**The critical differences**:

1. **Direction of identity construction**:
   - **Threes build identity from the outside in.** They read the room, determine what success looks like, and adapt their persona to achieve it. The image serves the goal of being admired.
   - **Miley builds from the inside out.** She has repeatedly destroyed commercially successful personas because they stopped feeling true. She has sacrificed relevance for authenticity (Dead Petz, Younger Now's creative ambition if not its execution). A Three would never release a 23-track psychedelic album for free on SoundCloud.

2. **Relationship to failure**:
   - **Threes are devastated by public failure** and will adapt to avoid it. They suppress emotions that might interfere with their performance.
   - **Miley leans into the mess.** The VMA performance wasn't a calculated image play — it was an emotional exorcism. Her public breakdowns, the tongue, the nudity — these aren't Three-style polish. They're raw, sometimes self-sabotaging emotional expression.

3. **Emotional suppression vs. emotional immersion**:
   - **Threes disconnect from feelings** to stay productive and adaptable.
   - **Miley is defined by emotional intensity.** She stored rage in her body for years. She describes songwriting as "pseudo therapy." She described the dopamine crash cycle with devastating self-awareness. She doesn't suppress emotion — she is consumed by it.

4. **What she envies**:
   - **Threes envy others' success and status.**
   - **Miley envies others' wholeness and self-knowledge.** "I've always admired and somewhat envy people that know just exactly who they are." A Three knows exactly who they are — the successful one. They don't envy identity stability; they envy achievement.

5. **She has a 4w3, not a 3w4**: The Three wing gives Miley her ambition, her ability to complete projects, her comfort on stage, and her competitive drive. But the core motivation is always the Four question: "Who am I really?" — not the Three question: "Am I successful enough?"

**The key behavioral test**: Ask this question about any of Miley's major career decisions: "Was this optimized for success, or for self-expression?"

- Bangerz: Self-expression (destroyed her child-star brand)
- Dead Petz: Self-expression (free psychedelic album, bypassed her label)
- The VMA performance: Self-expression/identity demolition (not a calculated career move)
- Younger Now: The one time she tried to optimize for what she thought people wanted — and it was her least authentic, worst-received work
- Plastic Hearts: Self-expression (rock genre pivot, modest commercial expectations)
- Something Beautiful: Self-expression (visual album inspired by Pink Floyd and _Mandy_)

A Three would have followed up Bangerz with another hip-hop album that built on the commercial momentum. A Seven would have followed it with something fun and novel. A Four followed it with a free psychedelic experiment that cost $50,000 and confused nearly everyone.
