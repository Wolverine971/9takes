# Tyler Perry Research

## 1. Faith / Christianity

### How Central Is Faith?

Faith is the foundation of Tyler Perry's entire life and career. He attended seminary school in his late teens and early 20s, was a minister in his church, and preached and prayed for people. He buried Bibles under every soundstage and at the gates of Tyler Perry Studios.

### Key Quotes on Faith

**On faith being central to his success (Beliefnet interview):**

> "I am a Christian, I am a believer, and I know had I not been a person of faith, I couldn't be here in this place, and I wouldn't be walking the path that I'm on now."

**On the purpose of his path:**

> "I think the greater good of the path I am on now is to teach people to forgive and move on in a way that is done through the healing power of humor."

**On prayer growing up (Beliefnet interview):**

> "I had nothing but prayer. That's what let me know that there really is power in the prayer."

**On connecting prayer to greatness:**

> "So every night before I lay down at night when I've said my prayers all of those things are connected to wanting to be great."

**On persevering through the dark times (Instagram / Movieguide):**

> "Even in my darkest times when I wanted to give up, I kept believing, and I asked God to help me to believe past any naysayer, any doubt, and any problem."

**On his homeless years in Atlanta (Instagram / Movieguide):**

> "Atlanta has truly been the promised land for me. I came here with nothing, lived off Sylvan Road, ended up homeless and starving, but I was always praying and believing. I was always keeping the faith, knowing that if I worked hard, did my absolute best, kept my integrity, honored every blessing, and remained grateful through it all that everything would work out. And, it has, thank God."

**On persistence (Movieguide):**

> "Just know that it is possible to believe in your dream and still wonder how or when it's going to happen. Stay the course. Pray, work, believe, and repeat. You'll get there. Just believe for as much as you can, and ask God to help you believe for the rest!"

**On God's faithfulness and creative independence (Sonoma Christian Home, 2017):**

> "God has been too good to me to go and try to sell out to get some money... It ain't gonna happen... I've never been one to knock on the door and say, 'Please let me in'... I have always tried to make my own way."

**On Madea as a vehicle for faith (Sonoma Christian Home, 2017):**

> "I can slap Madea on something and talk about God, love, faith, forgiveness, family, any of those."

**On Hollywood and faith (Sonoma Christian Home, 2017):**

> "Hollywood is finally waking up to the fact that people who go to church also go to the movies."

**On content censorship:**

> "Did you know you can't say 'Jesus' in a sitcom? They told me that and I was like, You gotta be kiddin' me."

### Bibles Buried at Tyler Perry Studios

Perry's 330-acre studio complex in Atlanta is built on a former Confederate Army base. He buried Bibles under every structure.

**On why (Lakewood Church speech / Christian Post):**

> "From the moment I walked onto the property, I was haunted by it. So, as we built each of the 12 soundstages, we buried Bibles underneath them, as a way of refocusing the spirit of the place."

**On the land's history:**

> "The land itself was once a Confederate Army base, which meant there were people here fighting to keep my ancestors enslaved."

**On the studio gates:**

> "In order to get into the studio you've got to drive through the gates. And to go through the gates you've got to pass through the arms and the handle. Buried under that when you're going in and coming out is the open word of God. I've buried the Bible at the gates of the studio. So you can't come in without crossing the Word and you can't leave out without crossing the Word."

**On hope (Christian Post):**

> "I just want people to know that no matter how dark, no matter how bad, no matter how dismal the scene or situation you're in, there's still hope."

### Lakewood Church Speech

Perry spoke at Joel Osteen's Lakewood Church in Houston. He revealed he had gone to seminary school, was a minister, and preached. Key quotes:

> "I was running from poverty and failure wasn't an option. I knew there was something higher for me and I had to go with everything I had."

> "I had to risk everything, I had to put everything on the line."

### Black Church Influence on His Work

- Perry's gospel plays on the "chitlin circuit" (1998-2004) were rooted in Black church culture
- His plays and films feature church settings, worship styles, and gospel music commonly found in predominantly Black churches
- Characters regularly pray, attend church, and reference Scripture
- Themes of forgiveness, redemption, and divine justice run throughout his work
- He attributes his roots directly to faith: "I attribute a lot of [my success] to going back to what my roots are and my faith."
- The chitlin circuit itself was church-adjacent: "Do not despise where you come from because God will plant you somewhere where you can grow so strong and so tall."

### His Mother's Influence

His mother and grandmother greatly impacted his attitude toward prayer. He grew up in a Christian home where his mom taught him about God. He has said the foundation of his life is the faith in Jesus his mother passed on to him.

## 2. Tyler Perry and AI / Sora

### The Story

In February 2024, Tyler Perry halted an $800 million expansion of Tyler Perry Studios in Atlanta after seeing demonstrations of OpenAI's text-to-video AI model Sora. The expansion had been in planning for four years and would have added 12 soundstages to the 330-acre property. He gave an interview to The Hollywood Reporter that became one of the most-cited statements by any entertainment figure about AI.

### Key Quotes (The Hollywood Reporter, February 2024)

**On halting the expansion:**

> "I was in the middle of, and have been planning for the last four years, about an $800 million expansion at the studio, which would've increased the backlot a tremendous size -- we were adding 12 more soundstages. All of that is currently and indefinitely on hold because of Sora and what I'm seeing. I had gotten word over the last year or so that this was coming, but I had no idea until I saw recently the demonstrations of what it's able to do."

**On the technology's impact:**

> "Being told that it can do all of these things is one thing, but actually seeing the capabilities, it was mind-blowing."

**On what AI could replace:**

> "I no longer would have to travel to locations. If I wanted to be in the snow in Colorado, it's text. If I wanted to write a scene on the moon, it's text, and this AI can generate it like nothing."

**On jobs at stake:**

> "It makes me worry so much about all of the people in the business. Because as I was looking at it, I immediately started thinking of everyone in the industry who would be affected by this, including actors and grip and electric and transportation and sound and editors, and looking at this, I'm thinking this will touch every corner of our industry."

**On urgency:**

> "I am very, very concerned that in the near future, a lot of jobs are going to be lost."

**On regulation:**

> "There's got to be some sort of regulations in order to protect us. If not, I just don't see how we survive."

**On collective action:**

> "The only way to move forward in this is to galvanize it as one voice, not only in Hollywood and in this industry, but also in Congress."

### Why This Matters

Perry is notable in the AI conversation because:

- He's not a technophobe -- he acknowledged the tool's potential for his own productions
- He's a mogul who owns his own studio, so halting an $800M expansion is a concrete, material action, not just talk
- He framed it as a labor issue, not just a creative one -- naming specific crew roles (grip, electric, transportation, sound, editors)
- He called for regulation at the industry and congressional level
- The interview became a flashpoint in the broader AI-in-entertainment debate

## 3. Tyler Perry's Philanthropy

### Walmart Layaway (December 2018)

- Paid off $434,000 worth of layaways for approximately 1,500 people at two Walmart stores in Atlanta
- $178,000 at one Atlanta store, $256,000 at the nearby Douglasville store
- Customers only had to pay one cent to collect their purchases
- Source: CBS News, TMZ, ABC News

### Grocery Bills for Elderly During COVID (April 2020)

- Paid the grocery bills for all elderly and high-risk shoppers at 44 Kroger stores in Atlanta and 29 Winn Dixie stores in New Orleans (73 stores total)
- Source: E! News, Revolt

### Restaurant Workers Tip During COVID (April 2020)

- Left a $21,000 tip ($500 per employee) for 42 out-of-work servers at Houston's restaurant in Buckhead, Atlanta
- He was just picking up a to-go order
- Source: GPB News, Atlanta Black Star, Just Jared

### Rayshard Brooks Funeral (June 2020)

- Paid all funeral expenses for Rayshard Brooks, a Black man killed by Atlanta police
- Committed to funding the college education of Brooks' four children (Mekai, 13; Blessing, 8; Memory, 2; Dream, 1)
- Source: Essence, AJC, CNN, Fox 5 Atlanta

### Haiti Earthquake Relief (2010)

- Initially pledged $250,000 through his foundation
- Raised his total commitment to $1 million
- Source: CinemaBlend, E! News

### Hurricane Relief (2017, 2019)

- Provided financial support for those affected by Hurricane Harvey in Texas (2017)
- Helped with Hurricane Maria in Puerto Rico (2017)
- Contributed to Hurricane Dorian relief (2019)
- Source: CinemaBlend

### NAACP Centennial Donation (2009)

- Gave $1 million to the NAACP for its centennial anniversary
- Also purchased artworks by Jacob Lawrence, Jonathan Green, Elizabeth Catlett, and Sam Gilliam
- Source: E! News

### Atlanta Seniors Property Taxes (February 2023)

- Donated $2.75 million total to help low-income seniors keep their homes amid rising property taxes
- $750,000 for immediate relief covering back property taxes (city, county, school taxes) for 300+ seniors
- $500,000 annually for four additional years
- Coverage of tax increases for 100 low-income seniors over 20 years through a pilot program
- Administered by Invest Atlanta Partnership
- Source: City of Atlanta press release, CBS News, Fox Business

### Food Insecurity During Government Shutdown (November 2025)

- Donated nearly $1.4 million to seven organizations during SNAP funding crisis
- Recipients: Atlanta Community Food Bank, Meals on Wheels Atlanta, Baby2Baby, Caring for Others, All for Lunch, Goodr, The Ron Clark Academy
- Quote: "If you've never been poor, then you may not fully understand the life-changing impact SNAP benefits mean to hard-working people, to our seniors, and to our children."
- Quote: "Compassion is not political. It is humanity. And we seem to be missing both now."
- Source: CBS News Atlanta

### Housing for Individuals

- Purchased a $350,000 home for the mother of gospel singer LaShun Pace
- In 2009, rented a house (furnished, with utilities) for 88-year-old Rosa Lee Ransby after her home was destroyed by fire
- Source: CinemaBlend

### Disney World Trip for Children (2009)

- Sent 65 children from a Philadelphia daycare on an all-expenses-paid trip to Walt Disney World after they were denied entry to a suburban Philadelphia swim club in a racially charged incident
- Source: E! News

### Prince Harry and Meghan Markle (2020)

- Sent his private jet to Vancouver to bring Harry and Meghan to California when their location was publicized
- Let them stay in his $18 million Los Angeles mansion
- Provided round-the-clock security after the royal family stripped their protection
- They stayed for six weeks
- Later became godfather to their daughter Lilibet Diana
- His quote on why: "It was a very difficult time for them. What I know about the two of them, that I wish the world knew, is how much they love -- these two people love each other. They found each other -- out of all these odds against them finding each other, they found each other. The love they have is really, really moving, and I just wanted to do anything I could to support them."
- Source: Today, Washington Post, CBS News

### Lakewood Church / Harvey Relief

- Donated $250,000 to Lakewood Church to help local families rebuild after Hurricane Harvey
- Source: Defender Network

## 4. Audience Loyalty and Cultural Dynamic

### Who Is Tyler Perry's Core Audience?

Perry's devoted fanbase consists primarily of southern, Black, lower- and middle-class churchgoers -- especially Black women. He tapped into the Black church demographic that had been largely forgotten and ignored by major advertisers, delivering an audience to Hollywood that advertisers had not been able to reach for decades.

### How He Built His Audience

Perry toured the "chitlin circuit" (now called the urban theater circuit) from 1998 to 2004, performing gospel-inflected stage plays in Black churches and theaters across the South. He built his following person by person, before social media existed.

**His own account of building the audience (HelloBeautiful):**

> "I was on the road from 1998 to 2004 during 300-and-something performances year all over the country. Before social media, I'm out at the end of my show saying, 'Sign up for my mailing list. Sign up for my website'... I had a few million people following me on my website so I could send out an email and sell out theaters and arenas all over before we even advertised anything. That's where it all started."

**On what the chitlin circuit meant (HelloBeautiful):**

> "It's been the people. Let me say this about this chitlin circuit: a lot of times us as African-American people have evolved so much that we look down our nose at certain things. But what I found out about this circuit is it was so wonderful... They had made me so famous within my own culture that I couldn't walk down the street without being recognized. Get to Hollywood, nobody knows my name."

### Why Fans Feel "Seen"

Perry's fans see characters who reflect their own lives -- church-going, family-centered, working-class Black life. His characters deal with themes of abuse, forgiveness, faith, family dysfunction, and redemption that are familiar to his community but rarely centered in mainstream Hollywood.

His work features church settings, gospel music, and worship styles found in predominantly Black churches. The humor, the drama, the moral arc -- all of it maps onto the rhythms of Black church culture.

### Perry's Own Words About His Audience

**On knowing his audience (Variety):**

> "I know to speak to the audience -- that is the business; that is the voice; those are the people that matter the most to me."

**On refusing to "cross over" (Stephen Follows):**

> "When I was coming up, people would say 'When are you going to cross over?' Meaning, change who you are to invite other people in. I never believed in that. I always believed in serving my audience, super-serving them."

**On critics vs. audience (Atlanta Black Star, 2020):**

> "Because if you're complaining about my writing, you're not the audience. My audience loves the way that it's done."

**On ratings proving his point (Atlanta Black Star, 2020):**

> "When I hear that kind of stuff, I'm thinking, 'Are y'all looking at the ratings? Do you understand that the audience is in love with this?'"

**On the language of his audience:**

> "My audience and the stories that I tell are African-American, stories specific to a certain audience, specific to a certain group of people that I know, that I grew up with, and we speak a language. Hollywood doesn't necessarily speak the language."

**On handling Spike Lee's criticism:**

> "If any criticisms stung, it would have been his because I had so much respect and admiration for him."

### The Class/Cultural Dynamic

The dynamic at the heart of Tyler Perry's career:

1. **Hollywood ignored the Black church audience.** For decades, mainstream studios did not create content for churchgoing, working-class Black audiences -- particularly Black women in the South. Perry filled that vacuum.

2. **Critics couldn't relate.** Film and TV critics, who were overwhelmingly white and male, could not identify with Perry's intellectual property. His work received poor critical reviews even as it generated enormous box office returns.

3. **Perry bypassed the gatekeepers.** He retained ownership and creative control, speaking directly to his audience rather than going through studio intermediaries. He never had a writers' room. He wrote everything himself.

4. **The audience responded with fierce loyalty.** Because Perry was the first person to center their stories -- their churches, their grandmothers, their struggles, their humor -- fans felt an ownership over his success. They saw themselves on screen for the first time.

5. **Two-sided tension.** Some Black intellectuals and filmmakers (notably Spike Lee) criticized Perry for reinforcing stereotypes. Perry's audience largely rejected that critique, seeing it as elitist and disconnected from their reality.

### Perry's Own Quote on Content Ownership as the Path Forward

> "I think change comes by becoming owners of studios, owners of projects, owners of content..."
