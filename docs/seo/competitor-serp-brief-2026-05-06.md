# 9takes Enneagram & Personality-Analysis SERP / Competitor Research Brief

**Date:** 2026-05-06
**Scope:** External SERP landscape only (internal cluster strategy lives in `master-topic-cluster-map-2026-05-06.md`)
**Sources cited:** 35+ live SERPs sampled via WebSearch; page-level fetches on 9takes and PDB

---

## TL;DR (5 bullets)

1. **The Enneagram head-term SERP is locked.** Enneagram Institute, Truity, Personality Path, Crystal Knows, integrative9, and EnneagramUniverse own the top 10 for "enneagram", "enneagram test", "enneagram type N", and "wings". 9takes will not crack these in the next 12 months and shouldn't try.
2. **Pop-culture / celebrity Enneagram is the soft underbelly.** Personality Database (PDB) and boo.world dominate by sheer URL count, but their pages are voting-widget shells with thin editorial copy. 9takes is already #1 for several emerging-celebrity queries (Doechii, Sabrina Carpenter, Jason Calacanis, Pedro Pascal long-tail, Sam Altman, Sydney Sweeney, Pete Davidson). This is the wedge.
3. **9takes is already in top 5 for the top traffic blog query** ("enneagram and mental illness") and in top 10 for "enneagram compatibility matrix". Mental-health-meets-Enneagram is a defensible, high-intent cluster.
4. **AI Overviews lean heavily on Truity, Enneagram Institute, Personality Path, and the Wikipedia Enneagram page** for definitional queries; for celebrity queries they synthesize across 3–6 conflicting sources, frequently flagging "the typing is debated." That ambiguity is an AEO opening — pages that add the disagreement explicitly + a citation-ready stat get pulled into AI answers.
5. **First-party corpus stats (Type 7 +27pp comedians, Type 4 +21pp musicians, Type 5 +13pp tech founders, Type 9 +5.5pp film/TV, Type 2 +9pp politics, Type 3 +28% creators)** are unique and currently undefended on the SERP. No competitor has a published, refreshable distribution dataset across domains. This is the strongest moat and the most underexploited surface.

## Content Patterns That Win

Six recurring formats across top-10 SERPs:

1. **Long-form type description page (2,500–4,000 words).** Enneagram Institute is the canonical structure: Basic Fear, Basic Desire, Levels of Development, Wings, Stress/Growth Lines, Famous Examples. Truity follows the same skeleton. Replication isn't enough — sites that rank are either (a) 8+ years old with backlink moat or (b) include a free quiz CTA.
2. **List-with-sub-h2s celebrity post.** "32 Enneagram Type 3 Celebrities" (enneagramtest.com), "9 Famous Enneagram 3s" (brainmanager). Each celebrity gets a 100–200 word blurb. These rank for `[famous] enneagram` long-tails by sheer mention.
3. **"Which X Are You Based on Enneagram"** quiz/list articles (Screenrant, ShowSnob). They rank for show-name queries because PDB lacks editorial.
4. **Voting/database widget pages (PDB, boo.world, Crystal).** Rank by URL count, not depth. Page is mostly "Vote: 3w4 (62%) / 3w2 (28%)". Empty editorial. **This is the format with the largest gap to exploit.**
5. **Comparison pages (Xw vs Yw, Type X vs Type Y).** Brainmanager, Personality Path, Crystal. Tight 1,200–1,800 word format with a comparison table. Featured snippets frequently land on a clean H2 + bullet block.
6. **Counseling / spiritual blogs ranking on type-trauma queries.** hannawoodycounseling.com, discovercounselingcollective.com, thepeopleplacecc.com — DR<30 sites holding top 5 for trauma-adjacent Enneagram queries because canonical Enneagram sites won't touch the topic. **Major gap.**

**Where featured snippets / AI Overviews are soft:**

- "What enneagram type are most [profession]?" — top result is often a forum thread or thin Boo database page. Easily defendable with a real stat.
- Mistype queries ("Type 9 mistype as 5", "4 vs 9", "5 vs 9") — owned by John Luckovich and Enneagram Institute, but pages are 3,000+ words of prose with no Q&A markup. AI Overviews struggle to extract clean answers.
- Wing comparisons (3w2 vs 3w4) — featured snippet usually a 2-line excerpt from Crystal or Brainmanager. Extractable formatting beats long prose.
- "Enneagram and [mental health condition]" — 9takes already wins mental illness; ADHD, anxiety, depression, trauma, autism all open.

## AI Overviews / AEO Citation Patterns

Based on direct observation of search snippets and synthesized AI responses across 25+ queries:

**Most-cited domains in AI answers:**

1. **Truity** — appears in nearly every AI Overview that needs a definition. Citation magnet because of clean H2 structure + "True You Journal" branded blog with consistent format.
2. **Enneagram Institute** — cited for canonical type descriptions, levels of development, wings.
3. **Wikipedia** — fallback for definitional queries. Important: AI answers cite Wikipedia's "dismissed by personality assessment experts as pseudoscience" line frequently. Any 9takes page on definitional Enneagram should acknowledge this without conceding ground.
4. **Personality Path** — cited for type comparisons, wing breakdowns. Heavy schema markup, FAQ blocks.
5. **integrative9.com** — cited for subtypes / instinctual variants.
6. **Crystal Knows** — cited because they include numerical claims (e.g., "Type 8 is 15.93% of users"), which AI loves to surface.

**9takes' current AI surface area:** Confirmed citations on `enneagram and mental illness` (#1 SERP, AI Overview pulls "personality framework, not a diagnostic tool" framing). Likely citations on Doechii, Sabrina Carpenter, Sam Altman, Pete Davidson queries (top 3 SERP positions). No citation for any definitional or list query.

**Why Crystal wins AI citations more than its content depth deserves:** numbers. "Type 5 is 4.8% of the population" is a perfectly extractable claim. 9takes' corpus stats are structurally identical but currently aren't deployed as AI bait.

**AEO format that wins on celebrity queries:** Acknowledge the typing disagreement explicitly in an early FAQ block. Example structure that gets cited:

```
Is [person] Enneagram Type 5 or Type 8?
> Most analysts type [person] as Type 5 (the Investigator), citing [traits].
> A minority case for Type 8 rests on [counter-evidence]. The Type 5
> framing is more consistent with [biographical anchor].
```

This format is exactly what the 9takes Elon page does. It's also why it ranks despite being newer than the incumbents.

## Notes on Personality Database (PDB) specifically

- WebFetch returned **403 Forbidden** on a celebrity profile URL — strongly suggests aggressive bot blocking and possibly degraded crawl signals.
- SERP search results for `site:personality-database.com enneagram` show that PDB's Wiki section (`wiki.personality-database.com`) is a separate subdomain that _does_ index — but it's a secondary, low-traffic property.
- PDB's main strength is MBTI listing pages (`/profile/{id}/{name}-{category}-mbti-personality-type`); their Enneagram editorial is essentially nonexistent. Vote distributions are user-generated and stale.
- **Practical implication:** any 9takes celebrity page with 1,500+ words of original copy + corpus-stat citations + clear typing rationale will outrank PDB on virtually any emerging-celebrity query within 3–6 months. The Doechii/Sabrina Carpenter/Sam Altman wins are already proof.
