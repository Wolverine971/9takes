# Social Ladder Map: Who Looks Up to Whom

The aspiration hierarchy behind 9takes blog strategy. People on lower rungs are fascinated by comparisons to people on higher rungs. Write comparison blogs that connect adjacent tiers to attract the lower-tier person's audience.

**Strategy**: If you compare Sam Parr to Jeff Bezos using personality, Sam Parr's audience finds it — and they're captivated because they also look up to Bezos.

## How to Use This Map

### The Formula

1. Pick a person on **Tier 3-5** (the person whose audience you want)
2. Identify who they **look up to** on **Tier 1-2** (the comparison that will fascinate that audience)
3. Write a personality comparison blog that decodes WHY person A admires person B — and where they diverge

### Why This Works

- **SEO**: People search for both names together ("Sam Parr Jeff Bezos")
- **Emotional hook**: "Am I like my hero?" is a universal human question
- **Enneagram angle**: Personality type reveals whether the admiration is aspirational or delusional
- **Shareability**: The person on the lower tier's audience shares it because it flatters their hero

1. Sam Parr & Shaan Puri vs. Bezos & Musk (tech bro audience, high search volume)
2. Olivia Rodrigo vs. Taylor Swift (massive Gen Z search volume)
3. Timothee Chalamet vs. Leonardo DiCaprio (confirmed mentorship, huge fanbase)
4. Shane Gillis vs. Dave Chappelle (comedy podcast audience overlap)
5. Kai Cenat vs. Kevin Hart (streaming → mainstream crossover moment)
6. MrBeast vs. PewDiePie (YouTube history, massive search volume)
7. Chappell Roan vs. Lady Gaga (hot right now, LGBTQ+ audience)
8. Alex Hormozi vs. Tony Robbins (business/self-help audience)
9. Tom Holland vs. Robert Downey Jr (MCU fanbase, confirmed mentorship)
10. AOC vs. Bernie Sanders (political audience, progressive pipeline)
