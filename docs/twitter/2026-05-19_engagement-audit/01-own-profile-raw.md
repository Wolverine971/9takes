### What dies

- **Thread continuations with link cards** (Mar 29 part 2, Mar 28 part 2) → 4–8 views. Twitter's algorithm punishes outbound link cards.
- **Bare link drops** (Feb 6) → 6 views, 0 engagement.

### Account-level problems

1. **30 followers after 5 years** = essentially no audience built. This isn't an algorithm problem; it's a base-rate problem.
2. **179 following / 30 followers** signals "noisy follower" rather than "creator worth following" to both X's algorithm and humans visiting the profile.
3. **Posting cadence is dead**: 6–8 posts across 4 months. The algorithm needs frequency signal to know what bucket to put you in; sporadic = invisible.
4. **No replies in scrollback** = no surface-area for discovery. The one reply (to @newstart_2024) got 17 views — more than half the original posts.
5. **No images, no video** in the captured posts. Twitter heavily rewards visual media.
6. **Bio doesn't promise a hook** — "300+ deep dives" is brag without payoff. Compare to creators who promise: "I tweet psychology threads that will [change your dating life / make you self-aware / etc.]"

## First-order recommendations from profile alone

- The "engagement = 0" problem is **mostly a distribution/cadence problem, not a content quality problem**. The content is interesting; nobody is seeing it.
- Reply-to-big-accounts is already outperforming originals (17 views vs 6 for a link drop). That's the lowest-hanging fruit.
- Pinned post: there appears to be no pinned post. A pinned listicle ("Self-sabotage by enneagram type") would be the immediate fix.
