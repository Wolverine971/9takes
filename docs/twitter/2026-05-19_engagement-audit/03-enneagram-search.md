## CRITICAL STRATEGIC INSIGHT

### Why MBTI wins on X and Enneagram doesn't

1. **MBTI is a portable language for fan communities.** K-pop fans type their idols ("jungkook is ISFP"), anime fans type characters ("Suguru Geto is INFJ"), and these typings travel with the fandom. **Enneagram doesn't have this fandom-typing language baked in.** When a K-pop fan tweets "jungkook is ISFP," 376K people see it. When DJ tweets "Druski is a Type 8," 160 people see it.

2. **MBTI has 4-letter codes that fit in tweets.** "ISFP", "INTJ", "ENFP" are immediate, scannable, identity tokens. **Enneagram codes (Type 5, 4w5, sx/sp/so) are less iconic and require more setup.** Twitter rewards instantly-readable identity tokens.

3. **MBTI is woven into pop culture identity discourse.** Idols, characters, BookTok, KpopTwitter all use MBTI casually. Enneagram is largely confined to therapy/coaching/self-help circles — which aren't on X. They're on Instagram, Substack, and YouTube.

4. **The "typology Twitter" subset that DOES use Enneagram on X uses it as a visual-aesthetic identity marker** (selfies tagged with sx4/sp8 etc.) — not as the analytical framework 9takes.com publishes. **DJ's content (3,000-word celebrity personality breakdowns) is built for SEO/Google, not for Twitter's culture.**

### Implication for strategy

**Stop fighting for #enneagram traction. The hashtag is dead.** Three real paths exist:

a) **Hijack MBTI's traffic.** Post Enneagram analyses of MBTI-typed K-pop idols and anime characters. Use both frameworks side-by-side. ("Jungkook says he's ISFP. That maps to Enneagram Type 9 with a 4-wing. Here's why.") This routes existing MBTI search and fan-engagement into 9takes content.

b) **Reposition as "personality + psychology" not "enneagram."** The active community is psychology-adjacent (attachment, BPD, parenting, Freud, Adler, regulation). Drop the enneagram-specific framing in tweets; use universal psychology language with enneagram explanation in the linked piece.

c) **Join "typology Twitter."** The instinctual-variant niche (@shug898, @bxnnysundae, @kamaz_jaja, @David_333_555, @telonator, @NENESHECHKA993) is small but engages. Follow them, reply, get into the tagged-selfie meme culture. This is small audience but real engagement.
