# Twitter Strategy + Follow/Unfollow Recommendations — 2026-05-19

> Synthesis of a full audit of @9takesdotcom's profile, home feed (Following + For You), enneagram/MBTI/personality search, attachment-style community, psychology community, and DJ's current following list.

## TL;DR — the diagnosis

**You're not getting engagement because nobody is in the room.** This is not a content quality problem. It's three compounding distribution failures:

1. **The enneagram community is genuinely dead on X.** The `#enneagram` hashtag is so quiet that your own 2024 posts are the _top_ results for it. The active enneagram community lives on Instagram, YouTube, Substack, and TikTok — not X.
2. **You're following the wrong people.** Your 179 follows are dominated by celebrity creators (Tim Ferriss, Chris Williamson, Jay Shetty), political/finance noise (Saylor, BrianMcGinnisNC), and adjacent-but-non-engaging accounts. You are not in any of the rooms where psychology + personality discussion is actually happening.
3. **You post like an SEO writer, not a Twitter user.** Your tweets read like blog intros: declarative, link-out, multi-part. The formats winning in your _own algorithmic feed_ (proof of what your audience wants) are "According to psychology…" hot takes, "my therapist told me…" personal frames, satirical lists, and one-line declarations of identity ("my anxious avoidant attachment style is my worst enemy").

The numbers: **30 followers / 179 following / 4–33 views per post / 0 likes on most / 1 reply if you're lucky** after **5 years on the platform**. This profile reads to both the X algorithm AND to humans visiting your profile as "low signal, do not engage."

## The unique strategy: become "Enneagram-flavored Psychology Twitter"

Stop trying to build "Enneagram Twitter." It doesn't exist on this platform. Instead, **infiltrate Psychology Twitter, Attachment-Style Twitter, and MBTI K-pop/anime Twitter with enneagram-flavored content.**

### The three lanes

**Lane 1: "According to psychology…" enneagram drops** (mass reach)

- Use the universal psychology framing that wins. Examples:
  - _"According to psychology, the people who can't apologize aren't bad people. They're operating from one of these three protective patterns: …"_
  - _"My therapist asked me, 'what would you do if you were cured?' (Alfred Adler's question). Here's what 9 different personality types answer — and what each answer reveals."_
- Drop the word "enneagram" from the hook. Use it in the body or in the linked piece.
- Target one of these per week. Reply-bait format (asks question, invites people to self-identify).

**Lane 2: "Personality-types-in-X" hijacks** (engagement amplification)

- The Mar 29 "AI wars personality types" post got your highest views (32) for a reason: **it stapled enneagram onto something the algorithm already cares about.** Do this on purpose, weekly.
  - Personality types of: AI founders, Succession characters, Lakers starting 5, the 2026 GOP primary field, NBA Finals coaches, JuJutsu Kaisen cast (huge MBTI overlap — 873K views for one Jujutsu Kaisen typing tweet!), Severance, K-pop idols.
  - **Especially anime + K-pop**: MBTI gets 800K-1M views typing anime/K-pop. Be the enneagram answer to that demand. _"You think Jungkook is ISFP? In enneagram terms he's a 9w1 — here's why his self-described 'vibe' fits the integration arrow."_
- These tweets do not need to link to 9takes.com. The link card kills reach. Drop the URL in a reply or in your bio.

**Lane 3: "Type-as-identity" one-liners** (in-community engagement)

- The "my anxious avoidant attachment style is my worst enemy" format gets 10K views from a small account. Mirror it:
  - _"my type 3 brain wakes up at 5am with a to-do list i never agreed to"_
  - _"being a 5 means having 47 browser tabs and zero of them are the one i was actually working on"_
- Daily. Low-effort. Builds in-community signal. Brings followers from typology Twitter.

### What to stop doing

- **Stop dropping links into your own thread continuations.** Every "Doing a series on X. Next up:…" follow-up that had a link card died at 4–8 views. The first tweet was decent (32 views); the link-card reply killed it. **Link in the bio. Or post the link as a reply to comments asking where to read more.**
- **Stop using #enneagram as a primary distribution mechanism.** It's a graveyard. Use it occasionally for in-community discovery, never as your bet on reach.
- **Stop tweet-and-disappearing.** You post every 1–2 weeks. The algorithm needs daily signal to bucket you. Target 1 post + 5 replies per day.
- **Stop posting from the "9takes brand" voice.** Your best-performing tweet was "wrote this, felt cute, might delete later" — the human-DJ voice. The brand voice ("Doing a series on the personality types in tech.") sounds like a marketing department.

- [ ] Move 9takes.com link from in-tweet to bio. Stop putting URLs in main tweet body.

### Week 2 — Establish a reply cadence (this is the unlock)

- [ ] Reply to 5 mid-tier psychology/attachment accounts per day (Heidi Priebe, Adam Lane Smith, Brian Maierhofer, daatdarling, Pammy, etc.). Reply must be a _take_, not a "great post." Your Apr 4 reply to @newstart_2024 already got 17 views — more than most of your originals. **Scale that.**
- [ ] Track the accounts whose replies you do well in. Follow 3 of _their_ engaging followers each week. This builds a real community of like-minded mid-tier accounts.

### Week 3 — Format experiments (1 post/day)

Run each of these formats once. Track which gets >100 views:

1. "According to psychology, [type X people] do [behavior]. Here's why: [enneagram explanation]."
2. "[Anime character or K-pop idol] is typed MBTI [XXXX]. In enneagram terms that's [type]. Here's the tell: [evidence]."
3. "my [type X] brain just [self-deprecating behavior]" — type-as-identity one-liner.
4. Satirical list à la Brian Maierhofer: "Just heal your nervous system by enneagram type: 1- [absurd version] 2- [absurd version]…"
5. "How to deal with [enneagram type X]:" — Dr. Nicole LePera format.
6. "Reply with your type, I'll guess your [favorite trope / breakup style / podcast / app]" — engagement bait that builds replies.
7. "Two psychology questions Alfred Adler asked his patients in their first session. The 9 enneagram types each answer them differently. Here's how to read your answer: [thread]"

### Week 4 — Double down on what worked

- Identify which 2 of the 7 formats above broke 100 views and got 5+ replies. Make those your weekly pillar formats.
- Start a recurring weekly post (e.g., "Sunday Type Watch: this week's [show/event] from each of the 9 perspectives"). Repetition trains the algorithm.
- Reply to one viral psychology tweet per day with a type-flavored take (Anki-style daily). Goal: get into the replies of accounts with 100K+ views.
