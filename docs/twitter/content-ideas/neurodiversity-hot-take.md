```
Type 1s with high standards calling it OCD.

Both can be true. That's the complexity.

3/6
The problem isn't self-diagnosis.

The problem is stopping there.

A label explains WHAT you experience.
It doesn't explain WHY you became this way.
It doesn't tell you what to DO about it.

4/6
Here's what the Enneagram adds that TikTok can't:

ADHD + Type 7 = running from pain with new shiny things
ADHD + Type 5 = hyperfocus as emotional avoidance
ADHD + Type 1 = executive dysfunction that feels like moral failure

Same diagnosis. Different wound. Different fix.

5/6
The real question isn't "what's wrong with my brain?"

It's "what happened that made me this way?"

The diagnosis is the starting line.
The childhood wound is the map.
The pattern is the thing you can actually change.

6/6
If you collected a TikTok diagnosis and stopped there:

I get it. Labels feel like relief.

But you deserve more than a label. You deserve understanding.

Reply if you're ready to go deeper than the diagnosis.
```

---

## Why This Works

- **Attack:** Calls out shallow self-diagnosis trend
- **Hug:** Validates that real neurodivergence exists AND that seeking understanding is valid
- **Specificity:** Type-specific examples of how ND + Enneagram intersect
- **No dismissal:** Doesn't say "you're not really neurodivergent" - says "there's more to understand"
- **CTA:** Invites conversation, not just platform promotion
