## Strategic Frameworks for X Growth

**What this section is:** These frameworks help you create content that matches where your audience is in their journey. Most accounts fail not because their content is bad, but because it's delivered to the wrong audience at the wrong time.

**Where these come from:** Adapted from the Viral Coach Masterclass, a professional content coaching course focused on short-form video and social media growth.

> **Deep dive:** [`/docs/marketing/viral-coach-framework-reference.md`](../../marketing/viral-coach-framework-reference.md) ‚Äî Full course notes with detailed explanations, video timestamps, and 9takes-specific applications.

### 1. The Short-Form Funnel (ToF/MoF/BoF)

All content falls into three funnel stages. **Know which stage you're creating for:**

| Stage                | Purpose                            | Content Type                                       | % of Posts |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| **Top of Funnel**    | Reach new people, boost visibility | Broad appeal, observations, "9 takes" posts        | 50%        |
| **Middle of Funnel** | Build trust, showcase expertise    | Deep insights, patterns, mental health connections | 35%        |
| **Bottom of Funnel** | Convert to action                  | Direct CTAs, link to blog, newsletter, platform    | 15%        |

**Key insight:** Most accounts over-index on bottom-of-funnel. You need 80% give, 20% ask.

### 2. Trust Onion Framework üßÖ

Your audience places blame at different layers. **Match your content to where they are:**

| Layer                        | Who They Blame                       | Content Strategy                                        | Example Post                                          |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Outer (Circumstances)**    | Time, money, algorithms, dating apps | Practical tips, quick wins, validate external struggles | "Why dating apps make Type 6s more anxious"           |
| **Middle (Specific People)** | Boss, ex, parents, friends           | Strategic advice, pattern recognition                   | "Why your parents' type shaped your attachment style" |
| **Inner (Self)**             | Take personal responsibility         | Motivational, coaching content                          | "The lie you keep telling yourself (by type)"         |

**Critical insight:** Most new followers are at the OUTER layer. Your current content skips to the INNER layer. Add more "circumstances" content to meet people where they are.

### 3. EEO Continuum (Voice Calibration)

**Calibrate your voice based on audience trust level:**

| Voice           | Description                       | Best For                             | Example                            |
| --------------- | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------- |
| **Observation** | Publicly verifiable data/patterns | Top of funnel (new followers)        | "I noticed Type 5s tend to..."     |
| **Experience**  | Personal stories and results      | Middle of funnel                     | "In my experience with Type 5s..." |
| **Expert**      | Authoritative statements          | Bottom of funnel (established trust) | "Type 5s do X because..."          |

**Your current voice:** Heavy Expert. This works for people who already trust you.
**What to add:** More Observation and Experience voice for new followers.

### 4. Give-to-Ask Ratio

Track your "gives" (pure value) vs "asks" (CTAs).

| Ratio              | Effect                                         |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------- |
| 90% give / 10% ask | Builds maximum brand equity, slower conversion |
| 80% give / 20% ask | **Sweet spot for growth phase**                |
| 70% give / 30% ask | Acceptable for established accounts            |
| <70% give          | Damages brand equity, feels salesy             |

**Track weekly:** Count your posts. How many had a CTA vs pure value?

## Part 2.5: Writing Quality Checks

### Reading Level Check

> "A huge portion of the population has a reading level below 6th grade."

**Before posting any thread:**

1. Paste your text into [Hemingway Editor](https://hemingwayapp.com/)
2. Target Grade 6 or lower
3. Simplify complex sentences
4. Replace jargon with everyday words

**Why this matters:** Simpler content reaches more people. Complexity isn't intelligence‚Äîclarity is.

### Time to Value & Value to Time

| Metric            | Goal    | What It Means                                 |
| ----------------- | ------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| **Time to Value** | LOW üìâ  | Get to the point FAST. Hook in first 5 words. |
| **Value to Time** | HIGH üìà | Pack maximum insight into minimum words.      |

**The test:** Would someone bookmark this? If not, add more value or cut more fluff.

### Hook Quality Check

> "The first 5 seconds are crucial. If your hook is bad, the rest doesn't matter."

**Hook rules:**

1. ‚úÖ Specific beats generic ("Type 2s" beats "some people")
2. ‚úÖ Problem beats description ("Why you're stuck" beats "About Type 2")
3. ‚ùå Never beg ("Please read this" or "This is important")
4. ‚ùå Never generic ("Here's something interesting")

**Test your hook:** Does it work WITHOUT the rest of the post? If someone only saw the first line, would they stop scrolling?
