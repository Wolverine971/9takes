## Quick Reference: Key Frameworks for 9takes

These are the frameworks most relevant to 9takes content strategy. Each is explained in detail in the course notes below.

### 1. Content Funnel (ToF/MoF/BoF)

**What it is:** All content serves one of three stages in the audience journey.

| Stage                      | Purpose                           | For 9takes                                     |
| -------------------------- | --------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **Top of Funnel (ToF)**    | Attract new readers, broad appeal | "Why dating apps are harder for certain types" |
| **Middle of Funnel (MoF)** | Build trust, showcase expertise   | "How each type self-sabotages success"         |
| **Bottom of Funnel (BoF)** | Convert to action                 | Newsletter signups, product CTAs               |

**9takes gap:** Most content is MoF/BoF. Need more ToF (50% target).

‚Üí See: [The Short-Form Funnel](#the-short-form-funnel)

### 2. Trust Onion Framework üßÖ

**What it is:** Your audience places blame at different layers. Meet them where they are.

| Layer      | They Blame                              | Content Strategy                        |
| ---------- | --------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| **Outer**  | Circumstances (time, money, algorithms) | Validate external struggles, quick wins |
| **Middle** | Specific people (boss, ex, parents)     | Pattern recognition, strategic advice   |
| **Inner**  | Themselves                              | Motivational, coaching content          |

**9takes gap:** Content often skips to Inner layer. New readers are at Outer layer.

‚Üí See: [Trust Onion Framework](#trust-onion-framework-üßÖ)

### 3. EEO Continuum (Voice Calibration)

**What it is:** Calibrate your voice based on audience trust level.

| Voice           | Use When          | Example                            |
| --------------- | ----------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| **Observation** | New/cold audience | "Studies show Type 5s tend to..."  |
| **Experience**  | Warming audience  | "In my experience with Type 5s..." |
| **Expert**      | Trusted audience  | "Type 5s do this because..."       |

**9takes gap:** Default voice is Expert. Use more Observation/Experience for ToF content.

‚Üí See: [EEO Continuum](#eeo-continuum)

### 4. Content Flywheel

**What it is:** High-effort content should generate low-effort content, creating momentum.

```
Blog Post (high effort) ‚Üí X Thread ‚Üí Standalone Tweets ‚Üí Engagement ‚Üí New Blog Ideas
```

**9takes application:** Every blog post should generate 5-10 social posts within 48 hours.

‚Üí See: [Effortless Content Flywheel](#effortless-content-flywheel)

### 5. Give-to-Ask Ratio

**What it is:** Track how often you provide value vs. ask for action.

| Ratio              | Effect                          |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------- |
| 80% give / 20% ask | **Sweet spot for growth phase** |
| <70% give          | Damages brand equity            |

‚Üí See: [Build & Liquidate Brand Equity](#build--liquidate-brand-equity)

### 6. Repost Strategy

**What it is:** Repost your best-performing content after 4-6 weeks.

**Why:** New followers haven't seen old content. Good content is still good later.

‚Üí See: [Repost](#repost)

### 7. Famous Person Effect Warning

**What it is:** Don't copy strategies from accounts with 1M+ followers. Study accounts 10K-50K (slightly ahead of you).

‚Üí See: [Famous Person Effect](#famous-person-effect)

## Video Styles

‚è±Ô∏è **Duration:** 4:10

- **Experience Matters for Podcast Clips:** You need years of experience to make unscripted podcast clips work on social media.
- **Mock Interviews:** These can be useful for creating engaging content, but they require a lot of post-production work.
