# Worldbuilding Map - The Conflict Poster

**Archetype:** Someone with a live relationship, workplace, family, or friend situation they cannot read cleanly. They do not primarily want "Enneagram content." They want to know what is really going on and how other people would interpret the same facts.
**Status:** v0 draft, 2026-07-08. DJ review.
**Parent map:** [9TAKES_WORLDBUILDING_MAP.md](9TAKES_WORLDBUILDING_MAP.md).

## Their world in one paragraph

The conflict poster walks in carrying one situation that has started to feel like a personality test they are failing. They have already replayed the text, the tone, the look, the silence. Their default read feels obvious, which is exactly the problem. The magic moment is not advice; it is decentering. They write their take first, then the Chorus opens and they see eight other emotional logics beside their own. The villain is the internet comment section that turns their vulnerable situation into a verdict, a dunk, or a majority opinion. 9takes gives them a room where the first move is honesty and the reward is perspective.

## The 7 elements, from inside their world

### 01 - Named Concepts

- **The live situation** - the atomic prompt: "here is what happened."
- **The give-first unlock** - the cost of entry is their honest read before advice can influence them.
- **The Chorus** - the reveal of nine emotional reads.
- **The blind alarm** - the type-specific feeling they cannot easily generate: fear, shame, or anger in a register they do not understand.
- **Open source conflict resolution** - conflict becomes solvable when the nine reads are visible.

### 02 - Re-defined Terms

- **"Advice"** - not one answer; a spread of motives and risks.
- **"Being difficult"** - often an alarm system you do not share.
- **"Objective"** - not emotionless; it means seeing multiple emotional logics before acting.
- **"Winning the argument"** - not getting agreement; leaving with a better map.

### 03 - Weaknesses

- They want validation and perspective at the same time.
- They frame the other person as irrational because that is emotionally cheaper.
- They may write the situation to make themselves look reasonable.
- They may confuse the loudest or most comforting take with the truest one.

**Their comeback pattern:** "I thought they were being difficult. Then I saw the alarm I do not have."

### 04 - Characters & Settings

**Their cast:** spouse or partner, friend group, coworker, boss, sibling, parent, roommate, therapist, the brutally honest friend, the friend who always takes their side.

**Their settings:** the text thread, kitchen-table fight, post-meeting Slack, family group chat, awkward car ride, date that went sideways, coworker 1:1, the anonymous question page.

### 05 - Interests & Values

Fairness, repair, social accuracy, emotional safety, being understood without being flattered, practical next moves, and not being humiliated for asking.

### 06 - Lore & Legends

The founder origin belongs here: "I did not understand her fear, and she did not understand my anger." That is the conflict poster's exact pattern in founder-story form. Also relevant: Reddit threads reward early confidence, not slow truth.

### 07 - Common Enemy

**The verdict machine** - comment sections that turn a vulnerable situation into a ruling.

Henchmen: "break up immediately" advice, armchair diagnosis, side-taking friends, outrage replies, context collapse, and the urge to optimize the post for sympathy.

## How 9takes should show up for them

The product should feel like a protected room, not a forum. Their page needs a centered situation, a clear give-first lock, anonymity cues, a short private-feeling writing surface, then a reveal that seats their answer among the nine. After the reveal, the best CTA is not "share"; it is "ask the next situation" or "save what you noticed about yourself."
