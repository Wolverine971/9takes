# Worldbuilding Map - The Social Navigator

**Archetype:** A practical user who wants to read rooms better, date better, argue less badly, work with difficult people, and understand their own reactions. This is the tactical psychology / personality-maxing lane.
**Status:** v0 draft, 2026-07-08. DJ review.
**Parent map:** [9TAKES_WORLDBUILDING_MAP.md](9TAKES_WORLDBUILDING_MAP.md).

## Their world in one paragraph

The social navigator is not trying to become an Enneagram scholar. They are trying to stop losing moments they should have been able to read. A date goes cold, a coworker gets defensive, a friend overreacts, a partner worries, and generic advice says "communicate better." They want something sharper. 9takes gives them a map of the nine alarm systems so they can act with more precision and less judgment. The villain is vague self-help: soft language that sounds wise and changes nothing tonight.

## The 7 elements, from inside their world

### 01 - Named Concepts

- **Read the alarm** - identify whether fear, shame, or anger is driving the room.
- **Personality-maxing** - tactical self-knowledge that improves real interactions.
- **The 9-lens read** - before you act, ask how the other eight lenses might read the same moment.
- **Try this tonight** - every concept should cash out as a small action.
- **Social advantage through empathy** - the edge is understanding, not manipulation.

### 02 - Re-defined Terms

- **"EQ"** - not niceness; accurate emotional pattern recognition under pressure.
- **"Confidence"** - not force; knowing which approach the situation actually needs.
- **"Manipulation"** - using insight to control someone. 9takes should frame the opposite: using insight to stop misreading and repair faster.
- **"Communication"** - not more words; matching the other person's alarm system.

### 03 - Weaknesses

- They may want tactics without vulnerability.
- They may treat types as hacks instead of people.
- They may over-index on "social advantage" and under-index on repair.
- They may resist seeing their own pattern while analyzing everyone else's.

**Their comeback pattern:** "I stopped calling it irrational once I learned what alarm was firing."

### 04 - Characters & Settings

**Their cast:** date, partner, friend, boss, teammate, difficult client, anxious planner, perfectionist critic, direct challenger, conflict-avoidant peacemaker, the user's own defensive self.

**Their settings:** first date, group hang, tense meeting, sales call, family visit, post-fight conversation, DM thread, gym/car/coffee-shop debrief with a friend.

### 05 - Interests & Values

Tactical clarity, self-mastery, directness, competence, curiosity, fairness, no-fluff psychology, real-world experiments, and social courage.

### 06 - Lore & Legends

The founder's E8 journey is a useful cautionary tale: intensity can look like strength while still missing the room. The Enneagram became useful when it changed behavior in conflict, not when it produced a label.

### 07 - Common Enemy

**Vague advice that cannot survive contact with a real conversation.**

Henchmen: "just communicate," "be yourself," fluffy compatibility charts, manipulation-coded dating hacks, and personality stereotypes that make people less curious.

## How 9takes should show up for them

Content should use the pattern -> action -> micro-experiment rhythm. Product pages should make it easy to compare how each type reads the same social situation. Coaching/session copy can live here, but it should stay grounded in questions and take analysis rather than promising secret mind-reading.
