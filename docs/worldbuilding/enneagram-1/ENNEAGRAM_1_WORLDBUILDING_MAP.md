## 1. The World In One Paragraph

> **The Type 1 world is a place where everything arrives with a gap in it** - the gap between how things are and how they should be - and the gap feels personally assigned: mine to close. The One's magic system is the Inner Courtroom: a full-time tribunal that prosecutes every action, word, and impulse before the outside world gets the chance, because the deep equation says a mistake is not something you made but something you are. The villain is not imperfection; it is the Verdict - any voice, inner or outer, that grades a person like a work product and calls the grade deserved. The hero is the person who cares more than is reasonable to care, whose conscience is real even when the critic impersonates it. The growth arc is not lowering the bar. It is calling a recess: discovering that the court can adjourn, the work can still be excellent, and goodness was never actually the thing on trial.

## 3. Element-by-element Mapping

### 01 - Named Concepts

The portable ideas Type 1 content should teach.

**Recommended canon:**

- **The Inner Courtroom** - the core object, already live on the pillar page and in `enneagramTypeProfiles.ts`. The One serves as defendant, prosecutor, judge, and jury, and the trial never recesses on its own.
- **The Gap** - the perception system. Type 1 attention renders every scene as a diff between is and ought, and the diff feels personally assigned.
- **The Compression** - what happens to anger. Forbidden as evidence of badness, it gets pressed down and renamed (frustration, disappointment, concern), then leaks as criticism, rigidity, resentment, and self-righteousness. The tell: physically rigid and extremely polite.
- **The Resentment Ledger** - the bookkeeping structure of compressed anger. Doing more than asked, unpaid and unannounced, then billing silently in a currency nobody agreed to.
- **The Moving Goalpost** - the standard that shifts upward on approach, converting perfection into an admission price for rest, joy, and worth that never gets fully paid.
- **The Critic Is Not The Conscience** - the load-bearing distinction. The conscience holds values; the critic holds trials. Ones fear that firing the critic means losing their goodness. It does not; they were never the same voice.
- **The Recess** - the growth ritual. In a courtroom, a recess pauses the trial; in childhood, recess is play. The 1-to-7 growth line is both at once: adjournment you do not have to earn.
- **Serenity** - the classical virtue as destination, glossed for 9takes: standards without a trial. Peace with what is, precision about what could be.

**Bench:**

- "The Red Pen" (attention as markup).
- "Deliberate imperfection" (practice language, already in the blog).
- "The Nobody Died Log" (practice language, already in the blog).
- "Rigid and polite" (the anger tell, use inside Compression teaching).
- "The audit" (what happens to compliments and wins).
- "Keep the bar. Drop the trial." (compressed growth law variant.)

**Naming hierarchy:**

1. Core object: The Inner Courtroom.
2. Perception system: The Gap.
3. Mechanism: The Compression (with the Resentment Ledger as its bookkeeping).
4. Ritual: The Recess.
5. Growth law: Grade the work, not the soul.
6. Destination: Serenity.

### 02 - Re-defined Terms

The four core redefinitions to repeat across public content:

1. **Perfectionism** = a moral survival strategy, not high standards. The One is not chasing applause; anything less than right feels like evidence of personal badness. Being beyond criticism means being beyond condemnation.
2. **Criticism** = leaked self-judgment, and the One's dialect of care. The voice they aim at you is the quiet, edited version of the voice running at them since 7 AM. Both facts matter: the motive is love, the impact still lands.
3. **Anger** = compressed, not absent. It gets renamed on the way out - frustration, disappointment, concern - and what cannot be renamed becomes resentment, rigidity, and a very polite stiffness. The One is often the last to know they are angry.
4. **Rest** = maintenance, not a wage. The One treats rest and play as payment earned after perfection, and perfection never quite arrives. Redefinition: rest is part of the machine working, not a reward for the machine finishing.

**Supporting glossary, not additional public pillars:**

- **Should** = an inherited rule worth auditing, not an eternal law.
- **Good enough** = a completed good, not a moral compromise.
- **Integrity** = wholeness, not flawlessness.
- **Responsibility** = a load that can be shared without being betrayed.
- **Serenity** = acceptance with open eyes, not surrender of standards.

### 03 - Weaknesses

The relatable flaws that make Type 1 content land.

**Core weaknesses:**

- They correct the people they love and call it help, then feel wounded when it lands as rejection.
- They do more than was asked, tell no one, and bill for it later in resentment.
- They cannot start until conditions are right, and call the delay diligence.
- They audit compliments for the error that must be hiding inside.
- They mistake their taste for law and their preferences for principles.
- They defer joy until the work is done, and the work is never done.
- They believe self-punishment is a productivity system, and fear that self-compassion would make them sloppy.
- They keep the rules even when the rules hurt them, then resent the people who don't.
- Under enough pressure, the prosecutor becomes the martyr: "nobody understands how hard I try."

**Flagship comeback pattern:**

> "I spent years obeying a judge I never appointed, paying a fine that never went down, for a crime that was never named. Then one day I asked to see the charges. There weren't any."

**Best 9takes growth line:**

> You do not have to stop being good. You have to stop proving you are not bad.

**Confession lane:**

- "I said I was helping. I was also making sure nothing in my house could be criticized, including the people."
- "I called it high standards. It was also a way to never have to hear a verdict from anyone but me."
- "I kept a ledger of everything I did that nobody noticed. I never showed anyone the ledger. I just charged interest."

### 04 - Characters & Settings

Recurring people and places inside the Type 1 world.

**Characters:**

- **The Judge** - the internalized sentencing voice that sounds, from inside, like reason itself.
- **The Prosecutor** - the part that catches the mistake before the world can, because being caught first by yourself feels safer.
- **The Good Child** - the part that learned worth arrives through performance. A story shape, not a biography claim.
- **The Rule-Breaker Who Prospers** - the trigger figure: cuts corners, skips the line, thrives anyway. Walking proof the ledger is unfair.
- **The Sloppy Colleague** - the daily irritant whose "good enough" lands on the One's desk as more work.
- **The Worthy Peer** - the person whose standards are high enough that being seen by them feels safe instead of dangerous. (The Miyazaki-Takahata pattern.)
- **The Playmate** - the growth figure who interrupts seriousness with affection, and makes imperfection look survivable.
- **The Gracious Witness** - the person who sees the One's mistake happen and does not flinch, gasp, or file it away.
- **The Improver** - the healthy One: precise, warm, reliable, and finally off duty.

**Audience supporting cast:**

- partner who needs appreciation to arrive before advice
- child who reads correction as the family's love language and is deciding what that means
- direct report who hears "this could be better" as "you are failing"
- teammate who ships at 80% and cannot understand the One's 2 AM edits
- friend who wants the One at the party, not supervising the party
- coach or therapist who can tell the conscience from the critic and never says "just relax"

**Settings:**

- **The Courtroom** - the core setting; the trial that runs under every ordinary moment.
- **The Red-Pen Desk** - late-night markup of work that everyone else already approved.
- **The Kitchen After Guests Leave** - the One cleaning alone, ledger open, while everyone else laughs in the other room.
- **The Vacation That Can't Start** - the beach chair occupied by a body whose court is still in session.
- **The Workshop** - the redemption setting: craft, where high standards are joy instead of sentence. Same standards, no trial.
- **The Recess Yard** - the growth setting: play that does not need to be earned first.
- **The Podium / The Dissent** - the public setting where the One's anger becomes precision on behalf of a principle.

**Visual objects:**

- gavel resting on a child's report card
- red pen beside an unmarked page
- pressure cooker with a polished lid
- ledger of tiny debts
- clenched jaw relaxing
- a crooked picture frame left crooked on purpose
- courtroom doors open onto sunlight
- workshop bench with well-kept tools

### 06 - Lore & Legends

Recurring story shapes that make the type feel real. These are interpretive patterns, not required childhood events; a One does not need an identifiable critical parent or early burden to be a One, and the type must never be used to infer how someone was raised.

**Foundational Type 1 lore patterns:**

1. **The mistake that cost too much.** A remembered or symbolic moment when an ordinary error drew a disproportionate response, and error started to feel like identity.
2. **The praise for being good.** "So mature for your age." "Such a responsible kid." Worth arriving through performance, early and often.
3. **Anger punished, control rewarded.** The discovery that the household or classroom paid out for self-restraint and fined spontaneity.
4. **The unfairness never forgotten.** Watching wrongdoing go unpunished, or effort go unrewarded, and privately vowing to be the person who keeps the rules anyway.
5. **The years of invisible standard-keeping.** Doing the unasked work that keeps everything running, unnoticed, ledger accumulating.
6. **The resentment that finally spoke.** The cold comment, the disproportionate explosion, or the quiet withdrawal that surprised everyone, including the One.
7. **The first deliberate imperfection.** The crooked frame left crooked. Nobody died. The data point that starts the appeal.
8. **The recess.** The day play stopped needing to be earned, and the standards survived anyway.

**Internal 9takes typing hypotheses:**

Use as case-study candidates, not proof of Type 1 and not settled facts about a person. Any public claim needs a sourced profile showing repeated motivation across time.

- Hayao Miyazaki: the gap as personal assignment; completion as failure that beats stopping; the reformer's anger as caring beyond reason.
- Michelle Obama: anger converted into discipline so precise the world reads it as composure.
- Ruth Bader Ginsburg: rage encoded as a dissent collar and airtight prose; winning small and correct on purpose.
- Greta Thunberg: zeal with a 9 wing; the solitary strike; fury delivered through restraint.
- Mahatma Gandhi: self-purification as method; the reformer turned inward and outward at once.
- Martha Stewart / Anna Wintour: standards as empire; the cost and the moat.
- Steve Jobs, Tim Cook, Judge Judy, Emma Watson, James Clear, Noam Chomsky: on file as draft hypotheses with varying evidence quality.

**Intellectual lineage:**

- Riso-Hudson / Enneagram Institute: basic fear of corruption, superego separation, levels, arrows.
- Chestnut-Paes tradition: repressed anger, resentment, serenity, subtype countertype (sexual One as Zeal).
- The Narrative Enneagram: reaction formation, the Improver framing, somatic profile.
- Integrative9: strict perfectionist framing, "rigid and extremely polite," applied subtype language.
- Perfectionism research (Hewitt & Flett; Frost; Hill & Curran; Smith et al.): the strivings/concerns split as a general-psychology analogy - the harm tracks self-criticism, not standards.

### 07 - Common Enemy

The apex villain must not be standards, conscience, or the One's parents. Those framings either gut the type's gift or invent a biography.

**Recommended apex villain: the Verdict.**

The Verdict is the ruling - delivered by the inner judge, an outer authority, or a whole culture - that a person's worth is a case that can be decided, and that the latest mistake is admissible evidence of what you are.

> You are what your last mistake says you are.

**Two heads of the villain:**

1. **The impossible standard** - the internal head. Perfection as the admission price for rest, joy, love, and worth; a goalpost that moves on approach so the price is never fully paid.
2. **Graded love** - the external head. Any person, institution, or culture that scores humans like work products and calls the score deserved: report-card affection, performance-review friendship, purity spirals, shame dressed as holiness or excellence.

**Henchmen:**

- the moving goalpost
- earned rest
- should-inflation
- all-or-nothing scoring
- the compliment audit
- silent resentment billing
- criticism as the only dialect of love
- self-punishment sold as motivation
- purity policing
- "if you want it done right, do it yourself"

**Guardrails:**

- Do not make standards the enemy. The impossible standard is the enemy; high standards are the gift.
- Do not make conscience the enemy. The critic impersonates the conscience; the conscience survives the critic's firing.
- Do not make the One's family the villain. No invented childhoods, ever.
- Do not make loose, spontaneous people the heroes. The Seven is a direction of growth, not a superior species.
- Do not use OCD, OCPD, or anxiety as villain names or shorthand. Clinical terms are not Enneagram content.

## 4. The Type 1 Product/Content Flow

For Type 1 content, the ideal 9takes arc is:

1. **Recognition:** "You are not just picky. You are running a trial."
2. **Naming:** "That is the Inner Courtroom, and you are the defendant, the prosecutor, and the judge."
3. **Empathy:** "The critic was built to protect you: catch it first, and no one else can condemn you."
4. **Cost:** "The same court audits your wins, your rest, and the people you love."
5. **Growth:** "Call a recess. Grade the work, not the soul."
6. **Question:** "What mistake are you still serving a sentence for?"

## 5. Mirror Moment Template

**Bridge line:**

> Everyone carries a voice that says "not yet, not quite, not enough." The hard part is realizing the voice is not the same thing as your conscience.

**Question options:**

- What mistake are you still serving a sentence for?
- Whose voice does your inner critic borrow?
- What would you do today if nothing needed correcting first?
- If your worth weren't on trial, what would you still care about?

## 6. Decision Log

**Ratified by internal/external synthesis:**

- The Inner Courtroom stays the master concept; it is already product canon (`ctaHref: '#the-internal-courtroom'`) and no rival image is stronger.
- The perception system is the Gap (is vs. ought, personally assigned), sourced to the focus-of-attention teaching in all four schools and the Miyazaki draft's "their gap to close."
- Anger is framed as compressed, not absent; "resentment is anger with the lid held down" is the canonical line; "rigid and extremely polite" is the canonical tell.
- The growth ritual is the Recess, chosen for the courtroom-native double meaning (trial pause / play) that maps exactly onto the 1-to-7 arrow.
- The growth law is "grade the work, not the soul," backed by the perfectionism research split: strivings survive, self-punishment goes.
- The apex villain is the Verdict with two heads (impossible standard, graded love).
- Public repetition stays on four redefined terms: perfectionism, criticism, anger, rest.
- Childhood material appears only as story shapes with explicit hedges. The pillar blog must be brought into compliance.

**Open / taste decisions:**

- Should "the Recess" or "Grade the work, not the soul" become the flagship public phrase? Recommendation: Recess for product surfaces (it names an experience), Grade-the-work for social (it argues).
- Public label: keep "Perfectionist" in titles for search volume, or migrate toward "Reformer"? Current recommendation: keep both, Perfectionist for search surfaces, Reformer in product UI (already the case).
- Should the Resentment Ledger get its own question page, or live inside relationship content first?
- Whether "the Verdict" language is too heavy for social captions; test against softer variants ("the grade," "the report card").

## 7. Editorial Guardrails

- Write tendencies as tendencies: "may," "often," "when the pattern is active."
- Keep doctrine, 9takes interpretation, and psychology analogies visibly separate.
- No invented childhoods; story shapes only, hedged, with the not-required disclaimer.
- Never equate Type 1 with OCD, OCPD, or clinical perfectionism; never diagnose from type.
- Name both the protective intent of criticism and its real impact; motive does not erase harm.
- Do not tell Ones to relax, lower standards, or "let it go." Preserve agency: offer experiments, not commands.
- Arrow, wing, and subtype language is Enneagram doctrine with limited empirical support; hedge accordingly.
