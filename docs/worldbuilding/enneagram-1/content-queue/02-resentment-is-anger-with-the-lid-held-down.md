# Resentment Is Anger With The Lid Held Down

**Status:** queued
**Surface:** blog / carousel
**Source:** [ENNEAGRAM_1_WORLDVIEW_BIBLE.md](../ENNEAGRAM_1_WORLDVIEW_BIBLE.md)

## Hook

You're not angry. You're "frustrated." You're "disappointed." You're "fine." You've been fine for eleven years.

## Core idea

The Compression: for Ones, anger is evidence for the prosecution, so it gets renamed on the way out. What can't be renamed leaks (criticism, sighs, formality); what can't leak is stored (resentment). The tell is "rigid and extremely polite."

## Structure

1. The renaming habit: frustration, disappointment, concern.
2. The tell: sudden formality, clipped sentences, immaculate politeness.
3. The leak: correction with a temperature.
4. The storage: resentment as compound interest.
5. The move: notice the tell, ask what the anger knows, choose one direct sentence.

## Product tie-in

Pairs with a question page: "What are you calling 'frustration' that is actually anger?" Strong carousel: pressure-cooker image system.
