# The Critic Is Not The Conscience

**Status:** queued
**Surface:** blog / coaching prompt / carousel
**Source:** [ENNEAGRAM_1_WORLDVIEW_BIBLE.md](../ENNEAGRAM_1_WORLDVIEW_BIBLE.md)

## Hook

"If I silence my inner critic, who keeps me good?" Wrong question. They were never the same voice.

## Core idea

The load-bearing distinction in Type 1 growth: the conscience holds values and points at the world; the critic holds trials and points at the self. The test: conscience produces a next action ("apologize to her"); the critic produces a verdict ("you ruin things"). Firing the critic does not cost the One their goodness - the goodness had a different source all along.

## Structure

1. Why "silence your inner critic" advice bounces off Ones.
2. Two instruments sharing an office.
3. The sorting test: next action vs. verdict.
4. A week of sorting: label each self-criticism as instruction or sentence.
5. What survives is the conscience. It was enough the whole time.
