### Applied internal Type 2 docs

- `docs/content-research/overthinking-enneagram-research.md`
  - Type 2 loop: relationship analysis. "Did I say the wrong thing? Do they still like me? They didn't text back - what does that mean?"
  - Triggers: any social interaction, perceived distance, not being needed, signs of disapproval.
  - Internal experience: analyzing tone/word choice/expressions, replaying conversations, anticipating how to help, "monitoring relationship temperature."
  - Why they can't stop: connection = survival; analyzing feels like relationship threat-detection.
  - Interventions: trust that they ARE loved (not for what they do), response boundaries, sitting with relational uncertainty.

- `docs/content-research/manipulation-enneagram-outline.md`
  - Type 2 style: guilt and obligation. "After everything I've done for you..."
  - "Type 2 manipulation runs on the economy of obligation. They give, give, give - then collect the debt. Their generosity has strings attached, and guilt is the enforcement mechanism."
  - Tactics: guilt-tripping, martyrdom, unsolicited help (giving so you "owe"), conditional warmth.
  - Recognition: you feel indebted without asking; generosity comes with expectations; you feel guilty for having boundaries; they keep score.
  - Response language: "I appreciate the offer, but I didn't ask for help."

- `docs/content-research/red-flags-dating-outline.md`
  - What draws people in: warmth, attentiveness, anticipating needs before you knew them. "Finally, someone who cared."
  - Red flags: giving with strings attached (keeping score), guilt for not needing them enough.

- `src/blog/enneagram/first-impression-enneagram-playbook.md` (#type-2 anchor, load-bearing)
  - "They've been reading you since hello." The scan: openness - is there a person behind your role, warmth behind your competence, and will any warmth ever point back at them?
  - Inner monologue: "They keep rubbing their neck. Long day. Should I say something?" And underneath: "Do they notice what I'm doing here? Would they still be interested if I stopped?"
  - Pass the scan: receive their care like a gift; hold the spotlight one beat past their deflection; ask **"You seem like the one who takes care of everyone. Who takes care of you?"**
  - Fail it: cold professionalism ("competence without warmth is just a spreadsheet").
  - Misread: the attention feels personal; it's their default with everyone. "The warmth is real. The test is whether you offer any back."

The draft's key move: Rogers "built his whole method to defeat that ledger" - the refrain pointed outward ("you are loved, exactly as you are, and you do not have to earn it"), the giving ran "relentlessly outward, at the child, never back at the man on the screen." Demonstrates the type's gift lane at full power: the ledger defeated, not the caring.
- **Dolly Parton (2w3 hypothesis)** - the velvet glove with the iron fist: crying all night and still telling Elvis no on the publishing rights ("give to get" transcended by an intact self); the Imagination Library as "a wound given back as a gift"; the "Book Lady" title loved more than any Grammy. Demonstrates giving from overflow plus boundaries, in one person.

## External Follow-on Research

### Enneagram Institute (Riso-Hudson) - Type 2

Source: https://www.enneagraminstitute.com/type-2/

Useful findings:

- Type name: "The Helper," framed as "The Caring, Interpersonal Type: Generous, Demonstrative, People-Pleasing, and Possessive."
- Basic fear: "of being unwanted, unworthy of being loved."
- Basic desire: to feel loved.
- Key motivations: "want to be loved, to express their feelings for others, to be needed and appreciated, to get others to respond to them, to vindicate their claims about themselves."
- Passion: pride (with self-deception as its shadow partner).
- Stress arrow to Eight: "suddenly become aggressive and dominating." Growth arrow to Four: "become more self-nurturing and emotionally aware, like healthy Fours."
- Levels sketch: healthy Twos reach "deeply unselfish, humble, and altruistic" unconditional love; average Twos grow people-pleasing, intrusive, controlling, self-important, martyred; unhealthy Twos become manipulative, coercive, and "rationalize aggressive behavior while feeling victimized."
- Growth recommendations: self-care before helping, examine unconscious motives, ask what others actually need, stop highlighting good deeds, recognize love expressed in other dialects.

### Integrative9 - Type 2

Source: https://www.integrative9.com/enneagram/introduction/type-2/

Useful findings:

- Type name: "The Considerate Helper." Motivational need: to be liked and appreciated.
- Self-talk summary: "People depend on me for help. I must earn the love and appreciation of others by being there for them. I am only worthwhile if I am liked and needed."
- Gifts: warmth, anticipatory care, people-centered focus, generosity, confidence-boosting.
- Anger: Twos "can surprise people with their anger when they feel that people are taking advantage or undervaluing them"; pain and anger when "ignored, brushed aside or made to feel unappreciated."
- Pride mechanics: "feeling indispensable connects them to a sense of pride and self-worth"; less-healthy Twos appear "flattering and manipulative as they 'give to get.'"
- Subtypes:
  - Self-preservation 2 - "Privilege": **the countertype**; "cute" and childlike, charming but slightly shy; wants care while resisting dependency; withdraws when fearing rejection.
  - One-to-one/sexual 2 - "Enchant": seduction energy focused on key individuals; strong-willed, passionate, struggles with limits and boundaries.
  - Social 2 - "Ambition": intellectual seduction of groups; leadership roles; influence through competence and connection.

### CP Enneagram Academy (Chestnut-Paes) - Type 2

Source: https://cpenneagram.com/enneagram-type-two

Useful findings:

- Focus of attention: "relationships, gaining approval, and seducing others through helpfulness" to satisfy unmet needs indirectly.
- Emotional pattern: "Twos fear rejection and so they frequently repress their feelings in an effort to please others."
- Passion: pride as "a need for self-inflation... expressed as a false generosity in the service of seduction and self-elevation"; self-idealization alternating with self-criticism.
- Virtue: humility - accepting themselves authentically rather than maintaining an inflated helper image.
- Defense mechanism: **repression** - repressing emotions and disowning their own needs to maintain connection.
- Blind spots: "strategic helping"; not recognizing "supporting others can be a form of manipulation"; ignoring their own needs; denying dependency; minimizing "the need to be important."
- Subtypes:
  - Self-preservation Two - "Privilege" (**countertype**): a "youthful stance" that induces caretaking; wants special treatment "just for being who they are, not for what they give."
  - Social Two - "Ambition": leadership-oriented; cultivates "an image of being an influential, super-competent person worthy of admiration."
  - Sexual Two - "Seduction-Aggression": "classical seduction to attract a partner who will meet all their needs"; energetic, irresistible presence.

### The Narrative Enneagram - Type 2

Source: https://www.narrativeenneagram.org/enneagram-type/type-2-the-giver/

Useful findings:

- Type name: "The Giver." Core belief: "Givers believe you must give fully to others to be loved."
- Focus of attention: "others' needs, feelings and desires."
- Defense mechanism: repression - "suppressing 'unacceptable' feelings and converting them into a more acceptable form of emotional energy."
- Speaking style: "friendly, open, expressive, focused on others, and quick to support or give advice"; can land as "overly helpful, nagging, controlling or resentful."
- Somatic pattern: "full of energy and expressive in their upper bodies, but find it hard to stay grounded or sense their lower bodies"; "discharge anxiety or uncontained emotional energy through talking"; **"restrict their breathing while waiting for other people's responses."**
- Life lesson: "to develop the humility that comes from allowing yourself to be loved without being needed and to have needs of your own."
- First-person items: "I am preoccupied with the needs of others." "I take pride in giving and helping." "I sometimes feel that people take advantage of me." "I have a hard time expressing my own needs." "I can be manipulative and alter how I present myself to others."
- Relating tips: offer approval and appreciation, pay attention to THEIR needs, value warmth and partnership, "head off outbursts by addressing their dissatisfaction or resentment."

### Psychology Bridge: Communion vs. Unmitigated Communion

Use as supporting psychology analogies, not Enneagram doctrine. High scorers on these measures are not "Twos."

- **Fritz & Helgeson 1998, Journal of Personality and Social Psychology 75(1), 121-140** (https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9686454/): "Distinctions of unmitigated communion from communion: Self-neglect and overinvolvement with others." Defines **unmitigated communion** as "a focus on others to the exclusion of the self," distinct from **communion** (a positive caring orientation toward others). Across four studies: the two are correlated but distinct; unmitigated communion is uniquely tied to a negative self-view, turning to others for self-evaluative information, and psychological distress. Self-neglect and overinvolvement with others mediate the link to distress.
- **Helgeson & Fritz 1998, Personality and Social Psychology Review, "A Theory of Unmitigated Communion"** (https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/26d81209be1be1dbf3a5db17f19fab65988c6e9e): the theoretical statement; unmitigated communion involves outsourcing self-esteem to others' evaluations and difficulty receiving support.
- **Daily-diary follow-up work** (e.g., Journal of Research in Personality, https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S009265661500032X): links unmitigated communion to day-level interpersonal behaviors and lower well-being; Helgeson's CMU lab has extended the construct into health populations (cardiac, diabetes).
- **Practical copy implication (the Type 2 parallel to Type 1's strivings/concerns split):** the research lever is not "care less." Caring (communion) is not reliably harmful; the harm tracks the self-exclusion package - self-neglect, esteem outsourced to being needed, inability to receive. Type 2 content should therefore attack the self-erasure layer and leave the warmth standing. **"Keep the warmth. Drop the ledger."**
- **Guardrail:** do not equate unmitigated communion with Type 2, and do not use "codependency" as a clinical-sounding synonym for the type. The bridge is an analogy.

## Evidence And Interpretation Rules

1. **Enneagram doctrine:** fear/desire, passion (pride), virtue (humility), defense mechanism (repression), arrows, wings, and subtypes come from Enneagram teaching sources. Attribute disagreements rather than presenting one school as settled psychology.
2. **9takes synthesis:** the One-Way Mirror, the Invisible Ledger, the Shapeshift, the Hint Economy, the Window, and "keep the warmth, drop the ledger" are editorial concepts built from those sources. Useful metaphors, not validated constructs.
3. **Psychology bridges:** communion/unmitigated communion research describes general processes. It rhymes with Type 2 doctrine but does not validate the type.
4. **Biography:** public-person typings are hypotheses. A credible profile needs repeated evidence of motivation across contexts; warmth, generosity, or a helping profession alone is insufficient.
5. **Origin stories:** many Twos recognize a childhood story shape (helping earned warmth, needs cooled the room, praise for being the little helper). It illustrates the pattern when self-reported. It is not required, it is not causal proof, and the type must never be used to infer how someone was raised. This rule exists because the pre-2026 Type 2 blog violated it.
6. **Clinical separation:** Type 2 is not codependency, not dependent personality, not an attachment disorder. Never use clinical or pop-clinical labels as shorthand for the pattern, and never present type work as treatment.

## School Disagreements To Flag

- **Countertype: no disagreement, but note the contrast with Type 1.** Integrative9 and Chestnut-Paes both name the self-preservation Two ("Privilege") as the countertype - the Two who attracts care by being charming and subtly needful rather than by giving. Not every Two looks like a caretaker.
- **Sexual-subtype naming diverges:** "Enchant" (Integrative9) vs. "Seduction-Aggression" / the aggressive-seductive Two (Chestnut lineage). Use descriptive language rather than either brand name in public copy.
- **Type name diverges:** Helper (Riso-Hudson), Considerate Helper (Integrative9), Giver (Narrative/Palmer lineage), Befriender (some panels). 9takes canon: "The Helper" as archetype label (already in `enneagramTypeProfiles.ts`), with 2w1 "The Servant" and 2w3 "The Host" as wing aliases.
- **Passion language:** all schools say pride, but the emphasis differs - Riso-Hudson pairs it with self-deception; Chestnut-Naranjo emphasizes self-inflation and "false generosity"; the Narrative tradition emphasizes repression of needs. Attribute rather than harmonize.
- **Arrows are doctrine.** The 2-to-8 stress and 2-to-4 growth movements are Enneagram teaching; the 2021 systematic review found little empirical support for intertype movement. Keep the arrow hedge in public copy.

## Model Limitations

Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33332604/

- Hook et al. (2021), systematic review across 104 independent samples: mixed evidence for Enneagram reliability and validity; factor analyses typically yield fewer than nine factors; no clustering studies validating the nine-type structure; little support for secondary features such as wings and intertype movement; several studies report personal/spiritual growth benefits.
- Editorial implication: present Type 2 as an interpretive self-reflection framework, not settled clinical science. Applies with extra force to the 2-to-8 and 2-to-4 arrows.

## Synthesis

The shared pattern across internal and external sources:

1. Type 2 does not start from niceness. It starts from the working assumption that love must be earned, and that the currency is what you can do for people.
2. Attention organizes around other people's needs, feelings, and desires - rendered in high definition - while the Two's own needs stay dark. The One-Way Mirror is the right master image, and the dark room shows up verbatim in first-person accounts.
3. The pride is structural, not vain: being the one who needs nothing and provides everything is both the self-image and the defense. Needing openly would put the whole strategy at risk.
4. The giving keeps books. Unrequested help accrues obligation; guilt enforces it; nobody on the other side agreed to the debt. The ledger is invisible until collection day.
5. Anger is forbidden (it threatens connection), so it goes underground: hints, martyrdom, withdrawal, sudden disproportionate eruption on the 8 line, and a body that collapses the moment the caregiving stops.
6. The identity cost is real: after enough shapeshifting, "What do YOU want?" opens onto a blank.
7. The relational tragedy: Twos build relationships where they are needed but not known, then experience the invisibility they most feared.
8. Growth is not caring less. The research and the doctrine agree from different directions: the harm lives in the self-erasure layer (self-neglect, esteem outsourced to being needed, inability to receive), not the warmth layer. The mirror becomes a window when the Two lights their own side.
9. The Type 2 worldbuilding image should be a mirror that turns out to have been a window with the lights off, not a reformed martyr, a "learn to say no" listicle, or a warning that helpers are secretly manipulators.
