# The Invisible Ledger

**Status:** queued
**Surface:** relationship post / dating content
**Source:** [ENNEAGRAM_2_WORLDVIEW_BIBLE.md](../ENNEAGRAM_2_WORLDVIEW_BIBLE.md)

## Hook

They never asked for the favor. You billed them for it anyway.

## Core idea

When giving has to buy belonging, it keeps books. Unrequested help gets logged as obligation, guilt enforces the debt, and interest accrues silently until collection day. The trap is structural: nobody on the other side ever saw an invoice, so the collection lands as ambush, while the Two experiences the non-payment as betrayal. Distinct from the Type 1 Resentment Ledger: the One bills for unthanked corrections; the Two bills for unthanked care.

## Structure

1. The entries nobody signed (the check-ins, the casseroles, the absorbed overflow).
2. Guilt as the enforcement mechanism ("after everything I've done...").
3. Collection day.
4. Both sides of the unfairness.
5. The repair: say the need before you bill it.

Question page: "What's something you did for someone that they never noticed, and how long have you been carrying it?" Anchor for partner-facing Type 2 content (ask twice past "I'm fine").
