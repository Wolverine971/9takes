# Kept Is Not The Same As Loved

**Status:** queued
**Surface:** blog / social
**Source:** [ENNEAGRAM_2_WORLDVIEW_BIBLE.md](../ENNEAGRAM_2_WORLDVIEW_BIBLE.md)

## Hook

Being needed secures the role. It never quite secures the person.

## Core idea

The Type 2 strategy runs on a substitution: being needed as a reliable proxy for being loved. Indispensability does keep people around, which is why the strategy survives. But it keeps them around the function, not the person, so the more perfectly it works, the more invisible the Two becomes. The lighthouse problem: relied on, praised, and alone with the maintenance.

## Structure

1. The substitution (needed as proxy for loved) and why it made sense.
2. The evidence it seems to generate (people do stay).
3. What they're staying for (the role vs. the person).
4. The lighthouse image.
5. The test: who knows the version of you that needs things?

Redefinition anchor for all Type 2 content. Question page: "If you stopped being useful tomorrow, what do you believe would happen to your relationships?"
