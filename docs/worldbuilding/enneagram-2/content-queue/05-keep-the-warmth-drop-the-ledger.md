# Keep The Warmth, Drop The Ledger

**Status:** queued
**Surface:** growth explainer / work content
**Source:** [ENNEAGRAM_2_WORLDVIEW_BIBLE.md](../ENNEAGRAM_2_WORLDVIEW_BIBLE.md), research notes psychology bridge

## Hook

Nobody is asking you to care less. The research doesn't either.

## Core idea

Psychology's communion research splits caring into two ingredients: communion (a warm orientation toward others) and unmitigated communion (other-focus to the exclusion of the self, with self-esteem outsourced to being needed). They correlate, but only the second tracks distress, self-neglect, and the inability to receive (Fritz & Helgeson 1998, JPSP). The Type 2 parallel to the Type 1 strivings/concerns split: the warmth was never the problem; the self-erasure is. Growth costs less than Twos fear.

## Structure

1. The fear ("if I work on this, I'll become cold").
2. The two-ingredient split, plainly told.
3. What actually tracks the damage (self-exclusion, not caring).
4. The growth law: keep the warmth, drop the ledger.
5. One experiment: receive without repaying, once, this week.
