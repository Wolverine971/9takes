# The Three Helpers

**Status:** queued
**Surface:** subtype explainer
**Source:** [ENNEAGRAM_2_RESEARCH_NOTES.md](../ENNEAGRAM_2_RESEARCH_NOTES.md)

## Hook

One of the three kinds of Twos has never cooked you a casserole in their life.

## Core idea

The three instinctual Twos do not look alike, which is why many Twos reject the Helper label on sight. The self-preservation Two (the countertype) attracts care by being charming and subtly needful instead of by giving. The social Two helps whole rooms and builds influence through indispensability, often mistyped as 3. The one-to-one Two pours everything into chosen people and silently expects everything back, often mistyped as 4. Countertype guardrail: not every Two looks like a caretaker.

## Structure

1. Why the label bounces off a third of the type.
2. The self-preservation Two (the charming countertype).
3. The social Two (the ambitious room-tender).
4. The one-to-one Two (the devoted intensive).
5. The shared engine under all three (needed as the road to kept).
