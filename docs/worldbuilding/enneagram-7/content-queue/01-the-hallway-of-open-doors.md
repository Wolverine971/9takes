# The Hallway Of Open Doors

**Status:** queued
**Surface:** question page / carousel / Mirror Moment
**Source:** [ENNEAGRAM_7_WORLDBUILDING_MAP.md](../ENNEAGRAM_7_WORLDBUILDING_MAP.md)

## Hook

Where has keeping every door open turned your life into a hallway?

## Core Idea

Options can protect freedom. Permanent optionality can also prevent any choice from becoming a place to live. The issue is not having many doors; it is refusing to cross a threshold because the other doors would close.

## Structure

1. Why open doors feel safe.
2. The imagined loss inside every choice.
3. How comparison continues after choosing.
4. The cost: partial arrival, partial satisfaction, partial intimacy.
5. The experiment: choose one room for a defined period and fully enter it.

## Product Tie-in

Use the hook as the canonical Type 7 question page. Follow with: "Which door are you protecting, and what does it cost the room you are already in?"

## Guardrail

Do not imply that all options should close or that leaving a harmful situation is avoidance.
