# Enneagram 8 Audience Archetypes

Three audience maps for applying the Type 8 world to 9takes content and product surfaces. These archetypes describe the reader's job, not their Enneagram type.

## 1. The Armored Self-Recognizer

**Job:** Decide whether the Type 8 lens explains a recurring pattern in their own life without surrendering their complexity or agency.

**Arrives thinking:**

> People call me intense or controlling. They rarely ask what I am protecting or why I stopped trusting softer approaches.

**Needs:**

- recognition of protection before critique of impact
- direct language without alpha stereotypes
- permission to reject inaccurate parts
- growth framed as greater range, not becoming harmless
- experiments that preserve choice

**Resists:**

- invented trauma narratives
- commands to calm down, soften, or disclose
- strength being treated as pathology
- vulnerability being treated as unrestricted access

**Best doorway:** The Armor and the Power Map.

**Best question:** "What line needs to stay, and what wall no longer does?"

**Best CTA:** A private self-audit or give-first question before any social sharing.

**Success signal:** They can name both protective intent and actual impact without feeling erased by either.

## 2. The Person Across From The Eight

**Job:** Understand a partner, friend, leader, parent, or colleague without excusing intimidation, control, or harm.

**Arrives thinking:**

> I can see the care underneath the force, but I still need the force to stop landing on me.

**Needs:**

- distinction between motive and impact
- specific boundary language
- permission to stay calm without becoming compliant
- a model for direct repair
- clarity that Enneagram never excuses abuse

**Resists:**

- "They only act this way because they are hurt"
- advice to prove loyalty by tolerating more
- romanticizing domination as protection
- instructions to diagnose or manage the Eight

**Best doorway:** Power With versus Power Over.

**Best question:** "When does strength make you feel safe, and when does it make you feel controlled?"

**Best CTA:** Compare takes, name the behavior, and choose a boundary or repair request.

**Success signal:** They leave with language that is empathic, concrete, and protective of their own agency.

## 3. The Type 8 Analyst

**Job:** Write or evaluate a personality analysis without mistaking visible dominance for Type 8 motivation.

**Arrives thinking:**

> This person is forceful, independent, and confrontational. Is that actually Type 8, or only the public behavior I can see?

**Needs:**

- repeated evidence across contexts and time
- direct quotes about control, dependence, protection, and vulnerability
- countertyping against Types 1, 3, 6, and 7
- separation of fact, interpretation, and Enneagram doctrine
- evidence of both gift and cost

**Resists:**

- typing from leadership, anger, physique, politics, or gender presentation
- turning adversity into a neat origin story
- using diagnosis, addiction, or scandal as type evidence
- certainty about private motivation

**Best doorway:** The Power Map as a hypothesis to test.

**Best question:** "What repeated choice shows autonomy or protection mattered more than status, approval, certainty, or novelty?"

**Best CTA:** Open the evidence ledger, inspect counterarguments, then invite reader takes.

**Success signal:** The analysis remains persuasive even after every speculative sentence is labeled as interpretation.
