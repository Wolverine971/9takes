## Why This Roadmap Exists

`Think Again` and `How Minds Change` map directly to the 9takes mission:

- reduce polarization
- increase curiosity
- help people feel understood before they try to persuade
- make emotional context visible in disagreement

9takes already has strong pieces in this lane, but the framework is spread across posts. This roadmap organizes the cluster into one clear narrative.

## Gaps This Research Exposed

1. No single flagship page that states the full 9takes mind-change model end-to-end.
2. No explicit bridge from "change yourself" (`Think Again`) to "change conversations" (`How Minds Change`) to "change system design" (9takes product mechanics).
3. No practical script library for hard conversations (question prompts, sequencing, examples).
4. No reusable "belief shift stories" format showing how people actually updated views.
5. Limited explicit positioning language around "depolarization by design."

## Proposed Mind-Change Architecture for 9takes

### Layer 1: Personal Updating (Self)

- scientist mindset over preacher/prosecutor/politician mode
- confident humility
- joy of being wrong

### Layer 2: Relational Safety (Social)

- identity-safe dialogue
- motivational interviewing style questions
- ask "how" questions to reduce defensiveness

### Layer 3: Platform Design (System)

- give-first commenting to reduce priming and mimicry
- anonymous but contextual participation (type lens)
- one question, nine perspectives, visible common ground

## Core Question Prompt Bank (For /questions)

- What belief have you changed in the last 3 years, and what changed it?
- What issue do you disagree with your own "side" on?
- What emotion sits under your strongest political opinion?
- What evidence would make you update your view on this?
- What do people misunderstand most about your position?
- What is the strongest argument against your current view?
- What topic makes you feel, "if I'm wrong about this, who am I?"
- What would help you feel understood in this disagreement?
