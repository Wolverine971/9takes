## Core Thesis

Every generation's rebellion targets the dominant cultural authority. When political correctness and moral finger-wagging become the establishment, transgression flips to the right. Being edgy and politically incorrect becomes the new punk.

## Key Points to Cover

- **Generational rebellion lineage**: Boomers (rock/free love vs. conformity) → Gen X (punk/grunge) → Millennials (hipster irony) → Gen Z (politically incorrect edginess)
- **Angela Nagle's _Kill All Normies_**: The counterculture spirit migrated from left to right. Transgression as a value can be wielded by anyone.
- **Reactance theory (Jack Brehm)**: Telling people they can't do something makes them want to do it more. Shaming increases the behavior. Warning labels increase interest in restricted content — especially among people who already resist authority.
- **The hidden transcript**: Private Discord servers and group chats are significantly more extreme than public-facing content. The gap between public and private speech is growing.
- **Irony poisoning**: Saying something "ironically" enough times dissolves the ironic layer. Canadian Anti-Hate Network calls this "one of the main pathways to radicalization."
- **iShowSpeed / Kai Cenat as the authenticity counterweight**: Not all rebellion is dark — the demand for unpolished, unscripted, real content is the healthy version of the same impulse.
