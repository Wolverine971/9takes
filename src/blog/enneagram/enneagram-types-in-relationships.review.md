# Review Notes: Enneagram Types in Relationships

Extracted from `src/blog/enneagram/enneagram-types-in-relationships.md` page source on 2026-07-15.

## Reader feedback from 2026-03-20

### What is working

- The hook is great. The "we need to talk" scenario with different type reactions pulls you right in.
- The table at the top is super useful as a quick reference. I'd actually want to screenshot that and send it to friends.
- Each type section has a strong personality. The Type 5 "weekly schedule" bit and the Type 9 Thai food explosion are genuinely funny and feel real, not textbook-y.
- The writing voice is consistent and engaging throughout. Doesn't read like a Wikipedia entry, which is the trap most Enneagram content falls into.

### What feels overdone or duplicated

- The "tragic irony/tragic truth/tragic paradox/tragic miss/relationship tragedy" phrasing shows up in almost every section. By Type 5 I was mentally checking off "ah here comes the tragic something." Vary the framing. Some types could use "the real cost" or "what they don't see," or just drop the label entirely and let the insight land on its own.
- The day-of-the-week format (Monday, Tuesday, etc.) is used for both Type 5 and Type 6. It's clever the first time, repetitive the second. Change one of them.
- "It's exhausting" or "exhausting" appears for Type 2, Type 4, Type 6, and Type 8. Find different language for at least two of those.
- The "What breaks the pattern/cycle/loop" and "Partner tip" structure is identical for every type. By the end it feels like a template. Mix it up. Some types could get a "what your partner wishes you knew" or "the conversation you need to have" section instead.

### What is missing

- No real-world examples or stories. Even a one-line concrete moment would make the patterns feel less theoretical.
- Nothing about what healthy versions of each type look like in relationships. Paint the picture of a healthy Type 8 taking the armor off or a Type 5 opening up.
- The "Dance of Differences" section at the end re-summarizes what was already said. This would be a better spot for two or three common pairings with specific dynamics.
- No mention of attachment styles or how they intersect with Enneagram patterns.
- The growth edges at the bottom are good but feel rushed. Add context for how and when to practice each one.

### Minor notes

- "Read time: 20 minutes" feels closer to 12 to 15 minutes. Verify it.
- The emoji selection feels random. Some are objects and some are abstract.
- The links to the men's and women's dating guides in the intro feel forced. "These patterns apply across the board" undercuts the reason to click those gendered links.

### Overall

Strong piece. The voice carries it. The main opportunity is breaking out of the template feel in the back half and adding either real examples or healthy-type portraits to balance the "here's how you're broken" energy. The ending could hit harder with specific pairing dynamics instead of re-summarizing.
