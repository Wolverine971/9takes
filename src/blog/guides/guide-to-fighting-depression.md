<div class="bg-white p-8 rounded-xl shadow-md mb-8">
  <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-neutral-900 mb-6">Understanding the Depression Loop</h2>
  
  <p class="text-lg text-neutral-700 mb-6">Depression isn't random suffering—it follows predictable emotional patterns that we can learn to recognize and gently interrupt. When we understand these patterns, we create space for compassion and healing.</p>
  
  <div class="bg-neutral-100 p-6 rounded-lg mb-6">
    <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-neutral-800 mb-4">The Universal Pattern</h3>
    <div class="flex items-center space-x-4 text-center">
      <div class="bg-error-100 text-error-700 px-2 py-2 rounded-lg flex-1">Emotional Trigger</div>
      <span class="text-neutral-500">→</span>
      <div class="bg-warning-100 text-warning-700 px-2 py-2 rounded-lg flex-1">Overwhelm</div>
      <span class="text-neutral-500">→</span>
      <div class="bg-neutral-200 text-neutral-700 px-2 py-2 rounded-lg flex-1">Paralysis</div>
      <span class="text-neutral-500">→</span>
      <div class="bg-error-200 text-error-800 px-2 py-2 rounded-lg flex-1">Self-Criticism</div>
      <span class="text-neutral-500">→</span>
      <div class="bg-neutral-300 text-neutral-800 px-2 py-2 rounded-lg flex-1">Deeper Isolation</div>
    </div>
  </div>
  
  <p class="text-neutral-700">Each Enneagram type experiences this loop differently. Your personality influences which triggers affect you most deeply, what your inner critic sounds like, and which pathways toward healing feel most accessible.</p>
</div>

<div class="bg-warning-50 border border-warning-200 p-6 rounded-lg mb-8">
  <h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-warning-900 mb-4">Why Generic Advice Often Falls Short</h2>
  <p class="text-warning-800 mb-4">Well-meaning suggestions like "think positive" or "just get out more" can inadvertently minimize the specific emotional challenges each personality type faces:</p>
  
  <div class="grid md:grid-cols-3 gap-4">
    <div class="bg-white p-4 rounded-lg">
      <div class="font-semibold text-neutral-800 mb-2">Types 1, 3, 8</div>
      <div class="text-sm text-neutral-600">May interpret "just relax" as another standard they're failing to meet</div>
    </div>
    <div class="bg-white p-4 rounded-lg">
      <div class="font-semibold text-neutral-800 mb-2">Types 2, 6, 9</div>
      <div class="text-sm text-neutral-600">May feel guilty for needing help when told to "be more independent"</div>
    </div>
    <div class="bg-white p-4 rounded-lg">
      <div class="font-semibold text-neutral-800 mb-2">Types 4, 5, 7</div>
      <div class="text-sm text-neutral-600">May feel misunderstood when urged to "be more social"</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

        How Depression Appears
      </h4>
      <p class="text-neutral-700 mb-4">Paralyzed by the weight of everything being "wrong." The inner critic becomes relentless, creating a sense of failure despite extraordinary efforts to meet impossibly high standards.</p>
      
      <div class="bg-error-50 p-4 rounded-lg">
        <h5 class="font-semibold text-error-800 mb-2">Common Inner Dialogue</h5>
        <ul class="text-error-700 space-y-1 text-sm">
          <li>"I should be better than this"</li>
          <li>"Everyone can see I'm a failure"</li>
          <li>"If I can't do it perfectly, what's the point?"</li>
          <li>"I'm disappointing everyone who counts on me"</li>
          <li>"Nothing I do is ever good enough"</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    
    <div>
      <h4 class="text-lg font-semibold text-neutral-800 mb-4 flex items-center">
        <span class="w-3 h-3 bg-success-500 rounded-full mr-3"></span>
        Gentle Reframes & Steps Forward
      </h4>
      
      <div class="bg-success-50 p-4 rounded-lg mb-4">
        <h5 class="font-semibold text-success-800 mb-2">Compassionate Reframes</h5>
        <ul class="text-success-700 space-y-1 text-sm">
          <li>Progress honors your values more than perfection</li>
          <li>Mistakes are information, not character flaws</li>
          <li>"Good enough" often serves others beautifully</li>
          <li>Your standards are tools to help, not weapons to harm</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      
      <div class="bg-primary-50 p-4 rounded-lg">
        <h5 class="font-semibold text-primary-800 mb-2">Small, Caring Actions</h5>
        <ul class="text-primary-700 space-y-1 text-sm">
          <li>Set "minimum viable" daily goals (shower, one task, 10-minute walk)</li>
          <li>Practice the "B+ standard"—consciously do something imperfectly</li>
          <li>Write down three things you handled adequately today</li>
          <li>Ask: "What would I tell a dear friend in this situation?"</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
