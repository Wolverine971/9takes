> "I'm the worst guy in every room that I ever go in. Everything. I swear to you, this isn't even like a thing." — Benny Blanco, The Howard Stern Show, 2024

<p class="firstLetter">Partway through a 2025 interview, Benny Blanco was explaining that he cooks something different for every artist he records, and he decided to demonstrate. He asked the host his favorite food, learned it was his mom's chocolate chip pumpkin bread, and had a plan ready before the sentence finished: "I'd call your mother and I would beg her for the recipe before you came in, and then have it the next time you came in. That's what I would do for you." The mission statement had come seconds earlier: "I make every experience different for everyone" (Behind the Wall, 2025).</p>

The man plotting to cold-call a stranger's mother about bread ranked 16th on Billboard's 2025 list of the century's top Hot 100 songwriters, with 10 number-one hits as a writer through 2024 ([Billboard via Yahoo, 2025](https://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/music/articles/top-songwriters-21st-century-hot-173000758.html)). His credits run through [Rihanna](/personality-analysis/rihanna), [Katy Perry](/personality-analysis/katy-perry), Kesha, [Justin Bieber](/personality-analysis/justin-bieber), Maroon 5, and Sia. Interscope chairman John Janick announced their label deal by crediting him with "an almost divine ability to bring out the best in his collaborators" (Variety, 2017).

Benny's own scouting report sits at the top of this page. He records barefoot. He starts sessions with a meal. He refuses to rush anything: "I don't ever force the process. Ever." (Behind the Wall, 2025).

So the question worth sitting with: what is all that comfort for?

## What is Benny Blanco's personality type?

### Benny Blanco is an Enneagram Type 2

**Benny Blanco is a Type 2, the Enneagram's Helper.** The wiring: find what a person needs, supply it before they ask, and stake your own worth on being kept around. He runs that pattern on superstars for a living, and it made him the most wanted collaborator in pop.

He has explained the job himself many times, and the explanation never changes. "The thing that I think I can do is basically I'm a therapist. That's my job," he said on The Howard Stern Show in 2024. "You come in, I say, 'How you doing?' You might say, 'Oh, I'm good.' And then I dig at it and I try to find whatever the worst problem or insecurity is in your life, and then I have three hours to get it on a page so that you love it, I love it, and hopefully a bunch of other people like it, too."

Stern tested the claim on air. He role-played a superstar mid-session, and the exchange that followed is the shortest complete psychological profile Benny Blanco has ever given (The Howard Stern Show, 2024):

<div class="dialogue">
<p><b>Stern, playing the artist:</b> "I feel so ugly that no one could ever love me."</p>
<p><b>Blanco, instantly:</b> "I'd say, 'Me, too.'"</p>
</div>

That answer is songwriting technique. It is also true, and he knows it is true, and he said it anyway on the biggest radio show in America without a beat of hesitation.

This is the engine of an [Enneagram Type 2](/enneagram-corner/enneagram-type-2). Twos solve the problem of belonging by becoming the person the room cannot do without, and they learn to find the ache in any room fast, because tending it is how they stay. Most Twos run the pattern on a household. Benny scaled it into a production method, a house, a cookbook, a record label, and a marriage. The comfort he manufactures for everyone else is the exact substance he has never fully believed he deserves. Hence the instant *me, too*.

The typing makes him a statistical oddity. Of the 68 musicians profiled on 9takes, 26 type as Fours, the tortured-individualist lane. Only four type as Twos ([9takes corpus data](/corpus-stats)). It tracks: production is the one job in music where the deliverable is other people's best work.

An honest typing still has to take two rivals seriously. His anxiety, phobias, compulsions, and catastrophizing build a real case for Type 6, while his appetite, career pivots, and relentless cheer support Type 7. None of those traits is exclusive to one type. The case for Two rests elsewhere: his repeated interpersonal method is to find a need, disclose his own vulnerability, and become useful to the bond. The full argument, wings and all, lives in the rabbit hole below.

## Why Benny Blanco feeds pop stars before he records them

Watch the method under pressure. At [Ed Sheeran](/personality-analysis/Ed-Sheeran)'s writing camp for *Divide*, songwriter Julia Michaels locked herself in a room, panicking. "He went to the kitchen, grabbed ice and put it in my hands," Michaels told Billboard in 2018, describing a grounding technique straight out of a clinician's office. Then he sealed the room: "OK, you and me, we're gonna do this together. We're gonna be in this room, no cameras, just you and me, you got this." The session produced Sheeran's "Dive."

The Sheeran friendship itself began with a confession. The first email Benny ever sent him read, in full, "I have to take a s***." Sheeran wrote back: "Me too." Benny's conclusion: "Okay, now I know I'm going to be friends with this guy" (The Howard Stern Show, 2024).

The infrastructure is deliberate. "There's a bed in every studio I ever work in," he told Stern. "That's how I started making music. I was like in the bed. I'm always more comfortable. I don't like the big studio."

Sessions start late if you sleep late. They start with food. And there is a screening question older than any A&R checklist: "I don't work with people that I don't know personally. If I can't hang out with you, or you can't come over and meet my mom for dinner, what's the point?" (NME, 2021).

Inside the room, he demotes himself on purpose. "You're at the bench press," he explained in a 2025 Behind the Wall interview. "Some artists need you to spot them a little bit more, and some artists you just are there in case something happens. I just try to get in where I fit in." One rule governs everyone: "You walk through that door at the studio, you leave your ego at the door." He has released songs where a friend who happened to be crashing at his house walked away with a writing credit.

<div class="pull-quote">
"Benny is hands down the most tremendous human I know." — [Halsey](/personality-analysis/halsey), New York Times profile, 2018
</div>

Sometimes the music is almost incidental. Of his sessions with Juice WRLD, who died at 21: "We're making music, and then we went out to dinner. We talked about anxieties" (NME, 2021). The dinners became "Graduation," their 2019 single. His summary of the whole apparatus is one line from the Stern interview: "Food is the best bridge. It's the best lubricant in any room for conversation because it just brings people together."

The meal is the session. Everything after is transcription.

## Benny Blanco's childhood: "Shut up and go to school"

Benjamin Levin grew up in Reston, Virginia, the younger son of two New York transplants. His dad worked in the intimate-apparel business; his mom worked in assisted living. At five, he made his announcement: "I fell in love with music at such an early age that I was looking at my mom when I was 5 years old and I told her I'm going to be the biggest musician in the world" (The Howard Stern Show, 2024). Asked decades later who believed in him, his answer was one word: "Nobody."

His inner weather got the same reception. "I'd be like, 'Mom, I feel weird.' They'd be like, 'Shut up and go to school.'" Nobody in the house had language for what was happening in him, least of all Benny. "I didn't know what that stuff was. I always kept it to myself. I was like, oh, of course the room spins when you're about to take a test when you're 12."

One thing he kept to himself was a childhood compulsion. "When I was a kid, I used to have this thing where I had to go 'mmph,'" he told [Jake Shane](/personality-analysis/jake-shane) on <em>Therapuss</em> in 2025. "If I didn't do it, I thought my parents were going to die." Clinically, that describes thought-action fusion and supplies no Enneagram evidence. Psychologically, it gave a seven-year-old the impossible feeling that one private action stood between the people he loved and catastrophe. The later caretaking pattern requires separate adult evidence.

The kid nobody believed got busy being impossible to ignore. He messaged anyone with a pulse on MySpace at 13 and amassed 15,000 friends. He waited outside studios asking strangers, "Do you make music?" He cold-called Atlantic Records posing as Jay-Z's attorney to get his demos heard (Grantland, 2011).

He skipped school to DJ Brooklyn parties and slept in Penn Station, on park benches, and in the Times Square McDonald's. "My mom would have lost her mind if she knew I was sleeping on a park bench!" (InStyle, 2025).

The rap dream ended with a sentence he has repeated for years: "No one cares what a chubby Jewish kid from Virginia thinks" (New York Times, 2018). His conclusion: "I realized I'd be better behind the scenes." When your feelings register as noise and your face feels like a liability, you learn to bring something to the table instead. He got very, very good at tables.

## Benny Blanco at 21: a millionaire hiding in a hotel robe

At 18 he moved to Brooklyn and into the orbit of David "Disco D" Shayman, the producer who won him over with a dare: fill my studio with paying bookings for a weekend and the internship is yours. He filled it. Disco D deleted Benny's entire hard drive of beats and threw his CDs out a window as tough love. "He treated me like Mr. Miyagi, and I was like his grasshopper!" (Sound on Sound, 2012).

In January 2007, Disco D died by suicide. "He took his own life. I remember sitting in my bed and I was just crying," Benny told Jay Shetty in 2024. He nearly quit music, right before everything worked. The man who now runs every session like a wellness check lost his first mentor to pain nobody caught in time.

What worked next worked absurdly fast. Under Dr. Luke's wing, he helped build Katy Perry's "Teenage Dream" and "California Gurls"; Kesha's "TiK ToK," which he co-wrote, spent nine consecutive weeks at number one on the Hot 100 (Billboard). By 21, the scale and speed of the success had overwhelmed him.

In a 2024 Stern interview, Blanco described a mental breakdown at that age. Cognitive behavioral therapy, he said, "saved my life" and gave him a "toolbox" for interrupting anxious thoughts ([The Howard Stern Show, May 14, 2024](https://www.howardstern.com/show/2024/05/14/superstar-songwriter-record-producer-and-author-benny-blanco-makes-his-stern-show-debut/)). The distinction matters: therapy did not prove he was a Helper. It gave him a repeatable way to meet panic; his separate choice to bring that steadiness into sessions with artists is the interpersonal evidence.

The compulsions never fully left. He still does a small involuntary fake cough hundreds of times a day. Everything in his fridge faces forward. His verdict on all of it: "That's why I know how to produce, because I like order and I like bringing the chaos to order" (<em>Therapuss</em>, 2025).

When the Kesha and Dr. Luke litigation detonated the world he came up in, Benny offered one narrow statement: "I don't have any relationship to him anymore. I haven't had a relationship in many, many years. I think it's just a bad situation. And I feel terrible for what happened" (NPR, 2018). The public record does not support a broader claim that he emerged uniquely unscathed.

### Benny Blanco's Wing: 2w3

The 3 wing supplies everything the core type can't explain. Textbook Twos serve quietly and wait to be noticed; Benny announced at five that he'd be the biggest musician in the world, built a 15,000-friend MySpace network at 13, and impersonated Jay-Z's attorney to get heard. The wing shows in the packaging too: his label, both solo albums, and his 2026 podcast all carry the same brand name, Friends Keep Secrets, which is intimacy run like an enterprise. Five BMI Pop Songwriter of the Year awards (BMI) say the ambition is real. The 2w3 pattern, the Host, delivers care with showmanship: the party is for you, and he still tracks attendance. More on how <a href="/enneagram-corner/enneagram-wings-complete-guide">wings</a> shade a core type.

### Benny Blanco's Instinctual Subtype: Social 2 (with a one-to-one current)

Social Twos build influence by becoming essential to whole scenes, and that is his home base: the supper club with assigned dishes, the group threads with André 3000 and Ali Wong, the engineered seating ("You put Eric Andre next to my grandmother and they're going to have a great night"). His stated party metric is the group's craving for the next invitation. The one-to-one current runs underneath: sessions built on secrets, and a daily breakfast devotion to one person. A primer on <a href="/enneagram-corner/enneagram-instinctual-subtypes">instinctual subtypes</a> if the distinction is new.

### Stress and Growth Arrows

Under acute stress, Twos take on Eight behaviors, and his rare hard edges fit: mid-panic during a bad edible trip, the man who soothes everyone was reduced to shouting "Don't talk to me" at Kesha; at his own parties, he would rather leave his own house than tell guests to go home, until the night he starts issuing exit instructions. In growth, Twos move toward Four's emotional honesty. His 2018 pivot from anonymous producer to artist, the public crying, the grief candor about Juice WRLD, and an album literally titled *I Said I Love You First* all run that direction: the Helper finally saying his own feelings out loud instead of producing everyone else's.

### Counterarguments: Why Benny Blanco Might Not Be a Type 2

Seven is the loud rival. A cookbook, a food show, eating every two hours, and bounce-back optimism ("I bomb every day, but I wake up with amnesia the next day") all fit the Enthusiast. The evidence against it is not that Sevens cannot sit with pain. It is that Blanco's recurring reward seems to be another person's comfort and attachment, even when novelty is absent.

Six may be the stronger rival. He crossed the Atlantic by boat rather than board a plane, describes catastrophizing, seeks order, and has relied on durable therapeutic structure. Cooking does not disqualify Six. Type 2 leads only if the Stern "me, too," the meal-before-session ritual, the ice-in-hands response, and his insistence on personal trust are read as one belonging strategy: disclose, serve, become necessary. His refusal to lose and willingness to overrule labels belong just as clearly to the 3 wing. Confidence in the core typing: medium.

## The house menu: a night at Benny's, served in courses

The studio method never clocks out; it just changes clients. The public has known this since 2020, when *Matty and Benny Eat Out America*, his road-food show with chef Matty Matheson, started turning the private bridge into a public brand. The *Open Wide* cookbook followed in 2024. At home, the same machine runs on chosen family. His home life reconstructs from interviews like a tasting menu, and every course is doing quiet work.

**First course: the thread.** He runs a supper club of couples with assigned dishes coordinated over group text. His friend Dr. Dane flies in from Houston with suitcases full of meats and soups. SZA once ate an entire tray of his banana pudding (Yahoo, 2024). The photos of tonight's catfish go to the chat with Ali Wong and André 3000.

**Second course: the piano.** Before Billy Joel came over, Benny hired a piano player so live piano would simply be happening when Joel walked in, an invitation with no pressure attached. Joel didn't touch the instrument. Benny's postmortem: "Nothing happened" (The Howard Stern Show, 2024). The same house hosted SZA, Ed Sheeran, and [John Mayer](/personality-analysis/john-mayer) on a night when nobody played a single note. He builds the stage, then genuinely doesn't mind when no one uses it.

**Third course: the exits.** His cookbook <em>Open Wide</em> includes guest-ejection tactics for the host who cannot say "go home": tilt your head back and close your eyes; ask "Does anyone want to help clean up?"; announce an early morning. His nuclear option is perfect: "I leave my own house" (<em>Open Wide</em>, 2024). Pop's warmest host would rather exile himself than risk one guest feeling unwanted.

**Last course: the door policy.** Stern watched all this warmth and concluded, "You really enjoy people." Benny corrected him: "I enjoy the right people that we curate. I don't enjoy all people." His measure of a good party: "Have everyone come in and have a great time and wonder when they're going to be invited to the next one" (The Howard Stern Show, 2024). The generosity is real. So is the guest list, and so is the pull of the next invitation.

## Why the internet can't believe Selena Gomez married him

The one room he cannot curate is the internet. When [Selena Gomez](/personality-analysis/Selena-Gomez) went public with him, it treated the match as an error in the simulation. His inclusion in People's Sexiest Man Alive issue drew a viral 6.8-million-view post asking, "Did we run out of men or something?" At the 2025 Golden Globes, Nikki Glaser introduced him as the man who was there "because of the genie who granted him that wish." In February 2026, his podcast premiere featured his bare feet on the couch, and the verdicts ran to "divorce him." He answered on Jimmy Kimmel Live by taking his shoe off on air.

Buried in the Glaser story is the detail that matters. She texted him the joke in advance, asking permission. He ran it by Selena, then cleared it ([Us Weekly, 2025](https://www.usmagazine.com/entertainment/news/nikki-glaser-got-permission-from-benny-blanco-for-selena-gomez-joke/)). The most mocked man in pop approves the jokes at his own expense, personally, before air.

The incredulity also skips a decade of paperwork. Before he was the punchline boyfriend, he was her producer: they first worked together around the end of 2013, by his own recollection (Behind the Wall, 2025), and his credits run through "Same Old Love" in 2015, "Kill Em with Kindness" in 2016, and "I Can't Get Enough" in 2019. When they went public in December 2023, the internet saw a producer somehow landing one of the most famous women alive. The record shows a collaborator she had known since she was twenty-one, rather than a stranger who appeared in 2023.

He got to every one of those insults decades ago. The kid from Reston concluded his face disqualified him from the spotlight, and as an adult he narrates his body like a roast: "I believe that I'm actually so ugly and my features are so grotesque that I popped out the other side, and now I'm just like a hot guy" (Whiskey Ginger, 2024). He is "5'4" on a good day."

A Two who cannot believe he'd be chosen for himself makes the flaw into furniture, sets it out where everyone can touch it, and turns his own shame into the most relaxing thing in the room. Nobody straightens their posture around a barefoot man farting into a microphone. Confessing first is the same move as the Sheeran email, run in reverse: he supplies the "me too" before you need it.

The reflex runs so deep it works even mid-grief. He told Stern about the day his dog died in that same 2024 interview. Author's interpretive reconstruction:

<p class="inner-thought">The dog is gone. I'm crying on an actual sidewalk and a man is standing in front of me with his phone already out. He said he was sorry. He's still holding the phone. It costs me one second to be who he needs right now. Smile, Benny. You know this job.</p>

The photo exists: Benny Blanco, tears in his eyes, posing.

Her account of him points somewhere the mockery never looks. "Most of my anxiety right now is wanting him to speak because I feel like he's more talented than I am in this field," she told Interview Magazine in 2025. Her shorter version, to Vanity Fair: "He can't lie."

## From "I beat the system" to pop's most public marriage

The marriage put him in the one place he had spent his career avoiding: the middle of the room. In 2018, the year he finally stepped out as an artist, he told the New York Times: "I definitely don't want to be famous at all. I beat the system." He had spent twelve years as the anonymous author of inescapable songs, which suited him fine. Fame, when it came anyway, confirmed his suspicions: "That's like my worst nightmare. Doing live shows, anytime I had to do SNL or the AMAs I'm always just like, 'When's it over?' I just want to be home on my couch" (Hot Hits, 2025).

The critics never quite bought the artist project, and the smartest ones said why. Stereogum's Chris DeVille wrote in 2018 that few of his post-Luke productions were "instant classics" and read the solo pivot as "savvy optimization over creating something truly awesome." When <em>I Said I Love You First</em>, his 2025 album with Selena, hit No. 2 on the Billboard 200, it opened with 120,000 album-equivalent units, including 71,000 pure sales ([Luminate figures reported by Variety, 2025](https://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/selena-gomez-benny-blanco-said-163721740.html)). The Guardian's Alexis Petridis still gave it two stars and wrote that it "struggles to develop a clear identity of its own."

The critics are right about the shape and wrong about the failure. His records are dinner parties with track lists, rooms full of guests he arranged, because the room was always the instrument. His debut single "Eastside," with Halsey and Khalid, carried that format to No. 9 on the Hot 100 and No. 1 in the UK (Official Charts, 2018). "Lonely," the ballad he and Finneas built for Justin Bieber in 2020, is the therapist method pressed onto a record: the most famous child star of his generation singing about what fame did to him, over one spare piano figure, in barely two and a half minutes.

He said as much himself: "I'm not there to be the hero. I'm there to just help them along with the mission" (NME, 2021). The résumé backs the sideman thesis with a strange stat: 12 Grammy nominations, zero wins, five of them Album of the Year credits on other people's albums ([Recording Academy, 2026](https://www.grammy.com/artists/benny-blanco/11722/)).

Then the sideman threw the most public party of his life. He and Gomez announced their California marriage on September 27, 2025 ([Associated Press](https://apnews.com/article/selena-gomez-marries-benny-blanco-f101c3ba98e66f6050716b438057e9e7)). On February 24, 2026, <em>Friends Keep Secrets</em> launched as a weekly show filmed through more than 18 hidden cameras in Lil Dicky and Kristin Batalucco's home ([Sacks & Co.](https://sacksco.com/pr/friends_keep_secrets_podcast.html)).

The July update supplies the missing counterweight. Blanco announced <em>Hermoso</em>, a Spanish-language album due August 14 ([El País, July 13, 2026](https://elpais.com/icon/2026-07-13/benny-blanco-soy-el-ejemplo-de-que-puedes-lograr-lo-que-quieras-no-tenia-contactos-ni-millones.html)), with "Joven y salvaje" as its first glimpse ([Universal Music](https://www.universalmusic.ca/2026/07/10/benny-blanco-announces-forthcoming-studio-album-hermoso/)). Asked by El País to explain his career, he said there were probably more talented people, then supplied the missing counterweight to the Helper thesis: "I simply refuse to lose. If I fail, it doesn't matter; I get up ... and try again." That refusal is the part Type 2 alone cannot contain; the 3 wing and his own taste matter. His next book, <em>F*ck Failure</em>, is scheduled for September 15, 2026 ([Simon & Schuster](https://www.simonandschuster.co.uk/books/F%2Ack-Failure/benny-blanco/9781398567337)).

He will do anything to hold a room. He has still never learned to enjoy standing in front of one.

## "When's it going to get bad?"

Almost every morning, he wakes around six without an alarm and cooks Selena her favorite breakfast: chorizo and eggs in a tortilla, or a quesadilla, or over rice and beans, whatever shape she wants that day. "It's that time where we really get to be with each other," he told Stern. Her Valentine's Day was a rented movie theater with a nacho machine, fried pickles from a deep fryer he hauled in himself, and her grandmother consulted in advance.

He also told Stern the sentence he repeats to her: "I don't know a world where it could be better than this." Then, in the very next breath of the same 2024 Howard Stern Show conversation, the other one: "I always get scared cuz I'm like, when's it going to get bad?"

The childhood ritual belongs to the anxiety case. The adult belonging strategy appears at six in the morning: breakfast in whatever shape Selena wants that day. He can find the worst insecurity in any room within three hours, and he knows exactly what to cook for it. He has lived in his own room for thirty-eight years.

He is still cooking.
