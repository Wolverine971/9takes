> "Sometimes you have to throw your life down the drain just to see where you'll come out the other side." — Julia Fox, _Down the Drain_, 2023

<p class="firstLetter">In 2017, before <em>Uncut Gems</em>, before Kanye, before the memes, Julia Fox hung a downtown gallery with silk canvases she had painted in her own blood. She titled the show <em>R.I.P. Julia Fox</em>.</p>

She was twenty-seven and known to almost no one. She had already worked as a dominatrix, been banned from Bloomingdale's for life, and come back from a heroin overdose. She had been killing off versions of herself for years. This one just came with a gallery opening.

The world would decide, later, that it discovered her. A24 discovered her in 2019, cast as a jeweler's mistress opposite Adam Sandler. Kanye West discovered her in January 2022, and for six weeks she was the most photographed woman on the internet. Then the internet decided she was a joke: a hot girl with no thoughts, an accessory whose flat pronunciation of _Uncut Gems_ got turned into a punchline.

Every one of those stories casts Julia Fox as the object. Something looked at. Something discovered, dated, memed, discarded.

She has spent her whole life proving she is the one holding the brush.

## What is Julia Fox's personality type?

### Julia Fox is an Enneagram Type 4

Julia Fox is an Enneagram Type 4, the Individualist, with a strong Three wing (4w3) and a sexual-instinct intensity. Her whole life is one long project to become the one original nobody could throw away.

Three hundred actresses auditioned for the role that made her. Once Martin Scorsese and Adam Sandler came aboard _Uncut Gems_, the studio wanted a name, and Jennifer Lawrence, Scarlett Johansson, Lady Gaga, and Kim Kardashian were all floated. The part went to a non-actor who had known the director for a decade after a chance meeting at a SoHo café. As she told _Interview_ Magazine in 2019: "Josh always thought of me as a star, and I think he wanted to be the one to discover me."

That instinct, to be the undiscovered original no casting director could replace, is the engine of [Type 4, the Individualist](/enneagram-corner/enneagram-type-4). The Four's oldest terror is being ordinary, interchangeable, one more name on a call sheet, and its oldest hunger is to be seen and understood as exactly, unrepeatably herself. Fox has answered both the same way since she was a child: by making herself the one version of the story nobody could recast, in her own blood if that was the only paint she had.

Most Fours go quiet under that pressure. They withdraw into an inner world and cultivate their difference in private, which is why the typing surprises people who only know the tabloid version of Fox. Hers runs loud. A Three wing wants the uniqueness to be visible and celebrated, and a sexual-instinct intensity turns longing into performance instead of retreat. She is the Individualist who publishes.

In her 2023 memoir _Down the Drain_, she wrote the sentence that gives her away. She is, she says, "an artist in the role of a lifetime, playing Me." She is the subject of the artwork and the one who made it, and she needs you to know both. That is Type 4 in a single line: a self, authored and displayed with the byline left on.

## Why Julia Fox Grew Up Believing She Was Disposable

Fox was born in Milan in 1990 and left almost immediately. Until she was six she lived with her grandfather in Saronno while her mother finished college. Then she was sent across an ocean to a father in New York who, as she tells it in _Down the Drain_, either raged at her or forgot she existed. The memoir describes her left in the apartment for stretches without food.

<p class="inner-thought">You are a kid, the refrigerator is empty again, and the lesson arrives without drama: no one is coming. If you want to be kept, you will have to become something nobody can throw out.</p>

A child who gets left starts keeping score of what makes people stay. Being good was not enough and being quiet was not enough, so she reached for the only currency a forgotten kid can mint: becoming impossible to ignore. By fifteen she had moved into a drug dealer's apartment. By sixteen she was in a psychiatric ward after a suicide attempt, where a doctor wrote down borderline personality disorder. By seventeen she had overdosed on heroin and survived it.

The thread from that empty kitchen to the woman in the bloodstained Chanel is short and straight. Every reinvention since answers the same childhood question. Prove you are worth keeping. Build a version of yourself so specific that discarding it would feel like a crime.

## R.I.P. Julia Fox: The Art Show She Threw Before Anyone Was Watching

Before the world knew her name, Fox already had a body of work, and the medium was mostly her own damage. In 2015 she self-published a book of photographs called _Symptomatic of a Relationship Gone Sour_. In 2016 came one titled _PTSD_. In 2017, the blood canvases and the funeral show. She was building a career out of the exact material most people spend a lifetime burying.

Then the 2023 memoir, _Down the Drain_, a national bestseller made from the same private wreckage. She did not clean the story up for the reader. She handed it over wet.

Critics caught the doubleness. _Time_ called her "one of her generation's most authentic storytellers." A Slate reviewer heard two voices running at once through the book, one insisting she does not care what anyone thinks and one that plainly cares enormously. Both readings are correct, and the friction between them is the entire point. For a Four, sincerity and performance run on the same muscle. The most honest thing Fox can do is stage her honesty and put her name on it, the way [Frida Kahlo](/personality-analysis/frida-kahlo) turned a shattered spine into a wall of self-portraits nobody else could have painted.

## Why Everyone Thinks Kanye Discovered Julia Fox

For six weeks in early 2022, Julia Fox was everywhere on [Kanye West](/personality-analysis/kanye)'s arm. He filled a hotel suite with racks of clothes for a second date. He turned a warehouse into a restaurant for her friends. The public read it as a powerful man building a nobody into a somebody, and when she called the film that launched her "Uncut Gems" in that flattened downtown drawl, the internet turned it into the "Uncah Jams" meme and filed her under vibes with no thoughts.

Then look at the receipts. On _Call Her Daddy_ in February 2022, asked about the dinner with Madonna that everyone treated as proof Kanye had walked her into rooms she did not belong in, Fox corrected the record.

<div class="pull-quote">"Well, actually, I set up that dinner and I invited Kanye."</div>

She had narrated the romance herself before the tabloids got to it, in an _Interview_ magazine article she wrote about their second date. She was authoring the story of her own objectification while everyone assumed she was simply inside it. Even the origin myth follows the pattern. "Vine was this keyhole into her wild world. I was totally infatuated," Josh Safdie said of finding her online years earlier. She had already built the world. He climbed through the window.

It is easy to mock all of it as a woman chasing fame through a famous man. Sit with where the hunger comes from. A person who was left alone in an empty apartment does not experience a partner reorganizing a hotel around her as a red flag. She experiences it as being chosen, at last, completely. "It's cool to see myself through his eyes," she said on that same podcast, and then, more quietly, "I have my insecurities that no one else sees." The girl who learned she was disposable found a man who treated her as the opposite and said yes with her whole body. Understanding that costs nothing and excuses nothing. It only asks you to see why she needed it to be real.

Even the looks were hers. No stylist built them; she did, from whatever was around: a dress cut from an old beach towel with safety pins and scissors, body jewelry pressed from fallen leaves and resin, a crop top and boots covered in real condoms, each one filmed and posted as "end-of-the-world fashion." The images the world screenshots and calls iconic are ones she designed and captioned in her own apartment. The muse builds the muse.

By 2024 the culture had caught up to her instead of the other way around. [Charli XCX](/personality-analysis/charli-xcx) built a line of her album _Brat_ around her: "I'm everywhere, I'm so Julia." She wrote the song, she explained, "basically about how you started every trend of 2022." The woman filed under muse had quietly become the reference.

Every entry is the same move. The world hands her a verdict about who she is, and she claims it before the verdict can define her. For a kid who once had no control over whether she got fed, owning the story is the only safety she has ever trusted.

## Why Julia Fox Swore Off Men

In 2022 Fox stopped dating men and did not start again. By 2024 she posted it plainly: "2.5 years of celibacy and never been better." She frames it as reclaiming authority over her own body after Roe fell, "a way that I can take back the control." In an _Allure_ profile in 2025 she landed on pansexual, adding that she is "a vibes person" who can be pulled in by a man's mind while feeling nothing physical.

Set the choice against where she came from and it reads as the healthiest move she has ever made. The teenage Fox vanished completely inside relationships: the drug dealer at fifteen, the sugar daddy, the whirlwind where she remade her entire look because, in her words, "if I'm going to be seen with you, I need to step it up a little bit." Under stress a Four abandons themselves into another person and calls it love.

Two things pulled her the other way, and neither was a man. In 2019, the year _Uncut Gems_ made her famous, her best friend Gianna died of an overdose. They had met at an AA meeting and gotten sober beside each other, and in the memoir Fox describes consulting a medium to reach her afterward. This is the one wreckage she has never been able to make into anything. The woman who can title a bestseller after the worst thing anyone ever said to her, who can take a fabricated headline and wear it as a badge, has no move for this. There is no artifact to build, no verdict to reclaim, no version of Gianna's death she gets to author. The frame that runs the rest of her life fails here, and she knows it. Some of the grief went into staying clean. The rest of it just stays.

Then the baby. After her son Valentino was born in 2021, Fox came through postpartum depression and named him her "number one focus." Grief gave her a reason to stay sober and Valentino gave her something to stay sober for. Sobriety, celibacy, the discipline of finishing a book and building a slate of films: this is a Four reaching for structure instead of melancholy, which is the healthiest version of the type there is. She stopped waiting to be chosen and started doing the choosing herself.

### Julia Fox's Wing: 4w3

The Three wing is why Fox never reads like the classic Individualist. A pure Four cultivates uniqueness in private and often lets the work die in a notebook. Fox finishes and ships. A memoir, a fashion line, an E! series, film roles, a podcast, a debut single that _Variety_ cheerfully put on its worst-songs list and that she promoted anyway. The 4w3 "Aristocrat" wants to be seen as singular _and_ successful, channeling emotional intensity into visible output. Every raw thing she has lived becomes a product with her name on the spine. That is the Three wing handing the Four a distribution deal. She links her uniqueness to <a href="/enneagram-corner/enneagram-wings-complete-guide">the wing that makes it public</a>.

### Julia Fox's Instinctual Subtype: Sexual (sx), Social (so) Secondary

The sexual Four is the outlier of the type, the one who externalizes longing instead of retreating into it, competitive and shameless where other Fours are melancholic and reclusive. It maps onto Fox almost exactly: the intense romantic entanglements, the willingness to expose everything, the sense of being the exception in any room. Her strong social instinct shows up in how she operates through scenes and movements, the downtown art world, the trend cycle she once ran single-handedly. She belongs by being the most visible person in the subculture. See <a href="/enneagram-corner/enneagram-instinctual-subtypes">instinctual subtypes</a> for why the sx Four so often gets mistaken for a Three or an Eight.

### Stress and Growth Arrows

Under stress, a Four disintegrates toward Type 2, losing themselves in someone else and over-giving to keep from being left. You can watch it in every relationship of her teens and twenties, where she reshapes herself around whoever is looking. In growth, the Four integrates toward Type 1, trading melancholy for discipline and structure. Fox's sobriety, her celibacy-as-principle, her steady output as a mother and producer are textbook One energy: the artist who stops agonizing and builds. She is currently cycling toward the healthy line, and the memoir is the hinge.

### Counterarguments: Why Julia Fox Might Not Be Type 4

The strongest alternate case is Type 3. She reinvents relentlessly, manages her image with surgical care, and has said outright she has "earned" her place at the table. But a Three optimizes the self for admiration and hides the failures that would undercut the brand. Fox does the reverse: she foregrounds the sex work, the arrests, the overdoses, the psychiatric diagnoses, and insists on their authenticity as the actual product. Some readers also float sexual Eight, given the dominatrix past, the celibacy-as-power, and the confrontational fashion. Eight fits the toughness but misses the ache. Eights armor against vulnerability, while Fox exhibits hers on silk canvases and book jackets. If her art were ever mainly about winning rather than about being unmistakably herself, the Type 3 reading would win. It never quite is.
