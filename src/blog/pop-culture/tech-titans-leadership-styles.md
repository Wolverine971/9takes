<QuickAnswer question="How do different Enneagram types lead tech companies?">
Type 1s (Jobs, Cook) lead through moral standards and perfectionism. Type 3s (Jensen Huang) lead through achievement and adaptability. Type 4s (Altman) lead through vision-casting and narrative. Type 5s (Musk, Zuckerberg, Nadella, Gates) lead through systems-thinking and intellectual mastery. Type 6s (Thiel) lead through contrarian threat-detection. Type 8s (Bezos) lead through confrontation and dominance. Type 9s (Pichai) lead through consensus and steady diplomacy. The type doesn't determine success—it determines HOW they succeed and what blind spots they carry.
</QuickAnswer>

<p class="firstLetter">Silicon Valley believes leadership is a skill you can learn from a book. Read the right management frameworks, hire the right coach, attend the right conference. But watch how actual tech leaders operate and a pattern emerges that no framework explains.</p>

The same personality types keep showing up. And they lead in predictably different ways.

This isn't a coincidence. It's the Enneagram.

## Questions This Blog Will Answer

- **Why is Silicon Valley dominated by 5s?** Zuckerberg, Musk, Nadella, Gates, Dorsey, Amodei. The most common tech CEO type is the one most likely to retreat from human interaction. What does it mean that the people building the future are the ones most detached from human emotion?
- **Why do Type 1 leaders create obsessive cultures?** Jobs made people cry in hallways. Cook runs Apple like a Swiss watch. The 1's need for perfection either inspires or destroys—and sometimes both at once.
- **Why do 8 leaders burn through employees?** Bezos, Kalanick, Hastings. The 8's leadership style is confrontational by design. They test everyone. Some thrive. Most leave. Is that selection or destruction?
- **Why are 9s underrepresented as founders but overrepresented as successors?** Pichai didn't found Google. He inherited it. Why do boards consistently replace visionary founders with consensus-builders?
- **Can you predict a company's culture from its CEO's Enneagram type?** Apple's perfectionism (1). Meta's data-obsession (5). Amazon's intensity (8). Google's diplomacy (9). The CEO's type becomes the company's operating system.
- **Which leadership type actually produces the best outcomes?** Trick question. Each type optimizes for different things. But understanding those optimizations reveals which leaders are suited for which challenges.

## Type 1 Leaders: The Standard-Setters

## Type 4 Leaders: The Vision-Casters

- Few tech leaders are 4s: The tech industry selects for 5s and 3s—analytical and achievement types. 4s are rare in tech leadership because the industry values systems and metrics over narrative and meaning. Altman is the exception, and his exceptionality is itself a very Type 4 quality.

## Type 6 Leaders: The Contrarian Threat-Detectors

### Peter Thiel and David Sacks

<!-- TODO: Type 6 leadership analysis:

- 6 leadership is about threat detection and loyalty networks. Thiel doesn't invest based on returns alone—he invests based on whether he trusts the founder AND whether the market is threatening to underserve a critical need. Every Founders Fund bet is a calculated response to a perceived threat.
- The 6's superpower: seeing around corners. Thiel's "Zero to One" thesis—that the most valuable companies do something no one else does—is a 6's threat-aware worldview turned into investment philosophy. While everyone else is building incrementally, the 6 asks "what is everyone MISSING?"
- The 6's blindspot: paranoia can become self-fulfilling. When you see threats everywhere, you build defensive structures that create the adversarial dynamics you feared. Thiel's secretive, confrontational approach has made him enemies—which confirms his belief that the world is hostile.
- Counterphobic vs phobic: Both Thiel and Sacks are counterphobic 6s—they run TOWARD threats rather than away from them. This makes them look like 8s (confrontational, dominant) but the motivation is different. 8s confront for control. 6s confront to neutralize perceived threats.

## Rabbit Holes Worth Exploring

- **The Spouse Test**: Do tech CEOs' partners reveal type dynamics? Priscilla Chan (Zuckerberg), Lauren Sanchez (Bezos), Grimes (Musk). 5s often partner with emotionally expressive types. 8s often partner with high-status types. The partner reveals what the CEO can't express.
- **Board Composition by Type**: If CEOs are 5s and 8s, what types sit on their boards? The 1s and 6s on OpenAI's board clashed with the 4 CEO. Is every board drama a personality type collision?
- **The Founder-CEO Transition Problem**: Twitter replaced a 5 (Dorsey) with a series of non-founders, then sold to another 5 (Musk). Apple replaced a 1 with a 1. Microsoft replaced a 5 with a 5. Google replaced founders with a 9. Which transition pattern works best?
- **Type and Product Design Philosophy**: Can you predict a product's design language from the CEO's type? Apple's perfectionism (1). Meta's data-centrism (5). Amazon's ruthless efficiency (8). Google's soft edges (9). The CEO's psychology becomes the user experience.
