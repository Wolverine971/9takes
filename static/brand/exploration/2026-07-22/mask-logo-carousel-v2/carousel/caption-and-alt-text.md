# Instagram carousel package

## Suggested caption

You’ve heard of the seven deadly sins. The Enneagram names nine recurring passions.

Seven overlap: anger, pride, envy, greed, gluttony, lust, and sloth. The two additions are deceit and fear.

In Enneagram language, a passion is not a verdict. It is the automatic emotional pattern that takes over. A virtue is the quality that loosens its grip and creates another choice.

1 — Anger → Serenity  
2 — Pride → Humility  
3 — Deceit → Truthfulness  
4 — Envy → Equanimity  
5 — Avarice / Greed → Non-attachment  
6 — Fear → Courage  
7 — Gluttony → Sobriety  
8 — Lust → Innocence  
9 — Sloth → Action

Which passion has the strongest grip on you—and which virtue would loosen it?

One situation, 9 ways to see it.

## Framework note

This carousel uses traditional passions-and-virtues terminology. Enneagram schools differ, and the framework is offered as a reflective language rather than a clinical assessment.
