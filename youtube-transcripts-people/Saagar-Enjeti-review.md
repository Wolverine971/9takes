## Claims that are _directionally plausible_ but need tighter scope/wording

### 1) “3% of users generate nearly 50% of revenue”

- **Your claim:** “problem gamblers who make up 3% of users but generate nearly 50% of revenue.”
- **What’s supportable:** A Connecticut state impact study found **~1.8% problem gamblers accounted for ~51% of sports betting revenue** in CT. ([CT Mirror][9])
  ✅ **Fix:** Reframe as **state-specific** evidence, not a universal industry law.
  Suggested rewrite:

> “In Connecticut’s state impact study, **problem gamblers (~1.8% of residents)** accounted for about **half of sports betting revenue**.”

### 2) “Bankruptcies spiked up to 30% in states with mobile betting”

- Evidence exists for **~25–30% higher bankruptcy risk / filings** after online sports betting legalization (as reported/compiled by research summaries). ([Institute for Family Studies][10])
  ✅ **Fix:** avoid “spiked” (implies a clean measured jump everywhere) and state it as an estimate from studies.
  Suggested rewrite:

> “Some research estimates **bankruptcy risk rises ~25–30%** a few years after online betting legalization.”

### 3) “Domestic violence surged after lost bets”

- There is research connecting **sports outcomes** and IPV increases (and that effect may be **amplified** where betting is legal), but your phrasing is too categorical. ([opb][11])
  ✅ **Fix:** make it conditional and cite the specific mechanism (unexpected loss → emotional cue → IPV; amplified by legalized betting).
