### The Lonely Chapter of Personal Growth

**Chris:** I think of the gap from where people are in a place that they don't want to be until they get to a place that they do as a lonely chapter. Everybody that has got from a place where they don't want to be to one where they are -- there's a point where they're so different that they can't resonate with their old set of friends, but they're not yet sufficiently developed that they've created their new set of friends.

They make little changes -- they'll cut their hair or lose 5 pounds or switch from one company to another. If you want to go from where you are to where you want to be, you're going to have to do something that makes you more different, more weird, more easy to be mocked. If you don't have that level of enthusiasm, there is no support around you to tell you that the thing that you're trying to do -- the taking up the martial art, the talking to people on the internet -- is going to work.

I don't think that's what the experience of doing personal growth is like at all. In my experience, you're just swimming in uncertainty and fear and a lack of belief that it's even going to happen. You don't even have the promise of glory on the other side of it. You're doing Sam Harris's Waking Up meditation app, journaling, going to the gym, eating meat and fruit -- and if you don't have a good community of people that are also doing that, you're on your own.

Personal growth is kind of like a rocket ship taking off. As you take off, you've got a particular velocity. What you want is to find other people moving at the same velocity as you. But the quicker that you move, the fewer people are going to be like you.

The really bad realization is -- if you do it really well, you might have to let go of all your friends. And you might have to do that multiple times throughout your life. You find a group of people -- finally, I've landed. And then: I'm still going. I've outgrown this. I've got to do it again.

I think that explains why so few people make big changes. The temptation is always going to be to just go back to what's normal, go back to what I know.

America for all its faults is an enthusiastic and excitable country. You guys have kind of got permanent first-line cocaine energy about everything. It's a real infusing environment. It encourages me to do things, helps me to take risks. I love the fact that it makes me feel confident in doing difficult things.

When you get to the stage where you're faced with some personal growth decision, you're always going to have to make this value exchange: do I want to move forward on my own or do I want to go back with my friends?
