## Analysis & Key Insights

### What Stands Out

1. **He describes learning as the job.**
   The repeated move is “put myself in the hands of the best” rather than “protect my brand.”

2. **His Ridley story frames filmmaking as life-support.**
   The language is extreme on purpose: cinema isn’t product, it’s blood flow.

3. **He’s attentive to performances as layered communication.**
   In the Ledger/Hoffman bit, he talks about an “eye” layer that contradicts the spoken line — a practical way of naming subtext.

### Practical Takeaways (Non-Clinical)

- If you’re early, optimize for rooms that teach you (directors, sets, crews), not only outcomes.
- Build a grounding loop outside the industry narrative (pets, hobbies, friendships) so the work stays human.
- Study performers for “double signals” (line vs eyes/body) instead of only voice/intensity.
