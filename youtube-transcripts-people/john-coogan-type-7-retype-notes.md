# John Coogan Retype Notes: Enneagram Type 7

## Editorial decision

**User-confirmed assignment: John Coogan is Enneagram Type 7.** Treat this as the controlling typing decision for the John Coogan profile. Do not infer Type 3 from the old draft or from the shared TBPN brand.

Recommended working lock: **7w6, likely social/self-preservation, with a strong Type 5 competence pattern and a visible Type 3 achievement fix.** Core-type confidence is moderate rather than absolute because his technical depth makes Type 5 a serious alternative.

## The central correction

The former Type 3 case was partly built on speaker bleed in the joint Dialectic interview. These statements belong to **Jordi Hays**, not John Coogan:

- The daily-show maxim that mood and feelings do not matter because they have an obligation to go live.
- The confession that a profitable lifestyle business felt unsatisfying because nobody would check his LinkedIn and he did not feel in the big leagues.
- The claim that TBPN is designed for roughly 200,000 highly consequential viewers rather than a mass audience.
- The observation that technology is too inspired by technology and that TBPN finds more useful references outside the industry.

Do not reuse any of those as first-person evidence for John's psychology. In the same interview, Jordi explicitly distinguishes the partnership: John loves technology and goes deeper technically; Jordi loves business and deals.

Primary joint source: [Dialectic, “TBPN (John Coogan & Jordi Hays) — Inside Tech's Water Cooler”](https://jacksondahl.com/dialectic/tbpn)

## Direct motivational evidence for Type 7

John's own value hierarchy is the tiebreaker. In his 2024 *Audience of One* interview, he says:

> “Curiosity is my number one currency that I optimize for.”

He places curiosity above money, fame, health, and prestige. He says he has declined lucrative and prestigious opportunities when they offered no chance to learn anything new. His reaction to such a path is not envy or inadequacy; it sounds deadening to him.

Additional signals from the same section:

- Conventional advice to master one or two fields feels unavailable to him. He recognizes the danger of becoming a jack-of-all-trades, then says he will do what he finds interesting anyway.
- He would rather eat ramen after learning something new than eat steak after a boring day.
- When he finds himself weak at something, he enjoys attacking the new problem. He names coding and public speaking as skills he deliberately acquired.
- He describes comfort as psychologically intolerable and says he needs to be doing something uncomfortable or frightening. His revealing phrase is that he needs “control over chaos.”
- He frames curiosity as the upstream engine of innovation and worries that institutions beat it out of people.

Source: [Audience of One, episode 049 with John Coogan](https://poddtoppen.se/podcast/1671838630/audience-of-one/049-john-coogan-on-ai-ozempic-for-risk-taking-tech-media-curiosity), especially the curiosity discussion around 34 minutes.

## Repeated behavioral pattern

The same motivation recurs across his work:

1. **Serial curiosity, not serial status climbing.** Soylent, Lucy, documentary YouTube, *Power Law*, and TBPN let him enter new systems and learn them from the inside.
2. **Novelty inside discipline.** He can sustain a punishing daily structure because the news and questions change every day. He calls the prospect of performing the same scripted show repeatedly a waking nightmare.
3. **Humor lowers the cost of experimentation.** He says humor gives people permission to attempt something low-status. Soylent's name, fake yacht sponsors, “Temu Succession,” and strange on-air bits turn embarrassment into a launch surface.
4. **Breadth wins over specialization.** He learns by building, explaining, and moving into the next unanswered question. Even his documentary channel functioned as a serendipity engine.
5. **Optimism survives contact with risk.** He seeks difficult situations and sees discomfort as a way to stay awake rather than as proof of worthiness.
6. **Possibility generation.** In business interviews he rapidly produces models, edge cases, thought experiments, and new venture ideas. The mind keeps opening doors.

## Why he can look like Type 5

The Five case is real and should be treated honestly inside the Enneagram Rabbit Hole:

- John is obsessive about exact information. In an early telemarketing story, he could not proceed with the sales script until someone told him the exact value of the promised gift card.
- Unanswered technical questions can remain active in his mind for months or years.
- He has lifelong technical interests in cameras, cinematography, code, production systems, regulatory structures, and technological history.
- He goes deeper technically than Jordi and is comfortable occupying the analyst/explainer role.
- He protects a defined family/offline boundary outside the show.

The reason Type 7 still leads is directional. His knowledge drive expands into new experiences, skills, experiments, risks, and public projects. He explicitly rejects narrow specialization in favor of what remains interesting. A core Five would more often make mastery, sufficiency, privacy, or conservation the organizing principle. John uses depth in service of movement.

## Why he is not a core Type 3

John is highly competitive and achievement-oriented. He talks about impact, earning his stripes, and winning. TBPN's shared costume also makes both hosts appear status-maximizing. Those are genuine Three-fix signals.

They are not the clearest account of his ultimate currency. When forced to rank motives, John subordinates money, fame, status, and even comfort to learning and curiosity. He is also willing to do conspicuously weird or low-status things when they create an interesting experiment. The old draft's most status-confessional quote belonged to Jordi, which removes its strongest core-Three evidence.

## Type-challenge memo

- **Strongest case for the assigned type:** John explicitly prioritizes curiosity and interesting possibilities above money, fame, health, prestige, comfort, and specialist mastery.
- **Strongest case against:** His sustained technical depth, information compulsion, emotional reserve, and systems orientation make Type 5 plausible.
- **Named tiebreaker:** “I'm going to do what I find interesting, and that's that,” said while rejecting the specialist path even after acknowledging its advantages.
- **Behavior Type 7 does not fully explain:** His unusually exacting information needs and ability to remain with the same technical question for years.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high on Type 7; medium on 7w6 and SO/SP.

## Recommended article spine

- **Core tension:** curiosity versus confinement.
- **Psychological question:** How do you build something enduring without becoming trapped inside the thing you built?
- **Emotional thesis:** Boredom and comfortable certainty feel more threatening to John than risk. He keeps constructing demanding containers that give his mind somewhere new to go.
- **Opening frame:** The most revealing thing about John is not that he keeps winning. It is what he says he would trade the wins for: another day in which he learned something genuinely new.
- **Empathy turn:** His relentless pace can look like ambition without limits. From inside the Seven pattern, stillness is not automatically peace; it can feel like life narrowing.
- **Ending image:** A three-hour daily show that never repeats because the world has changed by tomorrow morning.
