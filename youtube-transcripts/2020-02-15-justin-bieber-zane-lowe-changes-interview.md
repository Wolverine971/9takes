## Interview Context

The interview was conducted to mark the release of "Changes," Justin Bieber's first album since 2015's "Purpose." Zane Lowe, Apple Music's global creative director and host of the interview series, sat down with Bieber for a deeply personal 43-minute conversation. The interview was filmed and broadcast as a video interview. Craig Sikora, who wrote a detailed analysis of the interview, noted that Bieber appeared physically uncomfortable at times, with "long stretches of zero eye contact, looking down, fidgeting," yet the content revealed meaningful personal growth. Sikora praised how Zane Lowe "pace[d] the conversation and gently flow[ed] between nuances within a topic" and resisted interrupting during Bieber's pauses, allowing him to work through difficult thoughts.

## Compiled Interview Quotes & Coverage

### On Cancelling the 2017 Purpose Tour

Bieber opened up about why he canceled the remaining dates of his 2017 Purpose World Tour. He revealed that his decision was driven by a combination of physical illness and emotional exhaustion.

> "I was really tired emotionally, physically, and I was sick and I didn't realize I was sick."

> "I had Epstein-Barr, which is like, it's called Mono. And then I have Lyme disease."

He explained that the exhaustion of being on tour compounded these health issues and made it impossible to continue performing at a stadium level:

> "I'm not going to be able to make it through that."

> "I felt shitty all the time."

### On the Dark Period & Suicidal Thoughts

When Zane Lowe asked about the tough period Bieber went through, during which he admitted to abusing drugs and suffering from depression, Bieber gave one of the most candid responses of the interview:

> "It was bad. I don't know if I'd be alive, for sure. It was dark, really dark."

This period followed his breakup from Selena Gomez and the cancellation of his tour. Bieber acknowledged battling drug abuse and depression during this time, expressing that without intervention, his trajectory was potentially fatal.

### On Human Nature & Internal Struggles

Bieber reflected philosophically on his own nature and the internal battles he faces:

> "I don't believe that humans are good."

> "I got to fight to not be that... That's not who I am."

These comments revealed Bieber's deeply introspective worldview, informed by his Christian faith — the belief that humans are inherently flawed and need to actively resist negative impulses. He discussed the constant tension between temptation and personal growth.

### On Faith Saving His Life

Bieber took a "deep dive" into his faith during his darkest period. He discussed how he came to understand "who Jesus really was" through the examples of other Christians in his life. He discussed grace and Christ with Lowe, explaining how faith became the anchor that pulled him out of his destructive patterns.

### On Marriage to Hailey Baldwin

Bieber expressed deep optimism and gratitude about his marriage to Hailey Baldwin (married November 2018, second ceremony September 2019):

> "I'm just excited to have babies with you [Hailey]... We are blessed, you know?"

He described his marriage as a transformative force in his life, alongside his faith. The interview revealed his commitment to personal change and his intention to be a better partner and eventually a good father.

### On Billie Eilish & Protecting Young Artists

Reflecting on his own experience of becoming world-famous as a teenager, Bieber expressed deep concern for Billie Eilish's well-being in the music industry. He emphasized the difficulties of early-career fame and indicated his willingness to support her if she ever needed it. He saw parallels between her trajectory and his own, and wanted to shield her from the same pitfalls he experienced.

### On Returning to Music & Ariana Grande at Coachella

Bieber discussed how fear had kept him away from performing for two years after canceling the Purpose tour. The turning point came when he joined Ariana Grande on stage at her headlining Coachella set in April 2019:

> "This is what I do. This is what I'm good at, and I don't need to run away from it."

The audience reaction during that surprise appearance reminded him of his capabilities and reignited his desire to perform and create music again. Bieber credited Grande for giving him the confidence boost he needed to return.

### On His Crew & Tour Plans

The interview revealed Bieber's intention to advocate for crew members on his upcoming tour — showing a maturity and awareness of the people around him that he may not have demonstrated earlier in his career.

### On the "Changes" Album

The album "Changes" was described as deeply personal, heavily influenced by his relationship with Hailey and his renewed sense of purpose. It was his first album in nearly five years, and the interview served as a way to contextualize the deeply personal nature of the music.
