## Analysis

### What This Episode Reveals

1. **Alex treats therapy as both healing and craft**: she watches _Couples Therapy_ like a sport, not only for voyeurism but as interview training (mirroring, leaning in, pacing).

2. **Boundary violations are framed as intimacy violations**: the therapist-recommendation story isn't only jealousy; it's about how a therapist holds the most private narrative version of you.

3. **She is unusually intentional about professional distance**: she describes building a bubble so celebrities and industry people read her as an interviewer/producer, not a fame-seeker.

4. **The Spotify dinner story is about status without hostility**: Dax calls them competitors; Alex agrees but highlights mutual rooting and the weirdness of being compared inside a platform ecosystem.

5. **Her ugly duckling history is tied to erasure and control**: she describes trying to delete old photos and hide the past, which is a clean explanation for the intensity of her public persona and aesthetic control.

6. **The soccer harassment segment is the most consequential**: she describes a power imbalance where attraction and resentment shaped playing time and safety, and she explicitly frames the aftermath as systemic (resigned without consequences).

7. **The documentary is positioned as a decision to stop the loop**: she describes calling the director and saying "I'm ready" once she realized how easily the system allows replication.

8. **Privilege is acknowledged but not used as an excuse to stay silent**: she repeatedly names the fear of retaliation and public fodder while choosing to speak anyway.

### Practical Takeaways

1. If you want professional respect, design your access patterns so others can't rewrite your motives.

2. If a system has no consequences, assume the behavior will repeat.

3. Boundaries are not just about saying no once; they're about maintaining the no.
