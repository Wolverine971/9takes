## Transcript (Parsed and Lightly Cleaned)

> Notes: Derived from [raw transcript](./_raw/aubrey-plaza-good-hang.raw.md). This section is a structured parse for
> readability (not a full verbatim transcript).

### 1) Margaret Qualley’s guest intro: why everyone loves Plaza

Qualley frames Plaza as universally loved (including “dad” appeal) and as an introverted artist in an extroverted
business. Plaza agrees with the “look at me / don’t look at me” tension.

### 2) Parks & Rec: early dynamic, play, and the April/Andy turn

Poehler and Plaza talk about early Parks energy and how Plaza’s on-set vibe mirrored April’s. They describe episodes
where play and chemistry became character direction, including a day of “just playing it” that helped define April and
Andy.

### 3) NYU, UCB obsession, and the dorm mice story

Plaza talks about arriving in New York and immediately chasing UCB classes alongside NYU film studies. The dorm story
becomes a highlight: mice taking over to the point she had to leave, including the “Thanksgiving Day parade” visual.

### 4) NBC page: tours, making things up, and getting pushed out

She recounts being an NBC page and giving tours while improvising facts — then getting fired / “encouraged to leave”
quickly.

### 5) Basketball and the WNBA

Plaza describes a real long-running love for basketball and talks about following the WNBA. She references growing up
in a school culture where basketball mattered and names specific players as part of that context.

### 6) Directing ambition + the Cinema Toast note

She explains she hasn’t directed a movie yet, but did direct an episode of Showtime’s _Cinema Toast_ (created by Jeff
Baena). She and Poehler riff on how some TV simply disappears.

### 7) Grief: the “Gorge” analogy and the ocean metaphor

Plaza talks about grief as ever-present — a visible “ocean” she can see at all times. She describes being functional
and grateful while acknowledging it as a daily struggle.

### 8) Comfort movies, teasing, and the “Bombardo” friend group

They talk comfort movies (including _You’ve Got Mail_) and what actually makes her laugh: long-running friendships,
teasing as a love language, and a women’s improv group that became a coven-like friend infrastructure (“Bombardo”).
