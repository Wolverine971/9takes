## Transcript (Parsed and Lightly Cleaned)

### 1) Delaware origin story (and staying weird about it)

Plaza and Maron riff on Delaware as a “small list of famous people” state. Plaza leans into the absurdity of being a
local celebrity there (including a story about being the grand marshal of a Wilmington parade).

### 2) NYU / UCB ecosystem and early creative proximity

Plaza describes being around a cluster of NYU film/comedy people (including Donald Glover and UCB-adjacent circles).
The emphasis is less “I had a plan” and more “we were all making things.”

### 3) The early break: audition tape logistics and acceleration

She recounts getting asked for a self-tape for a “secret project” while she was already on a small production, then
being flown out to read. Donald Glover helping with the tape is described as a practical friend move, not a lore
moment.

### 4) Comedy heroes and an actual starstruck moment

She names Rosie O’Donnell as a core early influence and tells a story about meeting Janeane Garofalo at UCB, stealing
the lineup sheet, and saying yes to an unfiltered cigarette just to have the moment.

### 5) Basketball, quiet competitiveness, and being underestimated

Plaza describes playing pickup at the Y, being better than people expect, and how men often get in their heads when
they’re “stuck” guarding her. She also mentions playing basketball with Gary Shandling and how awkward (and sweet)
that felt.
