## Analysis & Key Insights

### 1) “Delete the app” vs “change the device”

The clearest practical insight is that a simple “delete Instagram” move doesn’t work for some people. The guests make the analogy explicit: if you’re an addict, you don’t keep your dealer in your pocket — you remove the channel.

### 2) Noise is framed as anti-relationship

The conversation keeps returning to the idea that social media access collapses the old ability to “choose who you speak to.” That turns random strangers into constant relationship demands, which erodes trust and patience in real life.

### 3) The episode treats “persona” as a safety tactic

Even when they’re joking, there’s a real subtext: being public changes how you interpret everyone’s motives. That pushes people toward isolation, smaller circles, and tighter boundaries.

### 4) Faith language is used as a behavioral constraint

“God/devil” talk isn’t presented as abstract theology; it’s used as a concrete method to keep your actions aligned with your values when temptations and ego spikes hit.
