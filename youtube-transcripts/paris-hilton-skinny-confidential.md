### What This Episode Adds

1. **She’s explicit about incentives in the “troubled teen” industry.**
   The episode describes referral commissions, marketing brochures, and the way “troubled kid” labeling prevents kids
   from being believed.

2. **Her advocacy is framed as legislative work, not only storytelling.**
   She describes testifying and passing bills/laws, positioning her platform as leverage to build guardrails.

3. **The new threat model is digital.**
   Compared with older fame conversations (paparazzi, tabloids), this episode treats AI-generated explicit content as
   the next frontier of exploitation.

### Practical Takeaways (Non-Clinical)

- Reframe “too much” as a systems problem: design routines that absorb intensity.
- If you’re building a brand, separate “public character” from “private self” early, before the internet forces it.
- When law lags tech, assume abuse at scale and push for policy that targets incentives (platform profit + anonymity).

### 1) “The first influencer”: paparazzi-era visibility and accidental blueprint

They open by crediting Hilton as foundational to modern influencer/podcast culture. She says it wasn’t strategic at the
time — it was her life being watched — but she’s proud it became a blueprint.

### 2) ADHD reframed: creativity, future-focus, and the RSD downside

Paris talks about struggling in school, especially as ADHD in girls was minimized. She reframes ADHD as the source of
her entrepreneurial drive, while also naming overwhelm and rejection sensitivity dysphoria.

### 3) Fame and media scrutiny: surviving narrative pressure

They discuss how the fame machine treats women, how narratives stick, and what it takes to stay grounded when public
interpretation becomes constant.

### 4) Building the empire: work ethic, multi-tasking, and mentorship

She describes running many business verticals (media, products, IP) while still touring/DJing and parenting. She
references family work ethic and being mentored in business by her grandfather.

### 5) Wellness + longevity: red light, sauna, peptides, and “live forever” energy

She talks about routines and experiments (red light bed/sauna, IVs/peptides/biohacking) and even building a longevity
center — treating wellness as both fascination and output-enabler.

### 6) Provo and the “emotional growth school” pipeline: marketing, commissions, and disbelief

She explains how parents were shown a positive “growth school” story while consultants were incentivized to refer kids.
She describes trauma, shame, and how kids are labeled “troubled,” making them easy to dismiss.

### 7) Advocacy and policy: consequences, oversight, and passing laws

She describes speaking out, helping expose the scale of the industry, and working with lawmakers. She references
testifying and passing federal bills and state laws aimed at protecting children.

### 8) AI-generated explicit content: the next legislative fight

She describes working with legislators and advocates on laws to make AI-generated explicit content and deepfake videos
illegal, emphasizing how fast tech is moving relative to legal protection.

### 9) Love and partnership: safety, confidence, and shifting mindsets

They touch on her current relationship as stabilizing and discuss quieter confidence and boundary-oriented decision
making.

### 10) _Infinite Icon_: archive digging, music as lifeline, and telling the 2000s story differently

She describes creating the documentary via personal archive footage (camcorder, years of video), framing it as
therapeutic and as part of a “trilogy” with earlier projects.
