## Core Themes

1. **Indie film is mostly willpower + friends.**
   Linklater frames filmmaking barriers as low (technically) but attention barriers as high (culturally).

2. **Nostalgia is double-edged.**
   He distinguishes “artistic nostalgia” (often harmless, motivating) from “political nostalgia” (often destructive).

### 1) Early life, family context, and a pivot away from athletics

Feinberg walks Linklater through birthplace, family background, and how early interests (including sports) shifted as
his path changed.

### 2) Making films: the same story across decades

Linklater argues the basics haven’t changed: you gather people who believe, make something original, and get it seen
through festivals and distribution — the tools are cheaper, the volume is higher.

### 3) Nostalgia as an artistic tool (and a political weapon)

He reflects on how nostalgia works in his films and why nostalgia can be dangerous when it becomes a political driver.
