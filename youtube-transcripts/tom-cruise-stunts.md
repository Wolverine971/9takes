## Analysis & Key Insights

### What Stands Out

1. **Eastwood's core principle is "calculated and competent."**
   He repeatedly rejects a macho "no fear" framing and replaces it with prep depth and repeatable process.

2. **Cruise is positioned as an outlier in training volume.**
   The interview claims unusually high prep loads before marquee stunts, with capability built stepwise.

3. **Risk conversations are concrete, not abstract.**
   Eastwood discusses removing hazards (distance to obstacles, terrain adjustments, sign placement) before execution.

4. **Safety is described as a chain, not a single person.**
   Weapon/set safety comments focus on handoff discipline and role clarity across departments.
